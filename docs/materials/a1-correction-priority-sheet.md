# A1 Correction-Priority Sheet
## Instructor-Only — French A1 and Spanish A1

---

## 1. Purpose

This sheet is for the instructor. It is not shared with students.

Its job is to answer one question in real time: **is this error worth stopping the student to correct right now?** Correcting everything produces silent, anxious students. Correcting nothing produces errors that calcify by Week 6. The priority levels below give you a consistent decision framework across all 24 sessions.

---

## 2. Correction Philosophy

**Correct errors that:**
- Block communication (the student was not understood)
- Directly contradict the current week's grammar target
- Will solidify into a permanent habit if not addressed now (e.g., *soy cansado*, adjective agreement, partitive after negation)

**Do not stop a student to correct:**
- Pronunciation alone, unless it caused a misunderstanding
- Errors from future weeks' content (note it; address it then)
- Errors in structures the student has only just been introduced to today

**How to correct:**
- One error per student per round — correct the most important one and move on
- Model the correct form, ask the student to repeat it once, then continue
- Praise genuine repair: *"Exactly — that's exactly what I was going to say"*
- Never correct the same error twice in the same correction moment; save the second occurrence for the next speaking turn

**What correction sounds like:**
- **Not:** *"That's wrong."*
- **Yes:** *"Estoy — try it again."* / *"Presque — *patiente*, avec un e."* / *"Good attempt — me gusta."*

---

## 3. Correction Priority Levels

| Level | Label | When to use | Action |
|---|---|---|---|
| **P1** | Correct immediately | Error contradicts this week's grammar target OR is a foundational error that will compound if not stopped now | Interrupt briefly, model correct form, ask student to repeat once |
| **P2** | After student finishes | Error is significant but mid-utterance correction would break fluency | Let the student finish, then return: *"Just one thing before we move on..."* |
| **P3** | Note for later | Error is from a prior week and the student can be reminded gently at the next homework debrief | Do not interrupt; note it; mention it in wrap-up or next warm-up |
| **P4** | Let pass at A1 | Error is from future course content, a register issue, or a minor spelling/pronunciation variation | Skip entirely; if student asks, say "great question — that's A2" |

**Rule of thumb:** If you corrected the same error last week and it is still appearing, escalate from P3 to P1 and correct it every time.

## 4. French A1 — Top Correction Priorities

| Error pattern | Correct form | First appears | Priority | Quick cue |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| *Je suis les yeux bleus* (être for features) | *J'ai les yeux bleus* | Week 2 | **P1** | "Avoir — you **have** eyes. Être is what you're like." |
| *Elle est patient* (missing feminine -e) | *Elle est patiente* | Week 2 | **P1** | "*Patiente* — she needs the e. Il est patient, elle est patiente." |
| *J'ai vingt-cinq* (no *ans*) | *J'ai vingt-cinq **ans*** | Week 2 | **P1** | "*Ans* always follows the number — every time." |
| *Je parles / Il parlez* (wrong -er endings) | *Je parle / il parle* | Week 3 | **P1** | "In speech, *je/tu/il parle* all sound the same. In writing, *tu* keeps the -s; *il/elle* never takes -z." |
| *Je ne pas parle français* (*ne...pas* placement error) | *Je ne **parle** pas français* | Week 3 | **P2** | "*Ne...pas* sandwiches the verb — *ne* before, *pas* after. Never split them around anything but the verb." |
| Dropping *ne* in writing | *Je ne travaille pas* | Week 3 | **P1** (writing) | "Both halves required in writing. Spoken French often drops *ne* — written French never does." |
| *Je me appelle* (no elision) | *Je m'appelle* | Week 4 | **P1** | "*Me* before a vowel becomes *m'* — always. Like 'an apple.'" |
| Reflexive pronoun omitted (*Je lève*) | *Je me lève* | Week 4 | **P1** | "Reflexive pronoun — say 'reflexive pronoun' as the cue. The pronoun is required." |
| *Je vais à le café* (no contraction) | *Je vais **au** café* | Week 5 | **P1** | "*à + le = au* — always. It is mandatory. You cannot say *à le.*" |
| *Je ne prends pas **du** café* (partitive after negation) | *Je ne prends pas **de** café* | Week 6 | **P1** | "After *pas*, always just *de* — no gender, no article. *Pas de café, pas de bière, pas d'eau.*" |
| *J'aime du cinéma* (partitive after opinion verb) | *J'aime **le** cinéma* | Week 6 | **P2** | "Opinion verbs — *aimer, adorer, préférer* — take the definite article. *J'aime le cinéma.* Not a quantity." |
| *Je joue **du** tennis* (wrong preposition) | *Je joue **au** tennis* | Week 10 | **P1** | "*Jouer à* — sports use *à* with contractions. *Faire de* for activities. Two different verbs, two different prepositions." |
| *Je ne fais pas **du** sport* | *Je ne fais pas **de** sport* | Week 10 | **P1** | "Same rule as Week 6 — *pas de*, always." |
| *J'ai mal à le dos* (no contraction) | *J'ai mal **au** dos* | Week 11 | **P1** | "Same *au* contraction as *je vais au cinéma*. *À + le = au.*" |
| *J'ai de la fièvre depuis trois jours* with past tense | Use **present** tense with *depuis* | Week 11 | **P1** | "Still sick now → present tense. *J'ai de la fièvre depuis trois jours.* Not *j'avais.*" |

## 5. Spanish A1 — Top Correction Priorities

| Error pattern | Correct form | First appears | Priority | Quick cue |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| *Soy cansado/a* (ser for a state) | *Estoy cansado/a* | Week 2 | **P1 — every occurrence through A1** | "*Estoy* — how you feel is always *estar*. No exceptions." |
| *Estoy de México* (estar for origin) | *Soy de México* | Week 2 | **P1** | "*Soy* — where you're from is always *ser*." |
| *Soy treinta años* (ser for age) | *Tengo treinta años* | Week 5 | **P1** | "Age uses *tener* in Spanish. *Tengo* X años." |
| *Estoy hambre* (estar for tener expression) | *Tengo hambre* | Week 5 | **P1** | "Hunger, thirst, cold, sleep — all *tener* expressions. *Tengo hambre.*" |
| *Yo gusto el café* (wrong gustar structure) | *Me gusta el café* | Week 6 | **P1** | "The coffee is the subject — *me gusta el café.* The pronoun before the verb is who is pleased." |
| *Me gusta los tacos* (wrong verb agreement) | *Me **gustan** los tacos* | Week 6 | **P1** | "The verb agrees with the thing liked. Plural tacos → *gustan.*" |
| *Me encanto el tenis* | *Me **encanta** el tenis* | Week 10 | **P1** | "Same as *gustar* — the sport is the subject. *Me encanta / me encantan.*" |
| *Juego **de** fútbol* | *Juego **al** fútbol* | Week 10 | **P1** | "*Jugar a* — sports take *a* with contraction. *Al* = *a + el*." |
| *Hago **al** yoga* | *Hago yoga* | Week 10 | **P1** | "*Hacer* + activity — no preposition, no article. Just *hago yoga.*" |
| *Me duele **mi** cabeza* | *Me duele **la** cabeza* | Week 11 | **P1** | "Definite article with body parts — not possessive. The *me* already says it's yours." |
| *Me duele los pies* (wrong agreement) | *Me **duelen** los pies* | Week 11 | **P1** | "Plural feet → *duelen.* Same rule as *gustan* — verb agrees with the subject noun." |
| *Voy trabajar mañana* (missing *a*) | *Voy **a** trabajar* | Week 9 | **P1** | "The *a* is required. *Ir + a + infinitivo.* Always." |
| *Hay el parque al lado* (hay for known noun) | *El parque **está** al lado* | Week 8 | **P2** | "*Hay* introduces something new. *Está* locates something specific and known. The park is known — use *está.*" |
| *Sé a María* (saber for a person) | *Conozco a María* | Week 8 | **P2** | "People → *conocer*. Facts and skills → *saber*. And only *saber* takes a *que* clause." |
| *Deberías **de** descansar* | *Deberías descansar* | Week 11 | **P2** | "No preposition between *deber/deberías* and the infinitive. Ever." |

## 6. Week-by-Week Correction Focus

| Week | French: correct this week | Spanish: correct this week | What NOT to overcorrect |
|---|---|---|---|
| **1** | *Je m'appelle* vs. *je suis* distinction; *tu* vs. *vous* register; core introduction phrases; pronunciation only when it causes a misunderstanding | Ser/estar firewall: redirect all *estar* questions to Week 2; vowel glides (*no* should cut clean, not glide) | Pronunciation alone; number formation beyond 1–20 |
| **2** | Adjective feminine -e (P1 every time — the habit must be built this week); *j'ai* for features | *Soy cansado* → *estoy*: correct every single occurrence; *estoy de México* → *soy* | Invariable color adjectives (*marron*); irregular plural -e patterns |
| **3** | *-er* verb endings (*je parle / il parle*, not *je parles / il parlez*); *ne...pas* both parts in writing | *Él hablas* → *él habla*; *soy cansado* still appearing — continue P1 correction | Subject pronoun overuse (*yo hablo*) — note gently; don't obsess |
| **4** | Elision: *m'appelle* not *me appelle*; reflexive pronoun omission (*je lève* → *je me lève*) | *Yo comeo* → *como*; *me levanta* → *me levanto* (pronoun matches subject) | Accent in *je me lève* (mention; don't drill); stem-change paradigm for *acostarse* |
| **5** | *à + le = au* contraction (mandatory, P1); *à pied* vs. *en voiture* distinction | *Soy treinta años* → *tengo treinta años*; *mi hermanos* → *mis hermanos* | Full set of direction phrases beyond *tout droit/à droite/à gauche*; *aux* (plural) |
| **6** | *Pas de* after negation — not *pas du/de la* (P1 every time); *j'aime le cinéma* not *j'aime du cinéma* | *Yo gusto el café* → *me gusta*; *me gusta los tacos* → *me gustan* (P1 every time) | *Je veux* vs. *je voudrais* register issue; all six *gustar* persons (me/te/le sufficient) |
| **7** | *Cet* before vowel (*cet imperméable*); *un kilo de fromage* not *du fromage* after quantity | Gender agreement on demonstratives (*esta/este*); color agreement (*azules* not *azulas*) | *Aquel/aquella* production; exhaustive regional vocabulary variants |
| **8** | *Il y a* vs. *être + location* distinction; *de + le = du* contraction; *savoir* vs. *connaître* | *Hay* vs. *está* distinction; *del* contraction; *saber* vs. *conocer* | *Venir de + infinitif/venir de + infinitivo* for recent past (A2); minor neighborhood adj errors |
| **9** | No *de* between modal and infinitive (*je dois travailler*, not *je dois de travailler*); *la semaine prochaine* feminine agreement | *Voy a trabajar* — the *a* is always required; *hay que* is impersonal — no subject ever | *Pensar + inf* production if rushed; complex multi-modal combining in one clause |
| **10** | *Jouer à / faire de* distinction (P1 every time); negation of *faire de* (*pas de sport*, not *pas du sport*) | *Juego al fútbol*; *hago yoga* (no preposition, no article); *me gustan los deportes* agreement | Pronunciation of *je préfère* (accent change — note it; don't drill); *autant que* comparison |
| **11** | *Avoir mal au dos* — contraction (same as *au cinéma*); *depuis + présent*, not passé composé | *Me duele la cabeza* — definite article, not possessive; *doler* agreement (*duele/duelen*); *llevar* for duration not *de* | Inner/outer ear distinction (*l'oído/la oreja*); regional health vocabulary |
| **12** | Adjective agreement in all contexts (this is the A2 forward-pointer for French A1); *aller + infinitif* construction | Ser/estar distinction in all role play contexts; *gustar/doler* agreement; gender agreement on adjectives | Passé composé (French) / Pretérito (Spanish) — not A1 content; don't correct what students attempt from A2 |

## 7. Live Correction Phrases

### French — instructor says to the student:
| Situation | Phrase |
|---|---|
| Gentle correction with repeat | *"Presque — **patiente**, avec un e. Essayez encore."* |
| Restating the rule briefly | *"Après **pas**, c'est toujours **de** — pas de café, pas de bière. Continuez."* |
| Signaling a common error | *"Attention — **j'ai** pour les yeux, pas *je suis*."* |
| After the student self-corrects | *"Exactement — c'est ça."* |
| Deflecting a future-topic question | *"Bonne question — c'est le sujet de l'A2. Pour l'instant, utilisez la règle de cette semaine."* |

### Spanish — instructor says to the student:
| Situation | Phrase |
|---|---|
| Gentle correction with repeat | *"Casi — **estoy** cansado, no **soy**. Inténtalo otra vez."* |
| Restating the rule briefly | *"**Tengo** — la edad usa **tener** en español. Treinta años."* |
| Signaling the gustar structure | *"El pronombre va primero — **me gusta el café**, no *yo gusto*."* |
| After the student self-corrects | *"Exacto — eso es."* |
| Deflecting a future-topic question | *"Buena pregunta — eso es A2. Por ahora, usa la regla de esta semana."* |

### English — instructor explains to students:
| Situation | What to say |
|---|---|
| Why you're stopping them | *"I'm going to interrupt you here because this is the exact structure we're building this week — I want to get it right before it becomes a habit."* |
| Why you're not correcting everything | *"I'm letting that one go for now — we haven't covered it yet. I'll flag it when we get there."* |
| After a week's repeated error | *"You've been using [X] instead of [Y] — let's fix that habit today. [X] → [Y]. Try it."* |
| Before a speaking round | *"I'll correct one thing per person per round — the most important one. Everything else I'm noting."* |
| Setting expectations | *"A1 is about communication, not perfection. I'll correct what matters most. The rest is for A2."* |

## 8. What to Let Pass at A1

These errors are **not correction targets** at A1 level. Note them; do not address them in live class.

| Category | Examples | Why it passes |
|---|---|---|
| Pronunciation nuances | English-inflected vowels in French/Spanish; imprecise nasal vowels in French; rolled *r* not achieved | Does not block communication; improves across sessions with repetition |
| Accent marks in writing | Missing accents in *préfère*, *está*, *lève* — when spoken correctly | Mechanics, not grammar; mention once; do not drill |
| *H aspiré* vs. *h muet* (French) | *Cet hôpital* correct; *le hibou* vs. *l'hibou* — too fine for A1 | Applies to rare vocabulary; does not compound |
| *Vosotros* forms (Spanish) | Students defaulting to *ustedes* for all contexts | *Ustedes* is the international standard for this course; *vosotros* is a regional note |
| Register (formal/informal) | *Je veux* vs. *je voudrais*; *quiero* vs. *quisiera* | Both grammatically correct; register is an A2+ refinement |
| Complex word order | French adverb placement beyond the core examples; Spanish complex indirect object order | Only address when it causes misunderstanding |
| Regional vocabulary variants | French: *un portable* vs. *un téléphone*; Spanish: *patata/papa*, *vaqueros/jeans* | Mention in cultural context; do not penalize the regional variant |
| -ir and -re verbs (French) | *Finir, vendre* paradigm | Not part of French A1 core curriculum; extension only |
| Passé composé (French) | Any student attempt at past tense before Week 12 | A2 content; praise the attempt; say "A2" |
| Pretérito (Spanish) | Any student attempt at simple past before Week 12 | A2 content; praise the attempt; say "A2" |
| Conditional (except set phrases) | Any use beyond *je voudrais / quisiera* | A2 content |
| Subjunctive (both languages) | *Il faut que + subjonctif*; *para que + subjonctivo* | B1+ content; redirect to the A1 form (*il faut + infinitif*) |
| Plural agreement in speech (French) | *Ils sont grands* — the -s is inaudible; only matters in writing | Not a speaking error; correct in writing only |
| Extended *de + article* contractions | Beyond *du/de la/de l'* in French; beyond *del* in Spanish | Teach core contractions; do not lecture on full paradigm |

## 9. Final Performance Correction Rules

These rules apply to Sessions 23 and 24 (role plays) for both French A1 and Spanish A1.

**During the role play (Part A):**
- **Do not correct.** Note 2–3 items during the role play; do not speak them aloud.
- If the student freezes completely, ask an easy question to restart production — this is support, not correction.
- If the student makes a repeated ser/estar error (Spanish) or adjective agreement error (French), note it for the A2 forward-pointer. Do not stop the role play.

**After the role play — feedback formula:**
1. **Group feedback first** (2 min): Name 2–3 things the whole group did well. Be specific about structures, not just effort.
2. **Individual feedback** (30–45 sec per student):
   - **Strength** — one specific structure or phrase that worked
   - **Keep doing** — one habit they may not have noticed
   - **A2 forward pointer** — one item, phrased forward: *"For A2, we'll work on..."*
3. **Written feedback on Part B** (post-session, within one week): One paragraph. What worked first. One grammar point. One A2 forward pointer. Encouraging close. No red marks. No grades.

**Most likely A2 forward-pointers:**
- **French A1:** Adjective agreement (the most persistent error across the course)
- **Spanish A1:** Ser vs. estar (the most important distinction that continues into A2 and beyond)

**What the feedback formula is not:**
- Not a performance review
- Not a grade
- Not a comparison between students
- Not an occasion to list every error observed

## 10. Instructor Reminder

**At A1, the goal is communication — not perfection.**

A student who says *estoy de México* and is immediately understood has communicated successfully. The error matters because it will compound, not because it broke the exchange. Correct it to protect the habit, not to penalize the student.

**Confidence is the most fragile skill in a language classroom.** A student who stops themselves mid-sentence because they are afraid to make a mistake is harder to teach than one who makes errors freely. Your correction style directly shapes this. Correct often enough that the right forms stick; correct gently enough that students keep talking.

**Use P1 corrections as gifts, not interruptions:**
- *"Patiente — avec un e. Essayez encore."*
- *"Estoy — try it again."*
- *"Me gusta — the coffee is the subject."*

These are the moments students remember. A clear, calm correction that a student immediately repeats correctly is more valuable than ten uncorrected instances.

**At the end of Week 12, a well-taught A1 student should:**
- Speak with effort and make errors — and not be embarrassed by that
- Self-correct when they hear themselves use the wrong verb
- Know what they have learned and know that A2 is the next step
- Feel proud of what they can now do in the language

That outcome is not possible without consistent, prioritized correction. It is also not possible with constant interruption. The priority levels in this sheet exist to give you that balance in real time.
