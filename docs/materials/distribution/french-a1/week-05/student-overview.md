## What You Will Learn This Week

Week 5 introduces *aller* — to go — one of the most useful verbs in French. It is also the most irregular: it doesn't look anything like its infinitive. You will memorize it this week because you will use it constantly.

You will also learn to say where you are going, how you are getting there, and how to give and understand simple directions. These are real-world skills that apply immediately whether you are traveling, navigating a French-speaking city, or just talking about your week.

---

## What You Will Be Able to Say by the End of Week 5

By the end of Session 10, you should be able to have a short real-world exchange like this:

> **Vous:** *Excusez-moi, je cherche la pharmacie.*
> **Passant:** *La pharmacie ? Elle est à côté du supermarché — vous allez tout droit, puis vous tournez à droite.*
> **Vous:** *C'est loin d'ici ?*
> **Passant:** *Non, c'est à cinq minutes à pied.*

And talk about your week:

> *Le matin, je vais au bureau en métro. À midi, je vais souvent à la boulangerie — c'est juste en face de mon bureau. Le week-end, je vais au cinéma ou au parc. Samedi, je vais à Paris en train.*

## Aller — Learn It by Heart

*Aller* (to go) is irregular. There is no shortcut — you memorize these forms:

| Subject | Aller |
|---|---|
| je | **vais** |
| tu | **vas** |
| il / elle | **va** |
| nous | **allons** |
| vous | **allez** |
| ils / elles | **vont** |

Say them out loud: *vais, vas, va, allons, allez, vont.* Three times. Then cover the page.

---

## The à + Place Rule

When you say where you are going, *à* changes depending on the gender of the place:

| If the place is... | Use | Example |
|---|---|---|
| masculine (*le*) | **au** | *Je vais **au** café.* |
| feminine (*la*) | **à la** | *Je vais **à la** banque.* |
| starts with a vowel or silent h | **à l'** | *Je vais **à l'**hôpital.* |
| plural (*les*) | **aux** | *Je vais **aux** restaurants.* |

**You cannot say *à le café* in French.** It must always contract to *au.* This is not optional — it's just how the language works.

The easiest way to learn this: when you learn a new place, always learn its gender at the same time. *Le* café → I go *au* café. *La* banque → I go *à la* banque.

---

## One Thing That Surprises Students This Week

*Aller* is used for the near future too. When you say *Je vais travailler demain,* you're saying "I'm going to work tomorrow." You are using *aller + infinitive* — a structure you will study more in Week 9. For now, just notice that *je vais* + a place or an infinitive both work.

## What to Practice Between Sessions

**After Session 9 — before Session 10:**

1. **Say the aller conjugation from memory.** *Vais, vas, va, allons, allez, vont.* No looking.
2. **Write 5 sentences.** Where are you going this week, and how? *Je vais au cinéma samedi. Je vais au bureau en métro.*
3. **Say them out loud.** No notes on the third try.

**After Session 10 — before Session 11:**

1. **Practice the directions phrases.** *Tout droit, à droite, à gauche.* Say each with a clear hand gesture.
2. **Write a 4-line dialogue.** Someone asks where the pharmacy is; you give directions. Exchange written and spoken.
3. **Review your prepositions.** Can you say where 5 places in your neighborhood are, using *près de, à côté de, en face de*?
