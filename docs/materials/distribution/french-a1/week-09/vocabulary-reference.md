## Aller + Infinitif — The Near Future

*Aller + infinitif* is the most common way to talk about what you are going to do. Use the conjugated form of *aller* + any infinitive.

| Structure | Example | English |
|---|---|---|
| *je vais + inf* | *Je vais travailler demain.* | I'm going to work tomorrow. |
| *tu vas + inf* | *Tu vas appeler ta mère ce soir?* | Are you going to call your mum tonight? |
| *il/elle va + inf* | *Elle va voyager la semaine prochaine.* | She's going to travel next week. |
| *nous allons + inf* | *Nous allons dîner ensemble.* | We're going to have dinner together. |
| *vous allez + inf* | *Vous allez visiter Paris?* | Are you going to visit Paris? |
| *ils/elles vont + inf* | *Ils vont prendre le train.* | They're going to take the train. |

**Note:** *Aller* conjugation is already known from Week 5 (*vais/vas/va/allons/allez/vont*). Week 9 simply adds the infinitive after it.

**Negation:** Wrap *ne...pas* around *aller* — the infinitive stays outside.
- *Je ne vais pas travailler demain.* — I'm not going to work tomorrow.
- *Elle ne va pas venir.* — She's not going to come.

## Devoir + Infinitif — Obligation

*Devoir* is new in Week 9. It expresses obligation: *I have to, I must, I need to.*

| Pronoun | Devoir | Example |
|---|---|---|
| je | **dois** | *Je dois travailler demain.* — I have to work tomorrow. |
| tu | **dois** | *Tu dois appeler le médecin.* — You need to call the doctor. |
| il/elle | **doit** | *Il doit partir à 8h.* — He has to leave at 8. |
| nous | **devons** | *Nous devons finir ce soir.* — We need to finish tonight. |
| vous | **devez** | *Vous devez réserver à l'avance.* — You must book in advance. |
| ils/elles | **doivent** | *Ils doivent travailler le week-end.* — They have to work weekends. |

**Note on irregular forms:** *je dois, tu dois* (identical); *il doit; nous devons; vous devez; ils doivent.* Watch for *ils doivent* — the -ent is silent.

## Vouloir + Infinitif — Desire / Wish

*Vouloir* was introduced in Week 6. In Week 9 it appears in plans contexts alongside *devoir* and *pouvoir.*

| Pronoun | Vouloir | Example |
|---|---|---|
| je | **veux** | *Je veux aller au cinéma ce soir.* — I want to go to the cinema tonight. |
| tu | **veux** | *Tu veux venir avec nous?* — Do you want to come with us? |
| il/elle | **veut** | *Elle veut prendre l'avion.* — She wants to take the plane. |
| nous | **voulons** | *Nous voulons visiter Lyon.* — We want to visit Lyon. |
| vous | **voulez** | *Vous voulez boire quelque chose?* — Would you like something to drink? |
| ils/elles | **veulent** | *Ils veulent partir tôt.* — They want to leave early. |

*Je voudrais* — the polite conditional form introduced in Week 6. Still useful here: *Je voudrais réserver une table pour ce soir.*

## Pouvoir + Infinitif — Ability and Permission

*Pouvoir* was introduced in Week 6. In Week 9 it is used to express ability, possibility, and what you can or can't do in plans.

| Pronoun | Pouvoir | Example |
|---|---|---|
| je | **peux** | *Je peux te rejoindre à 18h.* — I can meet you at 6pm. |
| tu | **peux** | *Tu peux venir demain?* — Can you come tomorrow? |
| il/elle | **peut** | *Elle ne peut pas venir ce soir.* — She can't come tonight. |
| nous | **pouvons** | *Nous pouvons réserver en ligne.* — We can book online. |
| vous | **pouvez** | *Vous pouvez payer par carte?* — Can you pay by card? |
| ils/elles | **peuvent** | *Ils peuvent arriver à 20h.* — They can arrive at 8pm. |

## Three Modals in Combination — Plans and Constraints

The richness of Week 9 is using all three modals together to express what you have to do, want to do, and can or can't do:

| Pattern | Example | English |
|---|---|---|
| *devoir* + *mais* + *vouloir* | *Je dois travailler, mais je veux aller au cinéma.* | I have to work, but I want to go to the cinema. |
| *vouloir* + *mais* + *ne pas pouvoir* | *Je veux venir, mais je ne peux pas.* | I want to come, but I can't. |
| *pouvoir* + *si* | *Je peux venir si tu veux.* | I can come if you like. |
| *devoir* + confirmation | *Tu dois vraiment partir?* | Do you really have to leave? |
| *aller* + *devoir* | *Je vais devoir annuler.* — I'm going to have to cancel. | I'm going to have to cancel. |

## Pronunciation Notes

| Form | Notes |
|---|---|
| *je dois* | /ʒə dwa/ — "dwa", not "doiz" |
| *ils doivent* | /il dwav/ — final -ent is silent |
| *je vais aller* | /ʒə vɛ ale/ — liaison: *vais z-aller* if linking quickly, though at A1 pause is fine |
| *la semaine prochaine* | /la sɛmɛn prɔʃɛn/ — four syllables; students often rush |
| *je ne peux pas* | /ʒə nə pø pa/ — the final *pas* has no liaison |
