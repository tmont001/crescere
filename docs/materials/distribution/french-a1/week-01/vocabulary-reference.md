| Bonsoir | Good evening | bon-SWAAR | *Bonsoir ! Comment allez-vous ?* |
| Salut | Hi / Bye (informal) | sa-LÜ | *Salut, ça va ?* |
| Au revoir | Goodbye | oh reh-VWAAR | *Au revoir et à bientôt !* |
| À bientôt | See you soon | ah byaN-TOH | *À bientôt !* |

| À demain | See you tomorrow | ah deh-MaN | *À demain, bonne soirée !* |

## Introductions and Key Phrases

| French | English | Pronunciation note | Example |
|---|---|---|---|
| Je m'appelle... | My name is... | zheh ma-PELL | *Je m'appelle Claire.* (fixed phrase — do not change the structure) |
| Je suis de... | I am from... | zheh swee deh | *Je suis de New York.* |
| J'habite à... | I live in... | zha-BEET ah | *J'habite à Paris.* |
| Je suis [profession/nationality] | I am [profession/nationality] | | *Je suis médecin.* (no article) |
| Et toi ? / Et vous ? | And you? | ay TWAH / ay VOO | *Je m'appelle Sophie. Et toi ?* |
| Moi, c'est... | I'm... (casual intro) | mwah say | *Moi, c'est Lucie.* |

## *Être* — Present Tense (Core)

| French | English |
|---|---|
| je suis | I am |
| tu es | you are (informal) |
| il est / elle est | he is / she is |
| on est | one is / we are (informal) |
| nous sommes | we are |
| vous êtes | you are (formal or plural) |
| ils sont / elles sont | they are (masc.) / they are (fem.) |

**Pronunciation reminders:**
- *sommes* — the final -s is silent
- *êtes* — the hat accent (ê) sounds like the open e in "bed"
- *sont* — nasal vowel (rhymes with "on" in French)

## Numbers 1–100

Numbers 1–20 are the Session 1 focus. Practice 21–100 at your own pace as you go.

### 1–20

| | | | |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 — un | 6 — six | 11 — onze | 16 — seize |
| 2 — deux | 7 — sept | 12 — douze | 17 — dix-sept |
| 3 — trois | 8 — huit | 13 — treize | 18 — dix-huit |

### 90–99 (French counts 80+10)

## Pronunciation Reference: The Key French Sounds

Your instructor will walk you through these in class. Use this table as a reference when you are practicing at home.

| Sound | Description | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| Nasal *an/en* | Air through nose; no final n sound spoken | *dans, blanc, enfant, en* |
| Nasal *in/ain* | Shorter nasal; mouth more open | *vin, pain, demain, bien* |
| Nasal *on* | Rounded nasal; lips forward | *bon, nom, on, non* |
| French r | Uvular — from the back of the throat | *rouge, Paris, merci, restaurant* |
| Silent final consonant | Most final consonants are silent | *petit, vous, grand, américain* |
| Silent -e | Final -e is silent in most words | *parle, rouge, France, table* |
| Liaison | Final consonant spoken before a vowel | *vous êtes, ils ont, les enfants* |
