**Crescere | Beginner French** | Session 23

Complete before Session 24 | Estimated time: 25–30 minutes

## Overview

Session 24 is your final performance. This homework prepares you for it. Everything here is directly useful for tomorrow.

## Part 1 — Role Play Preparation (10 min out loud)

Choose one of the three scenario options from the final performance guide:

- **Option A:** You meet someone at a café or event. You get talking; you make a plan.
- **Option B:** You are helping a French-speaking visitor navigate your city.
- **Option C:** You meet at a language exchange. You both describe your lives and make a plan.

Once you have chosen, practice the four topic areas out loud — not in your head.

**Topic area 1 — Introduction (2 min)**
Say aloud: your name, where you are from, where you live, what you do, how long you have been learning French. Then add one personal detail (family, hobbies, something you love). Say this three times until it flows.

**Topic area 2 — Making a plan (2 min)**
Say aloud: propose an activity, suggest a day and time, ask if the other person is free, agree on a meeting point. Use *aller + infinitif* for your suggestion. Say this twice.

**Topic area 3 — Directions (2 min)**
Choose a real or invented place in your city. Give directions from a café to that place — at least 4 direction steps. Use: *tout droit, à gauche, à droite, à côté de, en face de.* Say this twice.

**Topic area 4 — Describe a place (2 min)**
Describe your home or neighbourhood in 3–4 sentences. Include: *il y a..., c'est à... minutes, j'aime/j'adore...* Say this twice.

**Your repair phrases** — know these cold:
- *Pouvez-vous répéter, s'il vous plaît?*
- *Comment dit-on [word] en français?*
- *Je cherche le mot pour...*

## Part 2 — Written Message Draft (12 min)

Write your Part B written message. This should be 8–10 sentences.

**Checklist — before you write:**
- [ ] I know what job / life details I will use (use your real life)
- [ ] I know 2 sentences about my daily routine
- [ ] I know one thing I enjoy doing (hobby, leisure, food)
- [ ] I know how to suggest meeting up using *aller + infinitif* or *je voudrais*
- [ ] I know one question to ask the other person

**After writing, check:**
- [ ] Article contractions: *au* not *à le*; *du* not *de le*
- [ ] Adjective agreement: *une ville intéressante*, not *un ville intéressant*
- [ ] *Aller + infinitif* structure is correct: *je vais visiter*, not *je vais à visiter*
- [ ] At least one partitive: *je bois du café, je mange des croissants*
- [ ] At least one question at the end

---

## Part 3 — Quick Grammar Check (5 min)

Write these from memory — no notes:

1. The full conjugation of *aller* (6 forms)
2. The full conjugation of *devoir* (6 forms)
3. The four article contractions: *au, aux, du, des* — one example each

**Answer key:**
1. *vais / vas / va / allons / allez / vont*
2. *dois / dois / doit / devons / devez / doivent*
3. *au cinéma / aux États-Unis / du café / des croissants*

If you missed any of these, write them out five times. These forms will appear in your role play and written message.

## Extension (Optional)

If you want to go further:
- Record yourself doing the role play practice out loud. Listen back. What sounds natural? What feels hesitant? Adjust.
- Write a second version of your introduction — a more formal one (using *vous*) and a more casual one (using *tu*). Know which register you will use in Session 24.
- Try a second scenario: if you chose Option A for your main practice, run through Option B briefly to make sure you have flexibility.
