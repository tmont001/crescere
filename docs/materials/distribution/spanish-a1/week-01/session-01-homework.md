## Part 1 — The Spanish Alphabet (8 min)

The Spanish alphabet has 27 letters — the same as English plus **ñ**. Read through the list once. Then try to say the full alphabet out loud. It is fine to look.

| Letter | Spanish name | Letter | Spanish name |
|---|---|---|---|
| A | a | N | ene |
| B | be | Ñ | eñe |
| C | ce | O | o |
| D | de | P | pe |
| E | e | Q | cu |
| F | efe | R | erre |
| G | ge | S | ese |
| H | hache (silent) | T | te |
| I | i | U | u |
| J | jota | V | uve / ve |
| K | ka | W | doble uve |
| L | ele | X | equis |
| M | eme | Y | ye / i griega |
| | | Z | zeta |

**Pronunciation notes:**
- **ñ (eñe):** a nasal y-sound — *mañana, español, año*
- **j (jota):** a strong h-sound from the back of the throat — *jefe, Juan*
- **h (hache):** always silent in Spanish — *hola, hablar, hotel*
- **r (erre):** when you see *r* alone in the middle of a word, it is a tap (like the *d* in "butter"); when written *rr* or at the start of a word, it is a trill

You do not need to memorize this before Session 2. Read it once so you know it exists. You will use it to spell words aloud later in the course.

**Practice:** Spell your first name and last name out loud using Spanish letter names.

## Part 3 — Ser Out Loud (5 min)

Cover the table below and try to say all six forms of *ser* from memory. Then check yourself.

| Subject | Ser |
|---|---|
| yo | soy |
| tú | eres |
| él / ella / usted | es |
| nosotros/as | somos |
| vosotros/as | sois (Spain only) |
| ellos / ellas / ustedes | son |

**Practice:** Say the full conjugation out loud three times. On the third time, cover the table and say it from memory.

Then say each of these out loud:
- *Yo soy [your name].*
- *Ella es de México.*
- *Nosotros somos estudiantes.*
- *Ellos son de Colombia.*

---

## Part 4 — Written Component (5 min)

Write the correct form of *ser* in each sentence. Do not look it up.

1. Yo ___ americano/a.
2. Ella ___ de Buenos Aires.
3. Nosotros ___ profesores.
4. ¿Tú ___ de España?
5. Ustedes ___ estudiantes.

**Answers:** 1. soy · 2. es · 3. somos · 4. eres · 5. son

## Optional Extension

If you finish early:

**Write about five people.** Use *ser* to write 1–2 sentences about five different people — real, invented, or characters you know. Use different forms of *ser*.
- Example: *Tom Hanks es de California. Él es actor americano.*

**Listen:** Find any 2-minute clip in Spanish — a song, a trailer, a YouTube video. Just listen. Notice the five vowels: do you hear how clean and consistent *a, e, i, o, u* sound? You do not need to understand the words.
