## Hay / No Hay

*Hay* means "there is" or "there are." It is invariable — the same form for singular and plural.

| Spanish | English |
|---|---|
| Hay una cocina. | There is a kitchen. |
| Hay tres habitaciones. | There are three bedrooms. |
| No hay garaje. | There isn't a garage. |
| No hay jardín. | There's no garden. |
| ¿Hay ascensor? | Is there a lift? |
| ¿Cuántas habitaciones hay? | How many rooms are there? |

**Key distinction — *hay* vs. *estar*:**
- *Hay* = existence ("there is") — introduces something new, unspecified.
- *Está / están* = location — tells us where a specific, known thing is.

| Spanish | English | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Hay un parque cerca de aquí. | There's a park near here. | — existence; first mention |
| El parque está al lado del colegio. | The park is next to the school. | — location of a known specific park |
| Hay una cocina. | There is a kitchen. | — announcing it exists |
| La cocina está al fondo del pasillo. | The kitchen is at the end of the corridor. | — locating a known kitchen |

### De + El = Del (Contraction)

When *de* is followed by the masculine article *el*, they contract to *del*:
- *al lado **del** salón* (not *al lado de el salón*)
- *encima **del** armario*
- *enfrente **del** baño*
- *al fondo **del** pasillo*

*De + la, de + los, de + las* do NOT contract: *al lado de la cocina, encima de las sillas.*

## High-Frequency Irregular Verbs

### Hacer — to do / to make

| Subject | Hacer |
|---|---|
| yo | **hago** |
| tú | haces |
| él / ella / usted | hace |
| nosotros/as | hacemos |
| vosotros/as | hacéis |
| ellos / ellas / ustedes | hacen |

**Only the yo form is irregular:** *hago.* All other forms follow regular -er endings.

**Key uses:**
- *¿Qué haces normalmente los sábados?* — What do you usually do on Saturdays?
- *Hago la compra los viernes.* — I do the shopping on Fridays.
- *Hace frío / calor / sol / viento.* — It's cold / hot / sunny / windy. (weather)
- *Hace dos años.* — Two years ago. (time expressions)

---

### Venir — to come

| Subject | Venir |
|---|---|
| yo | **vengo** |
| tú | **vienes** |
| él / ella / usted | **viene** |
| nosotros/as | venimos |
| vosotros/as | venís |
| ellos / ellas / ustedes | **vienen** |

**Irregular:** *yo → vengo* (irregular); *tú/él/ellos* have e→ie stem change. *Nosotros/vosotros* keep the regular stem.

**Key uses:**
- *¿De dónde vienes?* — Where do you come from?
- *Vengo de México.* — I'm from Mexico.
- *¿A qué hora vienes mañana?* — What time are you coming tomorrow?

---

### Decir — to say / to tell

| Subject | Decir |
|---|---|
| yo | **digo** |
| tú | **dices** |
| él / ella / usted | **dice** |
| nosotros/as | decimos |
| vosotros/as | decís |
| ellos / ellas / ustedes | **dicen** |

**Irregular:** *yo → digo*; e→i stem change in tú/él/ellos. *Nosotros/vosotros* keep regular stem.

**Key uses:**
- *¿Cómo se dice "neighbourhood" en español?* — How do you say "neighbourhood" in Spanish?
- *¿Qué dice el periódico?* — What does the newspaper say?
- *Mi vecina dice que el barrio es muy tranquilo.* — My neighbor says the neighborhood is very quiet.

---

### Saber — to know (a fact / how to do something)

| Subject | Saber |
|---|---|
| yo | **sé** |
| tú | sabes |
| él / ella / usted | sabe |
| nosotros/as | sabemos |
| vosotros/as | sabéis |
| ellos / ellas / ustedes | saben |

**Only yo is irregular:** *sé* (with accent to distinguish from *se* the reflexive pronoun). All other forms are regular.

**Uses:**
- *Sé dónde está el parque.* — I know where the park is. (fact)
- *Ella sabe hablar chino.* — She knows how to speak Chinese. (skill)
- *No sé.* — I don't know. (essential phrase)

---

### Conocer — to know / to be familiar with (a person or place)

| Subject | Conocer |
|---|---|
| yo | **conozco** |
| tú | conoces |
| él / ella / usted | conoce |
| nosotros/as | conocemos |
| vosotros/as | conocéis |
| ellos / ellas / ustedes | conocen |

**Only yo is irregular:** *conozco* (yo + -zco ending). All other forms are regular.

**Uses:**
- *Conozco Madrid muy bien.* — I know Madrid well. (familiarity with a place)
- *¿Conoces a Juan?* — Do you know Juan? (acquaintance with a person — personal *a*)
- *No conozco este barrio.* — I don't know this neighborhood.

### Saber vs. Conocer

| Use *saber* | Use *conocer* |
|---|---|
| Facts: *Sé que vive aquí.* | People: *Conozco a Pedro.* |
| Skills: *Sé nadar.* | Places: *Conozco Buenos Aires.* |
| Information: *¿Sabes a qué hora cierra?* | Familiarity: *Conozco bien este restaurante.* |

**The personal *a*:** When *conocer* refers to a person, the personal *a* is required before the person's name: *Conozco **a** María.* Not required with places: *Conozco Madrid.*

## Pronunciation Notes

- **Hay:** /aj/ — rhymes with English "eye"; one syllable; the h is silent
- **Habitación:** /aβitaˈθjon/ (Spain) / /aβitaˈsjon/ (Latin America) — five syllables; stress on the last syllable
- **Encima de:** /enˈsima de/ — three syllables for *encima*; stress on the second
- **Debajo de:** /deˈβaxo de/ — three syllables for *debajo*; the *j* = /x/ (like Scottish "loch")
- **Hacer:** /aˈθeɾ/ (Spain) / /aˈseɾ/ (Latin America) — two syllables; *h* is always silent
- **Hago:** /ˈaɣo/ — two syllables; the *h* is silent; stress on first syllable
- **Vengo:** /ˈbeŋgo/ — two syllables; *v* and *b* are interchangeable in Spanish
- **Digo:** /ˈdiɣo/ — two syllables; stress on first syllable
- **Sé:** /se/ — one syllable; accent mark distinguishes from *se* (reflexive pronoun); pronounced the same
- **Conozco:** /koˈnoθko/ (Spain) / /koˈnosko/ (Latin America) — three syllables; stress on the second
