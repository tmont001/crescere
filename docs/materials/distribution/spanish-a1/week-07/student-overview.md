## What You Will Learn This Week

Week 7 takes you into two of the most practical Spanish shopping scenarios: a clothing shop and an outdoor market.

In Session 13, you will learn how to find, describe, and buy clothing. You will also learn demonstrative adjectives — *este/esta/estos/estas* and *ese/esa/esos/esas* — the words for "this/these" and "that/those." These are some of the most useful words in everyday Spanish conversation.

In Session 14, you will learn how to shop at a Spanish-speaking market: asking quantities, checking prices, and completing a purchase.

You already know *querer* and *quisiera* from Week 6. This week you learn what to call what you want.

## What You Will Be Able to Say by the End of Week 7

**In a clothing shop:**
> *Hola. Busco un pantalón negro, talla M. ... ¿Tiene esta camisa en azul? ... ¿Me la puedo probar? ... ¿Cuánto cuesta? ... Me la llevo, gracias.*

**At a Spanish market:**
> *Hola, buenos días. Quisiera un kilo de tomates y media docena de huevos. ¿A cuánto está el queso? ... Póngame 200 gramos, por favor. Nada más, gracias.*

**Describing what someone is wearing:**
> *Llevo una camiseta blanca y unos vaqueros azules. Mi compañera lleva una chaqueta verde y unas botas negras.*

## Demonstrative Adjectives: Este and Ese

Spanish has two demonstrative series. The one you use depends on how far away the thing is.

### Este / Esta / Estos / Estas — this, these (near you)

| | Masculine | Feminine |
|---|---|---|
| Singular | **este** | **esta** |
| Plural | **estos** | **estas** |

*Este pantalón, esta camisa, estos zapatos, estas botas.*

### Ese / Esa / Esos / Esas — that, those (farther away)

| | Masculine | Feminine |
|---|---|---|
| Singular | **ese** | **esa** |
| Plural | **esos** | **esas** |

*Ese vestido, esa chaqueta, esos vaqueros, esas botas.*

**In a shop:** *Esta camisa* — the one I'm holding. *Esa camisa* — the one on the rack across the room.

**Agree with the noun:** The demonstrative must match the gender and number of the noun it goes with. Check the noun first, then choose the form.

---

## Color Adjective Agreement

Colors in Spanish come after the noun and agree in gender and number:

| Color | Masculine sing. | Feminine sing. | Plural (either gender) |
|---|---|---|---|
| black | negro | negra | negros / negras |
| white | blanco | blanca | blancos / blancas |
| red | rojo | roja | rojos / rojas |
| blue | azul | azul | azules |
| green | verde | verde | verdes |
| grey | gris | gris | grises |
| brown | marrón | marrón | marrones |

**Invariable colors — never change at all:**
- *naranja, rosa, beige, violeta* — same form always: *unos pantalones naranja, una camisa rosa*

## Quantities at the Market

After any quantity word, Spanish drops the article and uses just *de*:

- *un kilo **de** tomates* (not *de los* tomates)
- *una botella **de** vino*
- *una docena **de** huevos*
- *500 gramos **de** queso*

When you say how much of something you want, the article disappears and only *de* remains.

---

## Prices: ¿Cuánto cuesta? / ¿Cuánto cuestan?

- *¿Cuánto cuesta?* — for one item (singular)
- *¿Cuánto cuestan?* — for more than one item (plural)

The verb agrees with what you are asking about: one item → *cuesta*; several items → *cuestan.*

## What to Practice Between Sessions

**After Session 13 — before Session 14:**

1. **Learn 5 clothing items with their gender.** *Un pantalón, una camisa, una chaqueta, unos zapatos, unas botas.* Say each one with a color: *unos zapatos negros, una camisa blanca.*
2. **Practice the shopping phrases.** Say *"Busco una chaqueta verde, talla M"* out loud. Then: *"¿Me la puedo probar?"*
3. **Third try: no notes** on the shopping phrases.

**After Session 14 — before Session 15:**

1. **Say a market order out loud.** Invent a list of 4 items. Say the full order: *"Quisiera un kilo de tomates, por favor. Y medio kilo de queso."*
2. **Practice the price question.** *"¿Cuánto cuesta?"* — automatic. *"¿Cuánto cuestan?"* — when there are multiple items.
3. **Check demonstratives.** Can you say the right form (*este/esta/ese/esa*) for any clothing noun without thinking?

## A Note on Mistakes This Week

Two errors are very common:

1. **Demonstrative gender errors.** *Esta pantalón* (wrong) → *este pantalón.* The demonstrative must agree with the noun's gender. When in doubt, look at the article: *un pantalón* → masculine → *este pantalón.*

2. **Color agreement errors.** *Un vestido roja* (wrong) → *un vestido rojo.* The color must match the noun, not the item's real-world appearance. *Vestido* is masculine, so the color is masculine: *rojo.*

Both errors are mechanical — they come from not quite knowing the gender of the noun. The fastest fix is learning clothing nouns with their articles (*la camisa, el pantalón*) from the start.
