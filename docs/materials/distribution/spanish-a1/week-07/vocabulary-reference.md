## Demonstrative Adjectives

Spanish has **two distinct demonstrative series** — one for things that are near ("this/these") and one for things that are farther away ("that/those"). Unlike French, Spanish makes this distance distinction grammatically.

### Este series — This / These (near you)

| | Masculine | Feminine |
|---|---|---|
| Singular | **este** | **esta** |
| Plural | **estos** | **estas** |

*Este pantalón, esta camisa, estos zapatos, estas botas.*

### Ese series — That / Those (farther away)

| | Masculine | Feminine |
|---|---|---|
| Singular | **ese** | **esa** |
| Plural | **esos** | **esas** |

*Ese vestido, esa chaqueta, esos vaqueros, esas botas.*

### Aquel series — That over there (far — recognition only this week)

| | Masculine | Feminine |
|---|---|---|
| Singular | **aquel** | **aquella** |
| Plural | **aquellos** | **aquellas** |

*Aquel abrigo en la vitrina.* — That coat over there in the window.

Students learn to recognize *aquel/aquella*; active production of este/ese is the week's goal.

### Demonstratives in Shopping Context

| Spanish | English |
|---|---|
| ¿Cuánto cuesta **esta** camisa? | How much is **this** shirt? |
| Quiero **ese** pantalón negro. | I want **that** black pair of trousers. |
| ¿Me puede mostrar **esos** zapatos? | Can you show me **those** shoes? |
| ¿Tienen **esta** chaqueta en azul? | Do you have **this** jacket in blue? |

## Colors and Adjective Agreement

Color adjectives in Spanish agree with the noun in gender and number. They come **after** the noun.

| Color | Masculine sing. | Feminine sing. | Masculine pl. | Feminine pl. |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| black | negro | negra | negros | negras |
| white | blanco | blanca | blancos | blancas |
| red | rojo | roja | rojos | rojas |
| blue | azul | azul | azules | azules |
| green | verde | verde | verdes | verdes |
| yellow | amarillo | amarilla | amarillos | amarillas |
| grey | gris | gris | grises | grises |
| brown | marrón | marrón | marrones | marrones |
| purple | morado | morada | morados | moradas |

**Invariable colors (no gender change; some change for number):**
- *naranja* (orange) — invariable in all forms: *una camisa naranja, unos pantalones naranja*
- *rosa* (pink) — invariable: *una camiseta rosa, unos zapatos rosa*
- *beige* — invariable: *un abrigo beige*
- *violeta* — invariable: *una falda violeta*

**Note on azul, verde, gris, marrón:** These do not change for gender (same form for m./f.) but DO change for number by adding -es or -s: *azul → azules; verde → verdes; gris → grises; marrón → marrones.*

## Shopping Phrases

| Spanish | English | Register |
|---|---|---|
| Busco... | I'm looking for... | Neutral |
| ¿Tiene... en [color/talla]? | Do you have... in [color/size]? | Neutral |
| ¿Me lo/la puedo probar? | Can I try it on? | Neutral |
| ¿Dónde están los probadores? | Where are the fitting rooms? | |
| Me lo/la llevo. | I'll take it. (lit. I'll carry it) | |
| No me lo/la llevo, gracias. | I won't take it, thank you. | |
| ¿Tiene algo más barato? | Do you have something cheaper? | |
| ¿Cuánto cuesta? / ¿Cuánto cuestan? | How much is it / are they? | |
| ¿Aceptan tarjeta? | Do you accept cards? | |

**Lo/la agreement:** The pronoun *lo* refers to a masculine noun (*el pantalón → me lo llevo*); *la* refers to a feminine noun (*la camisa → me la llevo*). This is introduced as a practical formula — full object pronoun study is in A2.

## Pronunciation Notes

- **Este / esta / estos / estas:** /ˈeste, ˈesta, ˈestos, ˈestas/ — stress on the first syllable; the *e* is pure, not a diphthong
- **Ese / esa:** /ˈese, ˈesa/ — two syllables; stress on the first
- **Vaqueros:** /baˈkeɾos/ — in Spain; the *v* and *b* are interchangeable in Spanish; stress on the second syllable
- **Chaqueta:** /tʃaˈketa/ — three syllables; stress on the second; *ch* = /tʃ/ as in English "chair"
- **Cuánto cuesta:** /ˈkwanto ˈkwesta/ — two stress-bearing words; both start with the *kw* sound
- **Probadores:** /pɾoβaˈðoɾes/ — fitting rooms; five syllables; stress on the fourth
