## Gustar Structure

*Gustar* does not work like most Spanish verbs. The thing you like is the grammatical subject — not you. You are the indirect object.

| Who likes | Pronoun | Verb (if subject is singular) | Verb (if subject is plural) |
|---|---|---|---|
| I | **me** | me gusta | me gustan |
| you (informal) | **te** | te gusta | te gustan |
| he / she / you (formal) | **le** | le gusta | le gustan |
| we | **nos** | nos gusta | nos gustan |
| you all (Spain) | **os** | os gusta | os gustan |
| they / you all (formal/LA) | **les** | les gusta | les gustan |

**Key rule:**
- *Gusta* → when the thing liked is singular or an infinitive: *Me gusta el café. Me gusta comer.*
- *Gustan* → when the thing liked is plural: *Me gustan los tacos. Me gustan las empanadas.*

**The most common error:** English speakers say *Yo gusto el café* (wrong — this means "I am pleasing to coffee"). The correct form is always *Me gusta el café.*

### Examples

| Spanish | English |
|---|---|
| Me gusta el café. | I like coffee. |
| Me gustan los tacos. | I like tacos. |
| ¿Te gusta la paella? | Do you like paella? |
| No le gusta el pescado. | He/She doesn't like fish. |
| Nos gustan las verduras. | We like vegetables. |
| Me gusta comer en restaurantes. | I like eating in restaurants. |

## Encantar (Same Structure as Gustar)

| Spanish | English |
|---|---|
| Me encanta el chocolate. | I love chocolate. |
| Me encantan los postres. | I love desserts. |
| ¿Te encanta cocinar? | Do you love cooking? |

*Encantar* = to love / to really like (stronger than *gustar*). It works exactly like *gustar* — same structure, same rules.

## Querer — Conjugation (e → ie Stem Change)

*Querer* (to want) has a stem change: e → ie in all forms except *nosotros* and *vosotros.*

| Subject | Querer |
|---|---|
| yo | **quiero** |
| tú | **quieres** |
| él / ella / usted | **quiere** |
| nosotros/as | queremos |
| vosotros/as | queréis |
| ellos / ellas / ustedes | **quieren** |

**Polite formula:** *Quisiera...* (I would like...) — used in restaurants and shops. Introduced this week as a fixed phrase; the full conditional tense comes in A2.

| Ordering | Use |
|---|---|
| Quiero una tortilla, por favor. | Direct — acceptable, clear |
| Quisiera una tortilla, por favor. | Polite — preferred in formal restaurant settings |
| Para mí, una tortilla. | "For me, a tortilla" — very natural in group orders |

## Pronunciation Notes

- **Gustar:** /ɡusˈtaɾ/ — the *g* is soft (like in "go"); stress the final syllable.
- **Quisiera:** /kiˈsjeɾa/ — four syllables: ki-SIE-ra; the *s* is soft in most of Latin America.
- **Tortilla:** /toɾˈtiʎa/ in Spain (ll = /ʎ/); /toɾˈtiʝa/ in most of Latin America.
- **Quiero/quieres:** /ˈkjeɾo, ˈkjeɾes/ — stress on the first syllable; the *qu* is always /k/.
- **Ceviche:** /seˈβitʃe/ — three syllables: se-VI-che; *c* before *e* = /s/ in Latin America, /θ/ in Spain.
- **Paella:** /paˈeʎa/ in Spain; /paˈeʝa/ elsewhere. Two separate vowel sounds: pa-E-lla.
