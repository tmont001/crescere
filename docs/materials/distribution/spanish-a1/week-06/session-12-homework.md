## Part 1 — Querer from Memory

Write the full conjugation of *querer* without looking at your notes. Remember the stem change.

| Subject | Querer |
|---|---|
| yo | |
| tú | |
| él / ella / usted | |
| nosotros/as | |
| vosotros/as | |
| ellos / ellas / ustedes | |

**Check:** *quiero / quieres / quiere / queremos / queréis / quieren.*

Note which forms have the e→ie stem change (yo, tú, él/ella, ellos/ellas) and which do not (nosotros, vosotros).

## Part 2 — Restaurant Dialogue (8–10 lines)

Write a short restaurant dialogue in Spanish. You are the customer; write both sides (customer + waiter).

**Required elements:**
- [ ] Customer asks for a table (*¿Tienen mesa para...?*)
- [ ] Waiter asks what the customer will have (*¿Qué van a tomar?*)
- [ ] Customer orders at least 2 items using *quisiera* or *para mí*
- [ ] Customer asks one question (*¿Qué recomienda? / ¿Qué tiene de postre?*)
- [ ] Customer requests the bill: *La cuenta, por favor.*

**Example opener** (continue from here, or write your own):
> *Waiter:* Buenas noches, ¿tienen reserva?
> *Customer:* No, pero ¿tienen mesa para dos?
> *Waiter:* Sí, claro. Por aquí, por favor. ¿Qué van a tomar?
> *Customer:* *(continue here...)*

## Part 3 — Food Preferences: Gustar Paragraph (4–5 sentences)

Write 4–5 sentences about your food preferences. Mix *gusta* and *gustan.*

**Required:**
- [ ] At least 2 sentences with *me gusta/gustan*
- [ ] At least 1 sentence with *me encanta/encantan*
- [ ] At least 1 negative: *no me gusta/gustan*
- [ ] At least 1 sentence using a family member or friend: *A mi [person] le gusta/gustan...*

---

## Part 4 — Speaking Practice

Read your dialogue from Part 2 out loud. Say both parts — customer and waiter. Third try: no notes on the ordering phrases.

Practice saying:
- *La cuenta, por favor.* × 5 — automatic.
- *Quisiera...* × 5 — automatic.

Then say your Part 3 sentences without notes on the third try (notes on food vocabulary are fine; the *gustar* structure should be from memory).

## Extension (Optional)

Write 2 sentences comparing two foods or cuisines:

- *Me gusta más [X] que [Y].* — I like [X] more than [Y].
- *Prefiero [X] a [Y].* — I prefer [X] to [Y]. *(We study prefiero formally in Week 10 — you can try it now.)*
