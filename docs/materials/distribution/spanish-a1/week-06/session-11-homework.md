## Part 1 — Gustar: Gusta or Gustan?

Complete each sentence with *gusta* or *gustan.* Remember: the verb agrees with the thing liked, not with you.

1. Me _____ el café con leche.
2. Me _____ los tacos.
3. ¿Te _____ la paella?
4. No me _____ las gambas.
5. A mi hermano le _____ el ceviche.
6. Me _____ comer en restaurantes.
7. Nos _____ las empanadas.
8. ¿Te _____ cocinar?

**Answer key:**
1. gusta (el café — singular)
2. gustan (los tacos — plural)
3. gusta (la paella — singular)
4. gustan (las gambas — plural)
5. gusta (el ceviche — singular)
6. gusta (comer — infinitive)
7. gustan (las empanadas — plural)
8. gusta (cocinar — infinitive)

---

## Part 2 — Write Your Food Preferences (5 sentences minimum)

Write 5 sentences about your real food preferences. Mix the forms:

**Required:**
- [ ] At least 2 sentences with *me gusta* (singular noun or infinitive)
- [ ] At least 2 sentences with *me gustan* (plural noun)
- [ ] At least 1 sentence with *no me gusta / no me gustan*
- [ ] At least 1 sentence with *me encanta* or *me encantan*

**Optional:** use *a mi [family member] le gusta/gustan* to include a family member's preferences.

---

## Part 3 — Speaking Practice

Read your Part 2 sentences out loud three times. Third try: no notes on the *gustar* structure (notes on food vocabulary are fine).

Then try answering these questions out loud — no written preparation:
- *¿Qué te gusta comer para desayunar?* (for breakfast)
- *¿Qué no te gusta nada?*
- *¿Te gusta la comida picante?*

---

## Extension (Optional)

Write 2–3 additional sentences comparing preferences:

- *Me gusta más [X] que [Y].* — I like [X] more than [Y].
- *Prefiero [X] a [Y].* — I prefer [X] to [Y].

We will study *preferir* in Week 10, but you can try it now as a set phrase.
