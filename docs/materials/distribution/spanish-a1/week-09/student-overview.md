## What You Will Learn This Week

Week 9 is the week Spanish becomes genuinely useful for everyday life. You will learn the most natural way to talk about future plans — what you're going to do, when, and with whom. You'll also learn two essential structures for expressing what you have to do (*tener que* and *hay que*) and get more practice with *poder* for what you can or can't do. Combine them all, and you can have a real planning conversation in Spanish.

By the end of this week, you'll be able to discuss your plans, make arrangements, explain why you're busy, and suggest alternatives — the building blocks of any scheduling conversation.

---

## What You Will Be Able to Say by the End of Week 9

**Making a plan:**
> *Esta noche voy a ir al cine con unos amigos. Mañana por la mañana tengo que trabajar, pero por la tarde estoy libre. La semana que viene vamos a ir de vacaciones.*

**Planning a social arrangement:**
> *— ¿Estás libre este fin de semana?*
> *— Quiero ir, pero tengo que trabajar el sábado. ¡El domingo puedo! ¿A qué hora quedamos?*
> *— Quedamos a las tres delante del café.*

## The Near Future: Ir a + Infinitivo

The simplest way to say what you're going to do is: **conjugated *ir* + *a* + infinitive of the main verb.**

You already know *ir* (*voy/vas/va/vamos/vais/van*). Just add *a* and the action:

| Spanish | English |
|---|---|
| *Voy a trabajar mañana.* | I'm going to work tomorrow. |
| *¿Vas a llamar esta noche?* | Are you going to call tonight? |
| *Va a tomar el tren.* | He/she is going to take the train. |
| *Vamos a cenar juntos.* | We're going to have dinner together. |
| *No voy a salir esta noche.* | I'm not going to go out tonight. |

**This is how Spanish speakers talk about upcoming plans in everyday conversation.** The grammatical future tense (*trabajaré*) exists but is more formal — for now, *ir a + infinitivo* covers everything you need.

---

## Ir a vs. Pensar + Infinitivo

| Structure | Feeling | Example |
|---|---|---|
| *Voy a + inf* | Definite plan | *Voy a llamarle esta tarde.* — I'm going to call him this afternoon. |
| *Pienso + inf* | Intention / idea | *Pienso llamarle.* — I'm thinking of calling him. |

Both are useful. *Ir a* is more definite; *pensar* signals something is still an intention, not yet confirmed.

**Pensar** stem change (e→ie): *pienso, piensas, piensa, pensamos, pensáis, piensan.* Same pattern as *querer*.

## The Three Obligation/Ability Structures

| Structure | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **tener que** + inf | personal obligation | *Tengo que trabajar mañana.* |
| **hay que** + inf | general obligation (no subject) | *Hay que reservar con antelación.* |
| **poder** + inf | ability / possibility | *Puedo venir a las 18h.* |

### Tener Que vs. Hay Que

The difference is simple:
- *Tengo que estudiar.* — I personally have to study.
- *Hay que estudiar.* — It's necessary to study / one must study (general rule).

Use *tener que* when the obligation applies to a specific person. Use *hay que* for general rules or necessities.

---

## Combining the Structures

This is where planning conversations come alive:

| Situation | Example |
|---|---|
| You want to but have to do something else | *Quiero venir, pero tengo que trabajar.* |
| You want to but can't | *Quiero venir, pero no puedo.* |
| You can if they want | *Puedo ir si quieres.* |
| General rule applies | *Hay que reservar — el restaurante se llena.* |
| You're going to have to cancel | *Voy a tener que cancelar — lo siento mucho.* |

## Key Planning Phrases

| Spanish | English |
|---|---|
| *¿Qué vas a hacer este fin de semana?* | What are you going to do this weekend? |
| *¿Estás libre mañana?* | Are you free tomorrow? |
| *¿Quedamos cuándo / dónde?* | When / where shall we meet? |
| *¿A qué hora?* | At what time? |
| *Lo siento, no puedo.* | I'm sorry, I can't. |
| *¡Perfecto! / ¡Genial!* | Perfect! / Great! |
| *Quedamos a las...* | Let's meet at... |

## What to Practice Between Sessions

**After Session 17 — before Session 18:**
1. Say 3 plans for the coming week using *voy a + infinitivo.* Third try without notes.
2. Practice the time expressions until they come automatically: *mañana, esta noche, este fin de semana, la semana que viene.*
3. Try: say what you ARE going to do and what you are NOT going to do (*no voy a...*).

**After Session 18 — before Session 19:**
1. Write a short message to a (real or imaginary) Spanish-speaking friend proposing a plan. Include: *Voy a..., ¿Puedes...?, Tengo que..., No puedo... pero...*
2. Say the planning role play out loud from memory: suggest → hear no → negotiate → agree.
3. Learn *tengo que + your most common obligation* until it's automatic. *Tengo que trabajar. Tengo que estudiar. Tengo que volver a casa.*

## A Note on Mistakes This Week

The most common error this week is **forgetting the *a*** in *ir a + infinitivo* — saying *voy trabajar* instead of *voy a trabajar.* The *a* is always required.

The second is **adding a preposition** after *tener que*: *"tengo que de trabajar"* — wrong. *Tengo que trabajar.* Same with *poder*: *puedo ir*, not *puedo a ir.*
