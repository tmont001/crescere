## Doler — The Structure

*Doler* works exactly like *gustar.* The body part is the grammatical subject; the person is expressed with an indirect object pronoun.

**The body part is the subject → the verb agrees with the body part, not the person.**

| If what hurts is... | Verb form | Example |
|---|---|---|
| singular noun / infinitive | **duele** | *Me duele la cabeza.* |
| plural noun | **duelen** | *Me duelen los pies.* |

**Full pronoun table:**

| Pronoun | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|
| me duele / me duelen | my ... hurts/hurt | *Me duele el estómago.* |
| te duele / te duelen | your ... hurts/hurt | *¿Te duele la espalda?* |
| le duele / le duelen | his/her ... hurts/hurt | *Le duele la garganta.* |
| nos duele / nos duelen | our ... hurts/hurt | *Nos duelen los pies.* |
| os duele / os duelen | your (pl. Spain) ... hurt | *¿Os duele la cabeza?* |
| les duele / les duelen | their ... hurts/hurt | *Les duelen las rodillas.* |

**Critical rule — use the definite article, NOT the possessive:**
- Correct: *Me duele **la** cabeza.* ✓
- Wrong: *Me duele **mi** cabeza.* ✗ — In Spanish, body parts always take the definite article when used with *doler.*

**Strength modifier:**
- *Me duele mucho la espalda.* — My back hurts a lot.
- *Me duele un poco la cabeza.* — My head hurts a little.

## Deber + Infinitivo — Advice

*Deber* expresses obligation or advice. *Deberías* (conditional) is the polite form — introduced here as a formula for giving advice.

| Form | Use | Example |
|---|---|---|
| *debes + infinitivo* | direct advice (you should/must) | *Debes descansar.* |
| *deberías + infinitivo* | polite/softer advice (you should) | *Deberías beber más agua.* |
| *no debes + infinitivo* | you shouldn't | *No debes trabajar esta semana.* |

**Full *deber* conjugation (present):**
| yo | tú | él/ella | nosotros | vosotros | ellos |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| debo | debes | debe | debemos | debéis | deben |

**Examples:**
- *Debes descansar.* — You should rest.
- *Deberías ir al médico.* — You should go to the doctor.
- *No debes hacer deporte esta semana.* — You shouldn't do sport this week.
- *Debes tomar este medicamento dos veces al día.* — You should take this medicine twice a day.

## Llevar + Time + Gerund — Duration of Symptoms

*Llevar* + time period + gerund expresses how long an ongoing situation has been going on. This is the Spanish-specific structure for duration — different from English and from French (*depuis*).

| Spanish | English |
|---|---|
| *Llevo tres días con fiebre.* | I've had a fever for three days. |
| *Llevo una semana tosiendo.* | I've been coughing for a week. |
| *Llevo dos días sin dormir bien.* | I haven't slept well for two days. |
| *Llevas mucho tiempo así?* | Have you been like this for a long time? |
| *Llevo dos horas esperando.* | I've been waiting for two hours. |

**Structure:** *llevar* (conjugated) + time + gerund (-ando / -iendo)
- *tener fiebre* → *Llevo tres días **con** fiebre* (noun phrase after *con*)
- *toser* → *Llevo una semana **tosiendo*** (gerund)

**Alternative for noun phrases:** *Llevo + time + con + noun*
- *Llevo tres días con esta tos.* — I've had this cough for three days.
- *Llevo una semana con dolor de cabeza.* — I've had a headache for a week.
