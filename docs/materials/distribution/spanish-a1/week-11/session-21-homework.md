## Part 1 — Body Parts with Articles (5 min)

Write the correct definite article (*el, la, los, las*) for each body part. Do not look at your notes first — check after.

1. ___ cabeza
2. ___ espalda
3. ___ ojos (pl.)
4. ___ garganta
5. ___ pie
6. ___ mano ← careful, this one surprises students
7. ___ rodillas (pl.)
8. ___ estómago

**Answer key:**
1. la cabeza
2. la espalda
3. los ojos
4. la garganta
5. el pie
6. **la** mano (feminine despite ending in -o)
7. las rodillas
8. el estómago

If you wrote *el mano*: *la mano* is feminine — one of the notable exceptions in Spanish. Write *la mano* five times.

## Part 2 — Doler: Duele or Duelen? (8 min)

Choose *me duele* or *me duelen* and complete the sentence. Add the correct article on the body part.

1. *_____ _____ cabeza — no puedo pensar.*
2. *_____ _____ pies después de caminar mucho.*
3. *_____ _____ estómago — no quiero comer.*
4. *_____ _____ rodillas cuando hace frío.*
5. *_____ _____ garganta — me cuesta tragar.*
6. *_____ _____ ojos — he trabajado demasiado.*

**Answer key:**
1. *Me duele la cabeza*
2. *Me duelen los pies*
3. *Me duele el estómago*
4. *Me duelen las rodillas*
5. *Me duele la garganta*
6. *Me duelen los ojos*

If you used *me duelo* anywhere: *doler* works like *gustar* — the body part is the subject, not you. *Me duele la cabeza* = la cabeza is what hurts.

## Part 3 — Symptoms (5 min)

Match each symptom to the most natural Spanish phrase. Use each phrase once.

*tengo fiebre / estoy resfriado/a / tengo mucha tos / estoy mareado/a / me siento muy mal*

1. I sneeze constantly and my nose is running. →
2. I have 38.5 degrees of temperature. →
3. I've been coughing for three days. →
4. When I stand up, the room spins. →
5. I have no energy and everything aches. →

**Answer key:**
1. *Estoy resfriado/a*
2. *Tengo fiebre*
3. *Tengo mucha tos*
4. *Estoy mareado/a*
5. *Me siento muy mal*

## Part 4 — Written Pharmacy Encounter (10 min)

Write a 6–8 line pharmacy conversation. You are the patient. Include:

- [ ] Enter and greet
- [ ] Say you don't feel well (*No me encuentro bien / Me siento mal*)
- [ ] Describe at least two symptoms — one using *doler*, one other symptom phrase
- [ ] Ask for a product (*¿Tiene algo para...? / Necesito algo para...*)
- [ ] Ask at least one follow-up question (*¿Necesita receta? / ¿Cuántas veces al día?*)
- [ ] Thank and close

Write the pharmacist's lines too — they only need to be 4–6 words each.

**Example opening (continue from here):**
> **Tú:** Buenos días. No me encuentro bien...
> **Farmacéutico/a:** ¿Qué le pasa?
> **Tú:** ...

---

## Part 5 — Speaking Practice (5 min out loud)

Read your pharmacy conversation aloud THREE times.
- First time: read from your notes
- Second time: glance at notes only for vocabulary
- Third time: say the patient's lines without notes

**Aim:** To produce your entry, symptom description, and request without hesitation on the key words.

## Extension (Optional)

If you finish early:
- Write a second scenario where your symptoms are different: back pain + fatigue (no cold or fever). Practice the different body part and article.
- Try using *llevar* in your symptom description: *Llevo dos días con fiebre / Llevo una semana tosiendo.* You will work with *llevar* formally in Session 22 — preview it here if you can.
- Add a line where the pharmacist asks something unexpected: *"¿Es alérgico/a a algún medicamento?"* — write how you would respond.
