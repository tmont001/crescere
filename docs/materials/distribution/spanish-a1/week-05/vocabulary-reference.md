## Tener — Conjugation (Irregular yo form)

| Subject | Tener |
|---|---|
| yo | tengo |
| tú | tienes |
| él / ella / usted | tiene |
| nosotros/as | tenemos |
| vosotros/as | tenéis |
| ellos / ellas / ustedes | tienen |

**Key note:** The *yo* form is irregular: *tengo* (not *teno*). All other forms follow a regular -er pattern.

**Pronunciation:** Stress falls on the stem in all forms except *nosotros/vosotros: TENgo, TIEnes, TIEne, teNEmos, teNÉis, TIEnen.*

## Age with Tener

In Spanish, age is something you **have**, not something you **are**:

| Spanish | English | Note |
|---|---|---|
| Tengo treinta años. | I am thirty (years old). | Never: *Soy treinta años.* |
| ¿Cuántos años tienes? | How old are you? | Literally: "How many years do you have?" |
| Tiene sesenta y cinco años. | She is 65 years old. | |
| Mis hijos tienen ocho y doce años. | My children are 8 and 12. | |

## Common Tener Expressions

| Spanish | English | Note |
|---|---|---|
| tener hambre | to be hungry | Lit. "to have hunger" |
| tener sed | to be thirsty | Lit. "to have thirst" |
| tener sueño | to be sleepy | Lit. "to have sleep" |
| tener frío | to be cold | Lit. "to have cold" |
| tener calor | to be hot | Lit. "to have heat" |
| tener miedo (de) | to be afraid (of) | Lit. "to have fear" |
| tener razón | to be right | Lit. "to have reason" |
| tener prisa | to be in a hurry | Lit. "to have haste" |
| tener ganas de + inf | to feel like doing sth | *Tengo ganas de descansar.* |

**These expressions use *tener*, not *estar*.** Students often make this mistake: *Estoy hambre* → Incorrect. *Tengo hambre* → Correct.

## Possessives — mi / tu / su (Introduction)

| Spanish | English | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|---|
| mi / mis | my | *mi hermano* | *mis hermanos* |
| tu / tus | your (informal) | *tu madre* | *tus padres* |
| su / sus | his / her / your (formal) | *su hijo* | *sus hijos* |

**Key rule:** Possessives agree with the noun they modify (singular/plural), NOT with the person's gender.
- *mi hermano* and *mi hermana* — both use *mi*
- *mis hermanos* and *mis hermanas* — both use *mis*

*Nuestro/a* (our) and the fuller possessive paradigm come in Week 6.

## Describing Family Members

Combining *tener + ser + estar + regular verbs:*

| Description | Verb | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Age | tener | *Tiene cincuenta años.* |
| Identity / profession | ser | *Es profesora.* |
| Origin / nationality | ser | *Es de Colombia.* |
| Current state | estar | *Está jubilado/a (retired).* |
| Habit or routine | -ar/-er/-ir verb | *Trabaja en un hospital.* |

## Telling Time — Reinforcement

| Spanish | English |
|---|---|
| Son las ocho. | It is eight o'clock. |
| Es la una y cuarto. | It is quarter past one. |
| Son las tres y media. | It is half past three. |
| Son las cinco menos cuarto. | It is quarter to five. |
| ¿A qué hora tienes clase? | What time do you have class? |
| Tengo clase a las nueve. | I have class at nine. |

**Note:** *Tener* is used for scheduled appointments and classes: *Tengo una reunión a las tres.*
