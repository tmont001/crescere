## Part 2 — Age and Tener Expressions (5 sentences)

Write complete sentences in Spanish. Do not use *ser* for age.

1. Write your own age.
2. Write the age of a family member or close friend.
3. Write the age of another family member using a different subject (*él, ella, ellos, mis...*).
4. Write a sentence using *tener + a physical expression* (hambre, sed, sueño, frío, or razón) — make it true or plausible.
5. Write a sentence about what a family member has — age, an expression, or a possession. Example: *Mi hermana tiene mucho trabajo.* / *Mis padres tienen hambre a las dos.*

---

## Part 3 — Family Description (5 sentences minimum)

Write a short description of your family or the people closest to you. Use *mi/mis* to link possessives to your family members. Aim for at least 5 sentences.

**Required in your paragraph:**
- [ ] At least two uses of *tener* (age or expression)
- [ ] At least one family member with *mi* or *mis*
- [ ] At least one regular verb (*trabaja, vive, estudia, habla,* etc.)
- [ ] At least one use of *ser* (identity, profession, or origin)
- [ ] No *soy X años* — age always uses *tener*

---

## Part 4 — Speaking Practice

Read your Part 3 paragraph out loud, no notes on the third try. You do not need to record yourself, but you should be able to say it clearly from memory (or near-memory) by the time Session 10 starts.

---

## Quick Check — Answers to Verify Part 2

Part 2 does not have fixed answers since the sentences are about your life. Use this to check your grammar:

- Age sentence: does it start with *Tengo / Tiene / Tienen* — NOT *soy / es / son*?
- *Mi/mis* check: if the noun after *mi/mis* is plural, did you use *mis*? If singular, *mi*?
- Tener expression: is the noun after *tengo* a bare noun (*hambre, sed, sueño*) without *un/una/el/la*? These expressions drop the article: *tengo hambre*, not *tengo la hambre*.
