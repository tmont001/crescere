# Spanish A1 — Week 6 Formative Check

**Administered:** Start of Session 13 (first session of Week 7)
**Time needed:** 6–8 minutes total
**Purpose:** Quick checkpoint — not a test. Confirms that the *gustar* structure, *querer,* and restaurant vocabulary are solid enough to enter Week 7 (shopping, demonstratives, colors, prices).

---

## Oral Prompts (5 questions — ~1 min per student for the full set)

| # | Prompt | Target response | What to listen for |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | How do you say "I like coffee" in Spanish? | *Me gusta el café.* | *Me gusta* — not *yo gusto*; definite article *el* with the noun |
| 2 | Give me all six forms of *querer* — go. | quiero / quieres / quiere / queremos / queréis / quieren | *quiero* not *quero*; stem change in yo/tú/él/ellos; no hesitation |
| 3 | How do you say "I like tacos" in Spanish? | *Me gustan los tacos.* | *Gustan* (plural) — not *gusta*; article *los* before the noun |
| 4 | Order a meal in Spanish — at least two items. | *Quisiera [item], y para el segundo [item], por favor.* | *Quisiera* or *para mí* used; food items are real; polite register |
| 5 | How do you ask for the bill in Spanish? | *La cuenta, por favor.* | Exact phrase; *la cuenta* not *el billete* or *la factura* |

---

## Written Prompts (5 questions — students write answers, ~3 min)

**Instructions to students:** Answer in Spanish. Short answers only.

1. Write: "I love pizza." (Use *encantar.*)
2. Write: "We like vegetables." (las verduras)
3. Write: "He doesn't like seafood." (los mariscos)
4. Complete: *Quisiera _______, por favor.* (choose a food or drink item)
5. Write: "Do you (informal) like cooking?" (Use *gustar + infinitivo.*)

**Answer key:**
1. *Me encanta la pizza.* (singular noun)
2. *Nos gustan las verduras.* (*gustan* — plural; *nos* — we)
3. *No le gustan los mariscos.* (*gustan* — plural; *le* — he)
4. Any valid food or drink item: *Quisiera una paella, por favor.*
5. *¿Te gusta cocinar?* (*gusta* — infinitive is always singular)

---

## Instructor Scoring Notes

Not graded. Use it to make one of three decisions:

| Result | What it means | What to do |
|---|---|---|
| Students handle all 5 oral prompts | Week 6 content is solid | Move into Week 7 (shopping, demonstratives, colors) |
| Students stumble on 1–2 prompts | Minor gaps — expected at this stage | 5-minute *gustar* warm-up at the start of Sessions 13 and 14; continue correcting on the spot |
| Students stumble on 3+ prompts | Gustar structure or *querer* conjugation not yet secure | Add 10-minute review block to Session 13; carry *gustar* as a warm-up habit into Week 7 |

**Most common Week 6 gaps:**
- *Yo gusto el café* (English word order with gustar) → the most persistent error; correct every instance firmly
- *Me gusta los tacos* (singular verb with plural subject) → the verb must agree with the noun: *me gustan los tacos*
- *Quero* instead of *quiero* (dropping the stem change) → the *e→ie* change is required in yo/tú/él/ellos
- *La cuenta* asked with wrong verb: *¿Me da la cuenta?* is fine; *¿Me trae la cuenta?* is also fine — both are correct; students are more likely to forget to ask at all
- Ser/estar errors with food: *El plato es muy rico* → should usually be *está muy rico* (current state / taste) — continue to reinforce the Week 2 distinction

---

## Ready to Move On

A student is ready for Week 7 (shopping) content when they can:

- [ ] Produce *me gusta / me gustan* correctly without thinking about the structure
- [ ] Conjugate *querer* from memory with *quiero* secure
- [ ] Use *quisiera* or *para mí* to order food politely
- [ ] Name 6+ Spanish or Latin American food items
- [ ] Navigate a basic restaurant ordering exchange: arrive, order two items, ask a question, ask for the bill

**Not a Week 7 gate:** Full six-pronoun *gustar* mastery (*me/te/le/nos/os/les* all at speed), *encantar* drilling, or all restaurant vocabulary. These continue to consolidate — especially *gustar*, which returns in Week 10 alongside *preferir* and other opinion verbs.
