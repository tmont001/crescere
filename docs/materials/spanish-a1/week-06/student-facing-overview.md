# Spanish A1 — Week 6 Student Overview

**Theme: Food, Preferences, and Restaurants**

---

## What You Will Learn This Week

Week 6 has two goals. First: you will learn one of the most unusual structures in Spanish — *gustar* — which lets you express what you like. Second: you will get enough restaurant vocabulary and phrases to order a full meal in Spanish.

The *gustar* structure surprises almost every English speaker. But once it clicks, it opens up a whole family of useful Spanish verbs (*encantar, doler, interesar, molestar*) that all work the same way.

---

## What You Will Be Able to Say by the End of Week 6

By the end of Session 12, you should be able to:

**Talking about preferences:**
> *Me encanta la comida española. Me gusta mucho la tortilla española. Me gustan también los tacos, pero no me gustan los mariscos. ¿A ti te gusta la paella?*

**Ordering at a restaurant:**
> *Buenas tardes. ¿Tienen mesa para dos? ... Quisiera ver la carta, por favor. ... Para mí, de primero la ensalada, y de segundo el pollo asado. ¿Qué recomienda de postre? ... La cuenta, por favor.*

And answer spontaneously:
- *¿Qué te gusta comer?* — *Me gusta mucho la pasta. Me encantan los postres.*
- *¿Qué van a tomar?* — *Para mí, quisiera una tortilla y una agua sin gas.*

---

## Gustar — The Essential Structure

*Gustar* works backwards from English. In English, you are the subject: **"I** like coffee." In Spanish, the coffee is the subject: *"Me gusta el café"* — literally, "Coffee pleases me."

**The two rules:**

1. The verb (*gusta / gustan*) must agree with the thing you like — not with you.
2. The indirect object pronoun (*me, te, le, nos, os, les*) tells us who is pleased.

| If you like… | Use | Example |
|---|---|---|
| A singular noun or an infinitive | **gusta** | *Me gusta el café. Me gusta cocinar.* |
| A plural noun | **gustan** | *Me gustan los tacos.* |

| Who likes | Pronoun |
|---|---|
| I | me |
| you (informal) | te |
| he / she / you (formal) | le |
| we | nos |
| you all (Spain) | os |
| they / you all (formal/LA) | les |

**The most common mistake:** *Yo gusto el café* — incorrect. The verb never agrees with you; it agrees with the food. Always: *Me gusta el café.*

---

## Encantar — Same Structure, Stronger Feeling

*Encantar* means "to love" (for things) and works exactly like *gustar*:

- *Me encanta el chocolate.* — I love chocolate.
- *Me encantan los postres.* — I love desserts.
- *¿Te encanta cocinar?* — Do you love cooking?

---

## Querer — For Ordering

*Querer* (to want) is one of the most useful verbs in a restaurant. It has a stem change: the *e* in the stem becomes *ie* in most forms.

| Subject | Querer |
|---|---|
| yo | **quiero** |
| tú | **quieres** |
| él / ella / usted | **quiere** |
| nosotros/as | queremos |
| vosotros/as | queréis |
| ellos / ellas / ustedes | **quieren** |

**Quisiera** — the polite form: "I would like." Treat it as a fixed phrase for now. It is slightly softer than *quiero* and always appropriate in a restaurant.

- *Quisiera una mesa para dos, por favor.*
- *Quisiera el menú del día.*

## What to Practice Between Sessions

**After Session 11 — before Session 12:**

1. **Say your food preferences.** *Me gusta..., me gustan..., no me gusta..., no me gustan...* Choose 3 real preferences. Say them without notes on the third try.
2. **Write 5 *gustar* sentences.** Mix singular and plural. Include at least one negative and one with an infinitive (*me gusta cocinar*).
3. **Check your verb agreement.** Every sentence: is the verb *gusta* or *gustan*? Does it match the noun, not you?

**After Session 12 — before Session 13:**

1. **Write a short restaurant dialogue.** 6–8 lines — you as the customer. Use *quisiera* at least twice and end with *la cuenta, por favor.*
2. **Say it out loud.** Third try: no notes on the ordering phrases.
3. **Learn one food item from a country you're interested in.** Look it up. Be ready to tell the class one thing about it.

---

## A Note on Mistakes This Week

**The *gustar* error is universal.** Every English speaker says *"Yo gusto el café"* at least once. Your instructor will correct it every time — not because it is a serious error, but because the habit of saying it correctly needs to form early. By Session 12, it should be automatic.

**Gusta vs. gustan:** if you are not sure, look at what comes after the verb. One thing → *gusta.* More than one thing → *gustan.* The verb agrees with the thing, not with you.

The goal this week is to get through a real restaurant interaction in Spanish. The grammar will come with practice.
