# Spanish A1 — Session 12 Slide Outline
## Restaurant Scenario: Querer, Quisiera, and the Full Meal

**Session length:** 55–60 minutes
**Session-end goal:** Every student can navigate a full Spanish-language restaurant interaction — arriving, ordering, asking a question, and paying — using *querer, quisiera,* and the *gustar* structure for preferences.

> **Pacing note:** The role play (Slide 7) is the session's core deliverable. Everything else serves it. Compress Slide 5 (cultural notes) to 3 minutes before cutting the role play. Every student plays both the customer and the waiter.

---

## Core 60-Minute Plan

### Slide 1 — Warm-Up: Gustar Recall (5 min)
**Purpose:** Confirm the *gustar* structure from Session 11 before extending into the restaurant scenario.
**Teacher notes:**
- Quick round: instructor says a noun or infinitive; student gives the full sentence with *me gusta/gustan.*
  - *el café* → *Me gusta el café.*
  - *los tacos* → *Me gustan los tacos.*
  - *comer en restaurantes* → *Me gusta comer en restaurantes.*
  - *las empanadas* → *Me gustan las empanadas.*
- Ask 2 students a *te gusta* question: *¿Te gusta la comida picante?* — students answer in a full sentence.
- Quick tempo. The goal is automatic production, not thinking time.
**Student activity:** 4-item gusta/gustan prompt → 2 *¿te gusta?* question-and-answer pairs.

---

### Slide 2 — Querer: Conjugation (8 min)
**Purpose:** Students learn the *querer* conjugation (e→ie stem change) and can use it to express wants and order food.
**Teacher notes:**
- Display the full table: *quiero / quieres / quiere / queremos / queréis / quieren.*
- Highlight the stem change: "The *e* in the stem becomes *ie* in four forms — yo, tú, él/ella, and ellos. The nosotros and vosotros forms keep the regular stem: *quer-emos / quer-éis.* This is called a stem-changing verb; there are many in Spanish, and we will meet more in A2."
- Do not launch a lesson on stem-changing verbs as a category this week — introduce them in Week 10 (*preferir, dormir*). This week: just learn *querer.*
- Choral drill × 3: slow → normal → fast.
- Spot check: each student gives one form at random (table covered).
**Student activity:** Choral drill × 3 → spot-check (one form per student, table covered).

---

### Slide 3 — Quisiera: The Polite Formula (5 min)
**Purpose:** Students learn *quisiera* as a fixed phrase for polite ordering.
**Teacher notes:**
- Introduce *quisiera* as a formula: "Technically this is the past subjunctive — a tense you will study in B1. For now, treat it as a fixed polite phrase: 'I would like.' It is softer than *quiero* and always appropriate in a formal restaurant."
- Model the difference in register:
  - *Quiero una paella.* — Direct, clear, perfectly acceptable.
  - *Quisiera una paella, por favor.* — Polite, formal, preferred in sit-down restaurants.
- Quick drill: instructor gives a food item; student says *quisiera + item + por favor.*
  - *una tortilla española* → *Quisiera una tortilla española, por favor.*
  - *un café con leche* → *Quisiera un café con leche, por favor.*
  - *la cuenta* → *Quisiera la cuenta, por favor.*
**Student activity:** 3-item *quisiera* drill → students each say one *quisiera* sentence.

---

### Slide 4 — Restaurant Vocabulary (8 min)
**Purpose:** Students learn the vocabulary and phrases they need for the role play.
**Teacher notes:**
- Menu structure: *la carta* (full menu), *el menú del día* (set lunch menu — typically two courses + drink at a fixed price), *el plato del día* (dish of the day).
- Key ordering phrases — customer:
  - *¿Tienen mesa para [número]?* — Do you have a table for [number]?
  - *¿Me puede traer la carta?* — Can you bring me the menu?
  - *Para mí, quisiera...* — For me, I'd like...
  - *¿Qué recomienda?* — What do you recommend?
  - *¿Qué tiene de postre?* — What do you have for dessert?
  - *La cuenta, por favor.* — The bill, please.
- Key waiter phrases (for students playing the waiter role):
  - *¿Qué van a tomar?* — What will you have?
  - *¿Les traigo algo más?* — Can I bring you anything else?
  - *¿Todo bien?* — Is everything OK?
  - *Aquí tiene.* — Here you are. (when bringing the bill or a dish)
- Regional note: *El camarero* (Spain) / *El mesero* (Latin America). Both understood everywhere.
**Student activity:** Repeat key phrases → each student says their full order in one sentence: *Para mí, quisiera... de primer plato... y de segundo...*

---

### Slide 5 — Cultural Notes: Restaurant Norms (5 min)
**Purpose:** Students understand the cultural norms of a Spanish-speaking restaurant so the role play feels authentic.
**Teacher notes:**
- **El menú del día (Spain):** A fixed-price lunch, usually €10–15, with two courses, bread, and a drink. Very common Monday–Friday. Ask for it with: *¿Tienen menú del día?*
- **Tipping:** In Spain: 5–10% appreciated but not mandatory. In Mexico: 10–15% expected. In Argentina: 10% common. Not automatic on the bill.
- **Calling the waiter:** In Spain: *"¡Oiga!"* or *"¡Disculpe!"* are acceptable. In Latin America: *"Disculpe"* or making eye contact is standard. Snapping fingers is considered rude.
- **Drinking water:** Tap water (*agua del grifo*) is safe in Spain and most of Western Latin America. In restaurants, asking for *agua del grifo* is acceptable in Spain; in some Latin American restaurants, bottled water is standard.
- **Ser vs. estar with food:** *Está rico.* (It's tasty — current state) vs. *Es rico.* (He/she/it is wealthy). Food context is a good reinforcement moment for the Week 2 distinction.
**Student activity:** No activity — listening + notes. Keep to 3 minutes if pressed.

---

### Slide 6 — Role Play Preparation (5 min)
**Purpose:** Students prepare their scripts before the live role play.
**Teacher notes:**
- Students get 4 minutes to prepare:
  - Customer role: arrive, ask for a table, order two courses + a drink, ask one question (*¿Qué recomienda?* or *¿Qué tiene de postre?*), request the bill.
  - Waiter role: greet, seat, take the order, respond to the question, bring the bill.
- Notes allowed for vocabulary. Ordering phrases (*quisiera, para mí, la cuenta*) should be from memory.
- Encourage students to use food items from the vocabulary file — the more specific, the more interesting.
**Student activity:** 4 minutes quiet preparation. Instructor circulates and answers vocabulary questions.

---

### Slide 7 — Full Restaurant Role Play (12 min)
**Purpose:** Every student navigates a complete Spanish restaurant interaction as both customer and waiter.
**Teacher notes:**
- Pairs: one student is the customer, one is the waiter. After 3–4 minutes, they switch.
- Customer checklist:
  - [ ] Asks for a table (*¿Tienen mesa para...?*)
  - [ ] Orders a drink and at least one food course
  - [ ] Asks one question (*¿Qué recomienda?*)
  - [ ] Requests the bill: *La cuenta, por favor.*
- Waiter checklist:
  - [ ] Greets the customer
  - [ ] Takes the order
  - [ ] Responds to the customer's question (invent a recommendation)
  - [ ] Brings the bill
- Instructor circulates and takes notes. Correct 1–2 items per student after their turn; do not interrupt during the role play.
- If online: breakout rooms of two. Instructor joins each room for 1 minute.
- Most likely error to correct: *yo gusto* or *me gusta el tacos* (agreement) if students use gustar during the role play.
**Student activity:** Full restaurant role play × 2 (both roles).

---

### Slide 8 — Wrap-Up (5 min)
**Purpose:** Assign homework; close the food week.
**Teacher notes:**
- Group debrief: 2 things the class did well, 1 error pattern to continue working on.
- Most likely: *me gusta los tacos* → *me gustan los tacos;* wrong *querer* forms (*quero* → *quiero*).
- Homework preview: written restaurant dialogue + gustar sentences.
- Preview Week 7: "Next week: shopping. You'll learn to describe clothes, ask for sizes, and shop in a Spanish market."
**Student activity:** Note homework. Optional closer: *"¿Qué te gusta más — la comida española o la comida latinoamericana?"*

## Cut First If Running Behind

1. **Slide 5 (cultural notes):** Reduce to 2 minutes — menú del día and the ser/estar food note only.
2. **Slide 3 (quisiera):** Cover as a formula only (30 seconds); skip the mini-drill. Students can use *quiero* in the role play.
3. **Slide 4 (restaurant vocabulary):** Cover customer phrases only; skip waiter phrases — the waiter can improvise.
4. Do not cut Slide 7 (role play) or Slide 6 (preparation time below 3 minutes).

---

## Extension for Fast Cohorts

- **Querer + infinitivo:** *Quiero pedir la paella.* — I want to order the paella. "Notice that *querer* + infinitivo works like *tener que* — verb + infinitive, no connector needed."
- **Pedir (e→i stem change, not covered this week):** *¿Qué pido?* — introduce it briefly as a preview of stem-changing verbs; students may notice it in the waiter's speech (*¿Qué van a pedir?*).
- **Nos gustan / les gusta:** Students practice the third-person and first-person plural forms: *¿Les gusta la paella?* → *Sí, nos encanta.*

## Move to Homework If Needed

- Reservation dialogue (*Quisiera reservar una mesa para esta noche...*)
- *Pedir* (e→i) — introduced formally in Week 10 alongside other stem-changing verbs
- Expressing opinions about a meal (*Estaba muy rico* — past tense comes in A2)
- Additional gustar-type verbs: *interesar, molestar, parecer* (Week 10/A2)
