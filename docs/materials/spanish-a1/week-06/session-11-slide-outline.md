# Spanish A1 — Session 11 Slide Outline
## Gustar and Food Preferences

**Session length:** 60 minutes
**Session-end goal:** Every student understands the *gustar* structure, can produce at least one correct *me gusta* and one *me gustan* sentence, and can express 3 food preferences and 1 dislike in spoken Spanish.

> **Pacing note:** The *gustar* explanation (Slide 2) must not be rushed — students who leave Session 11 without the structure secure will produce persistent errors through Week 10. Protect Slide 2 (12 min) and Slide 6 (conversation round, 12 min). Cut Slide 5 (encantar) to 3 minutes if needed.

---

## Core 60-Minute Plan

### Slide 1 — Warm-Up: Week 5 Close (5 min)
**Purpose:** Close the Week 5 loop; activate *tener* before adding a new structure.
**Teacher notes:**
- Ask each student to say one sentence about a family member using *tener*: age, expression, or possession.
- If the Week 5 formative check showed widespread *soy X años* (ser for age): 30-second group reminder — "*Tener* for age in Spanish: *tengo, tienes, tiene.*"
- Do not re-teach. Activation only.
**Student activity:** Each student says one *tener* sentence. (30 seconds each max.)

---

### Slide 2 — Gustar: The Structure (12 min)
**Purpose:** Students understand that *gustar* works the opposite way from English and can produce the basic structure.
**Teacher notes:**
- Open with the comparison: "In English: **I** like coffee — I is the subject. In Spanish: *Me gusta el café* — the coffee is the subject. Literally: 'Coffee is pleasing to me.'"
- Write the structure on screen:
  > **[Indirect object pronoun] + [gusta/gustan] + [the thing you like]**
  > *Me gusta el café. / Me gustan los tacos.*
- Introduce the indirect object pronouns: *me, te, le, nos, os, les.* These tell us WHO is being pleased.
- Show the two verb forms:
  - *gusta* → singular thing or infinitive: *Me gusta el café. Me gusta cocinar.*
  - *gustan* → plural things: *Me gustan los tacos. Me gustan las empanadas.*
- Address the error directly: "Some students want to say *yo gusto el café.* That means 'I am pleasing to coffee' — the coffee likes you. The correct form: *me gusta el café.*"
- Choral drill × 2: *Me gusta el café. Me gustan los tacos.* — Students say it together.
- Spot check: instructor gives a noun, students supply *me gusta* or *me gustan.*
**Student activity:** Choral drill × 2 → gusta/gustan spot check (6 items).

---

### Slide 3 — Gusta vs. Gustan: Drilling the Rule (8 min)
**Purpose:** Students produce *gusta* and *gustan* correctly without thinking about it.
**Teacher notes:**
- Rapid drill: instructor says a noun or infinitive; student says *me gusta* or *me gustan* + the item.
  - *el chocolate* → *me gusta el chocolate*
  - *los tacos* → *me gustan los tacos*
  - *la paella* → *me gusta la paella*
  - *las gambas* → *me gustan las gambas*
  - *comer* → *me gusta comer*
  - *las ensaladas* → *me gustan las ensaladas*
  - *el café* → *me gusta el café*
  - *los postres* → *me gustan los postres*
  - *viajar* → *me gusta viajar*
  - *la tortilla española* → *me gusta la tortilla española*
- Move fast — 30 seconds per item maximum. The goal is automaticity.
- Address errors immediately; slow down only if 2+ students make the same error.
**Student activity:** Rapid gusta/gustan drill (10 items, table visible).

---

### Slide 4 — Spanish and Latin American Food Vocabulary (10 min)
**Purpose:** Students learn 10–12 food items with cultural context and can say them with *me gusta/gustan.*
**Teacher notes:**
- Introduce 6 Spanish items and 4–6 Latin American items, grouped:
  - Spain: *la tortilla española, la paella, el bocadillo, las gambas al ajillo, el gazpacho, las croquetas*
  - Latin America: *los tacos, el ceviche, las arepas, las empanadas, el guacamole*
- After each item: students repeat it + say *me gusta* or *me gustan* with it.
- Add brief cultural context for 2–3 items: "The tortilla española is not a flatbread — it's a thick omelette made with potato and egg. It's eaten hot or cold, any time of day." "Ceviche is fish cured in lime juice — it's raw but not uncooked; the acid denatures the protein."
- Do not try to cover all items in the vocabulary file. Quality over quantity — students must say these words aloud.
**Student activity:** Repeat each item → say it with *me gusta* or *me gustan.*

---

### Slide 5 — Encantar (5 min)
**Purpose:** Students add *encantar* (to love) as a stronger version of *gustar* using the same structure.
**Teacher notes:**
- Say: "*Encantar* works exactly like *gustar* — same structure, same rules. It's just stronger: 'Me gusta el chocolate' (I like chocolate) vs. 'Me encanta el chocolate' (I love chocolate)."
- One additional rule: *Me encanta / me encantan* — same singular/plural agreement.
- Quick drill: instructor says a noun; students choose between *me gusta / me gustan / me encanta / me encantan.*
- Do not spend more than 5 minutes here. *Encantar* is a useful addition, not the core lesson.
**Student activity:** 4-item drill mixing *gusta, gustan, encanta, encantan.*

---

### Slide 6 — Conversation Round: Food Preferences (12 min)
**Purpose:** Every student expresses food preferences in spoken Spanish using *gustar.*
**Teacher notes:**
- Each student answers 3 questions from the instructor:
  1. *¿Qué te gusta comer?* — student gives 2 items (one singular, one plural if possible)
  2. *¿Qué te gusta más — la comida española o la comida latinoamericana?* — student compares
  3. *¿Qué no te gusta?* — student gives one dislike
- Notes allowed for food vocabulary. No notes for the *gustar* structure — this must be produced from memory.
- Correct one item per student: *me gusta los tacos* → *me gustan los tacos.*
- After all students: draw out 1 error pattern + 1 thing the group did well.
**Student activity:** Each student answers 3 food preference questions.

---

### Slide 7 — Wrap-Up (8 min)
**Purpose:** Preview Session 12 and assign homework.
**Teacher notes:**
- Preview Session 12: "Next session we go to the restaurant. You will learn *querer* (the verb for ordering) and a polite formula — *quisiera* — then we do a full restaurant role play."
- Homework preview: *gustar* sentences + food preferences writing.
- Optional closer: "Tell me one food item you learned today that you want to try."
**Student activity:** Note homework. Optional: one food preference sentence using *encantar.*

## Extension for Fast Cohorts

- **Other pronouns:** *¿Te gusta la paella? ¿Le gusta el café?* — Students ask each other and report back: *"A ella le gustan las croquetas."*
- **Gustar + infinitive vs. noun:** The difference: *me gusta comer* (I like eating — infinitive) vs. *me gusta la comida española* (I like Spanish food — noun). Practice mixing both in the same sentence: *Me encanta comer paella los domingos.*
- **No me gusta nada:** "I don't like it at all" — add *nada* after the object: *No me gusta nada el picante.* Double negative is required in Spanish.
