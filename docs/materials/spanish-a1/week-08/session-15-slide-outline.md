# Spanish A1 — Session 15 Slide Outline
## Describing a Home: Hay and Prepositions of Place

**Session length:** 60 minutes
**Session-end goal:** Every student can describe their home in 5–6 sentences — what rooms exist (*hay*), what furniture is in those rooms, and where things are — using *hay/no hay, estar + preposition,* and correct *del* contractions.

> **Pacing note:** The home description speaking round (Slide 6) is non-negotiable. Every student speaks before the session ends. Compress Slide 4 (furniture, to 6 min) before cutting Slide 6.

---

## Core 60-Minute Plan

### Slide 1 — Warm-Up: Week 7 Close (5 min)
**Purpose:** Close the Week 7 loop; activate demonstratives and shopping vocabulary before switching topics.
**Teacher notes:**
- Ask each student one sentence: describe one item of clothing using a demonstrative and a color. (*Esta chaqueta marrón cuesta cincuenta euros.*)
- If Week 7 formative check showed widespread este/esta errors: 30-second group reminder. Do not re-teach. Activation only.
- If *azules* vs. *azulas* came up as an error in Week 7: brief one-line reminder.
**Student activity:** One demonstrative + color sentence each.

---

### Slide 2 — Hay / No Hay and Hay vs. Estar (8 min)
**Purpose:** Students learn the existence phrase, its negation, and the critical distinction with *estar.*
**Teacher notes:**

**Part A — Hay (3 min):**
- Write *hay* and say: "*Hay* means 'there is' or 'there are.' It never changes — one bathroom, five bathrooms, *hay.*"
- Examples: *Hay una cocina. Hay tres habitaciones. No hay garaje.*
- Negation: "*No hay* — just add *no* before *hay.* The article disappears: 'no hay garaje,' not 'no hay un garaje.'" (Note: unlike French *il n'y a pas de*, Spanish *no hay* does not require an extra *de*. The article simply drops.)
- Quick drill: 3 items, students give affirmative and negative.
  - *un jardín* → *Hay un jardín. / No hay jardín.*
  - *un garaje* → *Hay un garaje. / No hay garaje.*
  - *una terraza* → *Hay una terraza. / No hay terraza.*

**Part B — Hay vs. Estar distinction (5 min):**
- This distinction is essential and worth explicit instruction. Say: "*Hay* introduces something that exists — you're announcing it for the first time. *Está/están* locates something specific and known."
- Side-by-side on the board:
  - *Hay un parque cerca de aquí.* — There's a park near here. (announcing existence)
  - *El parque está al lado del colegio.* — The park is next to the school. (locating a known park)
- Both sentences are about the same park. First we announce it with *hay*; then we locate it with *está.*
- Home context example:
  - *Hay un sofá en el salón.* → *El sofá está delante de la televisión.*
- Ask 3 students: *"¿Hay terraza en tu casa?"*
**Student activity:** Drill (3 items) → 3 students answer the terraza question.

---

### Slide 3 — Rooms Vocabulary (10 min)
**Purpose:** Students learn 8–10 room names with gender and can use them with *hay.*
**Teacher notes:**
- Introduce rooms in pairs where possible: *el salón / el comedor; el dormitorio / el cuarto de baño; la cocina / el despacho.*
- Also: *el aseo (small toilet room), el pasillo, la entrada, la terraza, el jardín, el garaje, el trastero.*
- Regional notes (one per room, max): *el dormitorio* (Spain) / *la habitación* (widely used); *el salón* (Spain) / *la sala* (Latin America); *el cuarto de baño* / *el baño.*
- After each room: students repeat + give one real *hay/no hay* fact.
- Floors: *en la planta baja, en el primer piso, arriba, abajo.* One example: *El dormitorio está arriba.*
**Student activity:** Repeat each room → give one true *hay/no hay* sentence.

---

### Slide 4 — Furniture and Household Items (8 min)
**Purpose:** Students learn 8–10 core furniture items and can combine them with *hay* and *estar.*
**Teacher notes:**
- Introduce items grouped by room:
  - *Dormitorio:* una cama, un armario
  - *Salón:* un sofá, una estantería, una mesa de centro
  - *Cocina:* un frigorífico (la nevera, el refrigerador), un horno, una lavadora
  - *Cuarto de baño:* una bañera / una ducha, un lavabo
- After each group: students give one *hay* sentence and one *está* sentence for the same item.
  - *Hay un sofá en el salón. El sofá está delante de la televisión.*
- Do not try to cover all items in the vocabulary file. 8–10 items used actively beats 20 on a list.
**Student activity:** Repeat each item → give *hay + está* pair for one item per group.

---

### Slide 5 — Prepositions of Place (10 min)
**Purpose:** Students learn 7 prepositions for interior space and apply *de + el = del.*
**Teacher notes:**
- Introduce each preposition with a clear physical demonstration:
  - *encima de* (on / on top of): *El libro está encima de la mesa.*
  - *debajo de* (under): *El gato está debajo de la cama.*
  - *delante de* (in front of): *El sofá está delante de la televisión.*
  - *detrás de* (behind): *El jardín está detrás de la casa.*
  - *entre* (between): *El despacho está entre el dormitorio y el salón.*
  - *al lado de* (next to): *El armario está al lado de la cama.*
  - *al fondo de* (at the far end of): *El despacho está al fondo del pasillo.*
- Address *del* immediately: "When *de* is followed by *el*, they contract to *del.* This is mandatory — you can never say 'de el' in Spanish. You already know *a + el = al* from Week 5 (al banco, al supermercado). The same thing happens: *de + el = del.* So: 'al lado **del** salón,' not 'al lado de el salón.'"
- Quick drill: instructor says two items; student places one using a preposition.
  - *la cama / la ventana* → *La cama está delante de la ventana.*
  - *el sofá / la televisión* → *El sofá está delante de la televisión.*
  - *el armario / el dormitorio* → *El armario está al fondo del dormitorio.*
  - *la estantería / la ventana* → *La estantería está al lado de la ventana.*
**Student activity:** 4-item placement drill using prepositions + *del* contraction.

---

### Slide 6 — Speaking Round: Describe Your Home (12 min)
**Purpose:** Every student describes their home in 5–6 sentences.
**Teacher notes:**
- Each student speaks for 60–90 seconds. Notes allowed for vocabulary; *hay* and prepositions from memory.
- Minimum required elements per student:
  - [ ] *Hay + [at least 3 rooms/features]*
  - [ ] *No hay + [something missing]*
  - [ ] At least 2 items placed with a preposition using *estar*
  - [ ] At least one *del* contraction (*al lado del armario, al fondo del pasillo*)
- Correct one item per student. Common targets: *de el* (should be *del*); *hay* used for location (*Hay el sofá delante* → should be *El sofá está delante*); *no hay de garaje* (should be *no hay garaje*).
- After all students: note 2 patterns across the group.
**Student activity:** Each student delivers a 5–6 sentence home description.

---

### Slide 7 — Wrap-Up (7 min)
**Purpose:** Assign homework; preview Session 16.
**Teacher notes:**
- Preview Session 16: "Next session we go to the neighborhood — and we add five essential irregular verbs: *hacer, venir, decir, saber, conocer.* You'll hear all five of them in almost every Spanish conversation."
- Homework: written home description + speaking practice.
**Student activity:** Note homework.

## Extension for Fast Cohorts

- **Adjectives for home:** *grande, pequeño/a, moderno/a, antiguo/a, luminoso/a, oscuro/a, acogedor/a.* Students describe rooms: *Tengo un salón luminoso pero una cocina muy pequeña.*
- **Hay vs. estar with neighborhood:** *Hay un supermercado en mi calle. El supermercado está al lado de la farmacia.* — practice the same pattern in a street context.
- **En + floor:** *Vivo en el tercer piso sin ascensor.* Students give their floor.
