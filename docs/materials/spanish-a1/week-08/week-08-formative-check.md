# Spanish A1 — Week 8 Formative Check
## Home, Neighborhood, and High-Frequency Irregular Verbs

**When to administer:** At the start of Session 17, or as a closing check at the end of Session 16.
**Format:** Oral section first (5 prompts); written section second (5 prompts).
**Purpose:** Diagnostic tool. Not a grade. Informs whether the cohort is ready to progress to Week 9 content (*ir a + infinitivo*, *tener que*, *hay que*, *poder*) or needs reinforcement of Week 8 structures.

---

## Oral Prompts (5 prompts — instructor reads aloud)

Administer to each student individually or in round-robin. Allow 10–15 seconds per prompt.

**Prompt 1:** "Tell me one thing that is in your home and where it is."
*Target:* *Hay un [noun] en [room]. El/La [noun] está [preposition] [noun].*
*Minimum pass:* Correct use of *hay* to introduce AND correct use of *está/están* to locate. Both in the same answer — this tests the *hay* vs. *estar* distinction directly.

**Prompt 2:** "Tell me one thing that is NOT in your home."
*Target:* *No hay [noun].*
*Minimum pass:* *No hay* without an extra article (*no hay un garaje* is actually also heard — accept, but the cleanest form is *no hay garaje*). No *de* after *no hay* (unlike French).

**Prompt 3:** "Place the sofa in relation to the television."
*Target:* *El sofá está delante de la televisión.* (Or another accurate preposition.)
*Watch for:* *De el televisor* (should be *del televisor*); *estar* with article-less noun (*el sofá está delante televisión* — missing *de*).

**Prompt 4:** "Give me the *yo* form of *hacer* and use it in a sentence."
*Target:* *Hago. Hago la compra los sábados. Hago mucho deporte.*
*Watch for:* *Yo haces* (→ *yo hago*); using *hace* for yo.

**Prompt 5:** "Tell me something you know how to do and someone or somewhere you are familiar with."
*Target:* One sentence with *saber* (skill or fact) + one sentence with *conocer* (person or place). *Sé nadar. Conozco bien Madrid.*
*Watch for:* *Sé a María* (→ *conozco a María*); *conozco que* (→ *sé que*); missing personal *a* before a person with *conocer*.

---

## Written Prompts (5 items — students write independently)

**Item 1:** Translate: "There is a bathroom next to the bedroom."
*Expected:* *Hay un cuarto de baño al lado del dormitorio.*
*Watch for:* *Al lado de el dormitorio* (must contract: *del*).

**Item 2:** Translate: "There isn't a garage but there is a terrace."
*Expected:* *No hay garaje pero hay una terraza.*
*Watch for:* *No hay un garaje* (acceptable but not the cleanest form); *no hay de garaje* (wrong — no *de* needed in Spanish).

**Item 3:** Fill in the blank: *El armario está al lado ___ dormitorio.*
*Expected:* *del* (*de + el = del*)
*Watch for:* *de el* — mandatory contraction in Spanish.

**Item 4:** Write one sentence using *venir* or *decir* in context.
*Expected:* *Vengo de Colombia. ¿Cómo se dice "bookshelf" en español?*
*Minimum pass:* Correct yo form used in a meaningful sentence.

**Item 5:** Write one sentence with *saber* and one with *conocer.* Both must be different from your oral answers.
*Expected:* Two sentences, each using the correct verb. *Sé dónde está la panadería. Conozco este barrio desde hace tres años.*
*Watch for:* Using the same verb both times; *conocer* with a *que* clause; *saber* with a person without a *que* or infinitive.

## Scoring Decision Table

| Student output | Decision |
|---|---|
| All 5 oral prompts pass + 4–5 written correct | Ready to move on to Week 9 |
| 4 oral pass + 3–4 written correct | Ready to move on — monitor *saber/conocer* in Week 9 |
| *Hay* and *no hay* both correct, but prepositions weak | Move to Week 9 — assign preposition consolidation as homework |
| *Saber/conocer* distinction not yet stable | Move to Week 9 — give one focused drill in Session 17 warm-up |
| *Hay vs. estar* distinction consistently wrong | Consider 5–8 minutes at Session 17 start on targeted drilling before introducing new content |
| Fewer than 3 oral prompts pass OR fewer than 3 written correct | Flag for instructor follow-up; do not delay Week 9 for whole cohort |

## Ready to Move On

Before beginning Week 9 (*ir a + infinitivo*, *tener que*, *hay que*, *poder*), a student should be able to:
- [ ] Say *hay / no hay* automatically, without hesitation
- [ ] Distinguish *hay* (existence) from *está/están* (location) in most contexts
- [ ] Use at least 3 prepositions of place accurately in speech
- [ ] Apply *del* contraction in at least 1 context
- [ ] Conjugate *hacer* correctly — especially *yo hago*
- [ ] Conjugate *venir* correctly — especially *yo vengo* and *ellos vienen*
- [ ] Choose correctly between *saber* and *conocer* in most cases
- [ ] Describe their home in 4+ spoken sentences

---

## Not a Gate

The following items are NOT required for progression to Week 9:
- Full mastery of all 5 verb conjugations (gaps in *decir* or *conocer nosotros/vosotros* forms are acceptable)
- Perfect *del* application in all preposition contexts (instinctive use develops over A1–A2 transition)
- Extended neighborhood vocabulary beyond 3–4 core words
- *Acabar de + infinitivo* (recent past) — extension material
- Perfect *hay vs. estar* in every context; consistent directional understanding is the target
- Personal *a* with *conocer* in every case — flag it, reinforce in Week 9, don't block progression
