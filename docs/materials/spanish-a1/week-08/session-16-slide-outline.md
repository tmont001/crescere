# Spanish A1 — Session 16 Slide Outline
## High-Frequency Irregular Verbs and the Neighborhood

**Session length:** 53 minutes (on plan) — can run 60 min for slower cohorts
**Session-end goal:** Every student delivers a 5–6 sentence description of their home AND neighborhood, using at least one of the five irregular verbs (*hacer, venir, decir, saber, conocer*) and demonstrating the *saber/conocer* distinction.

> **Pacing note:** The combined home+neighborhood speaking round (Slide 7) is non-negotiable. Compress individual verb blocks (each by 1–2 min) before cutting the speaking round. If 5 verbs is too dense in class, assign *decir* and *saber/conocer* as vocabulary reference and drill only *hacer* and *venir* live — but the speaking round stays.

---

## Core Plan

### Slide 1 — Warm-Up: Hay Recall (5 min)
**Purpose:** Re-activate Session 15 material; bridge to neighborhood.
**Teacher notes:**
- Ask each student one sentence: *"Dime una cosa que hay en tu casa."* (One *hay* or *está* sentence with a preposition.)
- Quickly correct any *de el* errors (should be *del*). Do not re-teach.
- Brief preview: "Today we go outside — and add five verbs that are essential in Spanish."
**Student activity:** One *hay/está + preposition* sentence each.

---

### Slide 2 — Hacer (7 min)
**Purpose:** Students learn and can use *hacer* in context.
**Teacher notes:**
- Write the full conjugation:

  | yo | tú | él/ella | nosotros | vosotros | ellos/ellas |
  |---|---|---|---|---|---|
  | **hago** | haces | hace | hacemos | hacéis | hacen |

- Address the yo form explicitly: "Only *yo* is irregular: *hago.* All other forms are regular -er endings. If you remember *hago*, you have this verb."
- Three key expressions to drill immediately:
  - *hacer la compra / ir al súper* — grocery shopping
  - *hacer deporte / hacer ejercicio* — exercise
  - *hace calor / hace frío / hace sol / hace viento* — weather (note: *hace* here is impersonal — the weather "does" these things in Spanish)
- Flag: *¿Qué haces?* — What are you doing? / What do you do? (extremely high frequency)
- Quick round: "What do you do on the weekend?" → *Hago...* — each student.
**Student activity:** Give one *yo hago* sentence about a weekend activity.

---

### Slide 3 — Venir (6 min)
**Purpose:** Students learn and can use *venir* for origin and arrival.
**Teacher notes:**
- Write the full conjugation:

  | yo | tú | él/ella | nosotros | vosotros | ellos/ellas |
  |---|---|---|---|---|---|
  | **vengo** | vienes | viene | venimos | venís | vienen |

- Address the yo form: "*Vengo* — the yo form is irregular (like *tengo, pongo*). The other forms have an e→ie stem change in the singular and 3rd plural: *vienes, viene, vienen.*"
- Two key uses:
  - Origin: *Vengo de México / de Colombia / de España.*
  - Arrival: *¿A qué hora vienes?* — What time are you coming?
- Quick round: "Where are you from?" → *Vengo de...* — each student.
**Student activity:** One *yo vengo de* sentence (origin).

---

### Slide 4 — Decir (5 min)
**Purpose:** Students learn *decir* for the most immediately useful phrase: *¿Cómo se dice...?*
**Teacher notes:**
- Write the full conjugation:

  | yo | tú | él/ella | nosotros | vosotros | ellos/ellas |
  |---|---|---|---|---|---|
  | **digo** | dices | dice | decimos | decís | **dicen** |

- Address the yo form: "*Digo* — another yo-go verb. *Dicen* in 3rd plural has e→i."
- Most useful phrase in class: *¿Cómo se dice "[word]" en español?* — How do you say "[word]" in Spanish? Students should say this phrase immediately after learning it.
- Secondary: *¿Qué dice?* — What does he/she say? (useful for role plays)
- Quick round: each student asks *¿Cómo se dice "[English word]" en español?* — instructor answers.
**Student activity:** Each student asks *¿Cómo se dice...?* for one word they don't know.

---

### Slide 5 — Saber vs. Conocer (8 min)
**Purpose:** Students learn both verbs, distinguish between them, and can use each correctly.
**Teacher notes:**
- Write BOTH conjugation tables side by side:

  **Saber:**
  | yo | tú | él/ella | nosotros | vosotros | ellos/ellas |
  |---|---|---|---|---|---|
  | **sé** | sabes | sabe | sabemos | sabéis | saben |

  **Conocer:**
  | yo | tú | él/ella | nosotros | vosotros | ellos/ellas |
  |---|---|---|---|---|---|
  | **conozco** | conoces | conoce | conocemos | conocéis | conocen |

- Key: *saber* = facts, skills, information. *Conocer* = familiarity with people or places.
- Personal *a* rule: "*Conocer* requires personal *a* before a person: *Conozco a María.* You know someone — the personal *a* marks the person as human.*"
- Four contrast examples — students identify which verb:
  1. "I know Madrid." → *Conozco Madrid* ✓
  2. "I know what time it is." → *Sé qué hora es* ✓
  3. "Do you know Carlos?" → *¿Conoces a Carlos?* ✓ (note personal *a*)
  4. "I don't know." → *No sé.* (universal)
- Quick test: say a sentence stem; students finish with *sé/conozco* (or another form):
  - *Yo ___ nadar.* → *sé*
  - *Tú ___ este restaurante?* → *¿Conoces*
  - *¿___ dónde está la estación?* → *¿Sabes*
  - *Ella ___ bien mi barrio.* → *conoce*
- Note: *no sé* is one of the most frequent Spanish phrases — it should be instant and automatic.
**Student activity:** 4-item *sé/conozco* identification → rapid-fire round.

---

### Slide 6 — Neighborhood Vocabulary (5 min)
**Purpose:** Students acquire the 5–6 key neighborhood words and phrases needed for the speaking round.
**Teacher notes:**
- Core places: *una panadería, una farmacia, una estación de metro / una parada de autobús, un parque, un colegio, un supermercado, un ayuntamiento.*
- Location phrases: *en la esquina de la calle, enfrente de mi casa, a cinco minutos andando, al final de la calle, cerca de.*
- Neighborhood adjectives: *tranquilo/a, animado/a, ruidoso/a, cómodo/a, agradable, residencial.*
- Quick round: "Describe your neighborhood in one adjective." → *Mi barrio es...*
**Student activity:** One adjective + location phrase about their neighborhood.

---

### Slide 7 — Speaking Round: My Home and My Neighborhood (12 min)
**Purpose:** Every student integrates Session 15 and Session 16 content in a connected description.
**Teacher notes:**
- Each student speaks for 60–90 seconds. Notes allowed for vocabulary; *hay, estar + preposition,* and at least one of the five verbs from memory.
- Minimum required elements per student:
  - [ ] *Hay + [at least 2 home features]* with at least one preposition
  - [ ] At least one of: *hacer, venir, decir*
  - [ ] *Conozco* OR *Sé* correctly used
  - [ ] At least 2 neighborhood sentences (adjective + what's nearby)
- Correct one item per student. Common targets: *yo haces* (→ *yo hago*); *de el* (→ *del*); *sé a María* (→ *conozco a María*).
- After all students: note 2 patterns across the group.
**Student activity:** Each student delivers a 5–6 sentence home + neighborhood description.

---

### Slide 8 — Wrap-Up: Week 8 Close (5 min)
**Purpose:** Assign homework; preview Week 9; close Week 8.
**Teacher notes:**
- Preview Week 9: "Next week we look ahead — how to talk about plans and intentions. *Voy a ir al cine esta noche. Tengo que trabajar mañana.*"
- Homework: written home+neighborhood paragraph + all five verb conjugations from memory.
- Remind students: the Week 8 formative check will ask them to produce verb forms and demonstrate the *saber/conocer* distinction.
**Student activity:** Note homework.

---

## Cut First If Running Behind

1. **Slide 4 (decir):** Reduce to 2 minutes — teach only *¿Cómo se dice...?* and move on.
2. **Slide 6 (neighborhood vocabulary):** Reduce to 3 items; move vocabulary to homework reference.
3. **Slides 2–4 combined:** If very behind, do *hacer* in full; give *venir* and *decir* as reference only.
4. Do not cut Slide 7 (speaking round).

---

## Extension for Fast Cohorts

- **Venir de + infinitivo (recent past):** *Acabo de llegar a casa.* — I've just got home. (Note: Spanish uses *acabar de + infinitivo* for this, not *venir de.* If students ask, flag this difference from French.)
- **Hacer + weather questions:** *¿Qué tiempo hace en tu ciudad en verano?* — extended weather conversation.
- **Saber + indirect question:** *Sé dónde está la estación / qué hora es / cómo se llama.* Students add a different indirect question with *saber.*
- **Compare two neighborhoods:** *Mi barrio es más tranquilo que el centro pero menos animado.*
