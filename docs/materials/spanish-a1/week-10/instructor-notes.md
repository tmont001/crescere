# Spanish A1 — Week 10 Instructor Notes

**Sessions:** 19 and 20
**Theme:** Hobbies and Leisure

---

## Week Objective

By the end of Week 10, students can:
- Use *jugar a* correctly with sports and games — including *al* contraction
- Use *hacer* correctly with physical activities (no article)
- Use *practicar* with sports as a discipline (with definite article)
- Apply frequency adverbs (*siempre, normalmente, a veces, de vez en cuando, casi nunca, nunca*) in speech
- Express opinions about leisure using *gustar* (reinforced), *encantar, odiar, preferir*
- Conjugate *preferir* correctly (e→ie stem change)
- Make simple comparisons (*me gusta más... que, prefiero A a B*)
- Sustain a 3-minute free-time conversation with a partner

**Relationship to the curriculum:** This is exactly the Week 10 content specified. Session 19 = *jugar a / hacer / practicar* + frequency adverbs. Session 20 = *gustar* reinforced + *encantar/odiar/preferir* + comparisons.

**Prior knowledge students bring:**
- *Gustar* — full structure known from Week 6 (food context). Week 10 reinforces it with hobbies and sports.
- *Hacer* — full conjugation known from Week 8. *Hacer + activity* is a new use of a known verb.
- *Ir a + lugar* — *al* contraction (*a + el = al*) from Week 5 (directions). *Jugar al fútbol* extends the same contraction.
- *Querer* (e→ie) — from Week 6. *Preferir* follows the same stem-change pattern; connecting them explicitly saves teaching time.

## Common Student Mistakes — Week 10

| Mistake | Correction | When to address |
|---|---|---|
| *Juego de fútbol* (wrong preposition) | *Juego **al** fútbol.* Sports with an opponent → *jugar a* + *al/a la/a las.* | Session 19 immediately |
| *Hago al yoga* (wrong preposition) | *Hago yoga.* Activities with *hacer* take no preposition and no article. | Session 19 immediately |
| *No juego al fútbol nunca* (double negation with wrong placement) | *Nunca juego al fútbol.* OR *No juego nunca al fútbol.* Both are acceptable. The key is: *nunca* before verb = no *no* needed; *nunca* after verb = *no* required before verb. | Session 19 frequency block |
| *Me gusta los deportes* (wrong form) | *Me **gustan** los deportes.* Plural noun → *gustan.* | Session 20 gustar review block |
| *Me encanto el tenis* (using yo form) | *Me **encanta** el tenis.* *Encantar* works like *gustar* — the tennis is the grammatical subject, so the verb agrees with tennis (singular → *encanta*). | Session 20 encantar block |
| *Prefiero fútbol a baloncesto* (missing articles) | *Prefiero **el** fútbol **al** baloncesto.* Both nouns need articles in the comparison; the second requires *al* (*a + el*). | Session 20 preferir block |
| *Yo quiero nadar más que correr* (using *querer* for preference) | Fine at this stage — don't over-correct. Note: *Prefiero nadar a correr* is more natural for comparison. | If it arises — gentle redirect |
| *Odio de las verduras* (adding *de*) | *Odio las verduras.* *Odiar* is a regular -ar verb; takes direct object with definite article, no *de.* | Session 20 if it arises |

## Suggested Instructor Language

- **For *jugar a*:** *"Jugar a — to play a sport or game. The a contracts: a + el = al. 'Juego al fútbol' (masculine singular). 'Juego a las cartas' (plural). Same al contraction you used in Week 5 for directions: al banco, al supermercado. Same rule."*

- **For *hacer + activity*:** *"Hacer plus the activity name — no preposition, no article. 'Hago yoga.' 'Hago senderismo.' 'Hago footing.' No 'el', no 'la', no 'un'. Just hacer + the noun. This is very different from jugar a. A quick rule: if you're competing against someone, use jugar a. If you're doing something solo — running, yoga, hiking — use hacer."*

- **For *nunca*:** *"Nunca can go before the verb or after it. Before: 'Nunca juego al golf' — no extra 'no' needed. After: 'No juego nunca al golf' — the 'no' is required. Both are correct. The before-verb position is slightly stronger and more common in speech."*

- **For *gustar* review:** *"You already know gustar from Week 6 with food. It works exactly the same with hobbies. 'Me gusta el fútbol' — I like football (singular noun, gusta). 'Me gustan los deportes' — I like sports (plural noun, gustan). 'Me gusta jugar' — I like playing (infinitive, always gusta). Nothing changes from what you learned."*

- **For *preferir*:** *"Preferir has the same e→ie stem change as querer. Quiero — yo form. Prefiero — same pattern. So: prefiero, prefieres, prefiere... but preferimos, preferís. If you know quiero, you know prefiero."*

## What to Skip If Time Is Tight

- *Practicar* as a third verb option — mention once; don't drill in class
- *Tanto como* comparison (focus on *más que / menos que* first)
- Extended exclamation phrases (*¡Qué buena idea! ¡Qué interesante!*) — useful but not essential
- Full cultural note on regional sport variation (béisbol in the Caribbean, etc.) — one sentence max
- The difference between *odiar* and *no me gusta* in formal vs. colloquial registers

→ Students write 4–5 sentences about their sports habits; open Session 20 with sharing

If Session 20 conversation round overruns:
→ Students write a paragraph comparing two leisure activities using *preferir* and *me gusta más... que*; share at start of Session 21

## Cultural Context for Instructors

- **El fútbol:** In Spain and most of Latin America, football (soccer) is the dominant sport. *El Clásico* (Real Madrid vs. FC Barcelona) is a cultural moment. In Mexico, baseball (*el béisbol*) and *la lucha libre* are also major. In Venezuela, the Dominican Republic, Cuba, and Puerto Rico, baseball rivals or surpasses football. Flag this for cohorts with Latin American learners — don't assume fútbol is universal.
- **El pádel:** Pádel (paddle tennis) is enormously popular in Spain and spreading in Latin America. More common as an everyday sport than tennis in many Spanish cities. Worth including as a bonus vocabulary item if students are Spain-focused.
- **¿Haces deporte?:** This is the standard question to ask about someone's general sporting activity in Spain. In Latin America, *¿Practicas algún deporte?* is more common. Both are worth knowing at A1.
- **Hacer footing:** *Footing* is a Francophone-influenced term used in Spain for jogging. In Latin America, *correr* or *hacer ejercicio* is more common. Note the regional difference.
