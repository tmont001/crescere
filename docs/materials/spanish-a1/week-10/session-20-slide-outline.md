# Spanish A1 — Session 20 Slide Outline
## Preferences and Opinions: Gustar, Encantar, Odiar, Preferir

**Session length:** 47 minutes (on plan) — can run 60 min for slower cohorts
**Session-end goal:** Every student can sustain a 3-minute free-time preference conversation — sharing opinions, asking questions, and expressing a comparison using *preferir* and at least one comparative phrase.

> **Pacing note:** The free-time conversation round (Slide 6) is non-negotiable. Every student participates in a real extended exchange before the session ends. Compress the comparisons block (Slide 5, to 3 min) before cutting Slide 6. *Gustar* is known from Week 6 — the review here should move quickly.

---

## Core Plan

### Slide 1 — Warm-Up: Jugar a / Hacer Recall (5 min)
**Purpose:** Re-activate Session 19 content before building opinion language on top.
**Teacher notes:**
- Each student gives one sentence from their homework leisure description.
- Quickly correct any *juego de fútbol* or *hago al yoga* errors.
- Preview: "Today we learn to say what you love, hate, and prefer. After this session, you'll be able to have a full conversation about free time in Spanish."
**Student activity:** One *jugar a / hacer* sentence each.

---

### Slide 2 — Gustar Reinforced with Hobbies (7 min)
**Purpose:** Students consolidate *gustar* in the hobbies context; confirm *gusta* vs. *gustan* rule.
**Teacher notes:**
- Students know *gustar* from Week 6 (food). This slide transfers that knowledge to hobbies.
- Say: "Gustar works exactly the same here. The sport or activity is the grammatical subject."
- Quick examples:
  - *Me gusta el fútbol.* (singular noun → gusta)
  - *Me gustan los deportes.* (plural noun → gustan)
  - *Me gusta jugar al tenis.* (infinitive → always gusta)
  - *Me gusta hacer senderismo.* (infinitive → always gusta)
- Quick round: each student gives one *me gusta/n* sentence about a sport or leisure activity.
- Address any persistent errors from Week 6: *me gusto* (→ *me gusta*); *me gusta los deportes* (→ *me gustan*).
**Student activity:** One *me gusta / me gustan* sentence about a leisure activity each.

---

### Slide 3 — Encantar and Odiar (5 min)
**Purpose:** Students learn *encantar* (same structure as *gustar*) and *odiar* (regular -ar verb).
**Teacher notes:**
- *Encantar* — same structure as *gustar.* "If *gustar* means 'to like,' *encantar* means 'to love' or 'to be crazy about.'"
  - *Me encanta el tenis.* (singular → encanta)
  - *Me encantan los videojuegos.* (plural → encantan)
  - *Me encanta nadar.* (infinitive → encanta)
- *Odiar* — simple regular -ar verb. Takes definite article + noun.
  - *Odio el golf.* *Odio los lunes.* *Odias hacer ejercicio?*
  - Conjugation: *odio, odias, odia, odiamos, odiáis, odian* — regular, no surprises.
- Scale: *encantar → gustar → no gustar → odiar*
- Quick round: students give one *me encanta* and one *odio* sentence.
**Student activity:** One *me encanta* sentence + one *odio* sentence each.

---

### Slide 4 — Preferir Conjugation (8 min)
**Purpose:** Students learn *preferir* with the e→ie stem change and can use it to express preferences.
**Teacher notes:**
- *Preferir* is new. Write the full conjugation:

  | yo | tú | él/ella | nosotros | vosotros | ellos/ellas |
  |---|---|---|---|---|---|
  | **prefiero** | **prefieres** | **prefiere** | preferimos | preferís | **prefieren** |

- Make the connection to known verbs: "Same stem change as *querer* (Week 6) and *pensar* (Week 9). Yo → *prefiero.* Same pattern."
- Two key uses:
  - *Prefiero el fútbol al baloncesto.* — I prefer football to basketball. (*preferir A al/a la B*)
  - *Prefiero jugar al fútbol a hacer ciclismo.* — I prefer playing football to cycling. (infinitive comparison)
- Warning: *Prefiero el fútbol al baloncesto* — both nouns need articles; the second becomes *al* (*a + el*).
- Quick round: "What do you prefer — sport or a cultural activity?" Each student gives one *prefiero* sentence.
**Student activity:** Give one *prefiero A al B* sentence.

---

### Slide 5 — Comparison Structures (5 min)
**Purpose:** Students learn 2–3 comparison structures and can use them in conversation.
**Teacher notes:**
- Introduce concisely:
  - *Me gusta más el tenis que el golf.* — I like tennis more than golf.
  - *Prefiero la montaña al mar.* — I prefer the mountains to the sea.
  - *Me gusta tanto el tenis como el pádel.* — I like tennis as much as padel.
- Quick drill — students complete the comparison:
  - *Me gusta más [sport] que...*
  - *Prefiero [activity] a/al...*
- Do not over-engineer this. Two patterns used naturally beat five patterns used with anxiety.
**Student activity:** Students complete 2 comparison starters.

---

### Slide 6 — Free-Time Conversation Round (12 min)
**Purpose:** Every student sustains a 3-minute free-time preference conversation.
**Teacher notes:**
- Conversation structure (students improvise from this framework):
  - A asks: *"¿Qué haces en tu tiempo libre?"*
  - B describes: 2–3 leisure habits with frequency adverbs
  - A asks a follow-up: *"¿Te gusta? ¿Desde cuándo?"* or *"¿Qué prefieres?"*
  - B answers + opinion + comparison
  - B asks A the same
- Each pair or instructor-student exchange runs 2–3 minutes. Every student speaks.
- Minimum elements in each student's output:
  - [ ] At least 2 *jugar a / hacer* sentences
  - [ ] At least 1 frequency adverb
  - [ ] At least 1 opinion verb (*encantar, gustar, odiar*)
  - [ ] At least 1 *preferir* sentence or comparison
  - [ ] One reaction phrase (*¡Qué interesante! / ¿De verdad?*)
- Correct one item per student. Common targets: *me encanto el tenis* (→ *me encanta*); *prefiero fútbol al golf* (→ *prefiero el fútbol*); *yo quiero el tenis más* → suggest *prefiero.*
**Student activity:** Each student participates in a full 2–3 minute leisure exchange.

---

### Slide 7 — Wrap-Up: Week 10 Close (5 min)
**Purpose:** Assign homework; preview Week 11.
**Teacher notes:**
- Preview Week 11: "Next week we focus on health and the body — *me duele la cabeza, tengo fiebre, no me siento bien.* Pharmacy scenario and, in Session 22, a doctor's appointment. The *doler* structure works like *gustar* — you'll already know how it works."
- Homework: written preference paragraph + *preferir* conjugation.
- Remind students: the Week 10 formative check will ask them to describe leisure habits and use *preferir* correctly.
**Student activity:** Note homework.

## Cut First If Running Behind

1. **Slide 5 (comparisons):** Reduce to just *me gusta más... que* — one pattern only.
2. **Slide 3 (encantar / odiar):** *Encantar* only; skip *odiar* formal drill (just give the form).
3. **Slide 4 (preferir):** Reduce to *yo, tú, él* forms + one example; move full table to homework.
4. Do not cut Slide 6 (conversation round).

---

## Extension for Fast Cohorts

- **Exclamations:** *¡Qué buena idea! ¡Qué interesante! ¡Qué aburrido!* — short reactions that make conversation sound natural. Students collect 3 and use them in the round.
- **Superlative preview:** *Mi deporte favorito es el tenis.* — using *favorito/a* as an adjective.
- **Desde hace:** *Juego al tenis desde hace cinco años.* — Previewing the duration structure from Week 11.
- **Opinion on culture:** *Me encanta el cine español pero prefiero el cine latinoamericano. ¿A ti qué te gusta más?*

- Written preference paragraph comparing 3 activities

- Negative opinion sentences with reasons (*no me gusta... porque...*)
