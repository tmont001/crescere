# Spanish A1 — Session 19 Slide Outline
## Sports and Hobbies: Jugar a and Hacer

**Session length:** 50 minutes (on plan) — can run 60 min for slower cohorts
**Session-end goal:** Every student can describe their sports and leisure habits in 5–6 sentences, correctly using *jugar a* (with *al* contraction) and *hacer* (no article), with at least two frequency adverbs.

> **Pacing note:** The speaking round (Slide 6) is non-negotiable. Every student speaks before the session ends. The *practicar* block (Slide 4) is brief by design — it is context only, not a drilling priority.

---

## Core 60-Minute Plan

### Slide 1 — Warm-Up: Week 9 Close (5 min)
**Purpose:** Close the Week 9 loop; activate modal verbs before switching to leisure.
**Teacher notes:**
- Ask each student one sentence from Week 9: *"¿Qué vas a hacer este fin de semana?"* or *"¿Qué tienes que hacer esta semana?"*
- If Week 9 formative check showed widespread *tener que* errors: 30-second group reminder. No re-teaching.
- Preview: "This week we talk about hobbies and free time — one of the most natural conversations you'll have in Spanish."
**Student activity:** One planning or obligation sentence each from Week 9 vocabulary.

---

### Slide 2 — Jugar a (10 min)
**Purpose:** Students learn and can produce *jugar a* with the correct *al* contraction.
**Teacher notes:**
- Write *jugar a* on the board. Say: "Jugar a — playing against someone. A sport, a game, something competitive."
- Build the contraction rule visually:
  - *a + el = al* → *Juego **al** fútbol.*
  - *a + la = a la* → *Juego **a la** petanca.*
  - *a + las = a las* → *Juego **a las** cartas.*
- Introduce 8 sports/games:
  - *el fútbol, el tenis, el baloncesto, el golf, el béisbol, el voleibol, el ajedrez, las cartas*
- After each: students repeat + say *juego al/a la/a las* and then *no juego al.*
- Quick round: "What sports do you play?" Each student gives one true answer.
- Flag the most common error: *Juego de fútbol* — wrong. Say it wrong on purpose then correct it: "*No decimos juego **de** fútbol — decimos juego **al** fútbol. El 'a' no desaparece aquí.*"
**Student activity:** Give one true *juego al* sentence; identify the correct form for 4 sports.

---

### Slide 3 — Hacer + Activity (8 min)
**Purpose:** Students learn and can produce *hacer + activity noun* with no article.
**Teacher notes:**
- Write *hacer* on the board. Say: "Hacer — doing an activity, usually physical, usually alone. Yoga, senderismo, footing."
- **Key rule — teach immediately:** "After *hacer*, no article. Not *hago el yoga*, not *hago un yoga* — just *hago yoga.* That's it. No article."
- Introduce 7 activities:
  - *yoga, footing, senderismo, deporte, surf, natación, ciclismo*
- After each: students repeat + say *hago + activity* (no article).
- Negation: *No hago deporte los lunes.* — No article to worry about; just *no* before *hago.*
- Contrast drill: students decide:
  - *el tenis* → *Juego al tenis* (opponent → *jugar a*)
  - *yoga* → *Hago yoga* (solo → *hacer*, no article)
  - *las cartas* → *Juego a las cartas*
  - *footing* → *Hago footing*
**Student activity:** Give one true *hago + activity* sentence; contrast drill (4 items).

---

### Slide 4 — Practicar: Brief Note (4 min)
**Purpose:** Students understand *practicar* as a third option for sports, used at club/training level.
**Teacher notes:**
- Brief introduction only. "There's a third option you'll hear: *practicar*. It means practising a sport at a more formal or club level. 'Practico la natación en el club olímpico.' Note: *practicar* takes the article. But for everyday conversation, *hacer* covers most cases."
- Three examples only — no drilling:
  - *Practico la natación desde los 10 años.*
  - *Practica el atletismo en un equipo.*
  - *¿Practicas algún deporte?* — casual conversation opener.
- Flag: *¿Practicas algún deporte?* is a very common question in Spanish — students should be able to answer it.
**Student activity:** Students answer: *"¿Practicas algún deporte?"* — one sentence each.

---

### Slide 5 — Frequency Adverbs (7 min)
**Purpose:** Students learn 6 core frequency adverbs and can use them to make their descriptions natural.
**Teacher notes:**
- Introduce adverbs on a scale from always to never:
  - *siempre → normalmente → a veces → de vez en cuando → casi nunca → nunca*
- After each: students produce one sentence combining *jugar a* or *hacer* with the adverb.
  - *Siempre hago deporte por la mañana.*
  - *Normalmente juego al tenis los sábados.*
  - *Nunca juego al golf.*
- Flag *nunca* placement: "*Nunca* before the verb: 'Nunca juego al golf' — no extra *no* needed. *Nunca* after the verb: 'No juego nunca al golf' — you need the *no.* Both are correct."
- Quick: each student gives one sentence with a frequency adverb.
**Student activity:** One frequency adverb sentence each.

---

### Slide 6 — Speaking Round: My Leisure Habits (12 min)
**Purpose:** Every student describes their sports and leisure habits in 5–6 connected sentences.
**Teacher notes:**
- Each student speaks for 60–90 seconds. Notes allowed for vocabulary; *jugar a, hacer,* and frequency adverbs from memory.
- Minimum required elements per student:
  - [ ] At least 1 *juego al* sentence (with correct *al* contraction)
  - [ ] At least 1 *hago + activity* sentence (no article)
  - [ ] At least 1 negative sentence (*no juego al / no hago*)
  - [ ] At least 2 frequency adverbs
- Correct one item per student. Common targets: *juego de fútbol* (→ *juego al fútbol*); *hago el yoga* (→ *hago yoga*); wrong *nunca* placement.
- After all students: note 2 patterns.
**Student activity:** Each student delivers a 5–6 sentence leisure habits description.

---

### Slide 7 — Wrap-Up (4 min)
**Purpose:** Assign homework; preview Session 20.
**Teacher notes:**
- Preview Session 20: "Next session we add opinion language — how to say you love, hate, or prefer activities. We reinforce *gustar* with hobbies, add *encantar* and *odiar*, and introduce *preferir.*"
- Homework: written leisure description + frequency adverb practice.
**Student activity:** Note homework.

---

## Cut First If Running Behind

1. **Slide 4 (practicar):** Cut entirely. *Jugar a* and *hacer* are the priorities.
2. **Slide 3 (hacer):** Reduce to 5 activities; skip *surf* and *ciclismo.*
3. **Slide 5 (frequency adverbs):** Cover *siempre, a veces, nunca* only (3 instead of 6).
4. Do not cut Slide 6 (speaking round).

---

## Extension for Fast Cohorts

- **¿Desde cuándo juegas?** — *Juego al tenis desde hace cinco años.* (*Desde hace* previews Week 11 grammar — introduce informally here.)
- **Instruments:** *Tocar + instrument* — *Toco la guitarra / el piano.* Note: *tocar* for instruments, not *jugar a* or *hacer.*
- **Compound leisure descriptions:** *Los fines de semana normalmente juego al fútbol con mis amigos, pero de vez en cuando hago senderismo si hace buen tiempo. Nunca hago yoga — no tengo paciencia.*
- **El pádel:** If cohort is Spain-focused, add *jugar al pádel* as a bonus. Enormously popular in contemporary Spain.

## Move to Homework If Needed

- Full *jugar a* vs. *hacer* written contrast exercises
- Frequency adverb ranking (list in order from most to least frequent)
- Extended sports vocabulary beyond the core 8–9 items
- *Practicar* in written context
