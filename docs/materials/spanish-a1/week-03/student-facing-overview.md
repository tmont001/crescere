# Spanish A1 — Week 3 Student Overview

**Theme: Regular Verbs and Daily Routine**

---

## What You Will Learn This Week

Week 3 introduces the most important grammar pattern in Spanish: the regular *-ar* verb.

Most common Spanish verbs follow one of three patterns. This week you will learn the most common one — -*ar* — and use it to talk about your everyday life. You will also learn the single easiest negation in any language you have studied: just put *no* before the verb.

By the end of Week 3, you will be able to describe your daily routine in Spanish.

---

## What You Will Be Able to Say by the End of Week 3

By the end of Session 6, you should be able to say something like:

> *Por la mañana, tomo un café y preparo el desayuno. Trabajo de nueve a cinco. Normalmente escucho podcasts en el trabajo. Por la noche, miramos una serie o a veces descanso. No trabajo nunca los domingos.*

And describe someone else:

> *Mi compañero de trabajo se llama Marco. Trabaja en diseño. Habla inglés y un poco de italiano. Estudia fotografía en su tiempo libre. No descansa mucho — trabaja mucho.*

---

## The -ar Verb Pattern

Here it is in full for *hablar* (to speak):

| Subject | Form |
|---|---|
| yo | hablo |
| tú | hablas |
| él / ella / usted | habla |
| nosotros/as | hablamos |
| vosotros/as | habláis |
| ellos / ellas / ustedes | hablan |

**The good news:** Every form sounds different, so it is easier to hear the distinction than in French. The stress pattern helps: the stress falls on the stem (*HABLO, HABLAS, HABLA, haBLAmos, haBLÁis, HABLAN*).

**Drop the subject pronoun when it is clear:** *Trabajo en una oficina* (not *Yo trabajo en una oficina*). Spanish speakers drop the subject pronoun when the context is obvious — the verb ending tells you who is speaking.

---

## Negation Is One Word

To make any -ar verb negative, put *no* directly before it:

- *Trabajo → No trabajo.*
- *Habla inglés → No habla inglés.*
- *Descansamos → No descansamos.*

There is no two-part structure, no *ne...pas*, no exceptions. Just *no* + verb.

**With "never":** *Nunca trabajo los domingos.* Or: *No trabajo nunca los domingos.* Both are correct.

---

## One Thing That Surprises Students This Week

Spanish has three verb families (-ar, -er, -ir) and you are only learning one this week. The curriculum introduces -er and -ir verbs in Week 4. You may wonder why you cannot say "I eat" or "I live" yet — you will learn those verbs very soon, and the pattern will feel familiar.

For now, notice that *ser* and *estar* (which you already know) are *irregular* — they do not follow a predictable pattern. The -ar verbs you are learning this week are *regular* — they all behave the same way. Most new verbs you add from here forward will be regular.

## What to Practice Between Sessions

**After Session 5 — before Session 6:**

1. **Say the -ar endings from memory.** *-o, -as, -a, -amos, -áis, -an.* Say them ten times with *hablar* as your model. Then try *trabajar.*
2. **Write 5 sentences about yourself.** Use 5 different verbs. Include one negative.
3. **Say your sentences out loud.** Close your notes on the third try.

**After Session 6 — before Session 7:**

1. **Write your daily routine.** 5–6 sentences with sequencing words (*primero, luego, después, finalmente*) and time expressions (*por la mañana, a las + hour*).
2. **Add frequency.** At least two sentences with a frequency word: *siempre, normalmente, a veces, nunca.*
3. **Say it out loud.** Aim to deliver the whole routine without notes on your third try.

---

## A Note on Ser and Estar

You will still use *ser* and *estar* every week — they do not disappear when you learn -ar verbs. In fact, Week 3 gives you more chances to practice the ser/estar distinction in natural sentences:

- *Soy profesora. Trabajo en una universidad.* (ser for identity; -ar verb for action)
- *Estoy cansada. No descanso mucho.* (estar for state; -ar verb for habit)

Your instructor will continue to correct ser/estar errors. Each correction reinforces both what you learned in Week 2 and the new pattern you are building in Week 3.
