# Spanish A1 — Week 3 Instructor Notes

**Sessions:** 5 and 6
**Theme:** Regular -ar Verbs and Daily Routine

---

## Week Objective

By the end of Week 3, students can:
- Conjugate 8 regular -ar verbs in all six forms without looking at a table
- Build affirmative and negative sentences using *no*
- Use 4–5 frequency expressions correctly in a sentence
- Narrate their daily routine in 4–6 sentences using sequencing words and time expressions

Full mastery of all six forms is the goal. If the cohort is slow, protect *yo*, *tú*, and *él/ella* production and assign the *nosotros/vosotros/ellos* forms for homework drilling — but introduce all six in Session 5. Students who leave Week 3 without *ellos/ellas* will have gaps when -er and -ir verbs are added in Week 4.

**-er and -ir verbs:** The curriculum assigns -er/-ir verbs and reflexive verbs to Session 6. Given the pacing constraint (realistic 60-minute class), -er/-ir verbs are Week 4 material. Reflexive verbs are previewed as phrases only at the end of Session 6 (*me levanto, me acuesto*). Do not introduce the full -er/-ir paradigm in Week 3.

**Verb contrast with ser/estar:** -ar verbs give students a third conjugation pattern to track. Reinforce that *ser* and *estar* are irregular — the -ar pattern they are learning is what regular verbs look like.

## Session 5 Pacing — Regular -ar Verbs

**Theme:** What is a regular verb? How does the pattern work?

| Block | Content | Time |
|---|---|---|
| Warm-up | Week 2 formative check debrief; one ser/estar sentence from each student | 7 min |
| -ar verb pattern | Ending table; pronunciation note; choral drill | 10 min |
| Core verbs × 3 forms | *yo, tú, él/ella* for 6 core verbs in context sentences | 12 min |
| Sentence building | Students build sentences from a subject + verb prompt | 10 min |
| All six forms | Introduce *nosotros, vosotros, ellos* — complete the table | 5 min |
| Speaking round | Each student: 3 things they do / don't do in Spanish | 11 min |
| Wrap-up | Homework preview; full conjugation table assigned | 5 min |
| **Total** | | **~60 min** |

**What must be protected:** The speaking round (block 6). Every student must say at least one affirmative and one negative *yo*-form sentence about themselves before the session ends.

## Session 6 Pacing — Negation, Frequency, Daily Routine

**Theme:** Completing the pattern; talking about how often and when.

| Block | Content | Time |
|---|---|---|
| Warm-up | -ar verb warm-up: students conjugate *trabajar* and *estudiar* from memory | 6 min |
| Negation: no + verb | Drill and contrast with ser/estar negation; *nunca* introduced | 10 min |
| Frequency expressions | 6 expressions introduced and drilled in sentences | 10 min |
| Daily routine vocabulary | Time-of-day and sequencing words | 7 min |
| Daily routine narration | Students build and say a 5-sentence routine | 13 min |
| Reflexive verbs preview | *Me levanto* and *me acuesto* — phrases only, no grammar explanation | 5 min |
| Wrap-up | Homework preview; Week 3 routine paragraph assigned | 4 min |
| **Total** | | **~55 min** |

**What must be protected:** The daily routine narration (block 5). Every student must say their routine in 4–5 sentences out loud. This is the Week 3 speaking milestone.

## Common Student Mistakes — Week 3

| Mistake | Correction | When to address |
|---|---|---|
| *Yo hablo* in every sentence (subject pronoun overuse) | Spanish drops subject pronouns in fluent speech — *hablo* is enough. *Yo hablo* is not wrong, but sounds over-formal. Introduce this gently; don't drill the pronoun out of them. | Session 5, when you notice it |
| *Él hablas* (wrong ending for third person) | The *él/ella* form ends in *-a*, not *-as.* *Él habla, ella trabaja.* | Session 5, core verb block |
| *No trabajo nunca* vs. *Nunca trabajo* | Both are correct. Explain that when *nunca* comes first, *no* is dropped. | Session 6, negation block |
| Confusing -ar endings with -er/-ir endings | Don't over-emphasize this yet — they haven't learned -er/-ir. Just note that -ar verbs always end in -a for él/ella, not -e. | Session 5 if students ask |
| *Soy cansado / Soy bien* (ser for states — persisting from Week 2) | Correct consistently: *Estoy cansado, estoy bien.* Week 3 gives more opportunities to reinforce this. | Every time it appears |
| Forgetting *no* before the verb | *No* placement is simple: directly before the verb. If a student omits it, it's usually just habit — correct calmly. | Session 6 negation block |

## Suggested Instructor Language

- **For introducing the -ar pattern:** *"The good news about -ar verbs is that they all follow the same pattern. Drop the -ar, add the ending. The six endings are -o, -as, -a, -amos, -áis, -an. Once you know this, you can conjugate hundreds of verbs."*
- **For negation:** *"Negation in Spanish is one word — no — placed directly before the verb. That's it. No sandwich, no extra pronouns. Just: yo no trabajo los domingos."*
- **For nunca:** *"Nunca means 'never.' You have two options: No trabajo nunca — the no stays. Or: Nunca trabajo — no goes away. Both are correct. Use whichever feels natural."*
- **For the routine narration:** *"Tell me what you do on a typical Monday. Not an exciting day — an ordinary one. Use the verbs we've practiced. You can use your notes. Five sentences is the goal."*
- **For ser/estar reinforcement:** *"Notice how -ar verbs are different from ser and estar — those are irregular, which is why we learned them separately. From now on, most of the new verbs we learn will be regular and follow this pattern."*

---

## What to Skip If Time Is Tight

- *Vosotros* forms in depth — introduce in the table, note that it is Spain-only, do not drill it (unless cohort is Spain-focused)
- Detailed frequency adverb distinctions (*muchas veces* vs. *a veces*) — prioritize *siempre, a veces, nunca*
- Reflexive verb preview — skip if Session 6 is already full; introduce at the start of Week 4 instead
- -er and -ir verb preview — these are strictly Week 4 material; mention they exist but do not teach them

---

## What to Assign as Homework If Pacing Runs Long

If Session 5 runs out of time before the speaking round:
→ Students write their 3 sentences for homework; open Session 6 with sharing

If Session 6 runs out of time before the daily routine narration:
→ Students write their routine as homework and record it out loud; be ready to share at Session 7

---

## Week 3 Priority Reminder

The most important outcome of Week 3 is that students can **produce a sentence about what they do using a regular -ar verb without hesitation**. This is the foundation for every new verb added in Weeks 4–12. A student who leaves Week 3 uncertain about *él/ella* forms or the placement of *no* will carry those gaps forward. Correct both consistently.
