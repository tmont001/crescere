# Spanish A1 — Week 3 Formative Check

**Administered:** Start of Session 7 (first session of Week 4)
**Time needed:** 6–8 minutes total
**Purpose:** Quick checkpoint — not a test. Tells the instructor whether -ar verbs and negation are solid enough to introduce *tener*, time-telling, and possessives, or whether a short review is needed first.

---

## Oral Prompts (5 questions — ~1 min per student for the full set)

| # | Prompt | Target response | What to listen for |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Tell me three things you do on a typical day. | 3 sentences with 3 different -ar verbs | Correct endings (*hablo, hablas, habla*); not frozen in one verb; no subject pronoun required |
| 2 | Tell me something you never do. | *No [verb] nunca...* or *Nunca [verb]...* | Either form accepted; verb ending correct |
| 3 | How often do you [common activity]? | Sentence with *siempre, normalmente, a veces, casi nunca,* etc. | Frequency word present; positioned reasonably |
| 4 | Tell me what your [partner/colleague/family member] does for work or study. | *Trabaja en / estudia...* | Correct *él/ella* ending (-a); not *trabaje* or *trabajan* |
| 5 | Conjugate *estudiar* — give me all six forms. | estudio, estudias, estudia, estudiamos, estudiáis, estudian | All six; *yo* ends in -o; *él/ella* ends in -a |

---

## Written Prompts (5 questions — students write answers, ~3 min)

Paste into the session chat or distribute at the start of Session 7.

**Instructions to students:** Answer in Spanish. Short answers only.

1. Write: "She doesn't work on Saturdays."
2. Write: "We always listen to music at work."
3. Write: "They (masc.) study Spanish." (*estudiar*, plural)
4. What is the *él/ella* form of *preparar*?
5. Write: "I never rest on Mondays."

**Answer key:**
1. *Ella no trabaja los sábados.*
2. *Siempre escuchamos música en el trabajo.* / *Escuchamos siempre música en el trabajo.*
3. *Estudian español.*
4. *prepara*
5. *No descanso nunca los lunes.* / *Nunca descanso los lunes.*

---

## Instructor Scoring Notes

Not graded. Use it to make one of three decisions:

| Result | What it means | What to do |
|---|---|---|
| Students handle all 5 oral prompts | -ar verbs are solid | Move into Week 4 content immediately |
| Students stumble on 1–2 oral prompts | Minor gaps; expected | 5-minute -ar verb warm-up at the start of Sessions 7 and 8 |
| Students stumble on 3+ oral prompts | Verb pattern is not yet secure | Add a 10-minute -ar verb review to Sessions 7 and 8 before introducing *tener* |

**Most common Week 3 gaps:**
- *Él trabaja**s*** → *él/ella* form ends in *-a*, never *-as.* Correct every occurrence through Week 4.
- Missing *no* before the verb → simple habit; just point to the verb and say "no." 
- *Yo trabajo* in every sentence instead of dropping the pronoun → not wrong, but gently model the dropped form in your responses
- Ser/estar errors persisting into -ar verb sentences (*soy cansado, estoy de Argentina*) → continue correcting; Week 4 creates more opportunities to reinforce

---

## Ready to Move On

A student is ready for Week 4 content when they can:

- [ ] Conjugate any regular -ar verb from memory in all six forms
- [ ] Build a negative sentence with *no* without prompting
- [ ] Use at least two frequency expressions correctly in a sentence
- [ ] Narrate 4–5 things they do in a day without notes

**Note on reflexive verbs and -er/-ir verbs:** Do not gate Week 4 entry on reflexive verb production or -er/-ir knowledge. Students may have heard *me levanto* and *me acuesto* as phrases but the full grammar system is Week 4 material. -er/-ir verbs are also Week 4.
