# Spanish A1 — Session 6 Slide Outline
## Negation, Frequency, and Daily Routine

**Session length:** 60 minutes
**Session-end goal:** Students can narrate their daily routine in 5+ sentences using affirmative and negative -ar verb sentences, at least two frequency expressions, and sequencing words. Students hear reflexive verb phrases (*me levanto, me acuesto*) for the first time — not as a grammar topic.

> **Pacing note:** The daily routine narration (Slide 6) is non-negotiable. Every student must narrate their routine out loud. Trim Slide 5 (sequencing words) to 3 minutes before cutting the narration.

---

## Core 60-Minute Plan

### Slide 1 — Warm-Up: -ar Verb Drill (6 min)
**Purpose:** Verify that the -ar pattern is solid before adding negation and frequency.
**Teacher notes:**
- Ask students to conjugate *trabajar* from memory — full six forms together.
- Then call on 3 students: "Give me one sentence using *estudiar*, *escuchar*, and *preparar* in the *él/ella* form."
- Listen for: *él trabaja* (correct) vs. *él trabajes* or *él trabajas* (wrong ending). Note any consistent errors to address in Slide 2.
**Student activity:** Group conjugation of *trabajar* → 3 individual sentences in *él/ella* form.

---

### Slide 2 — Negation: Full Practice (10 min)
**Purpose:** Students can negate any -ar verb sentence reliably.
**Teacher notes:**
- Review: *no* goes directly before the conjugated verb. Demonstrate with 3 examples:
  - *Trabajo los sábados → No trabajo los sábados.*
  - *Ella habla alemán → Ella no habla alemán.*
  - *Estudiamos inglés → No estudiamos inglés.*
- Introduce *nunca:*
  - *No descanso nunca los lunes.* (no stays when nunca comes after)
  - *Nunca descanso los lunes.* (no drops when nunca comes first)
  - "Both mean exactly the same thing. Use whichever sounds natural to you."
- Oral transformation drill: 8 affirmative sentences → students give the negative. Mix in 2 sentences with *nunca.*
- Address the most common Session 5 error you noticed: if students were struggling with *él/ella* endings, reinforce here.
**Student activity:** Oral transformation drill — 8 affirmative → negative, 2 with *nunca.*

---

### Slide 3 — Frequency Expressions (10 min)
**Purpose:** Students can place a frequency adverb correctly in a sentence and understand what it means.
**Teacher notes:**
- Introduce on a scale from 0% to 100%:
  - *siempre* (always) → *Siempre tomo café por la mañana.*
  - *normalmente / generalmente* (usually) → *Normalmente trabajo de nueve a cinco.*
  - *a veces* (sometimes) → *A veces estudio por la noche.*
  - *de vez en cuando* (from time to time) → *De vez en cuando compro en el mercado.*
  - *casi nunca* (almost never) → *Casi nunca miro la tele.*
  - *nunca* (never) → *Nunca trabajo los domingos.*
- Position: frequency adverbs typically come before or after the verb. Spanish is flexible here — demonstrate both:
  - *Normalmente escucho música.* / *Escucho música normalmente.*
- Quick drill: instructor names a percentage, students give the Spanish adverb.
- Students add one frequency word to a sentence from their Session 5 homework (oral).
**Student activity:** Frequency adverb rapid-fire → add one adverb to a Session 5 sentence.

---

### Slide 4 — Daily Routine Vocabulary (7 min)
**Purpose:** Students have the time and sequencing expressions needed to narrate a routine.
**Teacher notes:**
- Time-of-day expressions: *por la mañana, por la tarde, por la noche.* Use in a sentence each.
- Specific time: *a las [number]* — *Empiezo a trabajar a las nueve. Termino a las cinco.*
- Sequencing words: *primero, luego / después, más tarde, finalmente / por último.*
- Model a 4-sentence routine: *Primero preparo el desayuno. Luego trabajo de nueve a seis. Después escucho música en el autobús. Finalmente descanso en casa.*
- Students do not need to memorize all of these — just use two in their narration.
**Student activity:** Repeat the model routine; call out the sequencing words.

---

### Slide 5 — Building the Daily Routine (3 min)
**Purpose:** Students plan before speaking.
**Teacher notes:**
- Give students 2–3 minutes to write or note a 5-sentence routine.
- Model structure on screen:
  1. *Por la mañana, [verb]...*
  2. *Primero / Luego / Después, [verb]...*
  3. *A las [hour], [verb]...*
  4. *Normalmente / A veces / Siempre, [verb]...*
  5. *No [verb] nunca / Nunca [verb]...*
**Student activity:** Students write or note their routine in silence (2–3 min).

---

### Slide 6 — Daily Routine Narration Round (13 min)
**Purpose:** Every student narrates their routine out loud.
**Teacher notes:**
- Every student takes a turn. Notes allowed. 5 sentences minimum.
- Correct one item per student: prioritize -ar verb endings (*él hablas* → *habla*), missing *no*, word order. Save group patterns for a debrief after.
- After everyone shares: pick 2–3 patterns and briefly address with the whole group.
- Close with: "You can describe your day in Spanish. That is a real and useful skill."
**Student activity:** Each student narrates their daily routine out loud (5 sentences minimum).

---

### Slide 7 — Reflexive Verbs: First Look (5 min)
**Purpose:** Students hear *me levanto* and *me acuesto* as phrases to start using — not as a grammar topic.
**Teacher notes:**
- Say: "Two more verbs that belong in your daily routine — and that you will hear everywhere in Spanish. We will teach the full grammar in Week 4. For now, I want you to have these as phrases."
- Write: *Me levanto a las siete. Me acuesto hacia las once.*
- Point to the reflexive pronoun: "There is an extra word — *me.* That is a reflexive pronoun. Treat these as two phrases to memorize. Week 4 will explain everything."
- Each student says one sentence with *me levanto* and one with *me acuesto.*
**Student activity:** Each student says: *Me levanto a las...* and *Me acuesto hacia las...*

---

### Slide 8 — Wrap-Up (4 min)
**Purpose:** Homework preview; Week 3 writing task assigned.
**Teacher notes:**
- Assign the Week 3 routine paragraph: 5–6 sentences, affirmative and negative, two frequency expressions, sequencing words.
- Close: "You have -ar verbs. You have negation. You have frequency. You can describe what you do and don't do, how often, and in what order. That is your daily life in Spanish."
**Student activity:** Note homework. Optional: one "surprise" sentence about something unusual they actually do.

---

## Cut First If Running Behind

1. **Slide 7 (reflexive preview):** Cut entirely — "We'll see these in Week 4." No loss to Week 3.
2. **Slide 5 (planning time):** Skip — students speak from improvisation in Slide 6.
3. **Slide 4 (sequencing vocabulary):** Cover *primero, luego, finalmente* only — 90 seconds.
4. **Slide 3 (frequency):** Cover *siempre, a veces, nunca* only. Assign the others as reference reading.
5. Do not cut Slide 6 (narration round).

---

## Extension for Fast Cohorts

- **-er and -ir verb preview:** Show *comer* and *vivir* — different endings. "We'll work on these next week. For now, notice that *como* ends in -o just like -ar verbs, but the other endings change." Do not drill.
- **Add clock times:** Students give specific clock times for every action in their routine. Reinforce *a las + hour.*
- **Describe someone else's routine:** Students narrate a family member or friend's typical day using *él/ella* forms. Include a frequency adverb and a negative.
