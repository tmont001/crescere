# Spanish A1 — Session 5 Slide Outline
## Regular -ar Verbs

**Session length:** 60 minutes
**Session-end goal:** Students can conjugate 6 regular -ar verbs in *yo*, *tú*, and *él/ella/usted* forms without looking at a table, build a complete sentence using those forms, and transform an affirmative sentence to negative using *no*.

> **Pacing note:** The speaking round (Slide 6) is non-negotiable. Every student must say at least one affirmative and one negative sentence about themselves before the session ends. Trim Slide 4 (sentence building) before cutting it.

---

## Core 60-Minute Plan

### Slide 1 — Warm-Up: Week 2 Review (7 min)
**Purpose:** Reconnect with Week 2 before introducing new grammar. Surface any ser/estar gaps before -ar verbs add a third pattern.
**Teacher notes:**
- Ask each student to say one sentence using *ser* or *estar.* Mix the prompt types:
  - "Tell me where you are right now." (*estar*)
  - "Tell me your profession." (*ser*)
  - "Tell me how you feel today." (*estar*)
- Listen for: *soy cansado* (ser/estar swap); *estoy de México* (ser for origin). Correct with one word: "estar" or "ser" — do not launch into an explanation. These corrections will continue all week.
**Student activity:** Each student says one ser/estar sentence.

---

### Slide 2 — The -ar Verb Pattern (10 min)
**Purpose:** Students understand the regular -ar ending pattern and can say all six endings.
**Teacher notes:**
- Write on screen: the six endings only — *-o, -as, -a, -amos, -áis, -an.* Then apply them to *hablar.*
- Key contrast with French: "Unlike French, every Spanish form sounds different. You can hear which person is speaking from the verb alone. This is why Spanish speakers often drop the subject pronoun — the verb does the job."
- Stress pattern: *HÁBlo, HÁBlas, HÁBla, haBLÁmos, haBLÁis, HÁBlan.* Stress shifts to the ending in *nosotros* and *vosotros.*
- *Vosotros* note: used in Spain; Latin American speakers use *ustedes* for both formal and informal plural. Flag this for your cohort if relevant.
- Choral drill × 3: slow → normal pace → fast.
- Spot check: instructor points to a subject pronoun, students give the correct form.
**Student activity:** Choral drill × 3 → spot-check of one form per student.

---

### Slide 3 — Core Verbs in Context (12 min)
**Purpose:** Students hear and produce 6 core verbs in real sentences.
**Teacher notes:**
- Introduce each verb with a sentence, not just a translation:
  - *hablar: Hablo inglés, pero quiero hablar español.*
  - *trabajar: ¿Trabajas desde casa o en la oficina?*
  - *estudiar: Ella estudia medicina — trabaja y estudia al mismo tiempo.*
  - *escuchar: Escucho podcasts cuando camino.*
  - *mirar: Miramos una serie todas las noches.*
  - *preparar: Preparo el desayuno antes de trabajar.*
- After each verb: choral repetition of the sentence. Then swap the subject: *¿Tú trabajas desde casa?* → *Sí, trabajo desde casa.* (note: drop *yo* in the response)
- Focus on *yo, tú, él/ella* — note the *nosotros* form for *mirar* but do not drill it separately yet.
**Student activity:** Choral repetition → swap subject pronouns in pairs.

---

### Slide 4 — Sentence Building (10 min)
**Purpose:** Students construct their own sentences using a verb + personal context.
**Teacher notes:**
- Give each student a verb and a partial prompt:
  - Student A: *trabajar* → *Trabajo en [place].*
  - Student B: *estudiar* → *Estudio [subject].*
  - Student C: *escuchar* → *Escucho [genre/type] de música.*
- After each sentence: ask the group to transform it to *él/ella:* "She listens to classical music — how do we say that?" → *Escucha música clásica.*
- Note to students who drop the subject pronoun correctly: "Good — that is natural Spanish. The verb ending tells us who you are talking about."
**Student activity:** Each student builds one sentence → group transforms it to third person.

---

### Slide 5 — All Six Forms + Negation Preview (10 min)
**Purpose:** Students complete the conjugation table and learn to negate.
**Teacher notes:**
- Complete the table: add *nosotros, vosotros, ellos/ustedes.* Drill *nosotros* and *ellos* specifically — these are the forms most likely to appear in description and narration.
- Introduce negation: put *no* directly before the verb. That is the whole rule.
  - *Trabajo → No trabajo.*
  - *Ella habla chino → Ella no habla chino.*
- Write side by side: *Je ne parle pas* (French) vs. *No hablo* (Spanish). "Spanish is simpler here — just one word."
- Quick transformation: 5 affirmative sentences → students give the negative.
**Student activity:** Complete the table on screen → 5-item negation transformation.

---

### Slide 6 — Speaking Round (11 min)
**Purpose:** Every student uses the new pattern to say something true about themselves.
**Teacher notes:**
- Each student says two sentences: one affirmative, one negative.
  - *Trabajo en un hospital. No estudio medicina — trabajo en administración.*
  - *Escucho podcasts de historia. No miro mucho la tele.*
- Notes allowed. Memorization not required.
- Correct: wrong ending for *yo* or *él/ella*; missing *no*; ser/estar swap in the same sentence. One correction per student.
- If a student says *yo trabajo*, do not correct the pronoun — it is not wrong. Just model the dropped form in your feedback.
**Student activity:** Each student says one affirmative + one negative sentence.

---

### Slide 7 — Wrap-Up (5 min)
**Purpose:** Homework preview; close with the pattern's significance.
**Teacher notes:**
- Say: "Homework tonight has two parts: say the endings from memory with two different verbs, then write five sentences about yourself using five different verbs. Include one negative."
- Preview Session 6: "Next session we add frequency words and build your first full daily routine in Spanish."
**Student activity:** Note homework. Optional: one more sentence before logging off.

---

## Cut First If Running Behind

1. **Slide 4 (sentence building):** Skip the group transformation step. Students say their sentence only.
2. **Slide 5 (all six forms + negation):** Cover *nosotros* and *ellos* but skip the written table practice; assign as homework. Introduce negation with 2 examples only.
3. **Slide 3 (core verbs):** Cover 4 verbs instead of 6. Add the remaining 2 at the start of Session 6.
4. Do not cut Slide 6 (speaking round).

---

## Extension for Fast Cohorts

- **All six forms drill:** Students conjugate all 10 verbs from the vocabulary list individually — one verb per student.
- **Subject pronoun drop:** Give students a paragraph of Spanish with all subject pronouns included. They identify which ones can be dropped and why.
- **-er/-ir preview:** Show *comer* and *vivir* — the yo form is *como, vivo* — endings are different. "These are Week 4. For now, just notice they exist."

---

## Move to Homework If Needed

- *Nosotros, vosotros, ellos* forms
- Written negation practice (5+ sentences)
- Additional verbs beyond the 6 in Slide 3 (*necesitar, tomar, comprar, descansar*)
- Any contrast between -ar endings and -er/-ir endings
