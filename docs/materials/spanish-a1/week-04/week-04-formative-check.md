# Spanish A1 — Week 4 Formative Check

**Administered:** Start of Session 9 (first session of Week 5)
**Time needed:** 6–8 minutes total
**Purpose:** Quick checkpoint — not a test. Tells the instructor whether -er/-ir verb recognition and reflexive verb production are solid enough to introduce *tener*, family vocabulary, and telling time in Week 5.

---

## Oral Prompts (5 questions — ~1 min per student for the full set)

| # | Prompt | Target response | What to listen for |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | ¿A qué hora te levantas? | *Me levanto a las [hour].* | Reflexive pronoun *me* present; correct verb form (*levanto*, not *levanta*) |
| 2 | Describe your morning routine in 3 sentences. | 3 sentences mixing reflexive + regular verbs | At least 2 reflexive verbs with correct pronoun; at least 1 -ar/-er/-ir verb |
| 3 | What does your partner/colleague do? (profession or daily habit) | *Trabaja en... / Come en...* | Correct third-person ending: *-a* for -ar, *-e* for -er/-ir |
| 4 | What time does your partner get up? | *Se levanta a las...* | *se* — not *me*; correct third-person reflexive |
| 5 | Give me the *yo* form of *comer* and *vivir*. | *como* and *vivo* | Both end in *-o*; no extra letters |

---

## Written Prompts (5 questions — students write answers, ~3 min)

Paste into the session chat or distribute at the start of Session 9.

**Instructions to students:** Answer in Spanish. Short answers only.

1. Write: "I get up at 7:30."
2. Write: "She doesn't shower in the morning." (*ducharse*)
3. What is the *él/ella* form of *leer*?
4. Write: "They (masc.) go to bed around 11." (*acostarse*)
5. Write: "We eat at home every day." (*comer*)

**Answer key:**
1. *Me levanto a las siete y media.*
2. *Ella no se ducha por la mañana.*
3. *lee*
4. *Se acuestan hacia las once.*
5. *Comemos en casa todos los días.*

---

## Instructor Scoring Notes

Not graded. Use it to make one of three decisions:

| Result | What it means | What to do |
|---|---|---|
| Students handle all 5 oral prompts | Week 4 content is solid | Move into Week 5 (*tener*, family, telling time) immediately |
| Students stumble on 1–2 oral prompts | Minor gaps; expected | 5-minute reflexive or -er/-ir warm-up at start of Sessions 9 and 10 |
| Students stumble on 3+ oral prompts | Week 4 patterns are not yet secure | Add 10-minute review to Sessions 9–10; carry reflexive verbs as a warm-up habit throughout Week 5 |

**Most common Week 4 gaps:**
- *Me levanta* (wrong pronoun — using *se* form for *yo*) → "The pronoun must match who is doing the action: *yo → me.*"
- *Él levanta* (missing reflexive pronoun entirely) → "Where's the *se*? Say it again: *él se levanta.*"
- *Yo comeo* or *yo viveo* (-er/-ir yo form with extra letters) → "*Yo* always ends in *-o* only. *Como, vivo.* No extra letters."
- *Son la una* (using plural for 1:00) → "*Es* la una — singular. All others: *son las.*"
- Ser/estar errors persisting (*soy cansado, estoy de Colombia*) → continue correcting; Week 5 creates more opportunities

---

## Ready to Move On

A student is ready for Week 5 content when they can:

- [ ] Produce *me levanto / me ducho / me preparo / me acuesto* in first person without prompting
- [ ] Give the correct *él/ella se...* form of at least 2 reflexive verbs
- [ ] Conjugate *comer* and *vivir* in *yo* and *él/ella* forms without looking at a table
- [ ] Narrate 5 sentences covering their morning and evening routine without notes
- [ ] Read and say times at the hour level (*son las siete, es la una*)

**Not a Week 5 gate:**
- Full six-form mastery of every reflexive verb
- Stem-change paradigm for *despertarse* and *acostarse*
- Vosotros forms for any verb family
- Negation with reflexive verbs (introduced as extension only)
