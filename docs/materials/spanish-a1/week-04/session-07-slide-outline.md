# Spanish A1 — Session 7 Slide Outline
## Regular -er and -ir Verbs

**Session length:** 60 minutes
**Session-end goal:** Students can conjugate 4 core -er verbs and 3 core -ir verbs in *yo*, *tú*, and *él/ella* forms, identify the pattern differences from -ar verbs, and build a sentence using each verb family.

> **Pacing note:** The speaking round (Slide 6) is non-negotiable. Every student must say one -er verb sentence and one -ir verb sentence before the session ends. Do not let the comparison table expand to fill the session.

---

## Core 60-Minute Plan

### Slide 1 — Warm-Up: Week 3 Recall and Formative Check Debrief (7 min)
**Purpose:** Activate -ar verbs and close any Week 3 gaps before adding -er/-ir verbs.
**Teacher notes:**
- Ask each student: say 2 sentences using -ar verbs — one affirmative, one negative. Any verbs from Week 3.
- If the Week 3 formative check showed that *él/ella* ending errors were common (*él trabaja**s***), do a 90-second targeted drill here: "What ending does *él* take for -ar verbs? -a. *Él trabaja. Él estudia. Él habla.*" Then move on.
- If the formative check was clean, skip to Slide 2 immediately.
**Student activity:** Each student says 2 -ar verb sentences from Week 3.

---

### Slide 2 — Introducing -er Verbs (12 min)
**Purpose:** Students understand how -er verb endings differ from -ar, and can produce 4 core -er verbs in context.
**Teacher notes:**
- Write both patterns side by side on screen and highlight the differences:
  - *tú: -as vs. -es*
  - *él/ella: -a vs. -e*
  - *nosotros: -amos vs. -emos*
  - *ellos: -an vs. -en*
  - (*yo* is the same: *-o*)
- Say: "Four out of six forms change. But the logic is the same — drop the infinitive ending, add the personal ending."
- Introduce 4 verbs with context sentences:
  - *comer: Como en casa todos los días. ¿Comes en la oficina?*
  - *beber: Bebo café por la mañana. Mi madre bebe té.*
  - *leer: Leo el periódico antes de trabajar. ¿Lees libros?*
  - *comprender: No comprendo esta instrucción. ¿Comprendes?*
- After each: choral repetition → swap the subject (*yo → él/ella*).
- Note *vender* as a useful fifth verb; students can find it in the vocabulary reference.
**Student activity:** Choral repetition of each verb sentence → swap the subject.

---

### Slide 3 — Introducing -ir Verbs (8 min)
**Purpose:** Students see that -ir verbs are nearly identical to -er verbs with two minor differences.
**Teacher notes:**
- Write -er and -ir patterns side by side: highlight *nosotros* (-emos vs. -imos) and *vosotros* (-éis vs. -ís) as the only differences.
- Say: "If you know -er verbs, you almost know -ir verbs. The only change is in *nosotros* and *vosotros* — and those forms are not the most common in everyday speech."
- Introduce 3 verbs quickly:
  - *vivir: Vivo en [city]. ¿Dónde vives?*
  - *escribir: Escribo muchos correos en el trabajo.*
  - *recibir: Recibo muchos mensajes de WhatsApp.*
- After each: repeat → swap subject.
**Student activity:** Choral repetition of each verb → swap subject.

---

### Slide 4 — Three-Family Comparison Drill (7 min)
**Purpose:** Students can distinguish -ar, -er, and -ir forms on the spot.
**Teacher notes:**
- Rapid-fire: instructor says a subject pronoun + infinitive, students give the correct conjugated form.
  - *yo + hablar* → *hablo*
  - *él + comer* → *come*
  - *tú + vivir* → *vives*
  - *ella + leer* → *lee*
  - *yo + beber* → *bebo*
  - *él + trabajar* → *trabaja*
  - *tú + escribir* → *escribes*
  - *ella + estudiar* → *estudia*
- Do 8–10 items. Vary the family each time so students cannot get into a rhythm with one pattern.
- If an error occurs: pause, give the correct answer, and note the principle: *"él with -er verbs always ends in -e, not -a."*
**Student activity:** Rapid-fire conjugation drill — 8–10 items.

---

### Slide 5 — Sentence Building (10 min)
**Purpose:** Students use all three verb families in sentences about themselves.
**Teacher notes:**
- Each student gets 3 prompts — one per verb family:
  - -ar: "Tell me something you buy or need."
  - -er: "Tell me when and what you eat."
  - -ir: "Tell me where you live or what you write."
- Students take 2 minutes to prepare, then share with the group.
- After each student shares: instructor or another student transforms one sentence to *él/ella:* "She lives in... She eats... She buys..."
**Student activity:** Build 3 sentences (one per family) → share → group transforms to third person.

---

### Slide 6 — Speaking Round (10 min)
**Purpose:** Every student produces at least one -er and one -ir verb sentence in a natural context.
**Teacher notes:**
- Each student says 3 sentences about their life — one per verb family (without being told which family each belongs to). The goal is natural use, not performance of a grammar rule.
  - *Vivo en Boston. Como en casa por la noche. Trabajo en diseño.*
- Notes allowed. Do not require the student to label which family each verb is.
- Correct: wrong ending (*él comes* → *come*); missing verb (*yo estudiar* → *estudio*); wrong family ending (*vivo**s*** → *vivo*).
**Student activity:** Each student says 3 sentences mixing verb families.

---

### Slide 7 — Wrap-Up (6 min)
**Purpose:** Homework preview; close with the completed verb picture.
**Teacher notes:**
- Say: "You now have all three regular verb families — -ar, -er, -ir. Together they cover the majority of Spanish verbs. From here, every new verb we add will fit into one of these three patterns, or it will be an irregular verb we name explicitly."
- Preview Session 8: "Next session we add reflexive verbs for your daily routine — getting up, showering, going to bed. And we'll start telling time."
**Student activity:** Note homework. Optional: one new word they want to learn — find out which verb family it belongs to.

---

## Cut First If Running Behind

1. **Slide 4 (comparison drill):** Reduce to 5 items, cut to 4 minutes.
2. **Slide 3 (-ir verbs):** Cover *vivir* only. Note that -ir endings are "like -er but with *-imos* for nosotros." Assign the others as vocabulary reference.
3. **Slide 5 (sentence building):** Students say one sentence each (any verb family). Skip the group transformation.
4. Do not cut Slide 6 (speaking round).

---

## Extension for Fast Cohorts

- **Negation with -er/-ir verbs:** *No como carne. No vivo en el centro.* Quick — it's the same rule as -ar verbs.
- **All six forms drill:** Students conjugate *comer* and *vivir* in all six forms without looking.
- **Five more verbs:** From the vocabulary reference — *vender, abrir, comprender* for -er/-ir students can start using immediately.

---

## Move to Homework If Needed

- Full six-form conjugation of -er/-ir verbs (*nosotros/vosotros/ellos*)
- Negation with -er/-ir verbs
- Additional -er/-ir verbs beyond the core 7
- Any time expressions beyond those used in sentence building
