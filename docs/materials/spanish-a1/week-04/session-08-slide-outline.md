# Spanish A1 — Session 8 Slide Outline
## Reflexive Verbs and Full Routine Narration

**Session length:** 60 minutes
**Session-end goal:** Every student narrates their complete daily routine — morning through evening — in 5+ sentences using reflexive verbs, regular -ar verbs, and at least one -er or -ir verb introduced in Session 7. Students can describe another person's routine using *se.*

> **Pacing note:** The routine narration round (Slide 6) is non-negotiable. Every student must narrate their day out loud. Trim Slide 4 (describing another person) before cutting the narration round.

---

## Core 60-Minute Plan

### Slide 1 — Warm-Up: -er and -ir Verb Recall (6 min)
**Purpose:** Verify that Session 7 verb patterns are solid before introducing reflexive verbs as a third layer.
**Teacher notes:**
- Ask each student: give me one -er verb sentence and one -ir verb sentence from their homework. No notes if possible.
- If any student cannot produce *ella come / él vive* without the wrong ending, give one focused correction and move on. Do not re-teach the full pattern here — that would compress the session.
- If Session 7 homework sharing was assigned as a fallback, collect it here (2–3 students share, then move).
**Student activity:** Each student says one -er and one -ir verb sentence from homework.

---

### Slide 2 — What Are Reflexive Verbs? (8 min)
**Purpose:** Students understand the concept, recognize the pronouns, and connect it to a verb they already know.
**Teacher notes:**
- Open with: "You already know one reflexive verb. You've been using it since Week 1: *me llamo.* It has always been reflexive — you were just using it as a phrase. Now you know why that *me* is there."
- Write the reflexive pronoun table on screen:
  - *yo → me, tú → te, él/ella/usted → se, nosotros → nos, vosotros → os, ellos/ellas/ustedes → se*
- Key principle: "The pronoun goes directly before the conjugated verb. It must match the subject. *Yo → me. Él → se. The verb itself conjugates like any regular verb.*"
- Say *me llamo* in all persons to show the pattern in action: *me llamo, te llamas, se llama, nos llamamos, os llamáis, se llaman.*
- Point out: "You see? The verb *llamar* conjugates normally — -ar endings. The only extra piece is the pronoun in front."
**Student activity:** Students repeat each form of *llamarse* after you — twice through.

---

### Slide 3 — Core Reflexive Verbs (10 min)
**Purpose:** Students hear 5 core daily routine reflexive verbs in context and can produce the *me* form.
**Teacher notes:**
- Introduce each verb with a context sentence and a brief human anchor:
  - *levantarse: Me levanto a las siete — no me gusta levantarme antes de las siete.*
  - *ducharse: Me ducho por la mañana. ¿Te duchas por la mañana o por la noche?*
  - *prepararse: Me preparo en veinte minutos. Mi hermana tarda una hora en prepararse.*
  - *despertarse: Me despierto a las seis y media, pero me levanto a las siete.* ⚠️ Stem change: "This one changes its vowel — *me despierto*, not *me despierto.* For now, just remember this form."
  - *acostarse: Me acuesto hacia las once.* ⚠️ Stem change: "Same thing here — the o changes to ue: *me acuesto.* The *nosotros* form is regular: *nos acostamos.*"
- After each verb: students repeat the *me* form → then give the *él/ella* form as a group.
- Do not drill all six forms for each verb. *Me* and *se* forms are the priority.
**Student activity:** Repeat *me* form → give *él/ella se...* form as a group.

---

### Slide 4 — Basic Clock Times (7 min)
**Purpose:** Students can anchor their routine to specific hours.
**Teacher notes:**
- Teach only what is needed for the narration:
  - *Son las siete.* — It is seven o'clock. (plural — use for all except one)
  - *Es la una.* — It is one o'clock. (singular only)
  - *Son las ocho y media.* — Half past eight.
  - *A las siete.* / *Hacia las once.* — At seven / Around eleven.
- Quick check: instructor says a time in Spanish, students write the number. 5 items. Fast.
  - *Son las nueve y media → 9:30*
  - *Es la una → 1:00*
  - *Son las seis → 6:00*
  - *A las once → 11:00*
  - *Son las dos y media → 2:30*
- Note: "When it's one o'clock, we say *es la una* — singular. All other hours use *son las.*"
**Student activity:** 5-item time comprehension check — write the time you hear.

---

### Slide 5 — Building the Full Routine (7 min)
**Purpose:** Students plan a 5–6 sentence routine mixing reflexive verbs, -ar verbs, and -er/-ir verbs before speaking.
**Teacher notes:**
- Give students 4 minutes to plan their narration. Model the target structure on screen without giving them the sentences:
  1. *Me despierto a las...* / *Me levanto a las...*
  2. *Me ducho y me preparo...*
  3. *[Regular -ar/-er/-ir verb in daily context]*
  4. *[What I do at work or during the day]*
  5. *Por la noche, [verb]...*
  6. *Me acuesto hacia las...*
- Encourage them to use sequencing words (*primero, luego, después, finalmente*) and at least one frequency expression or negative from Weeks 3–4.
- Walk the virtual room — look for missing reflexive pronouns and wrong verb forms to address after the narration.
**Student activity:** Students plan 5–6 sentence routine (4 min silence).

---

### Slide 6 — Routine Narration Round (12 min)
**Purpose:** Every student narrates their complete daily routine out loud.
**Teacher notes:**
- Every student takes a turn. Notes allowed. 5 sentences minimum.
- Correct one item per student: missing reflexive pronoun (*me levanto* → if they say *levanto*), verb ending error, wrong time expression. One item only.
- After all students share: pick 2 patterns you heard and address them briefly with the whole group.
- Close with: "You just described your day in Spanish — with verbs from three different families. That is Week 4 done."
**Student activity:** Each student narrates their daily routine (5+ sentences, morning through evening).

---

### Slide 7 — Describing Another Person's Routine (5 min)
**Purpose:** Students use *se* to talk about someone else — extending the reflexive pattern to third person.
**Teacher notes:**
- Quick round — ask 3–4 students: "Tell me one thing about your partner's or colleague's morning, using *él/ella se.*"
  - *Mi marido se despierta a las seis. Se ducha rápido.*
  - *Mi compañera se levanta tarde — hacia las ocho.*
- Correct: *él me levanta* (wrong pronoun) → *él se levanta.*
- If time is tight, reduce to 2 students only.
**Student activity:** 3–4 students give one *él/ella se...* sentence about someone else's routine.

---

### Slide 8 — Wrap-Up (5 min)
**Purpose:** Week 4 homework paragraph assigned; preview Week 5.
**Teacher notes:**
- Assign: complete written routine paragraph (5–7 sentences), reflexive + -ar/-er verbs, times, sequencing words.
- Preview Week 5: "Next week we add *tener* — the verb for possession — and talk about family. We'll also start telling time more precisely."
**Student activity:** Note homework. Optional: one reflexive sentence about their evening they did not say in the narration.

---

## Cut First If Running Behind

1. **Slide 7 (describing another person):** Cut entirely — assign as homework extension.
2. **Slide 4 (clock times):** Reduce to 3 minutes — teach *son las X* and *a las X* only; skip *y media*.
3. **Slide 5 (planning time):** Reduce to 2 minutes — students speak from improvisation.
4. **Slide 3 (core verbs):** Cover 4 verbs instead of 5 — skip *despertarse* and tell students to use *me levanto* as the morning start.
5. Do not cut Slide 6 (narration round).

---

## Extension for Fast Cohorts

- **Negation with reflexive verbs:** *No me levanto antes de las siete. No me ducho por la noche.* The *no* goes before the reflexive pronoun: *no + me/te/se + verb.*
- **Nos/os/se forms drill:** Students conjugate *levantarse* in all six forms without looking. Note the double *nos*: *nos levantamos.*
- **Stem-change preview:** Show *acostarse* (o→ue) and *despertarse* (e→ie) in all six forms. Point out the boot pattern — *nosotros* and *vosotros* do not change.
