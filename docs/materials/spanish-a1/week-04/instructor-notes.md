# Spanish A1 — Week 4 Instructor Notes

**Sessions:** 7 and 8
**Theme:** Regular -er/-ir Verbs, Reflexive Verbs, and Daily Routine

---

## Week Objective

By the end of Week 4, students can:
- Conjugate regular -er and -ir verbs in *yo, tú, él/ella* forms and recognize the full pattern
- Identify the difference between -ar, -er, and -ir verb endings
- Use reflexive verbs in *me, te, se* forms for daily routine description
- Sequence a morning and evening routine in 5–6 sentences mixing reflexive and -ar/-er/-ir verbs
- Read and say basic clock times (*son las + heure, a las + hour*)

Full mastery of all six reflexive forms and all three verb families is not a Week 4 requirement. *Me, te, se* are the priority for reflexive verbs. The *yo, tú, él/ella* forms are the priority for -er/-ir verbs.

**Stem-changing verbs:** *despertarse* (e→ie) and *acostarse* (o→ue) are stem-changing reflexive verbs that appear naturally in daily routine vocabulary. Introduce their *me* forms as phrases to use; do not teach the stem-change paradigm fully. Flag that the *nosotros* form does not change (*nos despertamos, nos acostamos*). Stem-changing verbs are introduced as they arise throughout the course — students encounter the pattern in *querer* (Week 6) and it is addressed more fully with *preferir* (e→ie) during the hobbies and preferences work in Week 10.

**Relationship to the curriculum:** The curriculum places time/dates and *tener* + family vocabulary in Week 4. Given the Week 3 deferral of -er/-ir verbs and reflexive verbs, Week 4 covers those deferred topics. Basic time-reading is included as a support skill for routine narration. Full telling-time, *tener*, and family vocabulary are deferred to Week 5.

## Session 7 Pacing — Regular -er and -ir Verbs

**Theme:** Extending the verb pattern from -ar to -er and -ir.

| Block | Content | Time |
|---|---|---|
| Warm-up | Week 3 formative check debrief; -ar verb quick recall (2 sentences each) | 7 min |
| -er verb pattern | Comparison table: -ar vs. -er; 5 core -er verbs in context | 12 min |
| -ir verb pattern | Comparison table: -er vs. -ir; 4 core -ir verbs in context | 8 min |
| Three-family comparison | Quick drill comparing yo/tú/él forms across all three families | 7 min |
| Sentence building | Students use -er and -ir verbs in sentences about themselves | 10 min |
| Speaking round | Each student: 3 sentences — one -ar, one -er, one -ir | 10 min |
| Wrap-up | Homework preview | 6 min |
| **Total** | | **~60 min** |

**What must be protected:** The speaking round (block 6). Every student must produce an -er and an -ir verb sentence before the session ends. Do not let the comparison table take 25 minutes.

## Common Student Mistakes — Week 4

| Mistake | Correction | When to address |
|---|---|---|
| *Yo comeo* or *yo comer* | The *yo* form drops the infinitive ending and adds *-o* only: *como, bebo, vivo.* | Session 7 immediately |
| *Él come**s*** (using *tú* ending for *él*) | *Él/ella* takes *-e* for -er/-ir verbs, *-a* for -ar verbs. | Session 7 drilling block |
| Confusing -er and -ir *nosotros* forms | *-emos* (-er) vs. *-imos* (-ir). Quick chant: *comemos* / *vivimos.* | Session 7 comparison table |
| *Yo me llamo**s*** | *Llamarse* in first person: *me llamo.* No -s. The -ar verb ending gives *-o* not *-os.* | Session 8 first occurrence |
| *Me levanta* (wrong pronoun) | The reflexive pronoun must match the subject: *yo → me, él → se.* | Session 8 speaking round |
| *Me acuesto* stem change not recognized | Mention it once: "This verb changes its vowel — o → ue — in most forms. For now, just remember *me acuesto* and *nos acostamos.*" | Session 8 when introducing *acostarse* |
| *Son la una* (plural with 1:00) | *Es la una* — one o'clock is singular in Spanish. | Session 8 time block |

## Suggested Instructor Language

- **For introducing -er verbs:** *"You've been using -ar verbs all week. Now we're adding -er verbs. The difference is small — mostly in the tú and él/ella forms. Tú gets -es instead of -as. Él/ella gets -e instead of -a. The yo form is the same: -o."*
- **For -ir verbs:** *"There are three verb families in Spanish — -ar, -er, and -ir. Good news: -ir verbs are almost identical to -er verbs. The only difference is in nosotros and vosotros. So if you know -er, you almost know -ir."*
- **For reflexive verbs:** *"You already know one reflexive verb — me llamo. It's been there since Week 1. Reflexive verbs just express actions you do to or for yourself: getting up, showering, going to bed. There's a small pronoun — me, te, se — that goes before the verb to signal this."*
- **For the pronoun matching:** *"Point at yourself: me levanto. Point at another person: se levanta. The pronoun changes with the person. It's not decorative — it's required."*

---

## What to Skip If Time Is Tight

- Full six-form conjugation tables for -er/-ir verbs — *yo, tú, él/ella* is the core; assign *nosotros/vosotros/ellos* as reference homework
- The *vosotros* -ir ending (*-ís*) — mention it exists; do not drill unless cohort is Spain-focused
- Stem-change explanation for *despertarse* and *acostarse* — use both as phrases; defer the paradigm
- -er/-ir negation (it's the same: *no* before verb) — mention once; do not drill separately
- Clock times beyond the hour and *y media* — full telling time is Week 5

## What to Assign as Homework If Pacing Runs Long

If Session 7 runs out before the speaking round:
→ Students write 3 sentences (one per verb family) for homework; open Session 8 with sharing

If Session 8 routine narration doesn't happen:
→ Students write and record their routine; share at the start of Session 9
