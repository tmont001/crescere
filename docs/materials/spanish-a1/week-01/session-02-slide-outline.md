# Spanish A1 — Session 2 Slide Outline
## Complete Introductions and First Questions

**Session length:** 60 minutes
**Session-end goal:** Students can give a complete self-introduction (name, origin, nationality, profession), ask 2–3 questions about another person, and hold a short back-and-forth exchange.

> **Pacing note:** Numbers 31–100 have been moved out of Session 2 core and into the homework. The session ends with a full group introduction round — that is the non-negotiable block. Trim earlier slides before cutting it.

---

## Core 60-Minute Plan

### Slide 1 — Warm-Up: Back in Spanish (8 min)
**Purpose:** Re-activate Session 1 language; catch the most common errors before adding new content.
**Teacher notes:**
- Open in Spanish: *¡Buenos días / Buenas tardes! ¿Cómo están?* Wait for responses.
- Ask each student: *¿Cómo te llamas?* → *Me llamo...*
- Then: *¿De dónde eres?* → *Soy de...*
- Listen for: (1) *Yo soy llamo* — correct it once, clearly; (2) vowel quality (glides on *o*/*e*); (3) anyone uncertain about the *ser* forms they should have practiced.
- 1–2 corrections per student maximum. Purpose is activation, not re-teaching.
**Student activity:** Each student answers both questions when called on.

---

### Slide 2 — Ser Review: Forms in Context (8 min)
**Purpose:** Confirm students have the conjugation; use all six forms in real sentences before moving on.
**Teacher notes:**
- Do not start by showing the table. Test first: display subject pronouns only and ask students to produce the forms.
- If the group is shaky on 2+ forms: one round of choral repetition from the table, then remove it.
- After confirming: use all six forms in context sentences:
  - *Yo soy de Nueva York.*
  - *Tú eres estudiante, ¿verdad?*
  - *Ella es médica.*
  - *Nosotros somos el grupo de español.*
  - *Ustedes son de diferentes países.*
- Gender alert: nationality and profession adjectives change. *Él es colombiano / Ella es colombiana.* Model it — do not teach the agreement rule yet. That is Week 2.
**Student activity:** Conjugation test (pronouns shown, students produce forms) → instructor reads subject in English, students say the Spanish sentence.

---

### Slide 3 — Nationality and Profession: Just What You Need (8 min)
**Purpose:** Students can say their own nationality and profession in Spanish.
**Teacher notes:**
- Start with the students in the room. "What's your nationality?" → give them the word. "What's your profession?" → give them the word. Do not pre-teach 20 nationalities and 20 professions.
- Two rules to flag briefly:
  1. No article after *ser* + profession: *Soy médico.* (Not *Soy un médico.*)
  2. Nationality adjectives have gender forms: *americano / americana.* Just demonstrate — do not explain adjective agreement rules (Week 2).
- If students ask about additional vocabulary: "It is in the vocabulary reference — we'll keep building on it as we go."
**Student activity:** Each student says their nationality and profession in Spanish. Instructor provides vocabulary as needed.

---

### Slide 4 — Three Questions That Open a Conversation (7 min)
**Purpose:** Students can ask 2–3 questions in Spanish.
**Teacher notes:**
- Teach three question frames only. Keep them functional and memorable.
  1. *¿Cómo te llamas?* — What's your name? (informal)
  2. *¿De dónde eres?* — Where are you from? (informal)
  3. *¿A qué te dedicas?* — What do you do? (very natural in Latin America)
- Optionally add: *¿Dónde vives?* — Where do you live? (treat as a fixed phrase for now)
- Model: ask these questions to a student, get their answers, show the exchange.
- Do not teach full question-formation rules. These are spoken frames they can use immediately.
**Student activity:** Students practice asking all three questions to a partner, getting real answers.

---

### Slide 5 — The Complete Introduction Dialogue (18 min)
**Purpose:** Students hold a real back-and-forth exchange in Spanish.
**Teacher notes:**
- Display the model dialogue. Give students 2 minutes to read silently.
- Instructor models with one student (instructor is the new person, student is themselves).
- Pairs practice for 8–10 minutes. Walk the virtual room — listen in, note errors.
- Debrief the 2–3 most common errors with the whole group after. Do not list every error — pick what matters most.
- Watch for: *soy llamo*; missing vowel quality; articles before professions (*soy un médico* → correct to *soy médico*).

**Model dialogue:**
> A: *¡Hola! Me llamo Ana. ¿Y tú?*
> B: *Hola, me llamo [name]. Mucho gusto.*
> A: *Mucho gusto. ¿De dónde eres?*
> B: *Soy de [city/country]. ¿Y tú?*
> A: *Soy de Madrid. ¿Dónde vives ahora?*
> B: *Vivo en [city]. ¿A qué te dedicas?*
> A: *Soy profesora de español. ¿Y tú?*
> B: *Soy [profession]. Soy [nationality].*
> A: *¡Qué interesante! Un placer conocerte.*
> B: *Igualmente.*

**Student activity:** Read silently (2 min) → instructor models with one student (3 min) → pair practice (8–10 min) → group debrief (3 min).

---

### Slide 6 — Full Introduction Round (8 min)
**Purpose:** Every student gives a complete introduction — the week's main speaking milestone.
**Teacher notes:**
- Every student takes a turn. Allow notes — do not require memorization.
- Format: each student gives 4–5 sentences. *Me llamo... Soy de... Soy [nationality]. Soy [profession].*
- Correct one thing per student, maximum two. Pick the most important error. Save the rest.
- End by asking one student to introduce a classmate from what they heard. This rewards listening.
- Close with: "You just introduced yourself in Spanish. That is a real skill."
**Student activity:** Each student gives their introduction. One student introduces a classmate.

---

### Slide 7 — Homework Preview and Wrap-Up (3 min)
**Purpose:** Set clear homework expectations; close positively.
**Teacher notes:** Keep brief. "The homework has three parts: the numbers pattern from 31 to 100 (I walk you through the logic — it's easier than French), your written introduction, and saying it out loud one more time."
**Student activity:** Students note homework. Optional: one word in Spanish each before logging off.

---

## Cut First If Running Behind

Cut in this order — most dispensable first:

1. **Slide 3 (nationality/profession):** If students already have their own words from Slide 2 context, skip the dedicated slide.
2. **Slide 4 (questions):** Reduce to just the first two frames (*¿Cómo te llamas? / ¿De dónde eres?*). The third is optional.
3. **Slide 5 (dialogue):** Shorten pair practice to 5 minutes. Skip the full group debrief.
4. Do not cut Slide 6 (introduction round). That is the session's reason for existing.

---

## Extension for Fast Cohorts

Only if you finish the introduction round with time remaining:

- **Numbers 31–69 (5 min):** Teach the regular tens (*treinta, cuarenta, cincuenta, sesenta*) and count together. The 31–99 system is completely regular — just tens + *y* + unit.
- **Common expressions with ser (3 min):** *Es fácil / Es difícil / Es interesante / Es verdad / No es verdad.* Quick recognition drill.
- **Tú/usted extended scenarios (4 min):** Additional register practice with less obvious cases (new colleague at a startup, a professor, a hotel receptionist).
