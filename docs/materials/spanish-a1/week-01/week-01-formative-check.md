# Spanish A1 — Week 1 Formative Check

**Administered:** Start of Session 3 (first session of Week 2)
**Time needed:** 6–8 minutes total
**Purpose:** Quick checkpoint — not a test. Tells the instructor which items need a brief review before moving into the ser vs. estar distinction.

---

## Oral Prompts (5 questions — ~1 min per student for the full set)

Ask these to the group or call on individual students. Listen for accuracy of the target item, not overall fluency.

| # | Prompt | Target response | What to listen for |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | *¿Cómo te llamas?* | *Me llamo [name].* | Correct phrase — not *Soy llamo* or *Yo soy llamo* |
| 2 | *¿De dónde eres?* | *Soy de [city/country].* | Correct use of *soy de*; clean vowels |
| 3 | Give me all six forms of ser — go. | soy, eres, es, somos, sois, son | All six; reasonable speed; *sois* understood as Spain-only |
| 4 | Count from 1 to 10. | uno, dos, tres, cuatro, cinco, seis, siete, ocho, nueve, diez | Pronunciation: the r in *tres/cuatro*; clean vowels throughout |
| 5 | How do you say "Nice to meet you" in Spanish? | *Mucho gusto* or *Encantado/a* | Either is correct; bonus if student can use both and notes the gender ending |

---

## Written Prompts (5 questions — students write answers, ~3 min)

Paste into the session chat or distribute at the start of Session 3.

**Instructions to students:** Answer in Spanish. Short answers only.

1. Write a sentence using *ser* to say "She is from Mexico City."
2. How do you say "Good morning" in Spanish?
3. What is the Spanish number for 45?
4. Write a sentence using *somos* to say "We are teachers."
5. Write a question asking where someone is from (informal).

**Answer key:**
1. *Ella es de Ciudad de México.* (accept *Es de México* or similar)
2. *Buenos días.*
3. *cuarenta y cinco* (tests the 31–99 pattern from Session 2 homework)
4. *Somos profesores/as.*
5. *¿De dónde eres?* (accept any correct informal formulation)

---

## Instructor Scoring Notes

Not graded. Use it to make one of three decisions:

| Result | What it means | What to do |
|---|---|---|
| Students handle all 5 oral prompts confidently | Week 1 content is secure | Move into Week 2 (ser vs. estar) immediately |
| Students stumble on 1–2 prompts | Minor gaps; expected | Quick review at start of Session 3 (5 min max), then move on |
| Students stumble on 3+ prompts | Week 1 needs reinforcement | Build a 10-minute review block into Sessions 3 and 4 before adding ser vs. estar |

**Most common Week 1 gaps:**
- *Me llamo* vs. *soy llamo* — correct it once more, clearly, and it usually sticks
- Ser forms *somos* or *son* (the less-practiced forms) — a quick 2-minute drill closes this
- Vowel quality — some students are still adding English glides to *o* and *e*; a brief demonstration usually recalibrates them

**Written prompt 3 note:** The 31–99 pattern is taught in the Session 2 homework, not live in class. If most students get 45 wrong, it likely means they did not work through the homework — address this briefly and move on. Do not treat it as a Week 1 content failure.

**Special note for Session 3:** Students are about to encounter *estar* for the first time. Before introducing it, confirm *ser* is secure. A student who is shaky on *soy/eres/es* will be confused by the contrast. If the group is uncertain, spend the first 5–8 minutes of Session 3 on *ser* review, then introduce the new material.

---

## Ready to Move On

A student is ready for Week 2 content when they can:

- [ ] Give a 4–5 sentence self-introduction without the script
- [ ] Produce all six forms of *ser* with reasonable speed
- [ ] Count to 20 reliably; pattern for 31–99 understood from homework
- [ ] Use *Buenos días / Buenas tardes / Hola* correctly for the time of day
- [ ] Use *tú* and *usted* correctly in the scenarios from Session 1

Pronunciation errors alone are not a gate for Week 2. Content readiness is. A student who says the vowels imperfectly but produces the right grammar is ready for Week 2.
