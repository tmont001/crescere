# Spanish A1 — Week 1 Instructor Quick Reference

---

## 1. Week 1 Snapshot

| | |
|---|---|
| **Theme** | Sounds, Greetings, and First Introductions with Ser |
| **Sessions** | 1 (Sounds, Greetings, and Ser) and 2 (Complete Introductions and First Questions) |
| **Communicative goal** | Students greet, introduce themselves using *ser*, ask 2–3 questions, and hold a short exchange |
| **Grammar focus** | *Ser* — all 6 forms; *tú* vs. *usted*; ser/estar firewall (do not introduce *estar* this week) |
| **Vocabulary focus** | Greetings, *me llamo / soy de / soy [profession/nationality]*, numbers 1–30 |
| **End-of-week outcome** | Every student gives a full spoken introduction using *ser*; holds a basic two-way exchange |

**Session 1 scope note:** Session 1 exposes students to all six *ser* forms but only requires immediate productive mastery of *soy*. Full recall of the conjugation is confirmed in Session 2. Do not introduce *estar* at any point in Week 1.

---

## 2. Session 1 At-a-Glance

**Title:** Sounds, Greetings, and Ser
**Goal:** Students greet in Spanish, give name and origin using *soy*, recognize all six *ser* forms, begin counting to 20.

| Block | Time | Must protect? |
|---|---|---|
| Welcome and setup | 5 min | No |
| Five Spanish vowels (model + repetition — the most valuable 8 min in Week 1) | 8 min | **Yes** |
| Key consonants: r/rr, j, ñ — awareness only | 5 min | Cut if needed |
| Greetings and leave-takings (including *¿Cómo estás?* as fixed phrase — no *estar* explanation) | 8 min | Yes |
| Tú vs. usted | 5 min | Yes — state the rule even if you skip the scenarios |
| Ser — full present tense (table + choral drill + each student says *Yo soy [name]*) | 8 min | **Yes** |
| Me llamo and first phrases (model intro → students produce their own) | 8 min | **Yes — students must speak before leaving** |
| Numbers 1–20 | 7 min | Reduce first if behind |
| Wrap-up and homework preview | 2 min | No |

**Cut first:** Consonants slide (skip entirely; say "it's in your homework reference and we'll keep circling back"). Reduce numbers to 1–10 live. Cut *tú/usted* scenarios to just the rule.

**Move to homework:** Alphabet, numbers 21–30 and 31–100 pattern, c/z regional variation, syllable stress rules, *vosotros* extended practice.

**Homework assigned after Session 1:** Alphabet (read once; spell your name); numbers 1–30; *ser* out loud × 3 from memory; 5 written *ser* sentences.

## 3. Session 2 At-a-Glance

**Title:** Complete Introductions and First Questions
**Goal:** Full introduction (name, origin, nationality, profession); ask 3 questions; hold a back-and-forth exchange. Confirm all 6 *ser* forms before adding anything new.

| Block | Time | Must protect? |
|---|---|---|
| Warm-up (reactivate S1 phrases; correct *Yo soy llamo*; test vowel quality) | 8 min | Yes |
| Ser review in context — test first, show table only if needed | 8 min | **Yes — confirm all 6 forms before moving on** |
| Nationality and profession — students' own words only | 8 min | No — skip if handled in *ser* review block |
| Three question frames (*¿Cómo te llamas? / ¿De dónde eres? / ¿A qué te dedicas?*) | 7 min | Reduce to 2 frames if behind |
| Dialogue practice (model → pairs → debrief) | 18 min | Shorten pairs to 5 min; skip full debrief if needed |
| Full introduction round — every student speaks | 8 min | **Non-negotiable** |
| Homework preview and wrap-up | 3 min | No |

**Cut first:** Slide 3 (nationality/profession) if handled in *ser* block. Reduce questions to first two. Shorten dialogue pair practice. Do not cut the introduction round.

**Move to homework:** Numbers 31–100, adjective agreement rules, *vosotros* extended practice, inversion question formation.

**Homework assigned after Session 2:** Numbers 31–100 (pattern explanation in homework); write 5-sentence introduction using *ser*; say it from memory; answer 3 spoken questions out loud.

---

## 4. Do Not Cut

| Block | Why it is protected |
|---|---|
| Five Spanish vowels (S1 Slide 2) | Described as "the single best pronunciation investment you can make." Must come before students start speaking. |
| Ser — all 6 forms introduced (S1 Slide 6) | Full recall is expected by Session 2. Students need to hear and say all 6 forms in Session 1, even if production is partial. |
| Me llamo and first phrases (S1 Slide 7) | Students must produce a basic introduction before leaving Session 1 |
| Tú vs. usted rule (at minimum, state the rule) | Students will default to *usted* with everyone. They need explicit permission to use *tú*. |
| Ser/estar firewall | Do NOT introduce *estar* in any form this week. *¿Cómo estás?* is taught as a fixed social phrase only. If a student asks why it uses *estar*: "Week 2." |
| Full introduction round (S2 Slide 6) | Week's main milestone — every student must speak Spanish to the group |

## 5. Top Student Mistakes to Watch For

| Mistake | Quick correction cue | When to correct |
|---|---|---|
| Vowels with English glides (*no* sounds like "noh") | Model the difference: "English adds a glide at the end — Spanish cuts it clean. Say it again: *no*." Correct gently and consistently throughout Week 1. | Ongoing — throughout both sessions |
| *Yo soy llamo* (mixing *me llamo* with *soy*) | "Two separate phrases: *me llamo* for your name, *soy de* for where you're from. *Me llamo* is a fixed phrase — we'll explain in Week 3." | Immediately — correct once, clearly |
| No gender agreement on nationality (*soy americano* for a female speaker) | "Almost — *soy americana*. You'll hear the difference when you say both." Often self-corrects after hearing it once. | Flag in Session 2; don't over-explain |
| Using *usted* with classmates | "In this class, *tú* is right — using *usted* with your classmates sounds like calling a friend 'sir.'" | As it comes up |
| Asking about *ser* vs. *estar* | **Do not address this in Week 1.** Say: "Great question. That is the most important thing in Spanish grammar and it is all of Week 2. Hold it." | Redirect every time |
| Thinking *vosotros* is the only plural "you" | "In Latin American Spanish, *ustedes* is the form for everyone. We'll use *ustedes* as our default." | On first occurrence of *vosotros* in table |

## 6. Instructor Language to Use

| Moment | What to say |
|---|---|
| Vowels (S1) | *"In English the letter 'a' sounds like 'ay.' In Spanish, a always sounds like 'ah' — one sound, no glide, every time."* |
| Presenting *ser* | *"This is the most important verb in Spanish this week. By the end of tonight you will know all six forms. I am going to hold you to that."* |
| Introducing *me llamo* | *"Don't ask why it works this way yet. Just know the phrase — me llamo [name]. We'll explain the grammar in Week 3."* |
| *Tú* vs. *usted* | *"If you'd use someone's first name in English, use tú. If you'd say sir or ma'am, use usted. In this class, use tú with each other."* |
| Before the introduction round (S2) | *"You now know enough to introduce yourself in Spanish. I want each of you to do it — notes are fine, but say the words out loud."* |
| Correcting gently | Correct one item per student per round. Say: "Almost — try *soy* instead of *es*." Then move on. |
| Student who freezes | Wait 3–4 seconds. Then: "*Me llamo* — just start with that." |
| *Ser/estar* question | *"Great question. That is all of Week 2. Hold it."* |

## 7. Homework and Between-Session Reminder

| | What students do |
|---|---|
| **After Session 1** | Alphabet (read once; spell your name out loud); numbers 1–30 out loud and written; *ser* full conjugation × 3 from memory; 5 written *ser* sentences |
| **After Session 2** | Numbers 31–100 (fully regular pattern in homework — easier than French); write 5-sentence introduction using *ser*; say it from memory; answer 3 spoken questions out loud |
| **Before Week 2** | Remind students at end of Session 2: "Next session starts with a quick check on *ser* and the phrases from this week — nothing new, just what you've learned." |

**Formative check:** Filed at `week-01/week-01-formative-check.md`. Administer at the **start of Session 3** (first session of Week 2). Takes 6–8 minutes. Not graded — confirms whether *ser* is secure before introducing *estar*.

**Critical before Session 3:** Confirm *ser* is solid before introducing *estar*. A student uncertain about *soy/eres/es* will be confused by the contrast. If the group is shaky, spend the first 5–8 minutes of Session 3 on *ser* review, then introduce *estar*.

**If you have to move the introduction round to homework** (Session 2 ran out of time): ask each student to record a 30-second voice introduction and send it before Session 3. Not ideal — the group moment matters — but better than skipping.

---

## 8. Ready for Week 2

A student is ready to move into Week 2 when they can:

- [ ] Give a 4–5 sentence self-introduction without the script
- [ ] Produce all six *ser* forms with reasonable speed
- [ ] Count to 20 reliably; understand the 31–99 pattern from homework
- [ ] Use *Buenos días / Buenas tardes / Hola* correctly for the time of day
- [ ] Use *tú* and *usted* correctly in the Session 1 scenarios

**Pronunciation errors alone do not gate Week 2.** A student who says the vowels imperfectly but produces the right grammar is ready to move forward. Vowel quality is a Week 1–4 process; it improves with repetition across multiple weeks.

**Looking ahead:** Week 2 is entirely *ser* vs. *estar*. Students who arrive at Session 3 uncertain about *soy/eres/es* will struggle with the contrast. The formative check exists to catch this before it compounds.
