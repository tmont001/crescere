## Numbers 1–100

### 1–20
| | | | |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 — uno | 6 — seis | 11 — once | 16 — dieciséis |
| 2 — dos | 7 — siete | 12 — doce | 17 — diecisiete |
| 3 — tres | 8 — ocho | 13 — trece | 18 — dieciocho |
| 4 — cuatro | 9 — nueve | 14 — catorce | 19 — diecinueve |
| 5 — cinco | 10 — diez | 15 — quince | 20 — veinte |

### 21–29 (one word each — note accent marks)
21 veintiuno · 22 veintidós · 23 veintitrés · 24 veinticuatro · 25 veinticinco
26 veintiséis · 27 veintisiete · 28 veintiocho · 29 veintinueve

### 30–99 (tens + y + unit — three separate words)
30 treinta · 40 cuarenta · 50 cincuenta · 60 sesenta · 70 setenta · 80 ochenta · 90 noventa
*Example: 45 = cuarenta y cinco · 73 = setenta y tres · 88 = ochenta y ocho*

### 100
100 — cien (alone) / ciento (before another number: *ciento uno, ciento cincuenta*)

**Note:** Unlike French, Spanish 70–99 are completely regular — no counting-in-twenties or sixty-plus-ten patterns.

## Pronunciation Reference: Key Spanish Sounds

| Sound | Description | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| Vowels (all 5) | One clean sound each — no English glides | *a* = "ah"; *e* = "eh"; *i* = "ee"; *o* = "oh"; *u* = "oo" |
| r (single) | Tongue-tip flap — like English "d/t" in "butter" | *pero, cara, para* |
| rr (double/initial) | Tongue-tip trill | *perro, Roberto, rojo* |
| j | Guttural h — back of throat | *jefe, Juan, ojo* |
| ñ | Nasal y-sound | *mañana, español, año* |
| h | Always silent | *hola, hablar, hotel* |
| c/z regional | Spain: like "th" in "think"; Latin America: like "s" | *gracias: "grath-yas" (ES) / "gras-yas" (LA)* |
| ll / y | Usually the same sound in modern Spanish: like "y" in "yes" | *llamo, yo, lluvia* |
