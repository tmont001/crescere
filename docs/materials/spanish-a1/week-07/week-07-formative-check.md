# Spanish A1 — Week 7 Formative Check

**Administered:** Start of Session 15 (first session of Week 8)
**Time needed:** 6–8 minutes total
**Purpose:** Quick checkpoint — not a test. Confirms that demonstrative adjectives, color agreement, shopping phrases, and quantity expressions are solid enough to enter Week 8 (home description, *hay*, prepositions of location, and the high-frequency irregular verb block).

---

## Oral Prompts (5 questions — ~1 min per student for the full set)

| # | Prompt | Target response | What to listen for |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | How do you say "this shirt" and "that shirt" in Spanish? | *esta camisa / esa camisa* | Both demonstratives; correct feminine form (*esta/esa*, not *este/ese*) |
| 2 | Give me the color agreement: *un pantalón + negro.* | *un pantalón negro* | Masculine form; *negro* not *negra* |
| 3 | How do you ask "How much does it cost?" (one item / multiple items) | *¿Cuánto cuesta? / ¿Cuánto cuestan?* | Both forms; singular/plural distinction |
| 4 | Say "a kilo of tomatoes" and "a dozen eggs" in Spanish. | *un kilo de tomates; una docena de huevos* | *De* (not *de los / de los*) after quantity |
| 5 | Act out the key moment in a shop: ask if you can try on a garment. | *¿Me lo/la puedo probar?* | *Lo* for masculine / *la* for feminine; accept either; natural delivery |

---

## Written Prompts (5 questions — students write answers, ~3 min)

**Instructions to students:** Answer in Spanish. Short answers only.

1. Write "these black boots" (las botas = f. pl.)
2. Write the correct color agreement: *un jersey + azul* (plural: *unos jerseys*)
3. Write "a bottle of red wine."
4. Write "I want that green jacket." (la chaqueta = f.)
5. Write "How much per kilo?"

**Answer key:**
1. *estas botas negras* (*estas* for f. pl.; *negras* for f. pl.)
2. *unos jerseys azules* (*azul* → *azules* in plural — no gender change for *azul,* but number change applies)
3. *una botella de vino tinto*
4. *Quiero esa chaqueta verde.* (*esa* for f. singular)
5. *¿A cuánto está el kilo?* or *¿Cuánto cuesta el kilo?* (both accepted)

---

## Instructor Scoring Notes

Not graded. Use it to make one of three decisions:

| Result | What it means | What to do |
|---|---|---|
| Students handle all 5 oral prompts | Week 7 content is solid | Move into Week 8 (home, *hay*, prepositions, irregular verbs) |
| Students stumble on 1–2 prompts | Minor gaps — expected at this stage | 5-minute demonstrative/color warm-up at the start of Sessions 15 and 16 |
| Students stumble on 3+ prompts | Demonstratives or quantity *de* rule not yet secure | Add 10-minute review to Session 15; carry demonstratives as a warm-up habit |

**Most common Week 7 gaps:**
- *Esta pantalón* (wrong gender for demonstrative) → *este pantalón* — students must learn clothing noun genders; this one is persistent
- *Unas zapatillas naranjas* (adding -s to invariable color) → *naranja* never changes: *zapatillas naranja*
- *Un kilo de los tomates* (keeping the article after quantity) → drop the article: *un kilo de tomates*
- *¿Cuánto cuesta?* used for plural items → should be *¿Cuánto cuestan?* — the verb agrees with the noun being priced
- *Azul → azulas* (wrong plural) → correct plural of *azul* is *azules*, not *azulas*

---

## Ready to Move On

A student is ready for Week 8 (home and neighborhood) when they can:

- [ ] Use *este/esta* and *ese/esa* correctly (with correct gender) for 4+ clothing nouns
- [ ] Apply color adjective agreement for 4+ colors — including at least one invariable color
- [ ] Ask the price of an item and buy or decline (4–5 sentences)
- [ ] Use 4+ quantity expressions with *de* (not *de los/de las*)
- [ ] Complete a simple market transaction (3 items, one price question, closing)

**Not a Week 8 gate:** Active production of *aquel/aquella*, full 12-item clothing vocabulary, or all regional clothing variants. These continue to consolidate.
