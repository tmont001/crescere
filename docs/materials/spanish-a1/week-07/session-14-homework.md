# Spanish A1 — Session 14 Homework
## Market Quantities and Prices

**Due:** Before Session 15 (Week 8)
**Time needed:** 20–30 minutes

---

## Part 1 — Quantity Expressions: Add *de* Correctly

Write each phrase correctly. Remember: after a quantity word, the article disappears — use only *de*.

1. una botella + _____ (de / del) + vino tinto
2. un kilo + _____ (de / de los) + tomates
3. una docena + _____ (de / de los) + huevos
4. 500 gramos + _____ (de / del) + queso
5. un litro + _____ (de / de la) + leche
6. 100 gramos + _____ (de / del) + jamón
7. un poco + _____ (de / de la) + sal
8. medio kilo + _____ (de / de las) + fresas

**Answer key:**
1. una botella **de** vino tinto
2. un kilo **de** tomates
3. una docena **de** huevos
4. 500 gramos **de** queso
5. un litro **de** leche
6. 100 gramos **de** jamón
7. un poco **de** sal
8. medio kilo **de** fresas

---

## Part 2 — Write a Market Dialogue (8 lines minimum)

Write a dialogue at a Spanish-speaking market stall. You are the customer; write both sides.

**Required elements:**
- [ ] Customer greets (*Hola, buenos días!*)
- [ ] Customer buys at least 3 items with quantities
- [ ] Customer asks the price of at least one item: *¿A cuánto está...? / ¿Cuánto cuesta...?*
- [ ] Customer uses *ese/esa/esos/esas* at least once to point at an item
- [ ] Stall holder uses *"¿Y algo más?"* at least once
- [ ] Customer ends the transaction: *"Nada más, gracias. ¿Cuánto es en total?"*

---

## Part 3 — Speaking Practice

Read your dialogue from Part 2 out loud. Say both parts. Third try: no notes on the quantity expressions or price questions.

Practice saying these numbers and prices aloud:
- €15 → *quince euros*
- €37 → *treinta y siete euros*
- €89 → *ochenta y nueve euros*
- €2,50 → *dos euros con cincuenta*

## Extension (Optional)

Write a shopping list in Spanish for a dinner for 4 people. Include at least 6 quantity expressions. Think about what you would actually buy at a Spanish or Latin American market: starter, main, dessert, drinks.
