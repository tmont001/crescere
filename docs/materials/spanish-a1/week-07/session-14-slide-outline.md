# Spanish A1 — Session 14 Slide Outline
## The Market: Prices and Quantities

**Session length:** 55–60 minutes
**Session-end goal:** Every student can complete a market transaction in Spanish — asking for items by quantity, checking prices, and finishing the exchange — using *ese/esa* to point at stall items and quantity expressions correctly.

> **Pacing note:** The market role play (Slide 6) is the session's core deliverable. Compress Slide 5 (cultural notes) to 3 minutes before cutting the role play. Every student buys at least 3 items and asks a price.

---

## Core 60-Minute Plan

### Slide 1 — Warm-Up: Demonstrative and Color Recall (5 min)
**Purpose:** Confirm Session 13 content before extending into the market.
**Teacher notes:**
- Quick drill: 4 items → students supply demonstrative + color.
  - *chaqueta (f.) + verde* → *esta chaqueta verde*
  - *pantalón (m.) + negro* → *ese pantalón negro*
  - *botas (f. pl.) + marrón* → *esas botas marrones*
  - *jersey (m.) + azul* → *este jersey azul*
- Then: each student says one sentence from Session 13: *"Busco esta chaqueta en verde, talla M."*
**Student activity:** 4-item demonstrative + color prompt → one clothing sentence each.

---

### Slide 2 — Prices: ¿Cuánto Cuesta? (8 min)
**Purpose:** Students can ask about and state prices confidently.
**Teacher notes:**
- Introduce the two price questions:
  - *¿Cuánto cuesta?* — for one item (singular)
  - *¿Cuánto cuestan?* — for multiple items (plural)
  - *¿A cuánto está?* — common at markets, can be used for either
  - *¿Cuánto vale?* — alternative, slightly more casual
- Price responses: *Cuesta diez euros. Son quince euros. Está a tres euros el kilo.*
- Quick number review: students already know 1–100. Drill numbers that cause problems: *treinta y cinco, cuarenta y ocho, setenta y dos, ochenta y nueve.*
- Price format: *€5 = cinco euros; €15,50 = quince euros con cincuenta; €89 = ochenta y nueve euros.*
- Quick drill: instructor shows a price; students say it in Spanish and ask about it. 5 items.
**Student activity:** 5-item price pronunciation and question drill.

---

### Slide 3 — Ese / Esa in Market Context (5 min)
**Purpose:** Students use *ese/esa/esos/esas* to point at items across a market stall.
**Teacher notes:**
- Set the scene: "At a market stall, the items are across the counter — a little farther away. So you use *ese/esa*: 'Esa manzana, please' — that apple over there."
- Show 4–5 quick examples:
  - *Quiero esa manzana.* — I want that apple.
  - *¿Cuánto cuesta ese queso?* — How much is that cheese?
  - *Póngame esos tomates.* — Give me those tomatoes.
  - *¿Puede pesar esas naranjas?* — Can you weigh those oranges?
- This block is short because *ese* was introduced in Session 13. Just reinforce it in a new (market) context.
**Student activity:** 4 instructor prompts → students complete the sentence with *ese/esa/esos/esas.*

---

### Slide 4 — Quantity Expressions (10 min)
**Purpose:** Students learn the quantity + *de* pattern and can use it to buy market produce.
**Teacher notes:**
- Open with the rule: "After any quantity word, the article disappears — just *de* remains. So: *un kilo **de** tomates,* not *un kilo **de los** tomates.* The article *los* is dropped."
- Introduce quantity expressions one by one:
  - *un kilo de* → *un kilo de tomates*
  - *medio kilo de* → *medio kilo de fresas*
  - *500 gramos de* → *500 gramos de queso*
  - *un litro de* → *un litro de leche*
  - *una botella de* → *una botella de agua*
  - *una docena de* → *una docena de huevos*
  - *un poco de* → *un poco de sal*
  - *100 gramos de* → *100 gramos de jamón*
- After each: students repeat → give a new example with a different food.
- Error to anticipate and correct immediately: *un kilo de los tomates* — "Drop the article after a quantity word."
**Student activity:** Repeat each expression → give one new example.

---

### Slide 5 — Market Vocabulary and Cultural Notes (8 min)
**Purpose:** Students learn 10–12 market food items and understand how a Spanish-speaking market works.
**Teacher notes:**
- Introduce fruits and vegetables: *un tomate, una manzana, una naranja, una fresa, una patata/papa, una zanahoria, un limón, unas uvas.*
- Then staples: *unos huevos, la leche, el queso, el jamón, el pan.*
- After each item: students say a quantity + item: *un kilo de tomates, una docena de huevos, 200 gramos de queso.*
- Cultural notes (3 min max):
  - *El mercado vs. el supermercado:* In Spain, *el mercado municipal* (covered market hall) is a local institution. In Mexico, *el tianguis* is an outdoor weekly market. In Colombia, *la plaza de mercado.* Fresh produce is typically better quality and cheaper at markets.
  - *Nada más, gracias* — how to end the transaction ("Nothing else, thanks")
  - *¿Y algo más?* or *¿Qué más le pongo?* — the stall holder's prompt for "anything else"
  - Bargaining: acceptable in Latin American open markets, less common in Spanish *mercados.*
**Student activity:** Repeat each food item → combine with a quantity expression.

---

### Slide 6 — Market Role Play (12 min)
**Purpose:** Every student completes a full market transaction — 3+ items, one price question, full closing.
**Teacher notes:**
- Pairs: one is the customer, one is the stall holder. 3–4 minutes each; switch.
- Customer checklist:
  - [ ] Greets (*Hola, buenos días!*)
  - [ ] Asks for at least 3 items with quantities: *Quisiera un kilo de tomates...*
  - [ ] Uses *ese/esa* to point at something: *Esos tomates de ahí.*
  - [ ] Asks the price of one item: *¿A cuánto está el queso?*
  - [ ] Ends the transaction: *Nada más, gracias. ¿Cuánto es en total?*
- Stall holder checklist:
  - [ ] Greets the customer
  - [ ] Responds to each request (invents prices)
  - [ ] Asks *¿Y algo más?* at least once
  - [ ] Gives the total: *Son [X] euros en total.*
- Instructor circulates. Most likely corrections: *un kilo de los tomates* → *de tomates*; wrong *ese/esa* agreement.
**Student activity:** Full market role play (both roles).

---

### Slide 7 — Wrap-Up (5 min)
**Purpose:** Assign homework; close Week 7.
**Teacher notes:**
- Group debrief: 2 things the class did well, 1 error pattern.
- Most likely: *de los* after quantities (dropping *los* is the fix), and *cuesta/cuestan* confusion (singular vs. plural).
- Homework preview: market dialogue writing + shopping paragraph.
- Preview Week 8: "Next week: home and neighborhood — describing where things are, *hay*, prepositions of location, and a big block of high-frequency irregular verbs."
**Student activity:** Note homework.

## Extension for Fast Cohorts

- **¿A cuánto está vs. ¿Cuánto cuesta?:** Discuss the register difference — *¿a cuánto está?* is typical of market registers; *¿cuánto cuesta?* works everywhere.
- **Bargaining phrases:** *¿Me hace un descuento si compro dos kilos?* — Can you give me a discount if I buy two kilos? *¿Puede bajar el precio?* — Can you lower the price?
- **Llevar in the market sense:** In Latin America, market sellers say *"¿Qué le llevo?"* (What shall I get for you?) — a different use of *llevar.* Point this out to students who have been to Latin America.
