# Spanish A1 — Session 13 Slide Outline
## Clothing, Colors, and the Shop

**Session length:** 60 minutes
**Session-end goal:** Every student can navigate a clothing shop interaction in Spanish — describing what they want, asking for a size and color, trying it on, and buying or declining — using *este/ese* demonstratives and color adjective agreement correctly.

> **Pacing note:** The speaking round (Slide 6) is non-negotiable. Every student plays both customer and shop assistant before the session ends. Compress Slide 4 (color agreement, to 5 min) before cutting Slide 6.

---

## Core 60-Minute Plan

### Slide 1 — Warm-Up: Week 6 Close (5 min)
**Purpose:** Close the Week 6 loop; activate *gustar* and *querer* before moving to shopping.
**Teacher notes:**
- Ask each student to say one sentence: a food preference using *gustar* or *encantar.*
- If the Week 6 formative check showed widespread *yo gusto* (wrong structure): 30-second group reminder — "The indirect object pronoun goes first: *me gusta, te gusta.* Never *yo gusto.*"
- Do not re-teach. Activation only.
**Student activity:** One *gustar/encantar* sentence each.

---

### Slide 2 — Demonstrative Adjectives: Este and Ese (10 min)
**Purpose:** Students learn the *este/esta/estos/estas* (this/these) and *ese/esa/esos/esas* (that/those) series and can apply them to clothing nouns.
**Teacher notes:**
- Open with the key difference from French: "Spanish has TWO sets of demonstratives — one for things near you and one for things farther away. *Este/esta* = this/these (near, you could touch it). *Ese/esa* = that/those (over there)."
- Display both tables side by side.
- Drill — este series: instructor says a clothing noun; students give *este/esta/estos/estas.*
  - *pantalón (m.)* → *este pantalón*
  - *camisa (f.)* → *esta camisa*
  - *zapatos (m. pl.)* → *estos zapatos*
  - *botas (f. pl.)* → *estas botas*
- Drill — ese series: same format.
  - *vestido (m.)* → *ese vestido*
  - *chaqueta (f.)* → *esa chaqueta*
  - *vaqueros (m. pl.)* → *esos vaqueros*
  - *zapatillas (f. pl.)* → *esas zapatillas*
- Note that *aquel/aquella* exists for "that over there" — show one example; do not drill production this week.
**Student activity:** 4-item *este* drill → 4-item *ese* drill (8 items total).

---

### Slide 3 — Clothing Vocabulary (10 min)
**Purpose:** Students learn 10–12 core clothing items with their genders and can use demonstratives with them.
**Teacher notes:**
- Introduce clothing items in pairs where possible: *un pantalón / una falda; un jersey / una chaqueta; un vestido / una camiseta.*
- After each item: students repeat + say it with *este/esta:* *"Este pantalón / esta falda."*
- Also introduce: *unos vaqueros, una camisa, un abrigo, unos zapatos, unas botas, unas zapatillas.*
- Emphasize that *pantalón* and *vestido* are masculine (*este pantalón, este vestido*) — students often assume clothing items worn by women must be feminine.
- Do not try to cover all items in the vocabulary file. 10–12 said aloud beats 20 on a list.
**Student activity:** Repeat each item → say it with *este/esta/estos/estas.*

---

### Slide 4 — Colors and Adjective Agreement (8 min)
**Purpose:** Students apply color adjectives to clothing items with correct gender and number agreement.
**Teacher notes:**
- Introduce 6 core colors: *negro/negra, blanco/blanca, rojo/roja, azul (invariable for gender), verde (invariable for gender), gris (invariable for gender).*
- Agreement rule: "The color comes AFTER the noun and must agree in gender AND number. Check the noun: masculine → *negro*; feminine → *negra*; plural → add -s or -es."
- Highlight that *azul, verde, gris* don't change for gender but DO change for number: *azul → azules.*
- Point out invariable colors: *naranja, rosa, beige, violeta* — never change.
- Quick drill: instructor says a clothing item + color; students combine with agreement.
  - *camisa + blanco* → *una camisa blanca*
  - *pantalón + negro* → *un pantalón negro*
  - *zapatos + marrón* → *unos zapatos marrones*
  - *chaqueta + verde* → *una chaqueta verde*
  - *vestido + rojo* → *un vestido rojo*
  - *zapatillas + naranja* → *unas zapatillas naranja* (invariable!)
**Student activity:** 6-item color-agreement drill.

---

### Slide 5 — Shopping Phrases (7 min)
**Purpose:** Students learn the core phrases for a shop interaction.
**Teacher notes:**
- Introduce phrases in sequence of a natural shop visit:
  1. Shop assistant: *"Hola, buenos días. ¿Le puedo ayudar?"*
  2. Customer: *"Sí, busco..."* or *"No gracias, solo estoy mirando."*
  3. Customer: *"¿Tiene esta camisa en azul, talla M?"*
  4. Customer: *"¿Me la puedo probar?"*
  5. Customer: *"¿Cuánto cuesta?"*
  6. Customer: *"Me la llevo."* or *"No, gracias, no me la llevo."*
- Note the *lo/la* pronoun: *Me lo llevo* (masculine item: el pantalón); *Me la llevo* (feminine item: la camisa). Introduce as a formula — full object pronoun study is in A2.
- Pronunciation drill: *¿Me la puedo probar?* × 3. *Me la llevo.* × 3.
- Cultural note: In Spain, shop assistants typically greet you when you enter. *"Solo estoy mirando"* (I'm just browsing) is perfectly acceptable.
**Student activity:** Repeat key phrases → each student says the core sequence once (one sentence per step).

---

### Slide 6 — Speaking Round: Clothing Shop (12 min)
**Purpose:** Every student navigates a clothing shop interaction as both customer and shop assistant.
**Teacher notes:**
- Pairs: one student is the customer, one is the shop assistant. 3 minutes each role; switch.
- Customer checklist:
  - [ ] Greets (*Hola / Buenos días*)
  - [ ] States what they are looking for: *Busco... en [color], talla [M/L/etc.]*
  - [ ] Uses a demonstrative: *¿Tiene esta chaqueta en verde?*
  - [ ] Asks to try it on: *¿Me la puedo probar?*
  - [ ] Asks the price: *¿Cuánto cuesta?*
  - [ ] Buys or declines: *Me la llevo* or *No, gracias*
- Shop assistant checklist:
  - [ ] Greets the customer
  - [ ] Responds (can invent stock and price)
  - [ ] Gives the fitting room direction: *Los probadores están al fondo.*
- Instructor circulates; corrects one item per student after their turn.
- Most likely corrections: wrong demonstrative gender (*esta pantalón* → *este pantalón*); wrong color agreement (*camisa rojo* → *camisa roja*).
**Student activity:** Full clothing shop role play (both roles).

---

### Slide 7 — Wrap-Up (8 min)
**Purpose:** Assign homework; preview Session 14.
**Teacher notes:**
- Preview Session 14: "Next session we go to the market — fresh food, quantities, prices. You will use *ese/esa* to point at things across a stall."
- Homework preview: demonstrative drill + clothing description paragraph.
**Student activity:** Note homework. Optional closer: name one item you're wearing today in Spanish with its color.

## Cut First If Running Behind

1. **Slide 4 (color agreement):** Reduce to 5 minutes — cover 4 colors; skip invariable explanation (assign as vocabulary reference).
2. **Slide 3 (clothing vocabulary):** Cover 8 items instead of 12.
3. **Slide 5 (shopping phrases):** Cover steps 1–4 only (find, ask, try on, price). Buy/decline can be improvised.
4. Do not cut Slide 6 (speaking round).

---

## Extension for Fast Cohorts

- **Aquel/aquella in context:** *"¿Me puede mostrar aquel abrigo que está en el escaparate?"* — Can you show me that coat in the window? Students who are ready can try using *aquel* actively.
- **Lo/la for other items:** *¿Lo tiene en otra talla?* (Do you have it in another size? — masculine item). *¿La tiene en negro?* — feminine item. Practice switching the pronoun based on the item.
- **Clothing with llevar:** *"¿Qué llevas hoy?"* — Students describe what they're wearing today using color + adjective agreement.

## Move to Homework If Needed

- Extended clothing vocabulary beyond 12 items
- *Tener que + probarse* (I need to try it on — formal obligation)
