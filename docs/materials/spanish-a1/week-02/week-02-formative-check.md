# Spanish A1 — Week 2 Formative Check

**Administered:** Start of Session 5 (first session of Week 3)
**Time needed:** 6–8 minutes total
**Purpose:** Quick checkpoint — not a test. Tells the instructor which ser/estar patterns need brief review before introducing new Week 3 vocabulary and regular -ar verbs.

---

## Oral Prompts (5 questions — ~1 min per student for the full set)

| # | Prompt | Target response | What to listen for |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | *¿Cómo estás hoy?* | *Estoy [emotion/state].* | Correct verb (*estar*, not *ser*); gender agreement if applicable |
| 2 | *¿Dónde estás ahora mismo?* | *Estoy en [location].* | Estar + *en*; not *soy en* |
| 3 | *¿De dónde eres?* | *Soy de [place].* | Correct ser; not *estoy de* |
| 4 | Tell me one thing about yourself — profession or a permanent trait. | *Soy [profession / adjective].* | Ser used for identity; any valid answer |
| 5 | Give me all six forms of *estar* — go. | estoy, estás, está, estamos, estáis, están | All six; stress on final syllable for forms 1–3 and 6 |

---

## Written Prompts (5 questions — students write answers, ~3 min)

Paste into the session chat or distribute at the start of Session 5.

**Instructions to students:** Answer in Spanish. Short answers only.

1. Write: "I am nervous because I have a presentation."
2. Write: "She is a doctor." (profession)
3. Write: "They are tired." (male group)
4. Translate: *¿Estás lista?* — what does this mean, and when would you use *ser* instead of *estar* with *lista*?
5. Write: "The restaurant is near here." (location)

**Answer key:**
1. *Estoy nervioso/a porque tengo una presentación.*
2. *Ella es médica.*
3. *Están cansados.*
4. "Are you ready?" — You would use *ser* (*Es lista.*) when describing someone as clever/smart — a permanent trait.
5. *El restaurante está cerca de aquí.*

---

## Instructor Scoring Notes

Not graded. Use it to make one of three decisions:

| Result | What it means | What to do |
|---|---|---|
| Students handle all oral prompts correctly | Week 2 content is solid | Move into Week 3 immediately |
| Students stumble on 1–2 prompts | Common gaps; expected | Quick 5-minute ser/estar review to open Session 5 |
| Students stumble on 3+ prompts | Ser/estar needs reinforcement | Add a 10-minute review to Sessions 5 and 6; ser/estar is foundational for every future week |

**Most common Week 2 gaps:**
- *Soy cansado* (using ser for a state) → the most persistent error; correct every instance through Week 4
- *Estoy de México* (using estar for origin) → correct each time; one brief re-explanation
- *Estoy lista* said by a male speaker → agreement error; quick gender correction
- *¿Dónde eres?* for asking location → reteach: *ser* = identity, *estar* = location

**On meaning-changing adjectives:** Do not gate Week 3 on these. If students can identify *aburrido/a* and *listo/a* in context, that is enough. Independent production is a Week 4 goal.

---

## Ready to Move On

A student is ready for Week 3 content when they can:

- [ ] Conjugate *estar* in all six forms without looking at a table
- [ ] Use *estar* correctly for emotions, physical states, and location
- [ ] Use *ser* correctly for origin, identity, and profession
- [ ] Recognize (not necessarily produce) at least 2 of the 4 meaning-changing adjectives

Accent marks and pronunciation are not a gate for Week 3. Verb choice is.

**Note:** Ser vs. estar errors are expected throughout the A1 course. The goal of this check is not perfect accuracy — it is to confirm students have a working framework and are not systematically using the wrong verb for the highest-frequency contexts (emotions → estar; origin → ser).
