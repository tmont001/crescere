# Spanish A1 — Week 2 Vocabulary

**Theme: States, Emotions, and Location / Ser vs. Estar**

---

## Estar — Conjugation

| Subject | Estar |
|---|---|
| yo | estoy |
| tú | estás |
| él / ella / usted | está |
| nosotros/as | estamos |
| vosotros/as | estáis |
| ellos / ellas / ustedes | están |

**Pronunciation note:** The stress falls on the final syllable for all forms except *nosotros* and *vosotros*. *estóy, estás, está, estámos, estáis, están.*

## Ser vs. Estar — Quick Reference

| Use ser for... | Use estar for... |
|---|---|
| Identity / name | Location |
| Origin / nationality | Emotions / temporary states |
| Profession / role | Physical conditions (sick, tired) |
| Permanent characteristics | Ongoing actions (estar + -ando) |
| Time / dates | Results of a change |

## Meaning-Changing Adjectives (preview — covered in Session 4)

These four adjectives change meaning depending on whether they follow *ser* or *estar*:

| Adjective | With ser | With estar |
|---|---|---|
| aburrido/a | boring (personality) | bored (current state) |
| listo/a | clever, smart | ready |
| bueno/a | good (morally, as a person) | feeling well, tasty |
| seguro/a | safe (as a place or thing) | sure, certain |

**Examples:**
- *Él es aburrido.* — He is a boring person.
- *Él está aburrido.* — He is bored (right now).
- *¿Estás listo?* — Are you ready?
- *Es un hombre listo.* — He is a clever man.
