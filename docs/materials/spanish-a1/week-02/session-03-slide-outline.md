# Spanish A1 — Session 3 Slide Outline
## Estar: Emotions, States, and Location

**Session length:** 60 minutes
**Session-end goal:** Students can say how they feel and where they are using *estar* in a complete sentence. They have heard the first comparison with *ser* but are not yet responsible for choosing independently.

> **Pacing note:** The speaking round (Slide 6) is non-negotiable. Every student must produce *Estoy + emotion/state* out loud before the session ends. Trim Slide 7 (ser/estar preview) before cutting it.

---

## Core 60-Minute Plan

### Slide 1 — Warm-Up: Ser Review (5 min)
**Purpose:** Reactivate *ser* before introducing *estar*. Makes the contrast meaningful when it appears.
**Teacher notes:**
- Call on 3 students: "Tell me your name, where you are from, and one thing about yourself." In Spanish. Notes allowed.
- Listen for: correct *soy/eres/es* usage, correct use of *de* for origin.
- If anyone says *estoy de...* — note it mentally but do not correct yet. That is today's lesson.
**Student activity:** 3 students answer ¿De dónde eres? ¿Cómo te llamas? ¿Qué eres?

---

### Slide 2 — Introducing Estar (8 min)
**Purpose:** Students hear all six forms of *estar* and can say them in sequence.
**Teacher notes:**
- Write on board / share screen: *yo estoy, tú estás, él/ella está, nosotros estamos, vosotros estáis, ellos están.*
- Say each form; students repeat. Do it three times through: slow → normal pace → fast.
- Point out the stress pattern: all forms end with the stressed syllable except *nosotros/vosotros.*
- Quick paired drill: student A says a subject pronoun, student B gives the correct form of *estar.* 1 minute.
**Student activity:** Choral repetition × 3 → pairs drill subjects/forms for 1 minute.

---

### Slide 3 — Emotions and States (12 min)
**Purpose:** Students can say 6–8 emotion/state words with *estar* in a full sentence.
**Teacher notes:**
- Introduce each state with a sentence + a brief context:
  - *Estoy cansado/a.* — "It's 10 PM on a Monday."
  - *Estoy nervioso/a.* — "Before a job interview."
  - *Estoy contento/a.* — "I just got good news."
  - *Estoy triste.* — "It's raining. My coffee is cold."
  - *Estoy ocupado/a.* — "I have five meetings today."
  - *Estoy estresado/a.* — "I have a deadline tomorrow."
  - *Estoy emocionado/a.* — "I'm starting a new project."
  - *Estoy bien / estoy mal.* — "In response to ¿Cómo estás?"
- After each: students repeat the full sentence.
- Pause after 8: ask students *¿Cómo estás ahora mismo?* — How are you right now? Quick round — every student says one sentence. Notes allowed.
**Student activity:** Repeat each sentence → quick check-in round: ¿Cómo estás ahora mismo?

---

### Slide 4 — Gender Agreement with States (5 min)
**Purpose:** Students notice that most emotion adjectives change ending for feminine speakers.
**Teacher notes:**
- Point out: *cansado → cansada, nervioso → nerviosa, contento → contenta, ocupado → ocupada, emocionado → emocionada, estresado → estresada.*
- Exceptions: *triste* (no change), *bien/mal* (no change — they are adverbs).
- Do a brief call-and-response: instructor says the masculine form, female students give the feminine. Two passes. Do not over-drill — this is reinforcement, not a new lesson.
- Say: "Your gender determines the adjective ending. Your verb is always *estar.* The verb never changes based on gender — only the adjective does."
**Student activity:** Instructor says masculine form → female students call out feminine form (or all students).

---

### Slide 5 — Location (8 min)
**Purpose:** Students can say where they are right now using *estar.*
**Teacher notes:**
- Introduce 6 location phrases with *estar*:
  - *Estoy en casa.* / *Estoy en el trabajo.* / *Estoy en clase.* / *Estoy en el centro.* / *Estoy cerca del trabajo.* / *El libro está aquí.*
- Introduce the question: *¿Dónde estás?*
- Quick round: each student answers *¿Dónde estás ahora mismo?* One sentence. Very fast.
- Write on board: *Location = estar.* Say: "In Spanish, where you are is always *estar.* This includes buildings, cities, countries when talking about current location."
**Student activity:** Quick round — ¿Dónde estás ahora mismo? One sentence each.

---

### Slide 6 — Speaking Round: Estoy... y estoy en... (10 min)
**Purpose:** Students use *estar* twice in one sentence — once for state, once for location.
**Teacher notes:**
- Target structure: *Hoy estoy [emotion/state] porque [brief reason]. Estoy en [location].*
- Give an example: *Hoy estoy un poco cansado porque tengo mucho trabajo. Estoy en casa.*
- Every student takes a turn. Notes allowed. No memorization required.
- Correct: missing agreement (-a for female speakers); wrong verb (*soy* instead of *estoy*); location without *en.*
- Affirm what is right before correcting what is wrong.
**Student activity:** Each student says two sentences using estar — one emotion/state, one location.

---

### Slide 7 — Ser vs. Estar: First Look (7 min)
**Purpose:** Students see the contrast in two clear sentence pairs. No rules — just examples.
**Teacher notes:**
- Put two pairs on screen:
  - *Soy de México.* (origin — permanent) vs. *Estoy en México.* (location — where I am now)
  - *Ella es médica.* (profession — identity) vs. *Ella está cansada.* (state — how she feels)
- Say: "Same person. Two different verbs. Not because one is wrong — because they mean different things."
- Quick class check: show 3 sentences. Students call out *ser* or *estar.* Do not explain every rule — just let them see the pattern.
- Close with: "Your homework has more practice with this. We will go deeper in Session 4."
**Student activity:** 3 quick ser/estar identification sentences. Call out which verb.

---

### Slide 8 — Wrap-Up (5 min)
**Purpose:** Homework preview; close positively.
**Teacher notes:** 
- Preview homework: "Practice the estar conjugation from memory, then write 5 sentences about how you feel today and where you are."
- End with: "You now have two verbs — *ser* and *estar.* Together they cover most of what you need to describe yourself in the present. That is real progress."
**Student activity:** Note homework. Optional: one sentence about how they feel right now as they log off.

## Extension for Fast Cohorts

- **¿Por qué?** — practice: "Estoy nervioso *porque* tengo un examen." Students add a *porque* clause to their Slide 6 sentences.
- **Additional emotion vocabulary:** *frustrado/a, aliviado/a (relieved), sorprendido/a (surprised).* Students look up one emotion that describes them right now.
- **Estar + location challenge:** describe where 3 things in your home are right now: *El libro está en la mesa. Mi teléfono está aquí.*
