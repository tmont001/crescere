# Spanish A1 — Session 4 Slide Outline
## Ser vs. Estar / Meaning-Changing Adjectives

**Session length:** 60 minutes
**Session-end goal:** Students can choose between *ser* and *estar* correctly in the 8 most common contexts and recognize the 4 meaning-changing adjectives. They can sustain a short conversation using both verbs.

> **Pacing note:** The group conversation (Slide 6) is non-negotiable. Students must use both *ser* and *estar* in the same interaction before the session ends. Trim Slides 3 or 4 before cutting it.

---

## Core 60-Minute Plan

### Slide 1 — Warm-Up: Homework Sharing (7 min)
**Purpose:** Activate Week 2 vocabulary; surface any persistent *ser/estar* confusions before the contrast lesson.
**Teacher notes:**
- Ask 3–4 students to share their 5 sentences from the Session 3 homework.
- Listen for: *soy cansado* (wrong verb), *estoy de México* (wrong verb for origin), missing gender agreement.
- Note errors for the group debrief — do not stop to explain now. Say: "We'll look at some of those patterns in a minute."
**Student activity:** 3–4 students share their homework sentences. Group listens.

---

### Slide 2 — Ser vs. Estar: The Core Contrast (12 min)
**Purpose:** Students have a clear, memorable framework for choosing between ser and estar.
**Teacher notes:**
- Write two columns on screen:
  - **Ser:** identity, origin, profession, permanent characteristics, time, definitions
  - **Estar:** location, emotions, temporary states, physical conditions, ongoing actions
- Work through 8 sentence pairs — one with *ser*, one with *estar*, same subject where possible:
  1. *Soy de Colombia. / Estoy en Colombia.* (origin vs. location)
  2. *Es profesora. / Está cansada.* (profession vs. state)
  3. *Eres inteligente. / Estás nervioso/a.* (trait vs. emotion)
  4. *La reunión es en el café.* (events) vs. *Estamos en el café.* (location)
  5. *Son las tres.* (time) vs. *Está bien.* (condition)
- For each pair: ask students to predict which verb before you reveal it. "Ser or estar — quick, don't think too long."
- Common error to address: *Soy cansado* — say clearly: "In Spanish, how you feel is always *estar.* There are no exceptions for emotions or temporary states."
**Student activity:** Predict ser or estar before each sentence is revealed. Quick-fire.

---

### Slide 3 — Practice: Choose the Right Verb (10 min)
**Purpose:** Students apply the framework to 10 new sentences without help.
**Teacher notes:**
- Students choose *ser* or *estar* and fill in the correct form. Either written (in chat) or called out.
- Do these together as a class — the discussion between students is more valuable than individual silent work.

| # | Sentence | Answer |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Yo _____ estudiante. | soy |
| 2 | Ella _____ en Madrid ahora. | está |
| 3 | Nosotros _____ de Argentina. | somos |
| 4 | ¿Cómo _____ tú hoy? | estás |
| 5 | El café _____ bueno. (it's tasty) | está |
| 6 | Él _____ médico. | es |
| 7 | Ellos _____ cansados. | están |
| 8 | ¿Dónde _____ el banco? | está |
| 9 | La película _____ interesante. | es |
| 10 | Yo _____ muy bien, gracias. | estoy |

- After each answer, give the one-word reason: origin, profession, emotion, location, etc.
- Note: item 5 (*el café está bueno* = the coffee tastes good today) is a preview of meaning-changing — flag it when you get to it, do not explain yet.
**Student activity:** Call out the verb + a one-word reason for their choice.

---

### Slide 4 — Meaning-Changing Adjectives (10 min)
**Purpose:** Students can recognize the 4 most common meaning-changing adjectives and understand why both forms exist.
**Teacher notes:**
- Introduce the concept before the words: "Some adjectives in Spanish have a completely different meaning depending on whether you use *ser* or *estar.* This is not an error — it is the system working correctly."
- Work through 4 pairs using the reference handout ([reference-ser-vs-estar.md](../reference-ser-vs-estar.md)):

| Adjective | Ser | Estar |
|---|---|---|
| aburrido/a | He is a boring person (*Es aburrido.*) | He is bored right now (*Está aburrido.*) |
| listo/a | She is clever/smart (*Es lista.*) | She is ready (*Está lista.*) |
| bueno/a | He is a good person (*Es bueno.*) | He feels well / it tastes good (*Está bueno.*) |
| seguro/a | This is a safe road. (*Es seguro.*) | I am certain. (*Estoy seguro.*) |

- For each pair: say both sentences out loud; students repeat. Then ask: "What changed? The verb — not the adjective."
- Note: students do not need to produce these independently this week. Recognition is the goal.
- Close with: "You don't need to memorize all of these today. The reference handout has them. What you *do* need is to notice that the verb you choose can completely change your meaning."
**Student activity:** Repeat each sentence pair. Identify which meaning belongs to which verb.

---

### Slide 5 — Group Conversation Practice (12 min)
**Purpose:** Students use both *ser* and *estar* in a natural interaction.
**Teacher notes:**
- Introduce three questions students should be able to answer using both verbs:
  - *¿De dónde eres?* (ser — origin)
  - *¿Cómo estás hoy?* (estar — state)
  - *¿Dónde estás ahora mismo?* (estar — location)
- Add one question that requires a choice: *¿Es o está?* — students decide based on context.
- Pairs: one student asks all three questions, the other answers. Switch.
- Then open to the full group: instructor asks each student one question, rotating through the three types.
- Correct: verb choice errors (ser/estar swap); missing agreement on adjectives.
**Student activity:** Pair Q&A with all three question types → full group round.

---

### Slide 6 — Wrap-Up (4 min)
**Purpose:** Reference handout reminder; close with a clear picture of what students know.
**Teacher notes:**
- Say: "You now have the two most important Spanish verbs — *ser* and *estar.* You will use them in every single session from now on."
- Remind students the reference handout is there for when they are unsure. It is not cheating to use it.
- Homework preview: ser vs. estar written practice + sentences using the meaning-changing adjectives.
**Student activity:** Note homework. Optional: one sentence using *ser* and one using *estar* before logging off.

---

## Cut First If Running Behind

1. **Slide 4 (meaning-changing adjectives):** Introduce only *aburrido* and *listo*. Note that *bueno* and *seguro* are on the reference handout. Cover the rest in Session 5 warm-up.
2. **Slide 3 (practice items):** Do 6 instead of 10; assign items 7–10 as written homework.
3. **Slide 2 (core contrast):** Cover 5 sentence pairs instead of all 8; select the clearest contrasts (origin vs. location, profession vs. state, emotion vs. trait).
4. Do not cut Slide 5 (group conversation).

---

## Extension for Fast Cohorts

- **Estar + gerund preview:** *Estoy hablando. Está comiendo.* Mention that *estar* is also used for present progressive — do not drill, just show the pattern.
- **More meaning-changing adjectives:** *malo/a* (ser = bad/evil as a person; estar = sick/feel bad), *rico/a* (ser = wealthy; estar = delicious). These are Week 4 content but curious students can preview them.
- **Conversation challenge:** students describe a real or fictional person using at least 4 sentences — two with *ser*, two with *estar* — without notes.
