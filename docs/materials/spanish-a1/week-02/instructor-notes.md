# Spanish A1 — Week 2 Instructor Notes

**Theme: Estar / States, Emotions, Location / Ser vs. Estar Introduction**

---

## Week Objective

By the end of Week 2, students can:
- Conjugate *estar* in all six forms without looking at a table
- Express their current emotional state and physical condition using *estar*
- State where they or someone else is located
- Distinguish *ser* vs. *estar* in the most common beginner contexts (identity/origin vs. location/emotions)
- Recognize the four meaning-changing adjectives; begin to use *aburrido/a* and *listo/a* in context

Full mastery of ser vs. estar distinction is a *course objective*, not a Week 2 objective. Students leave Week 2 with a working intuition and the reference handout. Precision comes over time.

---

## Session 3 Pacing — Estar, Emotions, Location

**Theme:** What is estar? How does it feel in use?

| Block | Content | Time |
|---|---|---|
| Warm-up | Ser review — 3 students say where they are from and what they do | 5 min |
| Conjugation | Introduce estar — choral drill, all 6 forms | 8 min |
| Emotions | 8 core emotion/state words with estar; pair check-in round | 12 min |
| Location | *¿Dónde estás?* — 6 location phrases; say where you are right now | 8 min |
| Speaking round | Each student: *Hoy estoy... y estoy en...* | 10 min |
| Ser vs. estar preview | Two side-by-side sentences; one difference explained | 7 min |
| Wrap-up | Homework preview | 5 min |
| **Total** | | **~55 min** |

**What must be protected:** The speaking round (block 5). Every student must produce *estoy + emotion/state* out loud before the session ends.

## Session 4 Pacing — Ser vs. Estar; Meaning-Changing Adjectives

**Theme:** When to use which verb; meaning changes with *aburrido* and *listo*.

| Block | Content | Time |
|---|---|---|
| Warm-up | 3–4 students share Session 3 homework; catch estar errors | 7 min |
| Ser vs. estar side-by-side | Core contrast with 6–8 sentence pairs | 12 min |
| Practice — identity vs. state | 10 sentence-completion items; pair check | 10 min |
| Meaning-changing adjectives | 4 pairs: aburrido, listo, bueno, seguro | 10 min |
| Group conversation | Students ask and answer: ¿Cómo estás? ¿Dónde estás? ¿De dónde eres? | 12 min |
| Wrap-up | Remind students of reference handout; homework preview | 4 min |
| **Total** | | **~55 min** |

**What must be protected:** The group conversation (block 5). Students must use both *ser* and *estar* in the same conversation.

## Common Mistakes to Anticipate

| Mistake | Correction | When to address |
|---|---|---|
| *Soy cansado.* (using ser for a state) | *Estoy cansado.* | Correct every time, starting Session 3 |
| *Estoy de México.* (using estar for origin) | *Soy de México.* | Correct every time, starting Session 4 |
| *¿Dónde eres?* (using ser for location) | *¿Dónde estás?* | First occurrence in Session 3 — explain clearly; correct after |
| *Él está aburrido.* when meaning "He is a boring person" | *Él es aburrido.* | Session 4 meaning-changing block |
| Forgetting gender agreement on estar adjectives | *Estoy cansada.* (for a female speaker) | Introduce the pattern once; correct consistently |

**Ser vs. estar persistence:** This distinction will not be clean in Week 2. Students will make mistakes throughout the course. The goal of Session 4 is not to eliminate errors — it is to give students a usable framework and the reference handout to consult.

---

## The Ser/Estar Firewall

Do not let *ser* and *estar* become a grammar lecture. The safest frame for A1:

- *Estar* = how you feel or where you are (right now, changeable)
- *Ser* = who you are or where you are from (identity, not changeable)

Do not introduce the full ruleset yet. The reference handout ([reference-ser-vs-estar.md](../reference-ser-vs-estar.md)) is for students to consult, not to study front to back. If students ask about an edge case in Week 2, say: "Great question — that is a Week 4 topic. For now, use this rule."

The student-facing reference handout is available at [reference-ser-vs-estar.md](../reference-ser-vs-estar.md). Distribute it at the start of Session 4 or end of Session 3. Students should have it in hand during the meaning-changing adjective block.

## What Must Be True Before Week 3

Students should arrive at Session 5 able to:

- [ ] Conjugate *estar* from memory (all six forms)
- [ ] Say *Estoy [emotion].* and *Estoy en [location].* in response to a prompt
- [ ] Identify whether a sentence calls for *ser* or *estar* in the 8 most common contexts
- [ ] Recognize the four meaning-changing adjectives without a reference sheet

Students do NOT need to use meaning-changing adjectives independently before Week 3. Recognition is enough.
