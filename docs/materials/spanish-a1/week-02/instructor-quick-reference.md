# Spanish A1 — Week 2 Instructor Quick Reference

---

## 1. Week 2 Snapshot

| | |
|---|---|
| **Theme** | Estar, States, Emotions, and Location / Ser vs. Estar Introduction |
| **Sessions** | 3 (Estar: Emotions, States, Location) and 4 (Ser vs. Estar / Meaning-Changing Adjectives) |
| **Communicative goal** | Students can express their current state, emotion, and location using *estar*; distinguish *ser* vs. *estar* in the most common beginner contexts |
| **Grammar focus** | *Estar* (all 6 forms); ser vs. estar contrast; gender agreement on state adjectives; 4 meaning-changing adjectives (recognition only — not required in production yet) |
| **Vocabulary focus** | 8 core emotions/states; 6 location phrases; ser/estar reference handout |
| **End-of-week outcome** | Students use both *ser* and *estar* correctly in the 8 most common contexts; hold a short conversation using both verbs |

**Full mastery of ser vs. estar is a course objective, not a Week 2 objective.** Students leave Week 2 with a working intuition and the reference handout. Precision builds across A1. Do not treat ongoing errors as failures.

## 2. Start-of-Week Reminder

**Week 1 formative check** is at `week-01/week-01-formative-check.md`. Administer at the **start of Session 3**, before any Week 2 content. Takes 6–8 minutes. Not graded.

| Result | Action |
|---|---|
| Confident on all 5 oral prompts | Move directly into Week 2 content |
| Shaky on 1–2 prompts | Quick 5-minute review, then proceed |
| Shaky on 3+ prompts | 10-minute review block in Sessions 3 and 4 before *estar* |

**Critical:** Confirm *ser* is secure before introducing *estar*. A student uncertain about *soy/eres/es* will be confused by the contrast. If those forms are shaky, spend the first 5–8 minutes of Session 3 on *ser* review, then introduce *estar*.

## 3. Session 3 At-a-Glance

**Title:** Estar: Emotions, States, and Location
**Goal:** Students can say how they feel and where they are using *estar* in a complete sentence. First comparison with *ser* is preview only — students are not yet accountable for choosing independently.

| Block | Time | Must protect? |
|---|---|---|
| Warm-up (*ser* review — 3 students answer two questions in Spanish; reactivate before contrast appears) | 5 min | Yes |
| *Estar* conjugation (all 6 forms; choral drill × 3; pairs drill; stress pattern note) | 8 min | **Yes** |
| 8 emotions and states with *estar* (*cansado/a, nervioso/a, contento/a, triste, ocupado/a, estresado/a, emocionado/a, bien/mal*); quick check-in round | 12 min | Yes |
| Gender agreement on state adjectives (brief call-and-response; flag *triste/bien/mal* as no-change) | 5 min | Condense to 60 seconds if behind |
| Location with *estar* (6 phrases; *¿Dónde estás?* round — every student says one sentence) | 8 min | Yes; reduce to 2 examples if behind |
| Speaking round: *Hoy estoy... y estoy en...* — every student says two *estar* sentences | 10 min | **Non-negotiable** |
| Ser vs. estar first look (2 sentence pairs — preview only; cut entirely if behind) | 7 min | Cut first if needed |
| Wrap-up and homework preview | 5 min | No |

**Cut first:** Ser/estar preview (Slide 7) — cut entirely; say "We'll look at this in Session 4." Gender agreement to 60 seconds ("If you're a woman, add -a to most of these words — it's in your homework"). Location to *en casa* and *en el trabajo* only.

**Move to homework:** Gender agreement drill, full ser/estar comparison (Session 4 core), *¿por qué?* clauses.

**Homework assigned after Session 3:** *Estar* × 3 from memory; write 5 sentences using *estar* (emotion, physical state, location, third person); ser vs. estar preview identification exercise.

## 4. Session 4 At-a-Glance

**Title:** Ser vs. Estar / Meaning-Changing Adjectives
**Goal:** Students choose between *ser* and *estar* correctly in the 8 most common contexts; recognize 4 meaning-changing adjectives; sustain a short conversation using both verbs.

| Block | Time | Must protect? |
|---|---|---|
| Warm-up (3–4 students share Session 3 homework; surface *ser/estar* errors for later debrief) | 7 min | Yes |
| Ser vs. estar core contrast (2 columns; 8 sentence pairs; predict before reveal — quick fire) | 12 min | **Yes** |
| Practice: choose the right verb (10 items as a class; one-word reason after each answer) | 10 min | Yes; reduce to 6 items if behind |
| Meaning-changing adjectives: *aburrido, listo, bueno, seguro* — recognition goal, not production | 10 min | Yes; reduce to *aburrido* and *listo* only if behind |
| Group conversation: *¿De dónde eres? / ¿Cómo estás? / ¿Dónde estás?* — pairs then full group; both verbs required | 12 min | **Non-negotiable** |
| Wrap-up (reference handout reminder; homework preview) | 4 min | No |

**Cut first:** Reduce meaning-changing block to *aburrido* and *listo* only; note *bueno* and *seguro* are on the reference handout. Reduce practice to 6 items; assign items 7–10 as homework. Reduce core contrast to 5 clearest pairs (origin vs. location; profession vs. state; emotion vs. trait).

**Move to homework:** Items 7–10 from practice; *bueno/a* and *seguro/a* pairs; comparison sentences; estar + gerund preview.

**Homework assigned after Session 4:** Ser vs. estar practice (10 items); meaning-changing adjectives (5 sentences); 5–7 sentence description of a real person using both verbs at least twice each; say aloud from memory; answer 3 questions out loud.

## 6. Top Student Mistakes to Watch For

| Mistake | Quick correction cue | When to correct |
|---|---|---|
| *Soy cansado.* (ser for a state) | *"Estoy cansado. In Spanish, how you feel is always estar — no exceptions for emotions or temporary states."* Correct every instance. | Starting Session 3; every occurrence through A1 |
| *Estoy de México.* (estar for origin) | *"Soy de México. Where you're from is always ser."* | Starting Session 4; every occurrence |
| *¿Dónde eres?* (ser for location) | Explain in Session 3: "Where you are right now is always *estar* — *¿Dónde estás?*" | Session 3 first occurrence; correct after |
| *Él está aburrido.* when meaning "He is a boring person" | "Wait — is he boring (his personality) or is he bored (right now)? *Es aburrido* = boring person. *Está aburrido* = bored right now." | Session 4 meaning-changing block |
| Missing gender agreement (*estoy cansado* for female speaker) | "*Cansada* — you need the -a. The verb never changes for gender; the adjective does." | Every occurrence |
| Confusion about *vosotros* in the estar table | "If you're using Latin American Spanish, you'll use *ustedes están* — not *vosotros estáis*. Same as with ser." | On first occurrence of *estáis* |

## 7. Instructor Language to Use

| Moment | What to say |
|---|---|
| Introducing *estar* | *"You already know ser — that tells us what things are. Estar tells us how things are right now — or where they are."* |
| Core ser/estar frame for students | *Estar* = how you feel or where you are — changeable right now. *Ser* = who you are or where you're from — your identity. |
| On *soy cansado* error | *"In Spanish, how you feel is always estar. There are no exceptions for emotions or temporary states."* |
| For meaning-changing adjectives | *"Some adjectives mean one thing with ser and something completely different with estar. This is not an error — it is the system working correctly."* |
| On the reference handout | *"This handout is not cheating. It is there for when you are unsure — use it. That is what it is for."* |
| Before the group conversation (S4) | *"You now have two of the most important verbs in Spanish. I want each of you to use both — ser and estar — in this conversation."* |
| Student uses wrong verb | Correct it cleanly: "*Estoy* cansado, not *soy* cansado — try it again." One attempt, then move on. |
| Edge case question | *"Great question — that is a Week 4 topic. For now, use the rule we practiced today."* |

## 8. Homework and Between-Session Reminder

| | What students do |
|---|---|
| **After Session 3** | *Estar* × 3 from memory; write 5 sentences using *estar* (emotion + state + location × 3); ser/estar identification preview (6 sentence pairs) |
| **After Session 4** | Ser vs. estar practice (10 fill-ins); meaning-changing adjectives (5 sentences); write 5–7 sentence description of a real person using both *ser* and *estar* at least twice each; say it from memory; answer 3 spoken questions out loud |
| **Before Week 3** | Remind students at end of Session 4: "Next session starts with a quick check on *ser* and *estar* — same format as last time." |

**Ser/estar reference handout:** Filed at `reference-ser-vs-estar.md`. Distribute at end of Session 3 or start of Session 4. Students should have it in hand during the meaning-changing adjective block. It is a reference aid — not a memorization sheet. Do not test what is on the handout before students have had a session to use it.

**Formative check:** Filed at `week-02/week-02-formative-check.md`. Administer at the **start of Session 5** (first session of Week 3). Takes 6–8 minutes. Not graded.

**If the group conversation had to move to homework** (Session 4 ran long): ask each student to record a 30-second voice clip using *ser* and *estar* in the same exchange, and share before Session 5.

## 9. Ready for Week 3

A student is ready to move into Week 3 when they can:

- [ ] Conjugate *estar* in all six forms without looking at a table
- [ ] Use *estar* correctly for emotions, physical states, and location
- [ ] Use *ser* correctly for origin, identity, and profession
- [ ] Recognize (not necessarily produce) at least 2 of the 4 meaning-changing adjectives

**Accent marks and pronunciation are not a gate for Week 3. Verb choice is.**

**On ongoing errors:** Ser vs. estar mistakes are expected throughout the A1 course. The formative check confirms students have a working framework — not perfect accuracy. If most students are still using ser for emotions (*soy cansado*), add a 5-minute review to open Sessions 5 and 6 before introducing new Week 3 content. This error pattern is foundational and worth holding before compounding it.
