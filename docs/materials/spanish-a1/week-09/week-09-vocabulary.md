# Spanish A1 — Week 9 Vocabulary
## Plans and Obligations

## Ir a + Infinitivo — The Near Future

*Ir a + infinitivo* is the most common way to talk about what you are going to do in Spanish. Use the conjugated form of *ir* + *a* + any infinitive.

| Structure | Example | English |
|---|---|---|
| *voy a + inf* | *Voy a estudiar esta tarde.* | I'm going to study this afternoon. |
| *vas a + inf* | *¿Vas a llamar a tu madre esta noche?* | Are you going to call your mum tonight? |
| *va a + inf* | *Ella va a viajar la semana que viene.* | She's going to travel next week. |
| *vamos a + inf* | *Vamos a cenar juntos.* | We're going to have dinner together. |
| *vais a + inf* | *¿Vais a visitar Madrid?* | Are you (all) going to visit Madrid? |
| *van a + inf* | *Van a coger el tren.* | They're going to take the train. |

**Note:** *Ir* conjugation is already known from Week 5 (*voy/vas/va/vamos/vais/van*). Week 9 simply adds *a + infinitivo* after it.

**Negation:** *No* goes before *voy/vas/va...*:
- *No voy a trabajar mañana.* — I'm not going to work tomorrow.
- *No va a venir.* — He/she is not going to come.

## Pensar + Infinitivo — Intention

*Pensar + infinitivo* expresses intention — what you're planning or intending to do. It is slightly softer than *ir a*, which is more definite.

| Pronoun | Pensar (e→ie) | Example |
|---|---|---|
| yo | **pienso** | *Pienso llamarle mañana.* — I'm planning to call him tomorrow. |
| tú | **piensas** | *¿Qué piensas hacer este fin de semana?* — What are you thinking of doing? |
| él/ella | **piensa** | *Ella piensa estudiar en París.* — She's thinking of studying in Paris. |
| nosotros | **pensamos** | *Pensamos ir a la playa.* — We're planning to go to the beach. |
| vosotros | **pensáis** | *¿Pensáis quedaros en casa?* — Are you thinking of staying at home? |
| ellos/ellas | **piensan** | *Piensan viajar el mes que viene.* — They're planning to travel next month. |

**Stem change:** *pensar* has an *e→ie* change in all forms except *nosotros/vosotros*: *pienso, piensas, piensa, pensamos, pensáis, piensan.* The same pattern as *querer* (Week 6).

## Tener Que + Infinitivo — Personal Obligation

*Tener que* = to have to (personal, specific obligation). Students already know *tener* from Week 4.

| Pronoun | Tener que | Example |
|---|---|---|
| yo | **tengo que** | *Tengo que trabajar mañana.* — I have to work tomorrow. |
| tú | **tienes que** | *Tienes que llamar al médico.* — You need to call the doctor. |
| él/ella | **tiene que** | *Tiene que salir a las 8h.* — He/she has to leave at 8. |
| nosotros | **tenemos que** | *Tenemos que terminar esta noche.* — We need to finish tonight. |
| vosotros | **tenéis que** | *Tenéis que reservar con antelación.* — You (all) must book in advance. |
| ellos/ellas | **tienen que** | *Tienen que trabajar el fin de semana.* — They have to work weekends. |

---

## Hay Que + Infinitivo — General/Impersonal Obligation

*Hay que* = one has to / you have to (general, impersonal — no specific person). It never changes form.

| Expression | Example | English |
|---|---|---|
| *hay que + inf* | *Hay que estudiar para el examen.* | You have to study for the exam. (= one must) |
| *hay que + inf* | *Hay que reservar con antelación.* | You need to book in advance. |
| *hay que + inf* | *Hay que practicar todos los días.* | You have to practise every day. |

**Contrast:** *Tengo que estudiar* = I have to study (personal obligation on ME). *Hay que estudiar* = One must study / It's necessary to study (general rule).

## Poder + Infinitivo — Ability and Permission

*Poder* was introduced in Week 6. In Week 9 it is used to express ability, possibility, and what you can or can't do in plans.

| Pronoun | Poder | Example |
|---|---|---|
| yo | **puedo** | *Puedo quedar contigo a las 18h.* — I can meet you at 6pm. |
| tú | **puedes** | *¿Puedes venir mañana?* — Can you come tomorrow? |
| él/ella | **puede** | *No puede venir esta noche.* — She can't come tonight. |
| nosotros | **podemos** | *Podemos reservar por internet.* — We can book online. |
| vosotros | **podéis** | *¿Podéis pagar con tarjeta?* — Can you pay by card? |
| ellos/ellas | **pueden** | *Pueden llegar a las 20h.* — They can arrive at 8pm. |

## Three Structures in Combination — Plans and Constraints

The key skill of Week 9 is combining all three to express what you have to do, want to do, and can or can't do:

| Pattern | Example | English |
|---|---|---|
| *tener que* + *pero* + *querer* | *Tengo que trabajar, pero quiero ir al cine.* | I have to work, but I want to go to the cinema. |
| *querer* + *pero* + *no poder* | *Quiero venir, pero no puedo.* | I want to come, but I can't. |
| *poder* + *si* | *Puedo ir si quieres.* | I can go if you like. |
| *hay que* + context | *Hay que reservar — el restaurante se llena.* | You need to book — the restaurant fills up. |
| *ir a* + *tener que* | *Voy a tener que cancelar.* | I'm going to have to cancel. |

|---|---|

## Pronunciation Notes

| Form | Notes |
|---|---|
| *tengo que* | /ˈteŋgo ke/ — "tengo" not "tenco"; quick *que* after |
| *hay que* | /ai ke/ — *hay* is one syllable; *que* is unstressed |
| *pienso* | /ˈpjenso/ — *pie-* has a glide, like English "piano" start |
| *la semana que viene* | six syllables — students tend to drop *que*; keep it |
| *no puedo* | /no ˈpweðo/ — *pue-* has a glide; don't say *no puede* for yo |
