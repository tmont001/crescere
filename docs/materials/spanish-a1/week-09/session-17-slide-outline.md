# Spanish A1 — Session 17 Slide Outline
## Near Future: Ir a + Infinitivo

**Session length:** 50 minutes (on plan) — can run 60 min for slower cohorts
**Session-end goal:** Every student can describe 4–5 upcoming plans in connected sentences, using *ir a + infinitivo* with accurate future time expressions.

> **Pacing note:** The week-plan speaking round (Slide 6) is non-negotiable. Every student speaks before the session ends. Compress the *pensar* block (Slide 5) to 3 minutes or move it entirely to extension. *Ir* conjugation is new this session — build the paradigm on the board before moving to *ir a + infinitivo*. The time expressions and planning vocabulary need the most drilling time after that.

---

## Core 60-Minute Plan

### Slide 1 — Warm-Up: Week 8 Close (5 min)
**Purpose:** Close the Week 8 loop; activate irregular verbs from Session 16.
**Teacher notes:**
- Ask each student one sentence from Week 8 neighborhood context: *"¿Qué haces normalmente los fines de semana?"* (using *hacer* from Session 16)
- If Week 8 formative check showed widespread *saber/conocer* errors: one 30-second group reminder. No re-teaching.
- Brief preview: "This week we talk about the future — what you're going to do, when, and with whom."
**Student activity:** One *yo hago* sentence each.

---

### Slide 2 — Ir a + Infinitivo: Structure (8 min)
**Purpose:** Students understand the structure and can produce it immediately.
**Teacher notes:**
- Introduce ir conjugation first — it is new this session:
  - Write on the board: voy / vas / va / vamos / vais / van
  - Drill chorally; note that the forms look nothing like the infinitive ir
- Then build the structure step by step:
  1. *Voy al banco.* — going to a place (using the ir conjugation just learned)
  2. *Voy a trabajar mañana.* — going to do something (new use of ir)
  3. "Same ir conjugation — the only new thing: *a* + infinitive of what you're going to do."
- Full structure: *ir (conjugado) + a + infinitivo*
- Write all 6 forms with an example infinitive:
  - *voy a trabajar, vas a llamar, va a salir, vamos a cenar, vais a viajar, van a tomar*
- Negation immediately: *No voy a trabajar mañana.* — *no* before the conjugated *ir.*
- Quick transformation drill: each student turns a present-tense sentence into a near-future sentence.
  - *Trabajo hoy.* → *Voy a trabajar mañana.*
  - *Llama a su madre.* → *Va a llamar a su madre esta noche.*
**Student activity:** Transform 4 present-tense prompts into near-future sentences.

---

### Slide 3 — Future Time Expressions (8 min)
**Purpose:** Students learn 8 future time expressions and can attach them to plans naturally.
**Teacher notes:**
- Introduce time expressions in pairs:
  - *esta tarde / esta noche / este fin de semana* — this afternoon / tonight / this weekend
  - *mañana / mañana por la mañana* — tomorrow / tomorrow morning
  - *la semana que viene / el mes que viene / el año que viene* — next week / next month / next year
  - *en dos días / pronto* — in two days / soon
- After each pair: students produce one *voy a + inf + time expression* sentence.
- Quick slot-filling drill: instructor gives a verb + a time expression; student produces a sentence.
  - *salir / esta noche* → *Voy a salir esta noche.*
  - *llamar / mañana por la mañana* → *Voy a llamar mañana por la mañana.*
  - *viajar / el año que viene* → *Voy a viajar el año que viene.*
**Student activity:** Slot-filling drill (5 items); then each student gives one true plan with a time expression.

---

### Slide 4 — Planning Vocabulary (7 min)
**Purpose:** Students acquire event and activity vocabulary needed for the speaking round.
**Teacher notes:**
- Introduce in context, not as a list:
  - Events: *una cita, una reunión, una fiesta, una cena, un viaje, las vacaciones, un concierto*
  - Actions: *trabajar, estudiar, viajar, quedar con amigos, cocinar, volver a casa, reservar, cancelar*
- After each group: students produce one *voy a + event/action* sentence.
- Quick question drill: *"¿Qué vas a hacer esta noche?"* — 3 students answer.
**Student activity:** Give one plan using event or activity vocabulary.

---

### Slide 5 — Pensar + Infinitivo (5 min)
**Purpose:** Students learn an alternative for expressing intention (softer than *ir a*).
**Teacher notes:**
- Brief introduction: "Sometimes a plan is not 100% confirmed — it's more of an intention. For this, Spanish has *pensar + infinitivo.*"
- *Pienso llamarle mañana.* — I'm thinking of / planning to call him tomorrow.
- Stem change: e→ie in yo/tú/él/ellos: *pienso, piensas, piensa / pensamos, pensáis, piensan.*
- Quick comparison:
  - *Voy a ir al gimnasio mañana.* — fairly definite
  - *Pienso ir al gimnasio mañana.* — more of an intention, maybe less certain
- This distinction is subtle and not essential at A1 level. Present it briefly and move on.
**Student activity:** One *pienso + infinitivo* sentence each.

---

### Slide 6 — Speaking Round: My Week's Plans (12 min)
**Purpose:** Every student describes 4–5 upcoming plans in connected sentences.
**Teacher notes:**
- Each student speaks for 60–90 seconds. Notes allowed for vocabulary; *ir a + infinitivo* and time expressions from memory.
- Minimum required elements per student:
  - [ ] At least 3 *voy a + infinitivo* sentences
  - [ ] At least 2 different time expressions
  - [ ] At least 1 negative plan (*no voy a...*)
- Correct one item per student. Common targets: missing *a* (*voy trabajar* → *voy a trabajar*); wrong *ir* form (*tú voy* → *tú vas*); present tense used where future is intended.
- After all students: note 2 patterns across the group.
**Student activity:** Each student delivers a 4–5 sentence week plan.

---

### Slide 7 — Wrap-Up (5 min)
**Purpose:** Assign homework; preview Session 18.
**Teacher notes:**
- Preview Session 18: "Next session we add two essential structures for explaining constraints: *tener que* (I have to) and *hay que* (one must), and we review *poder* (I can). Combined with today, you'll be able to have a full planning conversation — proposing, accepting, declining, negotiating."
- Homework: written week plan + 3 planning questions.
**Student activity:** Note homework.

## Extension for Fast Cohorts

- **Pienso + inf extended:** *"¿Qué piensas hacer en el futuro?"* Students give one long-term plan using *pienso.* Contrast with *voy a* (this week vs. someday).
- **Reported plans:** *"Mi amigo va a viajar la semana que viene."* Students describe someone else's plans in 3rd person.
- **Question chains:** Students ask each other 3 planning questions in sequence; answer with *ir a + inf.*
- **¿Qué planes tienes?** — alternative phrasing; students answer using *tengo planes de + inf* as a bonus structure.
