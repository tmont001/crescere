# Spanish A1 — Week 9 Formative Check
## Plans and Obligations

**When to administer:** At the start of Session 19, or as a closing check at the end of Session 18.
**Format:** Oral section first (5 prompts); written section second (5 prompts); short role play (1 exchange).
**Purpose:** Diagnostic tool. Not a grade. Informs whether the cohort is ready to progress to Week 10 content (*jugar a / hacer + activity*, hobbies and leisure) or needs reinforcement of Week 9 structures.

---

## Oral Prompts (5 prompts — instructor reads aloud)

Administer to each student individually or in round-robin. Allow 10–15 seconds per prompt.

**Prompt 1:** "Tell me two plans you have for this week."
*Target:* Two *voy a + infinitivo* sentences, each with a different time expression.
*Minimum pass:* Both sentences have a correct *ir* form + *a* + infinitive. One time expression correct.

**Prompt 2:** "Tell me one thing you have to do this week that you don't particularly want to do."
*Target:* *Tengo que + inf... pero no quiero / no me apetece.* Or simply a *tengo que* sentence with a clear personal obligation.
*Minimum pass:* *Tener que* form correct (*tengo que*, not *yo tiene que* or *tengo de*).

**Prompt 3:** "Tell me something you want to do but can't do this week."
*Target:* *Quiero + inf, pero no puedo / tengo que + inf.*
*Minimum pass:* At least two of the three structures used correctly.

**Prompt 4:** "What is *hay que*? Give me one example."
*Target:* Student explains it's impersonal (general rule, no specific person) and gives one example: *Hay que estudiar / Hay que reservar con antelación.*
*Watch for:* Adding a subject (*yo hay que* — impossible); confusing with *tener que.*

**Prompt 5 — Mini role play:** Instructor says: *"Voy a organizar una cena el viernes por la noche — ¿puedes venir?"* Student must respond with a natural planning exchange: accept, decline with a reason, or propose an alternative.
*Target:* Student uses at least one of *tener que/poder/querer* in their response. *"El viernes no puedo — tengo que trabajar. ¡El sábado estoy libre!"*
*Minimum pass:* Natural response that includes a reason or alternative, however brief.

---

## Written Prompts (5 items — students write independently)

**Item 1:** Write one sentence about what you are going to do tomorrow.
*Expected:* *Voy a + infinitivo + tomorrow time expression.* E.g., *Voy a ver a mis amigos mañana por la tarde.*
*Watch for:* Missing *a* (*voy trabajar* → *voy a trabajar*); conjugated verb instead of infinitive (*voy a trabajo*).

**Item 2:** Translate: "She has to leave at 8 — she has a meeting."
*Expected:* *Tiene que salir a las 8 — tiene una reunión.*
*Watch for:* *Tiene que de salir* (→ *tiene que salir* — no extra preposition); *ella tiene que* vs. *ella tener que.*

**Item 3:** Fill in the blank: *___________ estudiar mucho para aprender un idioma.* (general rule)
*Expected:* *Hay que*
*Watch for:* *Tengo que* (would make it personal, not general); *Debes* (not introduced yet for this context).

**Item 4:** Write one sentence using *tengo que* AND one using *no puedo* — make them about the same situation.
*Expected:* *Tengo que trabajar el sábado. No puedo quedar este fin de semana.* (or similar connected pair)
*Watch for:* *No puedo* with wrong person (the blank says *I* — watch for *no puede*).

**Item 5:** Write 3–4 sentences proposing a plan to someone — include a time, a place, and a question asking if they can come.
*Expected:* *Voy a [activity] [time]. ¿Quieres venir? Quedamos a las [time] delante de [place]. ¿Puedes?*
*Minimum pass:* *Ir a + inf* used correctly; a question about availability present.

---

## Scoring Decision Table

| Student output | Decision |
|---|---|
| All 5 oral prompts pass + 4–5 written correct | Ready to move on to Week 10 |
| 4 oral pass + 3–4 written correct | Ready to move on — monitor modal use in Week 10 |
| *Ir a + infinitivo* correct but *tener que* forms weak | Move to Week 10 — give *tener que* as warm-up drill in Session 19 |
| *Hay que* vs. *tener que* not yet clear | Move to Week 10 — give one contrast drill in Session 19 warm-up |
| Time expressions inconsistent | Move to Week 10 — assign time expression practice as homework |
| Fewer than 3 oral prompts pass OR fewer than 3 written correct | Flag for instructor follow-up; do not delay Week 10 for whole cohort |

---

## Ready to Move On

Before beginning Week 10 (*jugar a / hacer + activity*, frequency adverbs), a student should be able to:
- [ ] Produce *ir a + infinitivo* automatically for 4+ different verbs
- [ ] Use at least 3 future time expressions accurately
- [ ] Use *tener que* correctly in *yo, tú, él/ella* forms
- [ ] Say *no puedo* as an automatic response when declining
- [ ] Distinguish *tener que* (personal) from *hay que* (general) in most cases
- [ ] Combine at least two structures in a single planning exchange
- [ ] Conduct a minimal planning role play: propose → decline → negotiate

---

## Not a Gate

The following items are NOT required for progression to Week 10:
- Full *poder* mastery in all 6 forms (gaps in *vosotros podéis* are acceptable)
- *Pensar + infinitivo* distinction from *ir a* (extension at A1)
- *Deber + infinitivo* (this comes in Week 11 for doctor advice context)
- Conditional forms (*podrías, deberías*) — A2 territory
- *Hay que* in all contexts — students who know it for general rules are on track
- Written exchange beyond 4–5 sentences
