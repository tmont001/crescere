# Spanish A1 — Session 18 Slide Outline
## Obligation and Ability: Tener Que, Hay Que, Poder

**Session length:** 50 minutes (on plan) — can run 60 min for slower cohorts
**Session-end goal:** Every student can conduct a short planning role play — proposing, declining, and negotiating — using *ir a + infinitivo, tener que, hay que,* and *poder* in combination.

> **Pacing note:** The scheduling role play (Slide 7) is non-negotiable. Every student participates in at least one exchange before the session ends. Compress the *hay que* block (Slide 4, to 3 min) and the *poder* review (Slide 5, to 2 min) before cutting Slide 7.

---

## Core Plan

### Slide 1 — Warm-Up: Ir a + Infinitivo Recall (5 min)
**Purpose:** Re-activate Session 17 material before adding the obligation/ability layer.
**Teacher notes:**
- Each student gives one sentence from their homework week plan. Correct *ir a + infinitivo* form and time expression only.
- Preview: "Today we add two new structures for explaining constraints: *tener que* (I have to) and *hay que* (one must). Plus we review *poder* (I can). With these, you can explain why you can or can't do something — and negotiate."
**Student activity:** One *voy a + inf + time expression* sentence each.

---

### Slide 2 — Tener Que + Infinitivo (8 min)
**Purpose:** Students learn and can use *tener que* to express personal obligation.
**Teacher notes:**
- Students already know *tener* from Week 4. Introduce *tener que* as an extension: *tener + que + infinitivo.*
- Write the full structure with all 6 persons:

  | yo | tú | él/ella | nosotros | vosotros | ellos/ellas |
  |---|---|---|---|---|---|
  | tengo que | tienes que | tiene que | tenemos que | tenéis que | tienen que |

- Three examples with different contexts:
  - *Tengo que trabajar mañana.* — I have to work tomorrow. (work obligation)
  - *Tienes que llamar al médico.* — You need to call the doctor. (advice/necessity)
  - *Tenemos que salir a las 8h.* — We have to leave at 8. (time constraint)
- Key warning: *Tengo que trabajar*, NOT *Tengo que de trabajar.* No extra preposition between *que* and the infinitive.
- Quick round: each student gives one *tengo que + infinitivo* sentence about their week.
**Student activity:** Give one *tengo que* sentence.

---

### Slide 3 — Tener Que in Contrast with Ir a + Infinitivo (5 min)
**Purpose:** Students distinguish obligation from neutral future and use both in context.
**Teacher notes:**
- Contrast drill — students decide which is more natural:
  - *"I'm going to leave at 8"* (neutral plan) → *Voy a salir a las 8.*
  - *"I have to leave at 8"* (obligation/reason) → *Tengo que salir a las 8.*
  - Both grammatically fine — the difference is whether obligation is implied.
- Practical planning phrase: *Voy a tener que cancelar.* — I'm going to have to cancel. (combining *ir a + tener que + infinitivo*).
- Quick drill: give a scenario; students produce either *voy a* or *tengo que.*
  - "You decided to cook dinner tonight." → *Voy a cocinar esta noche.*
  - "You have a meeting at 9 and can't be late." → *Tengo una reunión a las 9 — tengo que salir ya.*
**Student activity:** Classify 4 scenarios as *voy a* or *tengo que.*

---

### Slide 4 — Hay Que + Infinitivo (5 min)
**Purpose:** Students learn the impersonal obligation structure and can contrast it with *tener que.*
**Teacher notes:**
- *Hay que* is impersonal — no subject, never changes form.
- *Hay que + infinitivo* = one must / you have to / it's necessary to.
- Examples:
  - *Hay que estudiar para el examen.* — You have to study for the exam. (general necessity)
  - *Hay que reservar con antelación.* — You need to book in advance. (general rule)
  - *Hay que llegar puntual.* — One must arrive on time.
- Key contrast with *tener que*:
  - *Tengo que estudiar.* — I personally have to study.
  - *Hay que estudiar.* — It's necessary to study (general).
- Quick drill: 3 sentences — students decide if *hay que* or *tener que* fits better.
  - "You can't smoke here — it's the rule." → *Hay que salir a fumar.*
  - "You need to send that email today — it's your job." → *Tengo que mandar ese correo hoy.*
**Student activity:** Three *hay que* vs. *tener que* decisions.

---

### Slide 5 — Poder + Infinitivo Review (5 min)
**Purpose:** Students recall *poder* for planning conversations, especially *no puedo.*
**Teacher notes:**
- Not a re-teach. Students have *poder* from Week 6. Focus on *no puedo* as a planning response and *puedo* for suggesting/accepting.
- Quick form recall: *puedo, puedes, puede, podemos, podéis, pueden.* Students supply forms.
- Key planning uses:
  - *Puedo quedar contigo a las 18h.* — I can meet you at 6pm.
  - *No puedo — tengo una reunión.* — I can't — I have a meeting.
  - *¿Puedes venir mañana?* — Can you come tomorrow?
- Quick drill: 3 students give one *puedo/no puedo* sentence in a planning context.
**Student activity:** Three *poder* sentences.

---

### Slide 6 — Combining the Structures (10 min)
**Purpose:** Students combine *tener que, hay que, poder* in realistic planning exchanges.
**Teacher notes:**
- Introduce the four core patterns with examples:
  1. *Quiero venir, pero tengo que trabajar.* — I want to come, but I have to work.
  2. *Quiero venir, pero no puedo.* — I want to come, but I can't.
  3. *Puedo ir si quieres.* — I can go if you like.
  4. *Voy a tener que cancelar — lo siento mucho.* — I'm going to have to cancel — I'm really sorry.
- Guided scenario drill: instructor gives a situation; students produce the full response.
  - Situation: "You want to see friends Saturday but you have to work."
    → *Quiero ver a mis amigos el sábado, pero tengo que trabajar.*
  - Situation: "You can't come Thursday — you have a meeting."
    → *No puedo venir el jueves — tengo una reunión.*
  - Situation: "You need to cancel your dinner plans."
    → *Voy a tener que cancelar la cena — lo siento mucho.*
**Student activity:** Respond to 3 guided scenarios using the combining patterns.

---

### Slide 7 — Role Play: Make a Plan (12 min)
**Purpose:** Every student participates in a planning exchange — proposing, declining, and negotiating.
**Teacher notes:**
- Role play setup: Student A proposes a plan; Student B declines with a reason; Student A suggests an alternative; Student B accepts or negotiates further.
- Minimum script for each role (students improvise, not read):
  - A proposes: *"¿Quieres [activity] [time expression]?"*
  - B declines: *"Quiero, pero [tengo que/no puedo] — [reason]. ¿Puedes [alternative time]?"*
  - A accepts or offers another option: *"Sin problema. Entonces [alternative plan]."*
  - B confirms: *"¡Perfecto! ¿Quedamos a las [time] delante de [place]?"*
- Each student plays both roles across two exchanges (instructor-student first, then student-student if pairs allow).
- Correct one item per student. Common targets: *tengo que de trabajar* (→ *tengo que trabajar*); using *hay que* with a personal subject (*yo hay que* — impossible; use *tengo que*).
- After all students: note 2 patterns across the group.
**Student activity:** Each student participates in at least one full planning exchange.

---

### Slide 8 — Wrap-Up: Week 9 Close (5 min)
**Purpose:** Assign homework; preview Week 10.
**Teacher notes:**
- Preview Week 10: "Next week we talk about hobbies and leisure — *jugar a* for sports and games, *hacer* for activities. You'll combine all of this with your new planning language."
- Homework: written planning note + verb structures.
- Remind students: the Week 9 formative check will ask them to produce *tener que, hay que, poder* in context and conduct a planning exchange.
**Student activity:** Note homework.

## Cut First If Running Behind

1. **Slide 3 (tener que vs. ir a contrast):** Reduce to 2 min; just give the *voy a tener que cancelar* phrase.
2. **Slides 4–5 (hay que/poder):** Slide 4 to 2 min (one example + quick drill); Slide 5 to 2 min.
3. **Slide 6 (combining):** Give just 2 patterns instead of 4; students produce only 2 scenarios.
4. Do not cut Slide 7 (role play).

## Extension for Fast Cohorts

- **Deber + infinitivo:** Introduce *deber* as an alternative to *tener que* for stronger moral obligation: *Debes descansar* — you should rest. (Full *deber* teaching comes in Week 11 for doctor advice context per curriculum.)
- **¿Qué tenemos que hacer?** — group obligation planning: the class must plan a hypothetical trip using *hay que* + *tenemos que.*
- **Counter-offer role play:** More complex negotiation: each student must propose an alternative after declining and reach a confirmed plan.
- **¿Cuándo puedes?** — extended availability questions with multiple time options.
