# Spanish A1 — Session 24 Slide Outline
## Final Performance and Course Close

**Session length:** 60 minutes (on plan — adjust by cohort size per instructor notes)
**Session-end goal:** Every student has completed Part A (spoken role play) and Part B (written message or confirmed take-home); every student has received feedback; every student knows their next step; the course ends on a high.

> **Pacing note:** The spoken role plays (Slide 2) are non-negotiable. If cohort size is 5+, move Part B to take-home. The A2 preview and course close are brief but essential — do not skip them.

---

## Core Plan

### Slide 1 — Warm-Up: Last Check-In (5 min)
**Purpose:** Low-stakes entry; settle energy before the role plays.
**Teacher notes:**
- Ask each student: *"¿Qué recuerdas de tu primera clase de español?"*
- Accept anything — a word, a phrase, a feeling. This is a reflection moment.
- Brief framing: *"Hoy vais a mostrar todo lo que sabéis. Es una conversación — no un examen. Adelante."*
- Do not do a grammar review. Do not drill. The preparation is done.
**Student activity:** Each student gives one memory from Week 1 or the course.

---

### Slide 2 — Final Role Plays: Part A (20–35 min depending on cohort) ★ NON-NEGOTIABLE
**Purpose:** Every student completes the spoken final performance.
**Teacher notes:**

**Process:**
1. Set the scene: *"Imagina que estamos en un café en Madrid / Buenos Aires / Ciudad de México. Acabamos de sentarnos al lado. Empiezo yo."*
2. Open: *"¡Hola! ¿Eres de aquí?"*
3. Role play runs for 5–7 minutes.
4. Close naturally: *"Ha sido un placer hablar contigo. ¡Hasta pronto!"*
5. Note 2–3 specific items for feedback (not more).

**Per-student timing:**
- 2–3 min transition
- 5–7 min role play
- 1 min feedback note
- Total per student: 8–11 min

**Ser/estar targeting during the role play:**
The instructor should ask at least one question that naturally requires *ser* and one that requires *estar*:
- *"¿De dónde eres?"* → *ser* (origin)
- *"¿Cómo estás hoy?"* → *estar* (current state)
- *"¿Dónde está el banco más cercano?"* → *estar* (location)

**If a student freezes:**
- Wait 4 seconds. Then: *"¿Cómo te llamas?"*
- If still frozen: *"¿Eres de aquí?"*

**If a student rushes and finishes at 3 minutes:**
- Add: *"¿Cuánto tiempo llevas viviendo aquí?"* (activates *llevar*)
- Or: *"¿Qué te gusta hacer los fines de semana?"* (activates gustar and hobbies)

**Required elements to look for:**
- [ ] *Ser* used correctly (identity, origin, or profession)
- [ ] *Estar* used correctly (state, emotion, or location)
- [ ] *Ir a + infinitivo* for a plan
- [ ] At least one question produced by the student
- [ ] At least one obligation or ability structure (*tener que, poder*)

**Student activity:** Each student completes their 5–7 minute role play in sequence.

---

### Slide 3 — Written Message: Part B (12–15 min)
**Purpose:** Students complete the written message component.
**Teacher notes:**
- **Option 1 (preferred for cohorts of 1–4):** Students write in session — 12–15 minutes, independently. Instructor reviews role play notes.
- **Option 2 (cohorts of 5+):** Take-home, submitted before the next contact. Written feedback within one week.
- If in-session: students may use the phrase bank. No previously written draft.
**Student activity:** Write Part B independently (if in-session).

---

### Slide 4 — Feedback: What You Did Well (6–8 min)
**Purpose:** Every student receives specific, positive, forward-looking feedback.
**Teacher notes:**
- Two parts:
  1. **Group feedback:** Two or three things the whole cohort did well. Name the patterns — *"Todos vosotros / ustedes usasteis 'ir a + infinitivo' de forma muy natural."* Celebrate these.
  2. **Individual feedback:** One thing per student that worked; one thing to watch in A2. 30–45 seconds per person. **Ser/estar is the most likely item to mention for Spanish.**
- Frame forward: *"Para el A2..."* not *"Hiciste un error cuando..."*
- Written Part B feedback: delivered later by message or email.
**Student activity:** Students receive and note their individual feedback.

---

### Slide 5 — A2 Preview (5–8 min)
**Purpose:** Students understand what comes next and feel excited.
**Teacher notes:**
- Three to four sentences only. Do not teach content:
  - *"En A1 podéis hablar del presente y del futuro próximo. Os podéis presentar, hacer planes, orientaros en una ciudad."*
  - *"En A2 vais a aprender a hablar del pasado. ¿Qué hicisteis ayer? ¿Qué hacíais de niños? El pretérito y el imperfecto — eso lo cambia todo."*
  - *"Con A2, las conversaciones se vuelven mucho más ricas. Ya no solo habláis de lo que está pasando ahora — habláis de vuestra vida entera."*
- Concrete next step: *"Escuchad español cinco minutos al día. Un podcast, una serie, un artículo fácil. Eso es lo más importante entre A1 y A2."*
- **Spanish-specific preview:** *"En A2, la distinción ser/estar vuelve a aparecer con más detalles — incluyendo con participios y en el pasado. Vuestro trabajo en A1 es la base perfecta."*
**Student activity:** Students note one thing they are looking forward to learning in A2.

---

### Slide 6 — Course Close and Celebration (5 min)
**Purpose:** Mark the achievement. End on a high.
**Teacher notes:**
- Read aloud a summary of what students can now do in Spanish — not as a list but as real-world abilities: *"Podéis presentaros en español. Podéis pedir en un café, hacer compras, pedir ayuda en una farmacia, dar direcciones, describir vuestra vida. Podéis comunicaros en español. Eso es real."*
- *"Hace doce semanas no sabíais español. Ahora lo sabéis. No es perfecto — pero es vuestro."*
- Optional: each student says one sentence about what they are most proud of — in Spanish.
- Close: *"Gracias por aprender español conmigo. ¡Hasta pronto — en español!"*
**Student activity:** Optional: one sentence each about what they are most proud of, in Spanish.

---

## Cut First If Running Behind

1. **Slide 5 (A2 preview):** Reduce to one sentence: name the pretérito; give one self-study recommendation.
2. **Slide 4 (feedback):** Move individual feedback entirely to post-session. Give only group feedback verbally.
3. **Slide 6 (celebration):** Reduce to the closing summary paragraph — 2 minutes.
4. Do NOT reduce Slide 2 (role plays) or Slide 3 (written message).

---

## Extension for Fast Cohorts / Ahead of Schedule

- **Longer role plays:** If a student is clearly comfortable, extend by asking more follow-up questions — family, neighbourhood in detail, *"¿Cuánto tiempo llevas haciendo...?"* — naturally activating *llevar* from Week 11.
- **Second Part B topic:** Students who finish writing early add a second paragraph — describing a place in a Spanish-speaking country they would like to visit.
- **A2 taster:** If time and energy permit, introduce one pretérito sentence as a teaser: *"Ayer comí una paella buenísima."* Ask students to guess what tense it is and what it means. Do not drill — just open the door.
