## Part 4 — Staying Active Between A1 and A2

The most common reason students lose ground between courses is inactivity, not forgetting. Five minutes of Spanish per day does more than five hours of cramming before A2 starts.

**Recommended daily habits (choose at least one):**
- **Read:** *"Español con Juan"* or *"News in Slow Spanish"* (podcast + transcript — very accessible at A1/A2 level)
- **Listen:** *"Coffee Break Spanish"* podcast — excellent A1/A2 episodes
- **Watch:** *"Extra en español"* (YouTube — a sitcom designed for learners); Spanish series with Spanish subtitles
- **Write:** Keep a one-sentence Spanish journal. Just one sentence per day: *"Hoy he comido pizza."* You will be using the pretérito naturally before A2 even begins.
- **App:** Anki with a deck for irregular verbs — focus on the ones you missed in your self-assessment

**The one-sentence journal is the single most useful recommendation.** It keeps Spanish present, builds writing fluency, and accidentally prepares you for A2 past tenses.

## Part 5 — The Ser/Estar Foundation

The ser/estar distinction is the most important concept in Spanish A1 and it continues throughout A2 and beyond. Before starting A2, make sure you feel secure in these common contexts:

| Context | Verb | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Identity, origin, profession | ser | *Soy médico. Soy de México.* |
| Permanent or essential characteristics | ser | *Es inteligente. El cielo es azul.* |
| States, emotions, conditions | estar | *Estoy cansado. Está triste.* |
| Location of people and things | estar | *Estoy en casa. El banco está enfrente.* |
| Meaning-changing adjectives | both | *Es aburrido* (he's boring) vs. *Está aburrido* (he's bored) |

Write 3 sentences in each category from your own life before starting A2. This is the single strongest preparation for the A2 course.

## Part 6 — Optional A2 Preparation

If you want to get a head start:

**Irregular verb review:**
In A2, these verbs will appear constantly in the pretérito. Make sure you know the full present tense of: *ser, estar, ir, tener, hacer, querer, poder, saber, conocer, decir, venir.* These same verbs will have irregular pretérito forms — knowing the present cold makes the pretérito easier to learn.

**Preview the pretérito (do not worry about mastering it):**
The pretérito indefinido regular endings: -ar verbs (*hablé, hablaste, habló, hablamos, hablasteis, hablaron*). For -er/-ir verbs (*comí, comiste, comió, comimos, comisteis, comieron*). In your one-sentence journal, try: *"Hoy comí en casa."* / *"Esta mañana fui al supermercado."* You will be practicing A2 grammar naturally.

**Cultural immersion:** Watch one Spanish-language film or series. *Money Heist / La casa de papel* is engaging and Spanish-produced; *Élite* is accessible; *Club de Cuervos* is a good Latin American option. Do not worry about understanding everything — let the language wash over you with subtitles.
