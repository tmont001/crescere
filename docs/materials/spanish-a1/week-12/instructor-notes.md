# Spanish A1 — Week 12 Instructor Notes

**Sessions:** 23 and 24
**Theme:** Review and Final Performance

---

## Week Objective

By the end of Week 12:
- Every student has completed the final performance (Part A: spoken role play, Part B: written message)
- Every student has received feedback on their performance
- Every student understands what A2 involves and has a concrete next step
- The course ends on a high note — students leave feeling capable, not tested

**No new major grammar this week.** The curriculum lists the following as "reinforced in Session 23": ser/estar with meaning-changing adjectives, reflexive verbs, ir a + infinitivo, tener que/hay que/poder, saber vs. conocer, doler, demonstratives. These are not re-taught — they surface naturally in practice scenarios.

---

## Final Performance Overview

See `week-12-final-performance.md` for the full specification. Summary:

| | Part A | Part B |
|---|---|---|
| **Format** | Spoken role play with instructor | Written message, independently produced |
| **Length** | 5–7 minutes | 8–10 sentences |
| **Content** | Meet someone (ser/estar), make a plan (ir a + inf), give directions, describe a place | Introduce yourself, describe routine, suggest plans |
| **Required structures** | Ser, estar, ir a + inf, ≥1 question, ≥1 obligation/ability | Same range; gustar/doler; reflexives |

**Spanish-specific note:** Ser/estar is the foundational distinction of this course (Week 2). The final performance must give students an opportunity to use both — the instructor should ask questions that naturally elicit both forms: *"¿De dónde eres?"* (ser) and *"¿Cómo estás hoy?"* / *"¿Dónde está la farmacia?"* (estar).

## Keeping the Final Performance Low-Pressure

- **Name it correctly:** "La performance final" — not "el examen." Framing matters.
- **Be explicit about success criteria:** Communication is success. Ser/estar distinction is noticed, not demanded perfectly.
- **Model communication repair in Session 23:** Deliberately use *"¿Cómo se dice...?"* during the model walk-through. Show this is normal.
- **Let students use their real lives:** Their real job, their real neighbourhood, their real weekend plans. The cognitive load of invention adds unnecessary anxiety.
- **No audience pressure:** For individual role plays, avoid having all students watch simultaneously unless the group has strong rapport.
- **Praise first, always:** Every feedback moment begins with a specific, named strength.

## Spanish-Specific Instruction Notes for Session 23

**Ser/estar rehearsal:** Dedicate 2 minutes of the scenario practice block specifically to ser/estar — one question per student that requires one ser answer and one estar answer. Example:
- *"¿Eres optimista?"* (ser — personality/characteristic)
- *"¿Estás cansado/a hoy?"* (estar — current state)
This ensures the distinction gets live practice before Session 24.

**Direction phrases display:** Before drill 3 in the Slide 5 scenario practice block, write on the board: *todo recto / a la derecha / a la izquierda / al lado de / enfrente de.* These phrases appear in the Week 12 vocabulary file and in the Session 23 homework, but may not have been drilled before Session 23 (see Week 8 instructor notes). Board support is appropriate in Session 23 — the goal is fluency in Session 24.

**Doler review (if needed):** If the Week 11 formative check showed *doler* was still shaky, add one *doler* sentence to the model role play scenario: the stranger mentions they have a sore foot and asks where the pharmacy is. This gives *doler* and directions in the same exchange — realistic and efficient.

**Saber vs. conocer (if time):** During the directions drill, ask: *"¿Conoces este barrio?"* or *"¿Sabes si hay un supermercado cerca?"* This activates the Week 8 distinction without making it a focus.

## Common Student Issues During Final Performance

| Issue | What to do |
|---|---|
| Student freezes at the start | Begin with: *"Hola, ¿cómo te llamas?"* — the easiest question. Wait. Don't rescue in under 4 seconds. |
| Student mixes up ser/estar on adjectives | Note it; do not correct mid-role play. Flag it in feedback: *"Estoy listo/a para A2 excepto..."* |
| Student says *yo gusto el fútbol* (gustar error) | Note it; do not correct mid-role play. This is a persistent A1 → A2 transition item. |
| Student switches to English | Gently stay in Spanish: *"En español, por favor — ¡tú puedes!"* Model the Spanish if needed. |
| Student rushes and finishes in 3 minutes | Extend with: *"¿Y tu barrio — te gusta? ¿Cuánto tiempo llevas viviendo allí?"* — activates *llevar* naturally. |
| Student produces a memorized script | Ask an off-script question: *"¿Tienes hermanos? ¿Cómo está tu familia?"* Test genuine production. |

## Feedback Guidance

**Structure of feedback:**
1. **What worked well** (2–3 specifics — name the structure or phrase)
2. **One thing to keep doing**
3. **One thing to watch in A2** (forward-framed, not a criticism)

**Example feedback:**
> *"Usaste 'ir a + infinitivo' de forma muy natural — 'voy a quedar con mis amigos el sábado.' Perfecto. Tu forma de recuperarte cuando no encontrabas la palabra fue exactamente lo que queremos ver. Para el A2: la distinción ser/estar con adjetivos — a veces *estoy cansado* se convirtió en *soy cansado.* Eso es exactamente lo que trabajaremos en A2."*

**Written feedback on Part B:** Return within a week. One short paragraph. Note what worked, one grammar item, one encouragement for A2.

## A2 Readiness Conversation

5–8 minutes. Structure:

1. **Acknowledge achievement:** Name what students can now do in real Spanish-speaking situations.
2. **Name what A1 makes possible:** Introductions, café, directions, shopping, plans, health situations.
3. **Name what A2 adds:** The past tenses — *pretérito indefinido* (what you did) and *imperfecto* (what things were like, what you used to do).
4. **Make it exciting:** *"En A2 vas a poder hablar de tu infancia, de un viaje que hiciste, de una película que viste. Las conversaciones van a ser mucho más ricas."*
5. **Concrete next step:** see homework file.

**Do not teach pretérito content in Session 24.** Preview only.
