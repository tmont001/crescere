# Spanish A1 — Session 23 Slide Outline
## Final Performance Preparation

**Session length:** 60 minutes (on plan)
**Session-end goal:** Every student has practiced all four role play topic areas, rehearsed one full scenario with a partner, drafted their written message, and knows exactly what to expect in Session 24.

> **Pacing note:** Pair rehearsal (Slide 6) is non-negotiable. Every student practices one full role-play scenario before the session ends. The ser/estar drill (embedded in Slide 5) is the Spanish-specific priority — make sure every student produces at least one *ser* and one *estar* sentence naturally before Session 24.

---

## Core Plan

### Slide 1 — Warm-Up: Course Journey (5 min)
**Purpose:** Positive re-entry; remind students of how far they have come.
**Teacher notes:**
- Ask each student: *"¿Cuál es tu frase favorita en español — algo que te sientes orgulloso/a de poder decir?"*
- Accept anything: a complex sentence, a funny phrase, a pronunciation they mastered.
- Acknowledge the Week 11 achievement: *"La semana pasada hicisteis una consulta médica en español. Eso fue lo más difícil del curso. Esta semana usáis todo el español que tenéis."*
- **Spanish-specific:** Mention ser/estar explicitly — *"Esta semana también es la oportunidad de mostrar que entendéis la diferencia entre ser y estar — el tema más importante del A1."*
**Student activity:** Each student shares one favourite phrase or sentence.

---

### Slide 2 — Final Performance Overview (8 min)
**Purpose:** Students understand exactly what will happen in Session 24.
**Teacher notes:**
- Walk through the final performance document together:
  - Part A: spoken role play, 5–7 min, four topic areas
  - Part B: written message, 8–10 sentences
  - What the instructor is looking for (communication, range, recovery, ser/estar accuracy)
  - Repair phrases they can use
- *"No hay nota. No es un examen. Es una demostración — vais a mostrar lo que sabéis hacer."*
- Answer questions about ser/estar in the role play: *"¿Qué uso — ser o estar?"* → *"Si es una característica permanente o tu identidad: ser. Si es cómo te encuentras o dónde estás ahora: estar."*
**Student activity:** Students note their two chosen repair phrases.

---

### Slide 3 — Model Role Play Walkthrough (10 min)
**Purpose:** Students see what good A1 Spanish role play looks and sounds like — including recovery moments.
**Teacher notes:**
- Instructor narrates and performs both sides of the role play, or reads through a scripted example with commentary.
- The model must include:
  - *Ser* for identity and *estar* for a current state or location — both natural
  - A plan using *ir a + infinitivo*
  - Directions
  - A brief description of home or neighbourhood
  - At least one moment of communication repair (*"¿Cómo se dice...?"*)
- After the model: *"¿Qué habéis notado? ¿Qué estructuras reconocisteis?"*
- Point out: the model is not perfect Spanish. It is fluent A1 Spanish.

**Model scenario:**
> Student meets a Spanish-speaking new arrival at a food market. They introduce themselves (*ser* for origin, profession; *estar* for current feeling/location), discover they both enjoy cooking, agree to visit a local restaurant together on Saturday, and the student gives directions from the market to the restaurant.

**Key structures to highlight as they appear:**
- *Soy de... / Soy cocinero/a* (ser — identity)
- *Estoy muy bien hoy / El restaurante está al lado de...* (estar — state / location)
- *Vamos a ir al restaurante el sábado* (ir a + infinitivo)
- Directions (prepositions, al contraction)

**Student activity:** Students note one sentence from the model they could use or adapt.

---

### Slide 4 — Model Written Message (5 min)
**Purpose:** Students see what an A1 written message looks like and understand the standard.
**Teacher notes:**
- Ask a student to read the example from the student-facing overview aloud.
- After reading: What structures appear? (*soy, estoy, me levanto, me gusta, voy a, ¿estás libre?*)
- Is it perfect? No. Is it clear and complete? Yes.
- Note: it uses *ser* and *estar* in different ways — *soy diseñador* (identity) and *estoy muy contento* (state). Point this out.
**Student activity:** Students note one structure they want to include in their own message.

---

### Slide 5 — Scenario Practice: Four Topic Areas (15 min)
**Purpose:** Students practice each of the four topic areas, with a ser/estar sub-drill embedded.
**Teacher notes:**
- Run as four short drills (3–4 min each):
  1. **Introductions + ser/estar:** Students introduce themselves — name, origin, profession (*ser*), plus one thing about how they feel or where they are right now (*estar*). Two *ser* sentences; one *estar* sentence minimum.
  2. **Making plans:** Propose an activity using *ir a + infinitivo* + negotiate a time.
  3. **Directions:** Instructor calls out a city place; student gives directions using prepositions and *al* contraction.
  4. **Describing a place:** 3–4 sentences about home or neighbourhood — mix *hay, está, son.*
- Correct one item per student per drill. **Priority target: ser/estar confusion on adjectives.**
- For small groups: instructor participates in each drill.
**Student activity:** Students participate in all four short drills.

---

### Slide 6 — Pair Rehearsal: Full Role Play Run-Through (10 min) ★ PROTECTED
**Purpose:** Every student practices one complete role play scenario from start to finish.
**Teacher notes:**
- Each student runs through the full scenario with the instructor (or with a partner).
- They choose Option A, B, or C from the final performance guide.
- This is not the final performance — it is a rehearsal. Mistakes welcome.
- Give one piece of specific feedback per student: something that worked + one thing to try in Session 24.
- **Spanish-specific:** In your feedback note whether ser/estar came up and whether it was used correctly. Flag before Session 24 if it was not.
**Student activity:** Each student completes one full role-play rehearsal.

---

### Slide 7 — Wrap-Up: Preparation Checklist and Homework (7 min)
**Purpose:** Students leave with a clear plan for preparation before Session 24.
**Teacher notes:**
- Walk through the homework checklist together.
- *"Si practicáis en voz alta esta tarde, estaréis listos/as. No hace falta memorizar un guion. Practicad los temas — dejad que las palabras vengan solas."*
- Remind: written message should be drafted before Session 24.
- Optional: students tell the group one thing they feel confident about and one thing they want to improve.
**Student activity:** Note homework; optional confidence/challenge share.

## Cut First If Running Behind

1. **Slide 4 (model written message):** Cut — students can read this independently.
2. **Slide 3 (model role play):** Reduce to introduction + plan sections. Skip directions walkthrough.
3. **Slide 5 (scenario practice):** Run only introductions and plans drills — both are highest priority for Part A.
4. Do not cut Slide 6 (pair rehearsal).

---

## Extension for Fast Cohorts

- **Unexpected questions in rehearsal:** Add off-script questions that require genuine improvisation: *"¿Estás casado/a? ¿Qué hiciste el fin de semana pasado?"* (The past tense question reveals A2 awareness without requiring it to be correct.)
- **Written message in-session:** Fast cohorts draft Part B during Session 23 with instructor feedback.
- **Ser/estar challenge:** Give students three sentences to classify without looking at notes: *Estoy cansado / Soy médico / La tienda está cerrada.* Rapid-fire.
