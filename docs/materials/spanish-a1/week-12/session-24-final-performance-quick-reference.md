# Spanish A1 — Session 24 Final Performance Quick Reference
## Instructor-Only

## 1. Session 24 Snapshot

| | |
|---|---|
| **Session title** | Final Performance and Course Close |
| **Purpose** | Demonstration of A1 competency — not a pass/fail exam |
| **Student-facing message** | *"Es una conversación — no un examen. El objetivo no es ser perfecto/a. Es comunicar."* |
| **Instructor goal** | Every student completes Part A and Part B; receives forward-looking feedback; knows what comes next; course ends on a high |
| **What success looks like** | Getting meaning across. Using both *ser* and *estar* in natural contexts. Recovering from a freeze. A student who attempts *ir a + infinitivo* with a small error is doing something more impressive than one who avoids it. |

## 4. Part A Spoken Role Play Checklist

**Student must cover at least 3 of these 4 topic areas:**
1. Introduction — name, origin, profession, personal info (*ser* for identity; *estar* for states/location)
2. Plans — propose something to do; suggest time and place (*ir a + infinitivo*)
3. Directions — help the other person get somewhere in the city
4. Description — describe home, neighbourhood, or a place they like (*hay / estar / ser*)

**Required structures — look for these naturally in conversation:**
- [ ] *Ser* used correctly at least once (identity, origin, or profession)
- [ ] *Estar* used correctly at least once (state, emotion, or location)
- [ ] *Ir a + infinitivo* for at least one near-future plan
- [ ] At least one question produced by the student (any question word)
- [ ] At least one obligation or ability structure (*tener que, hay que,* or *poder*)

**Instructor ser/estar targeting — ask at least one question requiring ser and one requiring estar:**
- *"¿De dónde eres?"* → *ser* (origin)
- *"¿Cómo estás hoy?"* → *estar* (current state)
- *"¿Dónde está el banco más cercano?"* → *estar* (location)

**Instructor role in the role play:**
- Play the role of a Spanish-speaking stranger. Be supportive and curious.
- Open the scene: *"¡Hola! ¿Eres de aquí?"*
- Ask natural follow-up questions: *"¿Y tu familia vive aquí también? ¿Qué te gusta hacer los fines de semana? ¿Cómo se llega al parque desde aquí?"*
- Note 2–3 specific items for feedback. Do not correct during the role play.
- Close: *"Ha sido un placer hablar contigo. ¡Hasta pronto!"*

## 5. Part B Written Message Checklist

**Required elements:**
- [ ] Introduction: who you are, where from, what you do (*ser*)
- [ ] How you feel or something about your current situation (*estar*)
- [ ] Description of daily routine (at least 2 sentences)
- [ ] Something you enjoy doing (*gustar, encantar,* or *preferir*)
- [ ] Suggestion to meet or do something together (*ir a + infinitivo* or *quisiera*)
- [ ] At least one question for the other person

**Format:** 8–10 sentences. Clear and correct over long and muddled.

**Allowed:** Phrase bank. **Not allowed:** previously written draft.

**Check especially:** ser vs. estar (identity or state?); *gustar* agreement (*me gusta* / *me gustan*); *doler* agreement (*me duele* / *me duelen*); *al/del* contractions.

**Do not grade for perfection.** Look for communication, range of A1 structures, clear intent.

## 6. Feedback Formula

**Step 1 — Group feedback first (whole cohort, 2 min):**
Name 2–3 things the whole group did well. Be specific: *"Todos usaron 'ir a + infinitivo' de forma muy natural — exactamente lo que queríamos ver."*

**Step 2 — Individual feedback (30–45 sec per student):**
| Component | What to say |
|---|---|
| **Strength** | Name one specific structure or phrase: *"Tu uso de 'ir a + infinitivo' fue muy natural."* |
| **Keep doing** | Name a habit they may not have noticed: *"La forma en que te recuperaste cuando no encontrabas la palabra — sigue haciendo eso."* |
| **A2 forward pointer** | One item, phrased forward: *"Para el A2: la distinción ser/estar con adjetivos — a veces 'estoy cansado' se convirtió en 'soy cansado.' Eso es exactamente lo que trabajaremos en A2."* |

**Ser/estar is the most likely A2 forward-pointer for Spanish A1.** Use it constructively and specifically.

**Step 3 — Written Part B feedback (post-session, within one week):**
One short paragraph. What worked (first). One grammar point. One encouragement for A2. No red marks.

## 7. Freeze / Rush Protocol

| Situation | What to do |
|---|---|
| **Student freezes at the start** | Wait 4 seconds. Then: *"¿Cómo te llamas?"* If still frozen: *"¿Eres de aquí?"* Keep asking easy questions until the student is moving. Do not abandon the role play. |
| **Student finishes in under 3 minutes** | Ask: *"¿Cuánto tiempo llevas viviendo aquí?"* (activates *llevar*) — or *"¿Qué te gusta hacer los fines de semana?"* (activates gustar and hobbies). Keep asking naturally until 5 minutes. |
| **Student is reading a prepared script** | Introduce an off-script question: *"¿Y tu familia vive aquí también?"* / *"¿Cuánto llevas aprendiendo español?"* Test genuine production. |
| **Student cannot recall a word** | If they ask (*"¿Cómo se dice...?"*), give it. If they don't ask, wait — working around a gap is the skill being demonstrated. |
| **Student makes repeated ser/estar errors** | Note it; do not correct mid-role play. Address it in the A2 forward pointer: *"La distinción ser/estar — ese va a ser un punto de trabajo en A2."* |
| **Session is running behind** | Move individual verbal feedback to post-session message. Give only group feedback verbally. Reduce A2 preview to 1 sentence. Reduce celebration to 2 minutes. Do not cut role plays. |

---

## 8. Do Not Do

- **Do not correct during the role play.** Note items; feedback after.
- **Do not let students avoid ser or estar.** Ask questions that naturally elicit both — see the targeting questions in Section 4.
- **Do not turn the A2 preview into an A2 lesson.** Three to four sentences only. Name the pretérito; do not conjugate it.
- **Do not cut or shorten the spoken role plays.** If time is short, Part B can go take-home; Part A cannot.
- **Do not surprise students with new requirements.** Everything in Session 24 should feel like what was rehearsed in Session 23.
- **Do not make the final performance feel like a test.** Call it "la conversación final" or "la actividad final," never "el examen."
- **Do not compare students to each other** in group or individual feedback.
- **Do not overcorrect Part B written feedback.** One grammar point. No red marks.
- **Do not overcorrect pronunciation.** Communicative success is the goal. Accent and pronunciation errors are not A1 failures.

## 9. Language-Specific Watch Points

These are the structures most likely to appear, to succeed, and to need A2 forward-pointers in Spanish A1.

| Structure | What to watch for | Feedback framing |
|---|---|---|
| *Ser* vs. *estar* | *Soy cansado* → should be *estoy*. *Estoy de México* → should be *soy*. *¿Dónde eres?* → should be *¿Dónde estás?* | "Para el A2: ser/estar con adjetivos — nuestro punto de trabajo." |
| *Tener* for age | *Soy 35 años* → should be *Tengo 35 años* | Correct clearly; age is *tener*, never *ser* |
| *Me duele / me duelen* | *Me duele los pies* → should be *me duelen* (plural). *Me duele la cabeza* → correct. | Correct each instance if it comes up in role play |
| *Ir a + infinitivo* | Watch for *voy a + noun* (e.g., *voy a el café*) vs. *voy a + infinitive*. Also watch for missing *a*. | Strong if used naturally — name it as a success |
| Gender agreement | *Estoy cansado* for a female speaker → *cansada*. *Es aburrida* (boring as trait) vs. *Está aburrida* (bored right now). | Correct gender; praise the ser/estar choice if it was correct |
| Direction phrases | *Todo recto, a la derecha, a la izquierda, al lado de, enfrente de* — board display may still be available from Session 23. If a student needs a direction phrase, offer it — this was introduced with board support in Session 23, not expected from memory. | Do not penalize direction phrase gaps; support as needed |
| *Gustar* agreement | *Me gusta los libros* → should be *me gustan*. Watch the noun that follows. | Correct simply: *"Gustan — porque son plural."* |
| Repair phrases | *"¿Cómo se dice...?"* / *"Estoy buscando la palabra para..."* | Explicitly praise these — they are a skill, not a weakness |

## 10. After-Class Checklist

| Task | When |
|---|---|
| Send Session 24 homework document to students (`session-24-homework.md`) | Same day or next morning |
| Collect Part B written messages (if take-home for 5+ cohort) | Before next contact with students |
| Return written Part B feedback (one paragraph max; what worked first; one grammar point; one A2 forward pointer) | Within one week |
| Note one A2 readiness item per student (especially: ser/estar confidence level; direction phrases; gustar/doler accuracy) | Before writing Part B feedback |
| Note any course-level patterns (which structures were consistently strong; which needed more time across the cohort) | Within one week — useful for A2 planning and course improvement |
| Flag any students who may benefit from additional ser/estar review before A2 | In private notes only |

**Part B feedback format reminder:** One paragraph. What worked (always first). One grammar item. One forward pointer for A2. Encouraging close. No red marks. No grades.

**Spanish-specific note for A2 handoff:** The ser/estar distinction continues throughout A2 and beyond — with participios, in the past tense, and with meaning-changing adjectives. Students leaving A1 with a working framework (not perfect accuracy) are well prepared. Note their approximate confidence level so A2 can calibrate its opening sessions accordingly.
