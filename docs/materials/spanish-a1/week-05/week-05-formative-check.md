# Spanish A1 — Week 5 Formative Check

**Administered:** Start of Session 11 (first session of Week 6)
**Time needed:** 6–8 minutes total
**Purpose:** Quick checkpoint — not a test. Confirms that *tener*, age structure, *mi/mis/su/sus* possessives, and the multi-verb family description pattern are solid enough to enter Week 6 (food and restaurant vocabulary, *gustar* structure, partitive context).

---

## Oral Prompts (5 questions — ~1 min per student for the full set)

| # | Prompt | Target response | What to listen for |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | *¿Cuántos años tienes?* | *Tengo [X] años.* | *Tener* — not *soy*; *años* present |
| 2 | Give me all six forms of *tener* — go. | tengo, tienes, tiene, tenemos, tenéis, tienen | *tengo* (not *teno*) secure; no hesitation on *tienen* |
| 3 | Tell me one *tener* expression that is true for you right now. | *Tengo hambre / sed / sueño / frío / razón / prisa.* | Expression uses *tener* — not *estoy* |
| 4 | Describe a family member in two sentences. | e.g. *Mi madre tiene 60 años. Es médica y vive en Madrid.* | Correct possessive (*mi/mis*); *ser* for identity; *tener* for age |
| 5 | What does your family member do? Give me a verb other than *tener, ser,* and *estar.* | e.g. *Trabaja en un hospital. Vive en el campo. Estudia inglés.* | Regular verb with correct subject ending (*trabaja* not *trabajan*) |

---

## Written Prompts (5 questions — students write answers, ~3 min)

**Instructions to students:** Answer in Spanish. Short answers only.

1. Write: "I am thirty-four years old."
2. Write: "My sister is a teacher." (profession — not location or state)
3. Write: "My parents are tired." (current state)
4. Write a *tener* expression: "He is hungry."
5. Write: "My brothers are 28 and 31."

**Answer key:**
1. *Tengo treinta y cuatro años.* (Not *Soy treinta y cuatro años.*)
2. *Mi hermana es profesora.* (*ser* for profession; no article before profession in Spanish — *es profesora,* not *es una profesora*)
3. *Mis padres están cansados.* (*estar* for state; *mis* because *padres* is plural)
4. *Tiene hambre.* (Not *Está hambre.*)
5. *Mis hermanos tienen 28 y 31 años.* (*mis* — plural noun; *tienen* — third person plural)

---

## Instructor Scoring Notes

Not graded. Use it to make one of three decisions:

| Result | What it means | What to do |
|---|---|---|
| Students handle all 5 oral prompts | Week 5 content is solid | Move into Week 6 (food, *gustar*, restaurant scenario) |
| Students stumble on 1–2 prompts | Minor gaps — expected at this stage | 5-minute *tener* warm-up at the start of Sessions 11 and 12; continue ser/estar correction as needed |
| Students stumble on 3+ prompts | Tener conjugation or age structure not yet secure | Add 10-minute review block to Session 11; carry *tener* conjugation as a warm-up habit |

**Most common Week 5 gaps:**
- *Soy treinta años* (ser for age) → the most common error; treat every instance as a teachable moment through Week 6
- *Estoy hambre / estoy sueño* (estar for tener expressions) → explain once more: hunger, thirst, cold are things you "have" in Spanish — *tener* only
- *Mi hermanos* (mi + plural noun) → possessives agree with the noun; *mis* is always used before a plural
- Wrong *tener* forms (*tene*, *tienen* for yo, etc.) → drill the table again; *tengo* is the priority
- *Es cansada* vs. *está cansada* — ser/estar distinction continues; state = *estar*

---

## Ready to Move On

A student is ready for Week 6 (*gustar*, food, restaurant) when they can:

- [ ] Conjugate *tener* from memory with *tengo* secure
- [ ] Give their age using *tener* without being prompted (*Tengo [X] años*)
- [ ] Use at least 2 *tener* expressions without substituting *estar*
- [ ] Describe a family member with *mi/mis* and at least 2 different verbs (*tener* for age + *ser* for identity, or similar)
- [ ] Produce at least 3 connected sentences about a family member or close person in spoken Spanish

**Not a Week 6 gate:** Full *nuestro/vuestro* possessive paradigm, *tener que + infinitivo*, stem-changing verb paradigms, or full mastery of all six *tener* forms at speed. These continue to consolidate throughout A1. The test for readiness is whether the student can say who someone is, how old they are, and what they do — in Spanish, spontaneously.
