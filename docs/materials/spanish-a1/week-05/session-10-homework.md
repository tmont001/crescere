# Spanish A1 — Session 10 Homework
## Family Description Paragraph

**Due:** Before Session 11
**Time needed:** 25–35 minutes

---

## Part 1 — Family Description Paragraph (5–7 sentences)

Write a description of your family, household, or the people closest to you in Spanish. This is the written version of what you practiced in the speaking round — use your notes from class as a starting point, then expand.

**Required elements (must appear at least once each):**

- [ ] *Tener* used for age (*tiene/tienen X años*)
- [ ] *Ser* used for identity, profession, or origin
- [ ] *Estar* used for a state or current condition
- [ ] A regular verb (*trabaja, vive, estudia, habla, come, escribe...*)
- [ ] *Mi, mis, su,* or *sus* (possessive with correct number)

**Common mistakes to avoid:**

- *Soy / es / son* for age → always *tengo / tiene / tienen + años*
- *Mi hermanos* → the possessive agrees with the noun: *mis hermanos*
- *Es cansado/a* for a state → states use *estar*: *está cansado/a*
- *Estoy hambre* for hunger → *tengo hambre* (it is a *tener* expression)

**Example paragraph** (do not copy — write about your own people):

> *Tengo un hermano y una hermana. Mi hermano tiene cuarenta años y es ingeniero. Trabaja en una empresa en Barcelona. Está muy ocupado. Mi hermana tiene treinta y cinco años. Es profesora de inglés y vive en el extranjero. Mis padres tienen sesenta y ocho años. Están jubilados y viven en el campo.*

---

## Part 2 — Speaking Practice

Read your Part 1 paragraph out loud three times. Third time: no notes.

If you cannot get through it without notes on the third try, that is fine — it tells you which sentences need more work. Come to Session 11 ready to say it with minimal or no notes.

---

## Part 3 — Extension (Optional)

If you want to push further, write 2–3 additional sentences about someone outside your immediate family — a close friend, a colleague, a neighbour. Use a subject other than *yo*:

- *Mi mejor amiga se llama...*
- *Mi compañero de trabajo tiene...*
- *Una amiga mía es de... y vive en...*

Try using at least one verb or expression you have not used in Part 1.

---

## Quick Check — Self-Review Before Session 11

Read through your paragraph and check:

| Check | Question to ask yourself |
|---|---|
| Tener for age | Did every age sentence use *tener*? No *soy / es / son* for age? |
| Ser for identity | Did you use *ser* at least once for profession, origin, or identity? |
| Estar for state | Did you use *estar* at least once for a condition (*está cansado/a, está jubilado/a, está bien*)? |
| Possessives | If the noun is plural, did you write *mis/tus/sus* — not *mi/tu/su*? |
| Regular verbs | Did you include at least one -ar/-er/-ir verb (*trabaja, vive, come*, etc.)? |
