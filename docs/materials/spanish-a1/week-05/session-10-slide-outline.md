# Spanish A1 — Session 10 Slide Outline
## Family Description: Combining Tener + Ser + Estar

**Session length:** 55–60 minutes
**Session-end goal:** Every student can deliver a 5–6 sentence spoken description of their family (or their people), correctly combining *tener* (age/possession), *ser* (identity/profession/origin), *estar* (state/location), and at least one regular verb.

> **Pacing note:** The family description speaking round (Slide 6) is the session's core deliverable. If you run short on time anywhere, compress Slides 3 and 4 — never Slide 6. Every student speaks.

---

## Core 60-Minute Plan

### Slide 1 — Warm-Up: Tener from Memory (5 min)
**Purpose:** Consolidate Session 9 content before adding the multi-verb layer.
**Teacher notes:**
- Ask all students to write the *tener* conjugation — 60 seconds, no notes.
- Call on 2–3 students for specific forms. Focus on *yo* (tengo) and *ellos* (tienen) — the two most commonly confused forms at this stage.
- Ask one student: *¿Cuántos años tienes?* They answer. Ask another: *¿Cuántos años tiene tu madre?* — check that they use *tiene*, not *tengo*.
- Quick tener expression round: "Tell me one expression that is true for you right now." 3–4 students. 30 seconds max each.
**Student activity:** Write *tener* from memory → quick oral form check → one tener expression each.

---

### Slide 2 — Possessives: Mi/Tu/Su (8 min)
**Purpose:** Lock in the agreement-with-noun rule before students build longer descriptions.
**Teacher notes:**
- Display the three-column table: *mi/mis, tu/tus, su/sus.*
- State the rule once: "Possessives agree with the noun — not with you. Whether you are male or female, your father is *mi padre* and your parents are *mis padres.*"
- Targeted drill — instructor says a noun, students supply the possessive:
  - *...hermana* → *mi hermana*
  - *...padres* → *mis padres*
  - *...hijo* → *mi hijo / su hijo / tu hijo* (depending on context given)
  - *...amigos* → *mis amigos / sus amigos*
- Address *su/sus* ambiguity briefly: "Su can mean his, her, your (formal), or their — context usually makes it clear. If not, you can add *de + name*: *el hermano de María.*"
**Student activity:** Possessive drill (10–12 items); students supply *mi/mis/tu/sus* for each noun.

---

### Slide 3 — Combining Tener + Ser + Estar (10 min)
**Purpose:** Students see how the three essential verbs combine naturally in a single description.
**Teacher notes:**
- Write one example description on screen. Read it with the class:
  > *Mi madre tiene sesenta y dos años. Es profesora. Es de Sevilla pero vive en Madrid. Está jubilada ahora. Habla inglés muy bien.*
- Annotate each verb as the class reads: *tiene* (age — tener), *es* (profession — ser), *es de* (origin — ser), *está* (current state — estar), *habla* (regular verb).
- Ask the class: "Why *está jubilada* and not *es jubilada*?" — expected answer: *estar* is for temporary or current state; retirement is a state, not a permanent identity.
- Quick pair drill (1 minute): each student says one sentence about a family member using a verb that is NOT *tener* — they must choose *ser, estar,* or a regular verb.
**Student activity:** Annotate the example together → pair drill (1 sentence each, non-tener verb).

---

### Slide 4 — Time + Tener in Schedule Context (7 min)
**Purpose:** Brief reinforcement of clock time + tener in sentence context; it will arise naturally in family descriptions.
**Teacher notes:**
- Show the structure: *Tengo + [event] + a las + [time].* Example: *Tengo clase a las nueve. Tengo reunión a las tres.*
- Ask 4–5 students: *¿A qué hora tienes clase esta semana?* Students answer with *Tengo clase a las...*
- Add one level: *Mi hermano tiene trabajo a las ocho de la mañana.* — Note that family members can also be subjects.
- Do not re-teach the full time-telling block. Correct errors when they arise; do not drill if no errors appear.
**Student activity:** Answer *¿A qué hora tienes...?* (4–5 students); one sentence about a family member's schedule.

---

### Slide 5 — Building the Family Description (8 min)
**Purpose:** Students prepare their 5–6 sentence spoken description before the speaking round.
**Teacher notes:**
- Give students 4–5 minutes of quiet preparation time. Notes allowed.
- Post the checklist visibly:
  - At least 2 uses of *tener* (age or possession)
  - At least 1 use of *ser* (identity, profession, or origin)
  - At least 1 use of *estar* (state, emotion, or location)
  - At least 1 regular verb (*trabaja, vive, estudia,* etc.)
  - At least 1 family member referenced with *mi/mis/su/sus*
- Walk the room (or check the chat) during preparation. Answer vocabulary questions.
- Encourage students who "have no family to describe" to use close friends, colleagues, or describe a fictional family.
**Student activity:** 4–5 minutes quiet writing / preparation. Instructor circulates.

---

### Slide 6 — Family Description Speaking Round (12 min)
**Purpose:** Every student delivers their 5–6 sentence description in spoken Spanish.
**Teacher notes:**
- Each student speaks for approximately 60–90 seconds. Interrupt only for a persistent pattern (same error 2+ times); otherwise let them finish.
- After each student finishes: give one piece of positive feedback + correct one thing.
  - Common corrections to anticipate: *soy treinta años* → *tengo treinta años*; *mi hermanos* → *mis hermanos*; *es cansada* (should be *está cansada*); *son las... tengo clase* (wrong verb order).
- After all students speak: draw out 2 patterns you noticed across the group — one error, one thing they did well.
**Student activity:** Each student delivers their family description (~60–90 sec). Class listens.

---

### Slide 7 — Wrap-Up (5 min)
**Purpose:** Assign the Week 5 homework paragraph; preview Week 6.
**Teacher notes:**
- Homework: a written version of what they just said, 5–7 sentences, with all five checklist elements. Due before Session 11.
- Preview Week 6: "Next week we move to food and restaurants. You will need *tener* immediately — *tener hambre, sed, ganas de.* This week's investment will show up right away."
- Optional closer: "Tell me one thing about your family that you could not say in Spanish at the beginning of this session."
**Student activity:** Note homework. Optional: one new sentence they can say now.

---

## Cut First If Running Behind

1. **Slide 4 (time + tener):** Skip the group drill. Show one example sentence; move on. Students who need time-telling reinforcement get it organically in the speaking round.
2. **Slide 3 (combining verbs):** Use only the example description. Skip the pair drill. The speaking round (Slide 6) is the real practice.
3. **Slide 2 (possessives drill):** Shorten to 5 items from 12.
4. Do not cut Slide 6 (speaking round). Do not cut Slide 5 (preparation time) below 3 minutes.

---

## Extension for Fast Cohorts

- **Add a friend or colleague:** After the family description, students describe one person outside their family — *mi mejor amiga, mi compañero de trabajo* — in 2–3 additional sentences.
- **Tener que + infinitivo preview:** *¿Qué tienes que hacer mañana?* — students answer with *Tengo que + infinitivo.* Note explicitly: "Full study in Week 9 — but you can try it now."
- **Nuestro/nuestra:** If students are describing household dynamics, introduce *nuestro apartamento / nuestra casa.* Note it as the *nosotros* possessive that agrees in gender with the noun.

---

## Move to Homework If Needed

- Written family description paragraph (already the homework — assign it now rather than doing it in class)
- *Nuestro/a/os/as* and full possessive adjective paradigm
- *Tener que + infinitivo* (obligation) — formally Week 9
- *Tener ganas de + infinitivo*
- Extended time-telling in schedule context
