# Spanish A1 — Session 9 Slide Outline
## Tener: Age and Possession

**Session length:** 60 minutes
**Session-end goal:** Students can conjugate *tener* in all six forms with *tengo* secure, give their age correctly, use 3–4 *tener* expressions, name 6 family members using *mi/mis,* and say 2–3 sentences about their family.

> **Pacing note:** The speaking round (Slide 6) is non-negotiable. Every student must say their age and describe at least one family member in Spanish before the session ends. Trim Slide 4 (tener expressions) to 5 minutes before cutting it.

---

## Core 60-Minute Plan

### Slide 1 — Warm-Up: Week 4 Debrief (6 min)
**Purpose:** Close the Week 4 loop; activate Week 4 content before adding a new verb.
**Teacher notes:**
- Ask each student to say one reflexive verb sentence from their routine. Notes allowed.
- If Week 4 formative check showed *me levanta* (wrong pronoun) as a widespread error: 60-second targeted drill — "The pronoun must match who is doing the action. *Yo → me.* Say *me levanto.*"
- If -er/-ir verb errors were widespread: ask students to give the *él/ella* form of *comer* and *vivir.* Two forms only — do not re-teach.
- Do not linger. The goal is activation, not repair.
**Student activity:** Each student says one routine sentence (reflexive or -ar/-er/-ir verb).

---

### Slide 2 — Tener: Conjugation (8 min)
**Purpose:** Students learn all six forms of *tener* with the irregular *yo* form secure.
**Teacher notes:**
- Write the full table. Say: "*Tener* is mostly regular — if you know -er verbs, you know most of this. The only exception is *yo: tengo.* Not *teno* — *tengo.* Just like *ser* gives you *soy* and *estar* gives you *estoy*, tener gives you *tengo.*"
- Stress the pronunciation shift: *TENgo, TIEnes, TIEne,* but *teNEmos, teNÉis,* then *TIEnen* again.
- Choral drill × 3: slow → normal → fast.
- Spot check: instructor calls on each student for one form at random, table covered.
**Student activity:** Choral drill × 3 → spot-check (one form per student, table covered).

---

### Slide 3 — Age with Tener (8 min)
**Purpose:** Students can give and ask for ages correctly — never using *ser* for age.
**Teacher notes:**
- Say explicitly: "Age in Spanish is something you have, not something you are. *Tengo treinta años* — not *soy treinta años.* You will hear your instructor correct this every time it happens, so let's say it right from the start."
- Key question: *¿Cuántos años tienes?* — "How many years do you have?"
- Key answer: *Tengo [X] años.*
- Quick round: each student says their age. Ask for a family member's age as a follow-up: "How old is your mother?"
- Common error to prevent: students often say *Tengo treinta* without *años.* Both *años* are required. Correct every time.
**Student activity:** Each student gives their age → gives one family member's age using *él/ella tiene.*

---

### Slide 4 — Tener Expressions (8 min)
**Purpose:** Students recognize that Spanish uses *tener* where English uses "to be" for physical states.
**Teacher notes:**
- Introduce 5 expressions with a brief context:
  - *Tengo hambre* — "It's 2 p.m. and you haven't eaten."
  - *Tengo sed* — "It's hot outside."
  - *Tengo sueño* — "It's 6 a.m. on a Monday."
  - *Tengo frío* — "The air conditioning is too strong."
  - *Tengo razón* — "You gave the right answer."
- After each: students repeat. Then ask 3 students: "What do you have right now? *¿Qué tienes?*"
- Address the critical error: *Estoy hambre* is wrong. "*Estar* is for states and emotions — yes. But hunger, thirst, cold — Spanish puts these in *tener.* The rule is not perfectly logical; just memorize these expressions."
**Student activity:** Repeat each expression → answer *¿Qué tienes ahora mismo?* (3 students).

---

### Slide 5 — Family Vocabulary + mi/mis (10 min)
**Purpose:** Students can name 8 family members using *mi/mis* correctly.
**Teacher notes:**
- Introduce 8 core family words in pairs (masc./fem.): *hermano/a, padre/madre, hijo/a, abuelo/a, tío/a, primo/a, marido/mujer, novio/novia.*
- After each pair: choral repetition with *mi* or *mis*: "mi hermano / mi hermana / mis hermanos."
- Key possessive note: "In Spanish, possessives agree with the noun — not with you. Whether you are male or female, your sister is *mi hermana* and your sisters are *mis hermanas.* The possessive changes because the noun changes, not because you change."
- Quick drill: instructor says a family member, students say it with *mi* or *mis* in a sentence: *Tengo dos primos. Mi abuela tiene ochenta años.*
**Student activity:** Repeat each family term → give one sentence using *mi/mis + tener*.

---

### Slide 6 — Speaking Round: Your Age and Family (12 min)
**Purpose:** Every student combines *tener + family vocabulary* in spoken production.
**Teacher notes:**
- Each student says:
  1. Their age: *Tengo [X] años.*
  2. How many siblings / whether they have children / whatever is true for them: *Tengo [X] hermanos / No tengo hijos.*
  3. The age of one family member or close person: *Mi madre tiene [X] años.*
- Notes allowed for family member names. No notes for ages and *tener* forms.
- Correct one item per student: *soy treinta* (must be *tengo*), *mi hermanos* (must be *mis*), wrong *tener* form.
- After all students speak: quick group debrief on 2 patterns you corrected.
**Student activity:** Each student says their age + family info (3 sentences minimum).

---

### Slide 7 — Wrap-Up (8 min)
**Purpose:** Homework preview; close with the practical value.
**Teacher notes:**
- Preview the homework: *tener* conjugation from memory + sentences about family.
- Preview Session 10: "Next session we build full family descriptions — combining *tener, ser, estar*, and regular verbs in the same sentences."
- Say: "*Tener* shows up in a lot of places in Spanish — possession, age, feeling hot/cold/hungry, and in Week 9, obligation (*tener que + infinitivo*). This week's investment pays off all the way through A1."
**Student activity:** Note homework. Optional: one *tener* expression that is true right now.

---

## Cut First If Running Behind

1. **Slide 4 (tener expressions):** Cover 3 expressions only (*hambre, sueño, razón*). Assign the rest as vocabulary reference.
2. **Slide 5 (family + possessives):** Cover 6 family words instead of 8; introduce *mis* only with *hermanos* — note that all plurals follow the same rule.
3. **Slide 3 (age drill):** Do the quick round only — each student says their own age. Skip the follow-up family member age question.
4. Do not cut Slide 6 (speaking round).

---

## Extension for Fast Cohorts

- **All six *tener* forms + family drill:** Students give the age of every person in their immediate family using all six subject forms: *Tengo, tienes (?), mi hermano tiene, nosotros tenemos...*
- **Tener que + infinitivo preview:** *Tengo que trabajar mañana.* — "Notice that tener + que + infinitivo means 'have to.' We'll study this formally in Week 9. Try using it once."
- **Tener ganas de + infinitivo:** *Tengo ganas de descansar.* — "This means 'I feel like [doing something].' You have the desire."
