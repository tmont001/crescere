# Spanish A1 — Week 5 Instructor Notes

**Sessions:** 9 and 10
**Theme:** Tener, Age, Possession, and Family Description

---

## Week Objective

By the end of Week 5, students can:
- Conjugate *tener* in all six forms with the irregular *yo* form (*tengo*) secure
- Give their age and ask for others' ages using *tener + años*
- Use 4–5 idiomatic *tener* expressions (*tener hambre, frío, razón,* etc.)
- Describe their family or chosen household using *mi/mis/tu/su*, family vocabulary, and combined *tener/ser/estar*
- Give a short spoken family description in 5–6 sentences

**Possessives:** Introduce *mi/mis, tu/tus, su/sus* as a functional trio. The full possessive adjective paradigm (*nuestro/a/os/as, vuestro/a/os/as*) is extension or Week 6. The priority is that students can say *mi hermano, mis padres, su esposo* without hesitation.

**Telling time:** Reinforce *son las + number, es la una* as needed — it comes up naturally when students describe schedules (*Tengo clase a las nueve*). Do not re-teach the full time-telling block; just correct errors when they arise.

**Relationship to the curriculum:** The curriculum places *tener* and family vocabulary in Week 4. These were deferred because Week 4 needed to cover the -er/-ir verb families and reflexive verbs. Week 5 now picks up *tener* as planned, with family vocabulary as the main communication context.

## Common Student Mistakes — Week 5

| Mistake | Correction | When to address |
|---|---|---|
| *Soy treinta años* (ser for age) | *Tengo treinta años.* Age uses *tener* in Spanish. Explicit correction each time. | Session 9 age block |
| *Estoy hambre* (estar for tener expression) | *Tengo hambre.* These are *tener* expressions — hunger, thirst, cold, sleep are things you "have." | Session 9 expressions block |
| *Yo tengo* in every sentence (subject pronoun overuse) | *Tengo* is enough — the verb ending is unambiguous for *yo* in tener. Gently model the dropped form. | Session 9 if noticed |
| *Mi hermanos* (mi + plural noun) | *Mis hermanos.* Possessives agree with the noun: *mi* → singular, *mis* → plural. | Session 10 possessives block |
| *Su* used ambiguously | *Su* can mean his/her/your (formal)/their — acknowledge this can be ambiguous; use context to clarify, or add *de + name* (*el hermano de María*). | Session 10 if students ask |
| *Tiene* used for *yo* | *Yo tengo* — the irregular form. Students sometimes default to *tiene* for all persons. | Session 9 drilling block |
| Ser/estar errors persisting | Continue to correct throughout Week 5 — family description gives ample opportunity to reinforce. | Every time |

## Suggested Instructor Language

- **For *tener* vs. *ser* for age:** *"In Spanish, age is something you have — not something you are. So instead of 'I am thirty,' you say 'Tengo treinta años' — literally, 'I have thirty years.' You already know this pattern from French learners or from tener expressions — hunger, thirst, cold — same idea: you have them."*
- **For *tengo* (irregular yo):** *"Just like ser gives us 'soy' for yo, and estar gives us 'estoy' — tener gives us 'tengo.' The yo form is always irregular in these high-frequency verbs. After 'tengo,' all the other forms are regular -er endings."*
- **For possessives:** *"In English, 'my' is always 'my.' In Spanish, 'my' is 'mi' (singular) or 'mis' (plural) — it agrees with the noun, not with you. So whether you are male or female, your brothers are 'mis hermanos' and your sister is 'mi hermana.'"*
- **For the family description:** *"Tell me about the people closest to you — family, close friends, whoever 'your people' are. Give me five sentences. Use tener for age, ser for identity and profession, estar for how they feel or where they are, and any regular verb for what they do."*

## What to Skip If Time Is Tight

- *Tener ganas de + infinitivo* — mention it exists; do not drill
- Full possessive adjective paradigm (*nuestro/vuestro*) — *mi/tu/su* is enough for Week 5
- *Tener que + infinitivo* (obligation) — this is Week 9 content; do not introduce early
- More than 5 *tener* expressions — *hambre, sed, sueño, frío, razón* covers the most common set
- Extended time-telling — just correct errors when they arise in the schedule context

## What to Assign as Homework If Pacing Runs Long

If Session 9 runs out before the speaking round:
→ Students write 3 family sentences for homework; open Session 10 with sharing

If Session 10 family narration doesn't happen:
→ Students write their 5-sentence description and record it out loud; share at Session 11
