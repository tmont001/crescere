# Spanish A1 — Session 21 Slide Outline
## Body Parts and Doler

**Session length:** 50 minutes (on plan) — can run 60 min for slower cohorts
**Session-end goal:** Every student can describe at least two symptoms using *doler* correctly and navigate a pharmacy encounter from entry to exit without preparation notes.

> **Pacing note:** The pharmacy role play (Slide 6) is non-negotiable. Every student participates in a real pharmacy exchange before the session ends. The *doler* block (Slide 3) is the highest instructional priority — connect it to *gustar* immediately and return to the connection throughout the session. Twelve body parts is sufficient for the role play; do not expand to 20+.

---

## Core Plan

### Slide 1 — Warm-Up: Week 10 Debrief + Bridge to Health (5 min)
**Purpose:** Re-activate Week 10 content; create a natural transition to health vocabulary.
**Teacher notes:**
- Each student gives one sentence from their Session 20 homework preference paragraph.
- Correct any lingering *preferir* stem change or *gustar* agreement errors quickly.
- Bridge: *"Te gusta hacer senderismo. ¿Pero qué pasa si te duele la rodilla y no puedes correr? Esta semana hablamos de la salud — síntomas, la farmacia, el médico."*
- Preview: pharmacy scenario today, doctor's appointment in Session 22.
**Student activity:** One preference sentence each.

---

### Slide 2 — Body Parts: Las Partes del Cuerpo (8 min)
**Purpose:** Students learn 12 core body parts with the correct definite article.
**Teacher notes:**
- Teach head to foot. Say each word, students repeat. Twelve items is the target — not 20+.
- Core 12 (must cover): *la cabeza, los ojos, la nariz, la boca, la garganta, la oreja, el cuello, la espalda, el estómago, el brazo, la pierna, el pie.*
- Flag immediately: **la mano is feminine** despite ending in -o. This is one of the notable exceptions in Spanish. *la mano, not el mano.*
- Gender matters because *doler* uses the definite article on the body part.
- Quick round: point to a body part (on yourself or a diagram), students name it with article.
**Student activity:** Point-and-name round for 12 body parts.

---

### Slide 3 — Doler: Same Structure as Gustar (10 min)
**Purpose:** Students understand and immediately use the *doler* structure by connecting it to *gustar* (Week 6/10).
**Teacher notes:**
- Lead with the connection — do not teach *doler* in isolation:
  *"¿Recuerdas gustar? Me gusta el fútbol — el fútbol es el sujeto. Doler funciona igual. Me duele la cabeza — la cabeza es el sujeto."*

- Write the core pattern:

  | Subject (body part) | Verb | Example |
  |---|---|---|
  | singular | **duele** | *Me duele la cabeza.* |
  | plural | **duelen** | *Me duelen los pies.* |

- Immediate contrast with common error:
  - WRONG: *Me duelo la cabeza.* → *Doler* is NOT conjugated for the person.
  - WRONG: *Me duele mi cabeza.* → Body parts take the **definite article**, not possessive.
  - RIGHT: *Me duele la cabeza.*

- Quick drill — give a body part, students produce the *doler* sentence:
  - *la espalda* → *Me duele la espalda.*
  - *los pies* → *Me duelen los pies.*
  - *el estómago* → *Me duele el estómago.*
  - *los ojos* → *Me duelen los ojos.*
  - *la garganta* → *Me duele la garganta.*
  - *las rodillas* → *Me duelen las rodillas.*

- Round: each student gives one real *doler* sentence (real or invented).

**Student activity:** Six-item *doler* drill + one original sentence each.

---

### Slide 4 — Common Symptoms (8 min)
**Purpose:** Students acquire the 6–8 most useful symptom phrases for the pharmacy scenario.
**Teacher notes:**
- Teach as ready-made phrases. Students need these in the role play:
  - *No me encuentro bien.* / *Me siento mal.* — I don't feel well.
  - *Tengo fiebre.* — I have a fever. (no article — same pattern as *tener hambre, tener frío*)
  - *Estoy resfriado/a.* — I have a cold. (estar for states — Week 2 grammar)
  - *Tengo mucha tos.* / *Toso mucho.* — I have a bad cough. / I cough a lot.
  - *Estoy muy cansado/a.* — I'm very tired.
  - *Tengo náuseas.* — I feel nauseous.
- Flag: *Tengo fiebre* (no article) — same pattern as *tener hambre, tener miedo* from Week 4. *La fiebre* ≠ *tengo fiebre.*
- Quick round: each student gives one symptom sentence (real or invented).
**Student activity:** Each student produces one symptom sentence.

---

### Slide 5 — Pharmacy Vocabulary and Phrases (4 min)
**Purpose:** Students have the 4–5 essential phrases for the pharmacy role play.
**Teacher notes:**
- Four essential phrases (do not over-teach):
  - *¿Tiene algo para...?* (Do you have something for...?) — most useful opener after describing symptoms
  - *Necesito algo para el resfriado / la tos.* (I need something for a cold/cough)
  - *¿Necesita receta?* (Does it need a prescription?)
  - *¿Cuántas veces al día?* (How many times a day?)
- Product vocabulary (to understand pharmacist's response): *un jarabe, unas pastillas, una aspirina, pastillas para la garganta.*
- Note regional variation in passing: *tirita* (Spain) vs. *curita* (Latin America) — one mention, no drilling.
**Student activity:** Class reads pharmacy phrases aloud once; proceed to role play.

---

### Slide 6 — Pharmacy Role Play (12 min) ★ PROTECTED
**Purpose:** Every student navigates a full pharmacy encounter from entry to exit.
**Teacher notes:**
- Role play structure (instructor models first, then pairs or instructor-student):
  1. Enter: *Buenos días. No me encuentro bien.*
  2. Describe: *Tengo fiebre y me duele la garganta.*
  3. Ask: *¿Tiene algo para eso?*
  4. Pharmacist responds (instructor or partner): recommends product + dosage
  5. Student asks: *¿Necesita receta?* / *¿Cuántas veces al día?*
  6. Close: *Muchas gracias. / Hasta luego.*

- Each student plays the patient role at minimum. The instructor or a confident student plays the pharmacist.
- Correct one item per student. Priority targets:
  - *me duele mi garganta* → *me duele la garganta*
  - *me duelo la cabeza* → *me duele la cabeza*
  - *tengo la fiebre* → *tengo fiebre*

- For small groups: students can take turns as the pharmacist — adds productive output.

**Minimum output per student:**
- One greeting / entry line
- One *me duele* sentence with correct agreement
- One request phrase (*¿Tiene algo para...?*)

**Student activity:** Each student completes a full pharmacy scenario (minimum 3 student turns).

### Slide 7 — Wrap-Up: Preview Session 22 (5 min)
**Purpose:** Assign homework; preview the doctor's appointment.
**Teacher notes:**
- Homework: body parts + *doler* drill + written pharmacy encounter.

**Student activity:** Note homework.

## Cut First If Running Behind

1. **Slide 4 (symptoms):** Reduce to 3 phrases: *No me encuentro bien, tengo fiebre, me duele la garganta.* The role play only requires these three.
2. **Slide 2 (body parts):** Reduce to 8 core items: *cabeza, garganta, espalda, estómago, brazo, pierna, pie, rodilla.* Eight is sufficient for the pharmacy scenario.
3. Do not cut Slide 6 (pharmacy role play).

---

## Extension for Fast Cohorts

- **Full symptom range:** Add *estornudar, estar mareado/a, tener náuseas, vomitar* for a richer pharmacy exchange.
- **Pharmacist role:** Students take turns as pharmacist and must recommend a product with dosage instructions — adds oral production.
- **Spontaneous drill:** Instructor calls out a body part at random; student must produce the *doler* sentence with correct *duele/duelen* without pausing.
- **Preview *llevar*:** Fast cohorts can try *"llevo dos días con fiebre"* at the end of the role play — they will work with it in depth in Session 22.
