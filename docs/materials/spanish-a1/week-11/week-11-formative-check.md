# Spanish A1 — Week 11 Formative Check
## Health and the Body

**When to administer:** At the start of Session 23 (Week 12), or as a closing check at the end of Session 22.
**Format:** Oral section first (5 prompts); written section second (5 prompts).
**Purpose:** Diagnostic tool. Not a grade. Informs whether students are ready for Week 12 final performance tasks or need targeted reinforcement before the integrated sessions.

---

## Oral Prompts (5 prompts — instructor reads aloud)

Administer to each student individually or in round-robin. Allow 10–15 seconds per prompt.

**Prompt 1:** "Tell me where you have pain — give me two body parts, one singular and one plural."
*Target:* One *me duele + singular* sentence (with definite article) + one *me duelen + plural* sentence (with definite article).
*Minimum pass:* Both forms correct and articles correct. *Me duele mi cabeza* → fail (possessive). *Me duelo la cabeza* → fail (wrong conjugation). *Me duelen la rodilla* → fail (plural verb, singular noun).

**Prompt 2:** "Tell me three things — a symptom, how long you've had it, and one piece of advice for yourself."
*Target:* Symptom phrase (*tengo fiebre, estoy resfriado/a, me duele...*) + *llevar* sentence (*llevo tres días con fiebre*) + advice (*debes descansar* or *deberías beber agua*).
*Watch for:* *Tengo la fiebre* (delete article); *me duelo la garganta* (wrong form); *llevo tres días con fiebre pasados* (no past marker needed — *llevar* already implies duration).

**Prompt 3:** "Tell me a *llevar* sentence about how long you've been studying Spanish."
*Target:* *Llevo [X] meses/semanas estudiando español.* OR *Llevo [X] meses en este curso.*
*Watch for:* *He estudiado español por [X] meses* (using present perfect + *por* — English-influenced; correct in other contexts but *llevar* is the target here). Any sentence with correct *llevar* + time + gerund passes.

**Prompt 4:** "Give me the *yo* and *él/ella* forms of *doler* in a sentence."
*Target:* *me duele / le duele* — with a body part in each. E.g., *Me duele la espalda. Le duele la cabeza.*
*Watch for:* Confusion between *me duele* (I) and *me duelo* (not a form); *le duele* (correct) vs. *se duele* (not a form).

**Prompt 5:** "Walk me through the first three turns of a doctor's consultation — you're the patient."
*Target:* Student enters, greets, describes at least one symptom with *doler*, gives a duration with *llevar*, responds to the doctor's question.
*Minimum pass:* Three coherent patient turns including one *doler* sentence and one *llevar* sentence.

## Written Prompts (5 items — students write independently)

**Item 1:** Write four body parts with their articles. Include one masculine, one feminine, one plural, and the word for "hand."
*Expected:* e.g., *el estómago (m.), la garganta (f.), los pies (pl.), la mano.*
*Watch for:* *el mano* → *la mano* is feminine; wrong gender on common body parts.

**Item 2:** Complete the sentence: *Me duele _____ espalda. Me duelen _____ rodillas.*
*Expected:* *la espalda / las rodillas.*
*Watch for:* *mi espalda* (possessive → wrong); *duelen la espalda* (wrong agreement — singular → duele).

**Item 3:** Rewrite in Spanish: "I've been coughing for a week."
*Expected:* *Llevo una semana tosiendo.*
*Watch for:* *Tosando* → *tosiendo* (toser is -er → gerund is *-iendo*); using *desde hace* instead of *llevar* (grammatically fine, but *llevar* is the target structure).

**Item 4:** Write two pieces of advice for a sick friend — one with *debes* and one with *deberías.*
*Expected:* e.g., *Debes descansar.* + *Deberías beber más agua.* (any correct pair)
*Watch for:* *Deberías de descansar* (drop the *de*); *Debes a descansar* (no preposition after *deber*).

**Item 5:** Write 4–5 sentences describing an illness — include one *doler* sentence (correct agreement), one *tener* symptom phrase, one *llevar* sentence, and one advice sentence.
*Expected:* Connected sentences integrating Week 11 grammar. *Doler* agreement correct; *llevar* in present; *deber/deberías* + infinitive.
*Minimum pass:* All four elements present; *doler* agreement correct; *llevar* structure recognizable.

## Scoring Decision Table

| Student output | Decision |
|---|---|
| All 5 oral prompts pass + 4–5 written correct | Ready for Week 12 final performance |
| 4 oral pass + 3–4 written correct | Ready for Week 12 — monitor *doler* agreement in Session 23 warm-up |
| *Doler* form correct but agreement (*duele/duelen*) inconsistent | Ready for Week 12 — one *doler* drill at Session 23 open |
| *Llevar* structure attempted but gerund form wrong | Ready for Week 12 — note correction; one gerund item in Session 23 warm-up |
| *Tener fiebre* still has article (*tengo la fiebre*) | Ready for Week 12 — one *tener* expression review item at warm-up |
| Fewer than 3 oral prompts pass OR fewer than 3 written correct | Flag for instructor follow-up; do not delay Week 12 for whole cohort |

---

## Ready for Week 12

Before beginning Week 12 (final performance), a student should be able to:
- [ ] Name 8+ body parts with correct definite article
- [ ] Produce *me duele* (singular) and *me duelen* (plural) with definite article — no possessive
- [ ] Describe 3 symptoms accurately (*fiebre, resfriado, tos* minimum)
- [ ] Navigate a pharmacy encounter (entry → symptom → request) without preparation
- [ ] Use *llevar + time* to express ongoing duration (either pattern: gerund or *con*)
- [ ] Give advice using *debes + infinitivo* and *deberías + infinitivo*
- [ ] Handle the opening and description phases of a doctor's consultation

---

## Not a Gate

The following items are NOT required for progression to Week 12:
- Full range of body part vocabulary beyond the core 12
- *El oído* vs. *la oreja* distinction (inner vs. outer ear)
- The full *deber* conjugation table (only *debes/deberías* required for advice)
- *Desde hace* vs. *llevar* distinction (both express duration; *llevar* is the target but *desde hace* is also correct)
- Extended healthcare vocabulary (*el quirófano, la cirugía, el análisis de sangre*)
- Phone appointment vocabulary (*pedir cita por teléfono*)
- Regional variants (*tirita* vs. *curita*)
- Perfect pronunciation of *resfriado, estornudar, mareado*
