# Spanish A1 — Session 22 Slide Outline
## At the Doctor: Deber, Deberías, and Llevar

**Session length:** 48 minutes (on plan) — can run 60 min for slower cohorts
**Session-end goal:** Every student can describe symptoms with duration (*llevar*), give and receive advice (*deber/deberías*), and navigate a doctor's consultation from arrival to departure.

> **Pacing note:** The doctor's role play (Slide 6) is non-negotiable. Every student participates in a full consultation before the session ends. *Llevar + gerund* (Slide 3) is the highest-priority new grammar this session — it is a Spanish-specific structure students will not have seen before, and it needs enough drill time. The *deber/deberías* block (Slide 2) can move quickly because *deber* is a regular -er verb.

---

## Core Plan

### Slide 1 — Warm-Up: Pharmacy Recall + Bridge to Doctor (5 min)
**Purpose:** Re-activate Session 21 content; bridge to the doctor's appointment.
**Teacher notes:**
- Each student says one pharmacy scenario sentence from their homework.
- Correct any *doler* agreement errors quickly (*me duelo → me duele*; *mi cabeza → la cabeza*).
- Bridge: *"La farmacia es para los problemas pequeños. Si es más grave, necesitas ir al médico. Hoy: la consulta médica."*
- Preview: *deber* for advice, *llevar* for duration, then doctor role play.
**Student activity:** One pharmacy sentence each.

---

### Slide 2 — Deber + Infinitivo: Advice (8 min)
**Purpose:** Students learn to give and receive medical advice using *deber* and *deberías.*
**Teacher notes:**
- *Deber* is a regular -er verb. Students may have seen it on the skip list for Week 9 — teach it fully here.
- Write the two key forms side by side:
  - *Debes descansar.* — You should/must rest. (direct)
  - *Deberías descansar.* — You should rest. (polite, softer)
  - *No debes trabajar.* — You shouldn't work.
- Introduce *deberías* as a formula for polite advice — students do not need to learn the full conditional paradigm:
  *"Deberías is the polite way to give advice in Spanish. A doctor would say deberías beber más agua — it's gentler than debes. Learn it as a phrase: deberías + infinitive."*
- Five core advice phrases:
  - *Debes descansar.* / *Deberías descansar.*
  - *Debes beber mucha agua.* / *Deberías beber más agua.*
  - *No debes trabajar esta semana.*
  - *Debes tomar este medicamento dos veces al día.*
  - *Deberías ir al médico si no mejoras.*
- Warning: no preposition between *deber/deberías* and the infinitive — *deberías de descansar* is a hypercorrection that sounds odd.
- Quick round: student takes the doctor role — gives two pieces of advice to a sick classmate.
**Student activity:** Each student gives two advice phrases (one *debes*, one *deberías*).

---

### Slide 3 — Llevar + Time + Gerund: Duration (10 min)
**Purpose:** Students learn the Spanish-specific structure for expressing how long an ongoing situation has been going on.
**Teacher notes:**
- *Llevar* for duration is the most important new structure of Session 22 — and the most unfamiliar to English speakers. Give it full time.
- Two patterns:
  1. *Llevar + time + gerund:* for actions → *Llevo una semana tosiendo.* (I've been coughing for a week.)
  2. *Llevar + time + con + noun:* for states → *Llevo tres días con fiebre.* (I've had a fever for three days.)

- Write both forms on the board:

  | Pattern | Example | English |
  |---|---|---|
  | *llevar + time + gerund* | *Llevo una semana tosiendo.* | I've been coughing for a week. |
  | *llevar + time + con + noun* | *Llevo tres días con fiebre.* | I've had a fever for three days. |
  | *llevar + time + gerund* | *Llevo dos días sin dormir bien.* | I haven't slept well for two days. |

- Gerund review (for *llevar + gerund* pattern):
  - -ar verbs: *tosando* → WRONG; *tosando*... wait: *toser* is -er: *tosiendo* ✓
  - -ar: *hablar → hablando; caminar → caminando*
  - -er/-ir: *comer → comiendo; toser → tosiendo; dormir → durmiendo*
  - Key gerunds for health: *tosiendo, vomitando, durmiendo (mal)*

- Key question from the doctor: *¿Cuánto tiempo lleva así?* — How long have you been like this? Students must be able to respond with *llevar.*

- Quick drill: instructor gives situation, student responds with *llevar*:
  - *¿Cuándo empezaste a toser?* → *Llevo una semana tosiendo.*
  - *¿Desde cuándo tienes fiebre?* → *Llevo tres días con fiebre.*
  - *¿Cuándo empezaste a sentirte mal?* → *Llevo cuatro días sintiéndome mal.*

**Student activity:** Three-item *llevar* drill + one student-generated *llevar* sentence.

---

### Slide 4 — Doctor's Appointment Vocabulary (5 min)
**Purpose:** Students have the script elements they need to navigate the role play.
**Teacher notes:**
- Doctor's side (students recognize and respond):
  - *¿Qué le pasa?* — What's the matter?
  - *¿Cuánto tiempo lleva así?* — How long have you been like this?
  - *¿Dónde le duele exactamente?* — Where exactly does it hurt?
  - *¿Es grave?* → *No es nada grave.* — Is it serious? No, nothing serious.
  - *Le voy a hacer una receta.* — I'll write you a prescription.
  - *Vuelva en una semana si no mejora.* — Come back in a week if you don't improve.
- Patient's side (students produce):
  - *No me encuentro bien. Llevo cuatro días así.*
  - *Me duele la espalda y tengo fiebre.*
  - *Llevo tres días con fiebre.*
  - *¿Es grave?*
  - *Gracias, doctor/doctora.*
**Student activity:** Read through the script together once; proceed to role play.

---

### Slide 5 — Doctor's Appointment Role Play (14 min) ★ PROTECTED
**Purpose:** Every student navigates a complete medical consultation.
**Teacher notes:**
- Role play structure (instructor models first, then pairs or instructor-student):
  1. Patient arrives: *Buenos días, doctor/doctora. No me encuentro bien.*
  2. Doctor asks: *¿Qué le pasa?*
  3. Patient describes: *Me duele la garganta y tengo fiebre. Llevo tres días así.*
  4. Doctor asks: *¿Cuánto tiempo lleva con fiebre exactamente?*
  5. Patient responds with *llevar*: *Llevo tres días con fiebre — unos 38 grados.*
  6. Doctor gives advice: *Debes descansar. Deberías beber mucha agua. No debes trabajar esta semana.*
  7. Patient: *¿Es grave?*
  8. Doctor: *No, no es nada grave. Toma estos medicamentos dos veces al día y vuelve en una semana si no mejoras.*
  9. Patient: *Muchas gracias, doctor/doctora.*

- Each student must produce at minimum: symptom sentence with *doler*, duration sentence with *llevar*, and a closing response.
- Correct one item per student. Priority targets:
  - *me duele mi garganta* → *me duele la garganta*
  - *Tengo la fiebre desde tres días* → *Llevo tres días con fiebre*
  - *Deberías de descansar* → *Deberías descansar*

**Minimum checklist for each student:**
- [ ] One *me duele/me duelen* sentence with correct agreement and article
- [ ] One *llevar* sentence (either pattern)
- [ ] Response to the doctor's advice

**Student activity:** Each student completes a full doctor's consultation (minimum 4 student turns).

---

### Slide 6 — Wrap-Up: Week 11 Close and Week 12 Preview (6 min)
**Purpose:** Assign homework; preview Week 12 final performance; connect *llevar* to broader use.
**Teacher notes:**
- Homework: symptom description + *llevar* practice + advice paragraph.
- Broaden *llevar* explicitly: *"Llevar no es solo para la salud. Se usa para cualquier situación que continúa ahora mismo: Llevo tres meses estudiando español. Si puedes decir esto en la semana 12, vas a impresionar."* Write this sentence on the board.
- Week 12 preview: *"La semana 12 es la performance final — no es un examen, es una conversación. Si puedes hacer la consulta médica hoy, puedes hacer la performance final. Los escenarios de la semana 12 son: presentarte, hacer planes, dar direcciones. Todo lo que hemos practicado."*
- Reassure: the formative check is diagnostic, not a gate. Everyone moves to Week 12.
**Student activity:** Note homework; brief Week 12 questions if any.

## Cut First If Running Behind

1. **Slide 2 (*deber*):** Reduce to two forms only — *debes descansar* and *deberías beber agua*. Skip the not-form (*no debes*) — move it to homework.
2. **Slide 3 (*llevar*):** Reduce to one pattern only — *llevar + time + con + noun* (*llevo tres días con fiebre*). Skip the gerund form; introduce it in homework only.
3. **Slide 4 (vocabulary):** Skip — students have enough from the warm-up and Slides 2–3 to proceed to the role play.
4. Do not cut Slide 5 (doctor's role play).

## Extension for Fast Cohorts

- **Llevar in other contexts:** *Llevo tres meses estudiando español. Llevo dos años viviendo en esta ciudad.* Students produce two non-health *llevar* sentences — this previews Week 12 self-description.
- **Doctor role:** Students take turns as the doctor and must give three pieces of advice using *debes* and *deberías.*
- **Extended symptoms:** Student describes three different symptoms with *llevar* sentences: *Llevo tres días con fiebre, llevo una semana tosiendo, y llevo dos días sin dormir bien.* More realistic medical monologue.
- **Phone appointment:** *"Buenos días, quisiera pedir una cita con el doctor..."* Brief phone vocabulary for motivated students.
