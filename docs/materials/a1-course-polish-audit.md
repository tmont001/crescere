## Verdict: Teachable

Both courses are ready to teach. Grammar is accurate throughout. Pedagogy is consistent. Session pacing tables are realistic. The four-section slide format (core / cut-first / extension / move-to-homework) is stable from Weeks 1–12. No blocking errors were found.

What follows is a prioritized list of issues that should be addressed before or during the first live cohort, followed by polish recommendations and things intentionally left alone.

---

## Executive Summary

### Strengths
- Grammar content is accurate in all 193 files. No factual errors in conjugation tables, contraction rules, agreement rules, or sample sentences.
- Speaking-first principle is protected throughout. Every session has a spoken round marked non-negotiable, and every slide outline's cut guidance explicitly forbids cutting speaking blocks.
- Pacing tables are realistic and calibrated. ~50–60 min sessions; extension blocks present for fast cohorts; backup plans for slow ones.
- Common mistakes sections are the most valuable instructor resource in the course — specific, correctable, and tied to the exact moment each error tends to appear.
- Cross-week bridging is strong in the second half (Week 6 gustar → Week 11 doler; Week 11 depuis/llevar → Week 12 self-description). Students who do the homework will arrive at Week 12 with a natural toolkit.
- Final performance framing is excellent. "Demonstration not exam" is stated in five separate places; cohort-size pacing table handles the real logistics problem; rubrics are clear.
- Spanish course gives ser/estar the prominence it deserves. The distinction is flagged in Session 1, dedicated fully in Week 2, threaded through every subsequent week, and culminates in a dedicated ser/estar section in the Week 12 student overview and Session 24 rubric.

### Top Issues to Address
1. **P1 — Curriculum document is out of sync with materials** from Week 4 onwards (both languages). An instructor consulting the curriculum will expect the wrong topics from Weeks 4–10.
2. **P2 — Spanish Week 4 cross-reference is wrong.** Instructor notes say "Full stem-change coverage is Week 6" but no Week 6 stem-change block exists.
3. **P2 — Session 23 curriculum label says "error analysis"** but the materials do final performance prep. Language mismatch will confuse instructors reading the curriculum before opening the Week 12 folder.
4. **P2 — Both syllabi have a draft warning footer** that must be removed before sending to enrolled students.
5. **P3 — Curriculum "Materials Needed" tables** still say "Not yet created" for all items; the Markdown teaching materials should be acknowledged.

## French A1 — Overall Assessment

**Grade: A- (ready to teach; three polish items)**

All 96 French A1 files are present and correctly named. Grammar coverage matches the curriculum for the second half of the course (Weeks 6–12). The first half has a documented one-week slide (see P1 item). Vocabulary load is well-managed — approximately 650 active words are reachable across the 24 sessions.

Specific strengths:
- **Week 2 adjective agreement (Session 4):** BAGS rule is introduced correctly with explicit flagging of exceptions. The common error table catches gender agreement on profession adjectives, which is the highest-frequency Week 2 mistake.
- **Week 6 partitive articles:** The negation rule (pas de) is taught immediately alongside the affirmative forms, not separated into a later session. This is the right move — students who see partitives without immediate negation practice will produce pas du for weeks.
- **Week 9 modal verbs (Sessions 17–18):** All three modals (devoir/pouvoir/vouloir) are taught together in Week 9 with clear comparison. The Week 12 materials correctly reinforce these as a set rather than drilling them individually.
- **Week 11 depuis + présent:** The conceptual contrast with English (English uses past for ongoing states; French uses present) is addressed explicitly in the vocabulary file, instructor notes, and Session 22 slide outline. The non-health example (j'apprends le français depuis trois mois) bridges naturally to Week 12.
- **Week 12 final performance:** Session 23 model role play includes a deliberate repair moment (¿Cómo se dice... → Comment dit-on...) which models communication strategy, not just grammar.

Specific concerns:
- **Week 4 note mentions "deferred -ir and -re verbs from Week 3"** but the Week 3 curriculum spec assigns -ir/-re verbs to Session 6. The drift is self-documented in the instructor notes, which is good, but the curriculum is not updated. (See P1.)
- **Week 5 materials** — not fully verified in this pass. Given the Week 4 drift, Week 5 may contain Time/Dates content that the curriculum assigns to Week 4. Verify before first use.
- **French Week 12 vocabulary (phrase bank):** 12 categories, approximately 120–150 phrases. Excellent reference. Students need explicit instructions to use it as a cheat sheet during Part B, not as study material to memorize before Session 23.

## Spanish A1 — Overall Assessment

**Grade: A (strong; one accuracy issue to correct, rest are polish)**

All 97 Spanish A1 files are present and correctly named (the extra file is `reference-ser-vs-estar.md`). Grammar coverage is accurate throughout. The ser/estar thread is the standout structural achievement — no other online or textbook course at this level handles it this persistently.

Specific strengths:
- **Ser/estar threading:** Session 1 (Week 1 firewall), Week 2 (full dedicated week), Weeks 3–4 (persistence notes in instructor files), Week 8 (meaning-changing adjectives review), Week 11 (estar for illness states vs. tener for symptoms), Week 12 (dedicated section in student overview, ser/estar row in Part A rubric, explicit questions in Session 24 slide). This is the right level of treatment for the course's central challenge.
- **Gustar → doler bridge (Week 11):** Session 21 slide outline opens with the gustar connection before introducing doler. Students seeing "same structure as Week 6" immediately lower anxiety. This sequencing is genuinely helpful.
- **reference-ser-vs-estar.md:** Clean, accurate, appropriately scoped. The "one that surprises people" callout (location always uses estar, even for La Torre Eiffel) is exactly the insight students need before Session 3.
- **Regional vocabulary handling:** Billete/boleto, coger/tomar, el coche/el carro are flagged at point of introduction with a light touch. Not overwhelmed in Week 2 — introduced as they appear organically across Weeks 5–8.
- **Week 11 la mano flag:** The feminine-despite-o-ending exception is prominently flagged in vocabulary, instructor notes, and Session 21 slide outline. This is exactly the kind of exception that gets missed and corrected after students have already memorized the wrong pattern.
- **Week 4 stem-change flag:** Instructor notes correctly introduce despertarse (e→ie) and acostarse (o→ue) as phrases to use without drilling the full paradigm. The deferral is pedagogically sound.

Specific concerns:
- **Spanish Week 4 cross-reference error (P2):** Instructor notes say "Full stem-change coverage is Week 6." There is no Week 6 stem-change grammar block. Week 6 covers gustar structure and querer. Stem-changing verbs appear later as they arise (querer Week 6, preferir Week 10). The note should say something like "Stem-changing verbs are introduced as they arise throughout the course, most fully in Week 10 (preferir)." Fix before first use to avoid instructor confusion.
- **Spanish syllabus:** Says students will be able to "handle a basic pharmacy or health-related conversation" — this is Week 11 content and accurate, but the syllabus course outline table (Week 11 row) says "Health and the body / Body vocabulary; doler structure" which is technically accurate but undersells the full scope (también: deber, llevar + gerund). Minor.

### P1 — High priority (Should fix before first live cohort)

#### P1-A: Curriculum document does not match materials from Week 4 onwards (both languages)

**Where:** `docs/curriculum/french-a1.md` and `docs/curriculum/spanish-a1.md` — the 24-Session Breakdown sections.

**What's wrong:** Both curriculum documents describe the following session assignments:
- Week 3: -er verbs (S5) + -ir/-re verbs + reflexive verbs (S6)
- Week 4: Telling time (S7) + Family + possessives (S8)
- Week 5: City places + prepositions (S9) + Transportation + aller (S10)

But the actual materials drift from this starting in Week 3. Session 6 in both courses deferred -ir/-re verbs and reflexive verbs to Week 4. As a result:
- Week 4 materials cover reflexive verbs + basic time (not Time/Dates/Family as the curriculum states)
- Week 5 materials likely cover Time/Dates/Family + start of Getting Around

The materials themselves document this internally: Week 4 instructor notes explicitly say "The curriculum places telling time and family vocabulary in Week 4 (Sessions 7–8). Given that Week 3 deferred reflexive verbs, Week 4 covers reflexive verbs." The drift is acknowledged but only inside the Week 4 folder — not updated in the curriculum documents.

**Risk:** An instructor reading the curriculum before opening any week folder will have wrong expectations from Week 4 onwards. This is a real usability problem during course prep.

**Fix options:**
1. Update the curriculum 24-Session Breakdown to reflect actual week themes (preferred — keeps the curriculum as the source of truth)
2. Add an "Actual Teaching Sequence" note at the top of the 24-Session Breakdown sections in both curriculum docs (faster; doesn't require rewriting the session entries)
3. Do not modify curriculum docs (constraint from earlier); instead add a `SEQUENCE-NOTE.md` to the materials folder explaining the drift

**Constraint note:** Per standing constraints, curriculum documents should not be modified "unless absolutely necessary." This may qualify — an instructor going into their first session with wrong curriculum expectations is a real problem. Recommend escalating this decision.

### P2 — Medium priority (Polish before teaching; won't break the course but will create friction)

#### P2-A: Spanish Week 4 instructor notes — stem-change cross-reference is wrong

**Where:** `docs/materials/spanish-a1/week-04/instructor-notes.md`

**What's wrong:** Under the stem-changing verb note, the text says "Full stem-change coverage is Week 6." Week 6 covers the gustar structure and querer (which has an irregular pattern, not a true stem change). There is no dedicated stem-change grammar block in Week 6. Stem-changing verbs appear as they arise: querer appears in Week 6, preferir (e→ie) appears in Week 10 Session 20 per the curriculum.

**Fix:** Change "Full stem-change coverage is Week 6" to "Stem-changing verbs are introduced as they arise throughout the course. The pattern appears in querer (Week 6) and is addressed more fully in Week 10 (preferir e→ie)."

#### P2-B: Session 23 curriculum label says "error analysis" — materials do no such thing

**Where:** `docs/curriculum/french-a1.md` and `docs/curriculum/spanish-a1.md` — Week 12, Session 23 entry.

**What's wrong:** Both curriculum documents say:
> "Session 23 — Integrated Role Plays / Error Analysis"

But the Session 23 slide outlines in both courses are structured as Final Performance Preparation sessions: overview of the performance, model role play walkthrough, scenario drills, and pair rehearsal. There is no error analysis block (which would typically involve reviewing student errors systematically). An instructor reading this curriculum entry might plan an error-analysis segment that isn't in the materials.

#### P2-C: Both syllabi have draft warning footers — must be removed before student distribution

**Where:** `docs/materials/french-a1/syllabus.md` and `docs/materials/spanish-a1/syllabus.md` — final paragraph.

**What's wrong:** Both syllabi end with:
> "Note: This is a draft student-facing syllabus. Review all sections for accuracy before sending to enrolled students or prospective families. Verify session dates, final performance task details, and any platform-specific instructions before distribution."

This is appropriate for an internal draft. It must be removed (or replaced with session-specific dates and platform details) before the syllabus goes to any student or prospective family. Sending a document with "This is a draft" at the bottom to an enrolling student is a trust and professionalism issue.

**Fix:** Remove the Note paragraph. If session dates and Google Meet link details need to be added, add them at the top (Course at a Glance table) — not as a footer note.

#### P2-D: Week 12 phrase banks — student overview should tell students how to use them

**Where:** `docs/materials/french-a1/week-12/student-facing-overview.md` and `docs/materials/spanish-a1/week-12/student-facing-overview.md`

**What's wrong:** The Week 12 vocabulary files (phrase banks) contain 12 categories and roughly 120–150 phrases each. The student-facing overviews reference the phrase bank in the "How to Prepare" section but do not tell students how to use it:

> "Review the phrase bank — the Week 12 phrase bank has the most useful expressions from across French/Spanish A1."

This instruction could lead students to try to memorize the entire bank the night before Session 24, which would be exhausting and counterproductive. The phrase bank is a reference to have open during Part B (written message) — not a vocabulary test.

**Fix:** In both student-facing overviews, change the phrase bank instruction to something like: "Skim the Week 12 phrase bank to remind yourself what's in it. During Part B of the final performance, you may have it open. Don't try to memorize it — just know it exists and know where to look."

#### P2-E: Formative check timing language is inconsistent across three document types

**Where:** Both curriculum docs (Week 2 formative noted), both syllabi ("after weeks 1, 5, 7, 9, and 11"), and the Week 1 formative check files ("Administered: Start of Session 3").

**What's wrong:** Three documents describe the same event with three different labels:
- Curriculum: "Week 2 — Spoken: describe a photo of a person" (French) / "Week 2 — Spoken: 10 ser/estar sentences" (Spanish)
- Syllabus: "Formative checks happen at five points during the course (after weeks 1, 5, 7, 9, and 11)"
- Formative check file: "Administered: Start of Session 3 (first session of Week 2)"

The actual check file is in the `week-01/` folder and covers Week 1 content. The curriculum calls it a Week 2 event (because it happens Session 3). The syllabus calls it "after week 1." None of these is wrong, but an instructor might look for a Week 2 formative check in the `week-02/` folder and not find it.

**Fix (light):** Add a one-line note in the Week 2 instructor notes for both courses: "The Week 1 formative check is filed under `week-01/week-01-formative-check.md`. Administer it at the start of Session 3 before introducing Week 2 content." This requires no curriculum or syllabus edits.

## Things Not to Change

The following items appear intentional and should be preserved:

- **The "not a gate" language in every formative check.** This is a core philosophical commitment. Do not replace it with passing thresholds or letter grades.
- **The four-section slide format.** Core / Cut / Extension / Move-to-homework is established and consistent. Don't introduce a fifth section.
- **Session 24 A2 preview length (4 sentences, no content teaching).** The constraint "do not teach content" is correct — instructors who let the A2 preview become a first A2 lesson will overrun the session and leave students anxious rather than excited.
- **The gustar → doler explicit connection in Spanish Week 11.** This is the strongest pedagogical decision in the whole course. Don't bury it or move it into the skip list.
- **French depuis + présent contrast with English.** The explicit statement that English uses past where French uses present is the single clearest explanation students will encounter anywhere. Don't soften it into a footnote.
- **la mano feminine flag in Spanish Week 11.** It's flagged three times (vocabulary, instructor notes, slide outline). This is exactly the right frequency for a genuine exception that causes persistent errors if missed.
- **The Week 12 phrase bank as a reference, not vocabulary content.** The decision not to introduce new vocabulary in Week 12 is correct. All phrase bank items are drawn from prior weeks.
- **The cohort-size pacing table in Session 24 instructor notes.** Real logistics, specific numbers — leave as is.
- **The "il faut / devoir" distinction in French Week 11.** The general vs. personal obligation contrast is genuinely useful and correctly drawn.
- **The "debes / deberías" parallel in Spanish Week 11.** Introducing deberías as a formula without teaching conditional morphology is the right A1 call.
