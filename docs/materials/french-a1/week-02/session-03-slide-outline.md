# French A1 — Session 3 Slide Outline
## Avoir and Physical Description

**Session length:** 60 minutes
**Session-end goal:** Students can use *avoir* in all six forms, give their age, and describe their own appearance (hair color and length, eye color, height) in 3–4 sentences.

> **Pacing note:** The speaking activity in Slide 7 is non-negotiable — students must produce a description before they leave. Shorten Slides 4 or 5 before cutting Slide 7.

---

## Core 60-Minute Plan

### Slide 1 — Warm-Up: Back in French (5 min)
**Purpose:** Re-activate Week 1 language; transition students into speaking mode.
**Teacher notes:**
- Open in French. Ask each student to give their introduction from Week 1 — one sentence per person is fine. Listen for *je suis* vs. *j'ai* errors that surface (you will hear them now that avoir is coming).
- Correct any persistent Week 1 errors that come up. One correction per student maximum.
- Briefly say: "Last week we used être — 'to be.' This week we're adding avoir — 'to have.' Both are essential."
**Student activity:** Each student says one sentence about themselves from memory.

---

### Slide 2 — Avoir: Present Tense (8 min)
**Purpose:** Students know all six forms of *avoir* and have produced each one at least once.
**Teacher notes:**
- Display the full table. Say each form, students repeat.

| Subject | Avoir |
|---|---|
| j' | ai |
| tu | as |
| il / elle | a |
| nous | avons |
| vous | avez |
| ils / elles | ont |

- Pronunciation alerts: *j'ai* (sounds like "zhay," not "jay"); *ils ont* — the s is silent but liaison occurs before *ont* → *ils_ont*; *nous avons* — the s of *nous* links to avons.
- Choral drill × 2. Then test: display subject pronouns only, students produce the form.
- Use in context immediately: *Tu as quel âge? / J'ai vingt-huit ans. / Elle a les yeux bleus. / Nous avons cours ce soir.*
**Student activity:** Choral conjugation × 2 → conjugation test → instructor uses random forms in sentences, students give thumbs up/down for correct/incorrect.

---

### Slide 3 — Age with Avoir (6 min)
**Purpose:** Students can ask and give ages using *avoir* correctly.
**Teacher notes:**
- French expresses age with *avoir*, not *être*: *J'ai trente ans.* (Literally: "I have thirty years.")
- The word *ans* (years) is required. Students will drop it. Correct it every time.
- Quick numbers check: count by tens from 10 to 100. If numbers 70–90 are shaky, give a 1-minute spot drill.
- Model: *Tu as quel âge ?* → *J'ai [X] ans.* Ask every student.
**Student activity:** Instructor asks each student *Tu as quel âge ?* → student answers.

---

### Slide 4 — Hair: Describing Appearance (10 min)
**Purpose:** Students can describe hair color and length in French.
**Teacher notes:**
- **The structure:** *J'ai les cheveux [color/length].* Note that *cheveux* is always plural in French. Never *le cheveu.*
- **Hair colors to teach live:** noir, brun, châtain, blond, roux, blanc/gris. If a student has dyed hair in an unusual color, give them the word.
- **Hair length:** courts, longs, mi-longs.
- **Hair texture (optional):** lisse, frisé, ondulé. Mention if time allows; do not drill.
- Model: *J'ai les cheveux bruns et mi-longs.*
- Students describe their own hair. Do not require agreement yet on the adjectives — focus on getting the vocabulary right first.
**Student activity:** Each student says one sentence about their hair.

---

### Slide 5 — Eyes and a Few Colors (7 min)
**Purpose:** Students can describe eye color; students recognize basic color vocabulary.
**Teacher notes:**
- **Eye structure:** *J'ai les yeux [color].* Again, *yeux* is always plural.
- **Colors to teach:** bleus, verts, marron, noisette, noirs, gris. Flag that *marron* and *noisette* are invariable — they never change form.
- **Color adjective agreement preview** (30 seconds only): most colors will agree with the noun they describe — but not today. Say: "This week, just get the vocabulary right. Agreement practice is next session."
- Quick color recognition: display color swatches or names and have students call out the French.
**Student activity:** Each student says their eye color. Quick color drill — instructor shows/names a color, students call out the French word.

---

### Slide 6 — Height and Build (5 min)
**Purpose:** Students can describe height in one or two words.
**Teacher notes:**
- Teach three words only: *grand/grande* (tall), *petit/petite* (short), *de taille moyenne* (average height).
- Use être for these adjectives: *Je suis grand. Elle est petite.*
- Quick contrast for the class: *J'ai les cheveux blonds* (avoir + feature) vs. *Je suis grande* (être + adjective). This is a distinction that will matter all week.
- Optional: *mince* (slim), *costaud* (sturdy) — mention if time allows, do not drill.
**Student activity:** Students say their height description in one sentence.

---

### Slide 7 — Speaking: Describe Yourself (12 min)
**Purpose:** Students produce a complete 4-sentence self-description in French.
**Teacher notes:**
- Display the model structure on screen:
  1. *J'ai [X] ans.*
  2. *J'ai les cheveux [color + length].*
  3. *J'ai les yeux [color].*
  4. *Je suis [grand/petite/de taille moyenne].*
- Give students 2 minutes to write or note their four sentences.
- Pairs practice: share with a partner (3 minutes).
- Whole group: each student shares out loud. Correct one item per student.
- After everyone goes: instructor models describing one student to the group (*Elle a les cheveux bruns et courts. Elle a les yeux verts...*). Students try describing a classmate as a group activity.
**Student activity:** Write 4 sentences → pair practice → share with group → group describes one student.

---

### Slide 8 — Wrap-Up (3 min)
**Purpose:** Set homework expectations; close positively.
**Teacher notes:** Say: "You now have *avoir* and a full vocabulary to describe what someone looks like. The homework is 20 minutes — practice the forms from memory and write a description of one person."
**Student activity:** Students note homework. Optional: one French word each before logging off.

---

## Cut First If Running Behind

1. **Slide 6 (height/build):** Reduce to just naming the three words. Skip the contrast drill.
2. **Slide 5 (colors):** Skip the color drill. Just model eye descriptions and move on.
3. **Slide 7 (speaking):** Shorten pair practice to 2 minutes. Skip the "describe a classmate" extension.
4. Do not cut Slides 2 or 7 — avoir conjugation and the speaking activity are the session's non-negotiables.

---

## Extension for Fast Cohorts

- **Hair texture (3 min):** Introduce *lisse, frisé, ondulé.* Students add texture to their description.
- **Describe a famous person (5 min):** Instructor gives a name, students describe them. Good for generating natural vocabulary discussion.
- **Color agreement preview (4 min):** Take two sentences and show how the color adjective changes when the noun is feminine: *Il a les cheveux blonds / Elle a les cheveux blonds.* (No change here — same word. Then try *Il est grand / Elle est grande* to preview Session 4's topic.)

---

## Move to Homework If Needed

- Hair texture vocabulary (*lisse, frisé, ondulé*)
- Color adjective agreement rules (main topic of Session 4)
- Extended color vocabulary beyond the core 8
- Describing a third person in detail (will be Session 4's group activity)
