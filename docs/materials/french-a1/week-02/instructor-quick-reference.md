# French A1 — Week 2 Instructor Quick Reference

---

## 1. Week 2 Snapshot

| | |
|---|---|
| **Theme** | Avoir, Physical Description, and Personality |
| **Sessions** | 3 (Avoir and Physical Description) and 4 (Personality Adjectives and Adjective Agreement) |
| **Communicative goal** | Students can describe themselves and one other person — appearance, age, and 3–5 personality traits — with correct adjective agreement on regular patterns |
| **Grammar focus** | *Avoir* (all 6 forms); *j'ai* vs. *je suis* contrast; adjective agreement (gender -e; irregular -eux/-euse, -if/-ive, -il/-ille); plural -s |
| **Vocabulary focus** | Hair color/length, eye color, height, 8 core personality adjectives |
| **End-of-week outcome** | Every student describes a real person out loud in 4–5 sentences with correct gender agreement |

**Week 2 priority reminder:** Adjective agreement established now pays dividends through Week 12. Correct the feminine -e pattern consistently and positively every time a student drops it. Students who skip the habit in Week 2 produce agreement errors for the rest of A1.

---

## 2. Start-of-Week Reminder

**Week 1 formative check** is at `week-01/week-01-formative-check.md`. Administer at the **start of Session 3**, before any Week 2 content. Takes 6–8 minutes. Not graded.

| Result | Action |
|---|---|
| Confident on all 5 oral prompts | Move directly into Week 2 content |
| Shaky on 1–2 prompts | Quick 5-minute review, then proceed |
| Shaky on 3+ prompts | 10-minute review block in Sessions 3 and 4 before *avoir* |

**Watch especially:** Students uncertain about *je suis / tu es / il est* will struggle with the *avoir/être* contrast that runs through all of Week 2. If those three être forms are shaky, spend the first 5 minutes of Session 3 on them before introducing *avoir*.

## 3. Session 3 At-a-Glance

**Title:** Avoir and Physical Description
**Goal:** Students use *avoir* in all six forms, give their age, and describe their own appearance in 3–4 sentences.

| Block | Time | Must protect? |
|---|---|---|
| Warm-up (Week 1 intro round; listen for *suis/ai* errors) | 5 min | Yes — re-entry + carryover error check |
| *Avoir* conjugation (table + choral drill + spot-test) | 8 min | **Yes** — all six forms, especially *nous avons / vous avez / ils ont* |
| Age with *avoir* (*J'ai X ans* — round with every student) | 6 min | Yes — *ans* is required; correct every drop |
| Hair vocabulary (*J'ai les cheveux...*) | 10 min | Yes |
| Eye color (*J'ai les yeux...*; flag *marron* as invariable) | 7 min | Yes |
| Height (*grand/petite/de taille moyenne* — 3 words only) | 5 min | Reduce to naming only if behind |
| Self-description speaking activity (4 sentences per student) | 12 min | **Yes — every student speaks** |
| Wrap-up and homework preview | 3 min | No |

**Cut first:** Height block to one naming pass (no contrast drill). Skip color recognition drill in the eye block. Shorten pair practice to 2 min; skip the "describe a classmate" extension.

**Move to homework:** Hair texture (*lisse, frisé, ondulé*), color adjective agreement rules (Session 4 topic), extended color vocab, describing a third person in detail.

**Homework assigned after Session 3:** *Avoir* out loud × 3 from memory; write 4–6 sentence self-description (age + hair + eyes + height); vocabulary quick-check (5 items).

## 4. Session 4 At-a-Glance

**Title:** Personality Adjectives and Adjective Agreement
**Goal:** Students describe a real person using 3–4 personality adjectives with correct masculine/feminine agreement.

| Block | Time | Must protect? |
|---|---|---|
| Warm-up (share S3 homework; flag agreement errors before teaching agreement) | 8 min | Yes |
| 8 core personality adjectives (*sympa, drôle, sérieux, timide, bavard, gentil, patient, impatient*) | 10 min | Yes |
| Adjective agreement — gender (regular -e; -eux/-euse; -if/-ive; -il/-ille + rapid-fire drill) | 10 min | **Yes** |
| Adjective agreement — number (add -s; inaudible in speech; matters for writing) | 5 min | Condense to 2 min if behind |
| Build a description (choose real person; write 4–5 sentences; pair practice) | 15 min | Yes — skip pair step if behind; students write and share directly |
| Group description round (every student describes their person out loud) | 8 min | **Non-negotiable** |
| Wrap-up and homework preview | 4 min | No |

**Cut first:** Number agreement to a 2-minute statement ("add -s for plural") and assign written practice as homework. Agreement drill reduced to regular -e pattern + *sérieux/sérieuse* only; assign -if/-ive and -il/-ille as homework reading. Skip pair practice in the build block — students write and share directly with the group.

**Move to homework:** Number agreement written practice, -if/-ive and -il/-ille irregular patterns, BAGS adjective placement (Week 4 topic — do not teach it this week), comparing people with *plus/moins*.

**Homework assigned after Session 4:** 8-item adjective agreement practice sheet; 5–7 sentence written description of a real person (physical + personality, correct agreement); say description from memory; avoir vs. être extension drill.

## 5. Do Not Cut

| Block | Why it is protected |
|---|---|
| *Avoir* conjugation (S3 Slide 2) | *Nous avons / vous avez / ils ont* disappear without live drilling — students who read the table at home still miss them |
| *J'ai vs. Je suis* contrast | Addressed explicitly in S3 Slide 6 and runs through all of Week 2. Model the correct choice every time a student confuses them. |
| Speaking self-description (S3 Slide 7) | Students must produce a description before leaving Session 3 — same logic as the Week 1 introduction round |
| Feminine -e agreement drill (S4 Slide 3) | The habit must be established live with correction. Paper alone does not do it. |
| Group description round (S4 Slide 6) | Week's main speaking milestone — every student must describe a real person out loud |

## 6. Top Student Mistakes to Watch For

| Mistake | Quick correction cue | When to correct |
|---|---|---|
| *Je suis les yeux bleus* (être for features) | "Two different verbs: *J'ai* for things you have — eyes, hair. *Je suis* for what you're like — tall, funny." Correct every instance. | Starting Session 3; all week |
| *J'ai vingt-cinq* (no *ans*) | "*Ans* — the years — always comes after the number. *J'ai vingt-cinq **ans**.*" Correct every time. | Every occurrence |
| *Elle est patient* (missing feminine -e) | "*Patiente* — she needs the -e. Il est patient, elle est patiente." Model both in the same correction. | Every occurrence — this is the week's top priority |
| *Les yeux marrons* (adding -s to invariable) | "Marron never changes — no -s, no agreement. *Les yeux marron.*" Demonstrate once; correct after. | Once clearly; then briefly after |
| *Ils sont grand* (plural agreement missing) | "*Grands* — when they're plural, add -s. Inaudible in speech, but write it correctly." | In writing; not a speaking priority |
| *Sérieuxe* (wrong irregular feminine) | "The -eux ending changes completely: *sérieux → sérieuse.* It's not *-euse* added to *sérieux* — it replaces the whole ending." | On first occurrence |

## 7. Instructor Language to Use

| Moment | What to say |
|---|---|
| Introducing *avoir* | *"We have être from last week — 'to be.' Now we're adding avoir — 'to have.' In French, age is something you have, not something you are. So instead of 'I am thirty,' you say 'J'ai trente ans' — literally, 'I have thirty years.'"* |
| *J'ai vs. Je suis* rule of thumb | *"Here's the rule for this week: if you're talking about something you literally possess — eyes, hair — use avoir. If you're describing what you're like — tall, funny — use être."* |
| Adjective agreement | *"In French, an adjective is supposed to match the noun it describes — like a photograph fitting a frame. When the frame is feminine, the photo changes. Il est grand, elle est grande. I'll correct this every time until it becomes automatic."* |
| Before the description round (S4) | *"You now know enough to describe someone in French. I want each of you to describe a real person — a classmate, a family member, a famous person — in four sentences. I'll go first."* |
| Student freezes during description | "Start with their hair. *Il/Elle a les cheveux...* — just that one sentence." |
| Connecting Week 1 to Week 2 | *"Last week you introduced yourself. This week you describe yourself — and other people. Same être, same patterns, bigger picture."* |

**Formative check:** Filed at `week-02/week-02-formative-check.md`. Administer at the **start of Session 5** (first session of Week 3). Takes 6–8 minutes. Not graded.

---

## 9. Ready for Week 3

A student is ready to move into Week 3 when they can:

- [ ] Use *avoir* in all six forms without looking at a table
- [ ] Give their age correctly (*J'ai X ans*)
- [ ] Describe their own appearance in 3–4 sentences (*J'ai les cheveux... J'ai les yeux... Je suis...*)
- [ ] Apply the feminine -e to regular adjectives without prompting (*grand → grande, patient → patiente*)
- [ ] Use at least 4 personality adjectives with correct gender agreement

**Pronunciation and accent marks are not a gate for Week 3. Agreement is.**

**Looking ahead:** Week 3 introduces regular -er verbs and daily routine. Students who are uncertain about *avoir* forms will struggle to compare *j'ai* with *je parle* — both are core structures from Week 3 onward. If *nous avons / ils ont* are shaky after the formative check, drill them for 3 minutes at the start of Session 5 before moving forward.
