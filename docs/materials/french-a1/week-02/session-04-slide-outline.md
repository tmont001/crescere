# French A1 — Session 4 Slide Outline
## Personality Adjectives and Adjective Agreement

**Session length:** 60 minutes
**Session-end goal:** Students can describe a real person using 3–4 personality adjectives with correct masculine/feminine agreement and 3–4 physical description adjectives from Session 3.

> **Pacing note:** The group description round (Slide 6) is non-negotiable. Every student must describe a person out loud before the session ends. Trim Slides 3 or 4 before cutting it.

---

## Core 60-Minute Plan

### Slide 1 — Warm-Up: Homework Sharing (8 min)
**Purpose:** Activate Session 3 vocabulary; catch agreement errors before adding new content.
**Teacher notes:**
- Ask 3–4 students to share their written descriptions from the Session 3 homework. If a student did not write one, ask them to describe themselves in 2 sentences on the spot.
- Listen for: *J'ai/Je suis* confusion; missing *ans* after age; color adjective agreement (flag but do not lecture yet — that's Slides 3–4).
- Keep this moving. Activate, don't review.
**Student activity:** 3–4 students share their descriptions. Group listens and notices any French they did not use themselves.

---

### Slide 2 — Personality Adjectives: The Core Eight (10 min)
**Purpose:** Students know 8 personality adjectives and can use them in a sentence.
**Teacher notes:**
- Introduce each adjective with a context sentence, not just a translation. Students remember meaning better from usage than from word lists.
  - *sympa:* C'est quelqu'un de très sympa — toujours content d'aider.
  - *drôle:* Elle est drôle — elle fait rire tout le monde.
  - *sérieux:* Mon professeur est très sérieux en classe, mais il est drôle aussi.
  - *timide:* Au début, j'étais timide en classe de langue.
  - *bavard:* Tu es bavard — tu parles beaucoup !
  - *gentil:* Elle est très gentille — elle aide ses amis.
  - *patient:* Un bon professeur est patient.
  - *impatient:* Je suis parfois impatient quand ça prend du temps.
- After each word: choral repetition. Then pair discussion: is this word true of you? *Tu es timide ? — Non, je suis plutôt bavard.*
**Student activity:** Choral repetition of each adjective → pair discussion: which ones describe you?

---

### Slide 3 — Adjective Agreement: Gender (10 min)
**Purpose:** Students can change an adjective from masculine to feminine form.
**Teacher notes:**
- Start with what they already know: *grand / grande.* They have heard this. Now generalize.
- **The regular pattern:** add -e to the masculine form.
  - grand → grande, petit → petite, patient → patiente, impatient → impatiente, bavard → bavarde
- **Already ends in -e:** no change needed.
  - timide, drôle, sympathique, calme, optimiste, pessimiste
- **Irregular patterns (introduce with examples, not a rules chart):**
  - *-eux → -euse:* sérieux → sérieuse, nerveux → nerveuse, paresseux → paresseuse
  - *-if → -ive:* sportif → sportive, créatif → créative, actif → active
  - *-il → -ille:* gentil → gentille (doubled l)
- **Invariable:** sympa, drôle (already -e)
- Practice immediately: instructor says a masculine form, students give the feminine. *Sérieux ?* → *Sérieuse.* *Sportif ?* → *Sportive.* Do 8–10 rapid-fire.
**Student activity:** Instructor calls masculine form → students call feminine form (rapid drill, 2 minutes).

---

### Slide 4 — Adjective Agreement: Number (5 min)
**Purpose:** Students understand that adjectives also agree in plural.
**Teacher notes:**
- Keep this brief. The singular gender agreement is the priority this week; number agreement is important but secondary.
- **The rule:** add -s to the singular form (masculine or feminine).
  - grand → grands, grande → grandes
  - sérieux → sérieux (already ends in -x — no change); sérieuse → sérieuses
- **Spoken French:** plural agreement is usually inaudible — *il est grand* and *ils sont grands* sound identical. This matters for writing, not immediately for speaking.
- Say: "For now, add -s when you write plural adjectives. In speech, it won't change what you hear — but your eye should know it's there."
**Student activity:** 5 quick written sentences — students add the correct adjective ending for the gender/number given. Check together.

---

### Slide 5 — Building the Description (15 min)
**Purpose:** Students prepare a 4–5 sentence description of a real person combining physical and personality vocabulary.
**Teacher notes:**
- Each student chooses one real person: a family member, a classmate, a friend, or a well-known figure.
- Display the model structure:
  1. *[Name] est [nationality/profession].* (optional) 
  2. *Il/Elle a [age] ans.*
  3. *Il/Elle a les cheveux [color + length]. Il/Elle a les yeux [color].*
  4. *Il/Elle est [height].*
  5. *Il/Elle est [personality adjective 1] et [personality adjective 2].*
- Give students 4–5 minutes to write or note their description.
- Pairs: one student describes their person; partner listens and asks one question in French. Switch.
- Walk the virtual room — listen for agreement errors, take notes.
**Student activity:** Choose person → write 4–5 sentences → pair practice with one French question each.

---

### Slide 6 — Group Description Round (8 min)
**Purpose:** Every student describes their chosen person out loud to the group.
**Teacher notes:**
- Every student takes a turn. Notes are allowed — do not require memorization.
- Correct one item per student: prioritize agreement errors (missing -e on feminine adjective) above all else. Save other corrections for the group debrief after.
- After everyone shares: group debrief — pick 2–3 patterns you heard corrected and briefly address them with the whole group.
- Close with: "By the end of this week you can describe people in French. That is a real thing."
**Student activity:** Each student describes their chosen person. Instructor corrects one item per student.

---

### Slide 7 — Wrap-Up (4 min)
**Purpose:** Homework preview; close positively.
**Teacher notes:** Say: "The homework is describing one more person in writing — a family member or a well-known figure. Use the patterns from today. You'll also see a short adjective agreement practice sheet."
**Student activity:** Students note homework. Optional: one French adjective that describes someone in the room before logging off.

---

## Cut First If Running Behind

1. **Slide 4 (number agreement):** Cover in 2 minutes verbally — "add -s for plural" — and assign the written practice as homework.
2. **Slide 3 (agreement drill):** Reduce to just the regular pattern (-e) and *sérieux/sérieuse* as the only irregular. Assign -if/-ive and -il/-ille as homework reading.
3. **Slide 5 (building the description):** Skip pair practice. Students write their 4 sentences and share directly with the group.
4. Do not cut Slide 6 (group description round).

---

## Extension for Fast Cohorts

- **BAGS adjective placement (5 min):** Introduce the concept that some adjectives come before the noun rather than after. Give the four categories (Beauty, Age, Goodness, Size) with examples: *un beau garçon, un petit café, un bon film, une jeune femme.* Note: this is a preview — do not test it.
- **Comparing people (4 min):** Teach *plus + adjective + que* (more ... than) and *moins + adjective + que* (less ... than). Students make one comparison sentence: *Mon frère est plus grand que moi.*
- **Additional personality adjectives (3 min):** Students look up the French for one personality adjective that describes someone they know but is not on the list.

---

## Move to Homework If Needed

- Adjective number agreement written practice
- -if/-ive and -il/-ille irregular patterns (mention them; let students study the pattern from the vocabulary reference)
- BAGS adjective placement (Week 4 topic — mention it exists, do not teach it this week)
- Comparing people with *plus/moins que*
