# French A1 — Week 2 Formative Check

**Administered:** Start of Session 5 (first session of Week 3)
**Time needed:** 6–8 minutes total
**Purpose:** Quick checkpoint — not a test. Tells the instructor which items need brief review before introducing -er verbs and daily routine.

---

## Oral Prompts (5 questions — ~1 min per student for the full set)

| # | Prompt | Target response | What to listen for |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | *Tu as quel âge ?* | *J'ai [X] ans.* | Correct use of *avoir* + *ans*; not *Je suis [X].* |
| 2 | Describe your hair in French. | *J'ai les cheveux [color + length].* | Correct structure; *cheveux* plural; reasonable color word |
| 3 | *Comment est-il ?* (pointing to a male student or image) | *Il est [adjective].* | Agreement — masculine form used |
| 4 | *Comment est-elle ?* (pointing to a female student or image) | *Elle est [adjective].* | Agreement — feminine -e present |
| 5 | Give me the six forms of *avoir* — go. | j'ai, tu as, il/elle a, nous avons, vous avez, ils/elles ont | All six; *j'ai* not *je ai*; *ils ont* with liaison |

---

## Written Prompts (5 questions — students write answers, ~3 min)

Paste into the session chat or distribute at the start of Session 5.

**Instructions to students:** Answer in French. Short answers only.

1. Write: "She has long brown hair."
2. Write: "I am 28 years old."
3. What is the feminine form of *sérieux*?
4. Write: "He is patient and kind." (Use *gentil*.)
5. Write: "They (masc. plural) are tall."

**Answer key:**
1. *Elle a les cheveux bruns et longs.* (accept any correct word order for color + length)
2. *J'ai vingt-huit ans.*
3. *sérieuse*
4. *Il est patient et gentil.*
5. *Ils sont grands.*

---

## Instructor Scoring Notes

Not graded. Use it to make one of three decisions:

| Result | What it means | What to do |
|---|---|---|
| Students handle all 5 oral prompts without hesitation | Week 2 content is secure | Move into Week 3 immediately |
| Students stumble on 1–2 prompts | Minor gaps; expected | Quick 5-minute review at the start of Session 5 |
| Students stumble on 3+ prompts | Week 2 needs more work | Add a 10-minute review block to Sessions 5 and 6 before introducing -er verbs |

**Most common Week 2 gaps:**
- Missing *ans* after the age number → give it once more, correct firmly from now on
- Missing feminine -e (*elle est patient* instead of *patiente*) → one quick drill, then correct every instance throughout Week 3
- *Je suis les yeux bleus* (using être instead of avoir for features) → reteach the rule, add it to the warm-up for Sessions 5–6

---

## Ready to Move On

A student is ready for Week 3 content when they can:

- [ ] Use *avoir* in all six forms without looking at a table
- [ ] Give their age correctly (*J'ai X ans*)
- [ ] Describe their own appearance in 3–4 sentences
- [ ] Apply feminine -e agreement to regular adjectives without prompting
- [ ] Use at least 4 personality adjectives with correct gender agreement

Pronunciation and accent marks are not a gate for Week 3. Agreement is.
