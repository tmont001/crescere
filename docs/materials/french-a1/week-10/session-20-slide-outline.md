# French A1 — Session 20 Slide Outline
## Opinions and Preferences: Aimer, Adorer, Détester, Préférer

**Session length:** 48 minutes (on plan) — can run 60 min for slower cohorts
**Session-end goal:** Every student can sustain a 3-minute free-time preference conversation — sharing opinions, asking questions, and expressing a comparison using *préférer* and at least one *plus/moins que.*

> **Pacing note:** The free-time conversation round (Slide 6) is non-negotiable. Every student participates in a real extended exchange before the session ends. Compress the comparisons block (Slide 5, to 3 min) before cutting Slide 6.

---

## Core Plan

### Slide 1 — Warm-Up: Jouer à / Faire de Recall (5 min)
**Purpose:** Re-activate Session 19 content before building opinion language on top.
**Teacher notes:**
- Each student gives one sentence from their homework leisure description.
- Quickly correct any *joue du* or *fait au* errors.
- Preview: "Today we learn to say what you love, hate, and prefer — and how to compare activities. After this session, you'll be able to have a full five-minute conversation about free time."
**Student activity:** One *jouer à / faire de* sentence each.

---

### Slide 2 — Opinion Verbs + Definite Article (8 min)
**Purpose:** Students learn *adorer* and *détester* and reinforce the definite article rule after opinion verbs.
**Teacher notes:**
- Students already know *aimer* from Week 3 regular -er verbs. This slide extends the set.
- Write the four verbs on a scale: *adorer → aimer → ne pas aimer → détester*
- Examples with both noun and infinitive:
  - *J'adore le tennis.* / *J'adore jouer au tennis.*
  - *J'aime la natation.* / *J'aime nager.*
  - *Je n'aime pas le golf.* / *Je n'aime pas jouer au golf.*
  - *Je déteste les jeux vidéo.* / *Je déteste jouer aux jeux vidéo.*
- **Definite article rule — address immediately:** "After opinion verbs about general likes, use *le/la/les* — not *du/de la.* 'J'aime **le** cinéma' — I like cinema in general. Not 'j'aime **du** cinéma' — that would be partitive, meaning a quantity of cinema. These are two completely different things."
- Quick round: each student gives one opinion sentence with *adorer* or *détester.*
**Student activity:** Give one *j'adore* sentence and one *je déteste* sentence.

---

### Slide 3 — Préférer Conjugation (8 min)
**Purpose:** Students learn *préférer* with the accent change and can use it to express preferences.
**Teacher notes:**
- *Préférer* is a new verb, not seen before. Write the full conjugation:

  | je | tu | il/elle | nous | vous | ils/elles |
  |---|---|---|---|---|---|
  | **préfère** | **préfères** | **préfère** | préférons | préférez | **préfèrent** |

- Address the accent change: "The é in the infinitive becomes è in the stressed forms — je, tu, il, ils. In the unstressed forms — nous, vous — the é stays. Write it down: *je préfère, tu préfères, il préfère / nous préférons, vous préférez, ils préfèrent.*"
- Two key uses:
  - *Je préfère le tennis au golf.* — I prefer tennis to golf. (*préférer A au/à la B*)
  - *Je préfère faire du vélo plutôt que de courir.* — I prefer cycling to running. (infinitive comparison)
- Quick round: "What do you prefer — sport or cultural activity?" Each student gives one *je préfère* sentence.
**Student activity:** Give one *je préfère A à/au B* sentence.

---

### Slide 4 — Cultural and Leisure Activities (5 min)
**Purpose:** Students expand vocabulary to cultural activities for a richer conversation round.
**Teacher notes:**
- Brief vocabulary — these are mostly known or guessable:
  - *aller au cinéma, aller au théâtre, aller au musée, aller à un concert*
  - *regarder des films, lire, écouter de la musique, voyager, cuisiner*
- Quick round: students say one cultural activity they do or don't do.
- Note: *aller au cinéma* vs. *faire du cinéma* — going to the cinema vs. being an actor. One student always makes this joke; be ready.
**Student activity:** One cultural activity sentence each.

---

### Slide 5 — Comparisons (5 min)
**Purpose:** Students learn 3 comparison structures and can use them in conversation.
**Teacher notes:**
- Introduce concisely:
  - *J'aime le tennis **plus que** le golf.* — I like tennis more than golf.
  - *Il fait **moins de** sport que moi.* — He does less sport than me.
  - *Je préfère la montagne **à** la mer.* — I prefer the mountains to the sea.
- Quick drill — students complete the comparison:
  - *J'aime le vélo plus que...*
  - *Je préfère le cinéma à...*
- Do not over-engineer this. Two patterns used naturally beat six patterns used with anxiety.
**Student activity:** Students complete 2 comparison starters.

---

### Slide 6 — Free-Time Conversation Round (12 min)
**Purpose:** Every student sustains a 3-minute free-time preference conversation.
**Teacher notes:**
- Conversation structure (students improvise from this framework):
  - A asks: *"Qu'est-ce que tu fais comme loisirs?"*
  - B describes: 2–3 leisure habits with frequency adverbs
  - A asks a follow-up: *"Tu aimes ça? Depuis quand?"* or *"Tu préfères ça à quoi?"*
  - B answers + opinion + comparison
  - B asks A the same
- Each pair or instructor-student exchange runs 2–3 minutes. Every student speaks.
- Minimum elements in each student's output:
  - [ ] At least 2 *jouer à / faire de* sentences
  - [ ] At least 1 frequency adverb
  - [ ] At least 1 opinion verb (*adorer, aimer, détester*)
  - [ ] At least 1 *préférer* sentence or comparison
- Correct one item per student. Common targets: *j'aime du cinéma* (→ *j'aime **le** cinéma*); *je préfere* (→ *je **préfère***); *ils préfèrent* (→ *ils **préfèrent*** — confirm accent on board).
**Student activity:** Each student participates in a full 2–3 minute leisure exchange.

---

### Slide 7 — Wrap-Up: Week 10 Close (5 min)
**Purpose:** Assign homework; preview Week 11.
**Teacher notes:**
- Preview Week 11: "Next week we focus on health and the body — *avoir mal à la tête, j'ai de la fièvre, je ne me sens pas bien.* Pharmacy scenario and, in Session 22, a doctor's appointment."
- Homework: written preference paragraph + *préférer* conjugation.
- Remind students: the Week 10 formative check will ask them to describe leisure habits and use *préférer* correctly.
**Student activity:** Note homework.

## Cut First If Running Behind

1. **Slide 5 (comparisons):** Reduce to just *plus que* — one pattern only; skip *moins que* and *autant que.*
2. **Slide 4 (cultural activities):** Reduce to 4 items; skip *cuisiner* and *voyager.*
3. **Slide 3 (préférer):** Reduce drill to 2 examples; move written conjugation to homework.
4. Do not cut Slide 6 (conversation round).

---

## Extension for Fast Cohorts

- **Superlatives (light preview):** *Mon sport préféré, c'est le tennis.* — using *préféré(e)* as an adjective. Simple and useful.
- **Depuis + opinion:** *J'adore le cinéma depuis l'enfance.* — Previewing *depuis* (Week 11 grammar).
- **Expressing frequency with opinion:** *J'aime tellement la natation que j'y vais tous les jours.* — connecting *tellement que* informally.
- **Asking for a recommendation:** *Qu'est-ce que tu me recommandes comme film / comme sport?*

---

## Move to Homework If Needed

- *Autant que* comparison
- Written preference paragraph comparing 3 activities
- *Préférer + infinitif* vs. *préférer + nom* extended practice
- Negative opinion sentences (*je n'aime pas + reason*)
