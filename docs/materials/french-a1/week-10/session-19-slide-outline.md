# French A1 — Session 19 Slide Outline
## Sports and Activities: Jouer à and Faire de

**Session length:** 55 minutes (on plan) — can run 60 min for slower cohorts
**Session-end goal:** Every student can describe their sports and leisure habits in 5–6 sentences, correctly using *jouer à* (with contractions) and *faire de* (with contractions), with at least two frequency adverbs.

> **Pacing note:** The speaking round (Slide 6) is non-negotiable. Every student speaks before the session ends. The *jouer à* vs. *faire de* sorting drill (Slide 4) can be compressed or moved to homework if time is short.

---

## Core 60-Minute Plan

### Slide 1 — Warm-Up: Week 9 Close (5 min)
**Purpose:** Close the Week 9 loop; activate modal verbs before switching to leisure.
**Teacher notes:**
- Ask each student one sentence from Week 9: *"Qu'est-ce que tu vas faire ce week-end?"* or *"Qu'est-ce que tu dois faire cette semaine?"*
- If Week 9 formative check showed widespread *devoir* errors: 30-second group reminder. No re-teaching.
- Preview: "This week we talk about hobbies and free time — one of the most useful conversations you'll ever have in French."
**Student activity:** One planning sentence each from Week 9 vocabulary.

---

### Slide 2 — Jouer à (10 min)
**Purpose:** Students learn and can produce *jouer à* with the correct article contractions.
**Teacher notes:**
- Write *jouer à* on the board. Say: "Jouer à — playing against someone. A sport, a game, something competitive."
- Build the contraction rule visually:
  - *à + le = au* → *Je joue **au** tennis.*
  - *à + les = aux* → *Je joue **aux** échecs.*
  - *à + la = à la* → *Je joue **à la** pétanque.*
- Introduce 8 sports/games with their gender:
  - *le foot, le tennis, le basket, le golf, le rugby, le volley, les échecs (m. pl.), les cartes (f. pl.)*
- After each: students repeat + say *je joue au/aux* and then the negative *je ne joue pas au/aux.*
- Quick round: "What sports do you play?" Each student gives one true answer.
- Flag: *Je joue du tennis* — this is the most common error. Say it wrong on purpose, then correct it. "*Pas joue **du** — on dit joue **au**. Le 'à' ne disparaît pas ici.*"
**Student activity:** Give one true *je joue à* sentence; identify the correct contraction for 4 sports.

---

### Slide 3 — Faire de (10 min)
**Purpose:** Students learn and can produce *faire de* with the correct article contractions, including the negation rule.
**Teacher notes:**
- Write *faire de* on the board. Say: "Faire de — doing an activity, usually solo. Yoga, natation, vélo."
- Build the contraction rule visually (contrast with *jouer à*):
  - *de + le = du* → *Je fais **du** vélo.*
  - *de + la = de la* → *Je fais **de la** natation.*
  - *de + l' = de l'* → *Je fais **de l'**escalade.*
- Introduce 8 activities:
  - *le vélo, le yoga, le sport, le footing, la natation, la randonnée, la danse, l'escalade*
- After each: students repeat + say *je fais du/de la/de l'.*
- **Negation rule — teach immediately:** "After ne...pas, the article disappears — same rule as all negation. *Je ne fais pas **de** sport.* Not *pas du sport.* Same rule you learned in Week 6 with food — *pas de café, pas de lait.* Same here."
- Negation drill: 3 items. Students turn *je fais du yoga* → *je ne fais pas de yoga.*
**Student activity:** Give one true *je fais de* sentence + one negative sentence.

---

### Slide 4 — Jouer à vs. Faire de: Sorting Drill (5 min)
**Purpose:** Students consolidate which verb applies to each sport/activity.
**Teacher notes:**
- Quick drill — instructor says a sport/activity; students call out *jouer à* or *faire de.*
  - *le tennis* → *jouer au tennis* ✓
  - *le yoga* → *faire du yoga* ✓
  - *les cartes* → *jouer aux cartes* ✓
  - *la natation* → *faire de la natation* ✓
  - *le golf* → *jouer au golf* ✓
  - *le vélo* → *faire du vélo* ✓
- Memory rule: "Opponent / score / team → *jouer à.* Solo / physical / pursuit → *faire de.*"
- Do not over-engineer this. 4–5 items is enough; students will encounter edge cases but the core is simple.
**Student activity:** Sort 6 items → identify verb + produce full sentence with contraction.

---

### Slide 5 — Frequency Adverbs (7 min)
**Purpose:** Students learn 5 core frequency adverbs and can use them to make their leisure descriptions sound natural.
**Teacher notes:**
- Introduce adverbs on a scale from always to never:
  - *toujours → souvent → quelquefois → rarement → (presque) jamais*
- After each adverb: students produce one sentence combining *jouer à* or *faire de* with the adverb.
  - *Je fais toujours du sport le matin.*
  - *Elle joue souvent au tennis.*
  - *Je ne joue jamais aux cartes.*
- Flag *jamais* placement: "Jamais requires *ne* before the verb, just like *pas.* But notice — we say *je ne joue **jamais***, not *je ne joue pas jamais.* Just one negative word at a time."
- Quick: each student gives one sentence with a frequency adverb + *jouer à* or *faire de.*
**Student activity:** One frequency adverb sentence each.

---

### Slide 6 — Speaking Round: My Leisure Habits (12 min)
**Purpose:** Every student describes their sports and leisure habits in 5–6 connected sentences.
**Teacher notes:**
- Each student speaks for 60–90 seconds. Notes allowed for vocabulary; *jouer à, faire de,* and frequency adverbs from memory.
- Minimum required elements per student:
  - [ ] At least 2 *jouer à* OR *faire de* sentences (with correct contraction)
  - [ ] At least 1 negative sentence (*je ne fais pas de / je ne joue pas au*)
  - [ ] At least 2 frequency adverbs
  - [ ] At least 1 cultural or personal detail (*c'est mon sport préféré / depuis cinq ans / avec des amis*)
- Correct one item per student. Common targets: *joue du* (→ *joue au*); *fais au* (→ *fais du*); *pas du sport* (→ *pas de sport*).
- After all students: note 2 patterns.
**Student activity:** Each student delivers a 5–6 sentence leisure habits description.

---

### Slide 7 — Wrap-Up (6 min)
**Purpose:** Assign homework; preview Session 20.
**Teacher notes:**
- Preview Session 20: "Next session we add opinion language — how to say you love, hate, or prefer activities. And a new verb: *préférer.* Plus: how to make comparisons."
- Homework: written leisure description + contraction practice.
**Student activity:** Note homework.

## Cut First If Running Behind

1. **Slide 4 (sorting drill):** Cut to 3 items; move full drill to homework.
2. **Slide 3 (faire de):** Reduce to 5 activities; skip *l'escalade* and *l'équitation.*
3. **Slide 5 (frequency adverbs):** Cover *toujours, souvent, jamais* only (3 instead of 5).
4. Do not cut Slide 6 (speaking round).

---

## Extension for Fast Cohorts

- **How long have you been playing?** *Depuis quand tu joues?* — *Je joue au tennis depuis cinq ans.* (*Depuis* is introduced in Week 11 formally but is worth previewing here.)
- **Music and art verbs:** *Jouer de la guitare / du piano / de la flûte* — note: *jouer de + instrument* (different from sports). Say clearly: for instruments, it's *de*, not *à.*
- **Compound leisure descriptions:** *Le week-end, je fais souvent du vélo et quelquefois je joue au tennis, mais je ne fais presque jamais de yoga parce que je n'aime pas ça.*

- *Jouer de + instrument* distinction
