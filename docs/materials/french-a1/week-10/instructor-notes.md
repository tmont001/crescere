# French A1 — Week 10 Instructor Notes

**Sessions:** 19 and 20
**Theme:** Hobbies and Leisure

---

## Week Objective

By the end of Week 10, students can:
- Use *jouer à* correctly with sports and games — including *au, à la, aux* contractions
- Use *faire de* correctly with activities — including *du, de la, de l'* contractions
- Apply the negation rule for *faire de* (*je ne fais pas de sport, pas de natation*)
- Use 5+ frequency adverbs (*toujours, souvent, quelquefois, rarement, jamais*) in speech
- Express opinions about leisure using *aimer, adorer, détester, préférer*
- Conjugate *préférer* correctly with the accent change in stressed syllables
- Make simple comparisons (*plus que, moins que, préférer A à B*)
- Sustain a 3-minute free-time conversation with a partner

**Relationship to the curriculum:** This is exactly the Week 10 content specified. Session 19 = *jouer à / faire de* + frequency adverbs. Session 20 = opinion verbs + *préférer* + comparisons. No deferral from Week 9 carried into this week.

**Prior knowledge students bring:**
- *Aimer* — already a known -er verb from Week 3. Students say *j'aime* automatically; Week 10 extends this to *j'adore / je déteste / je préfère.*
- *Faire* — full conjugation known from Week 8. *Faire de* is a new use of a known verb.
- *Aller au* — article contraction *à + le = au* from Week 5 (directions). *Jouer au tennis* extends the same contraction.
- *Du/de la/de l'* — partitive articles from Week 6 (food). *Faire du vélo/de la natation/de l'escalade* reuses the same contractions students already know.

## Common Student Mistakes — Week 10

| Mistake | Correction | When to address |
|---|---|---|
| *Je joue du tennis* (using *de* with *jouer*) | *Je joue **au** tennis.* Sports with an opponent → *jouer à* (with contractions). *De* is for *faire de.* | Session 19 immediately |
| *Je fais au yoga* (using *à* with *faire*) | *Je fais **du** yoga.* Activities → *faire de* (with contractions). *À* is for *jouer à.* | Session 19 immediately |
| *Je ne fais pas du sport* (keeping *du* after negation) | *Je ne fais pas **de** sport.* Same rule as partitives: after negation, article collapses to *de.* | Session 19 *faire de* block |
| *Je jouais au...* (imperfect — too early) | Stay in present tense at A1. Gently redirect: *"Tu joues au tennis?"* | If it arises |
| *Je préfere la montagne* (wrong accent) | *Je **préfère**.* The accent changes: è in stressed syllables (*je, tu, il, ils*); é in unstressed (*nous, vous*). Write it on the board. | Session 20 préférer block |
| *J'aime du cinéma* (partitive after opinion verb) | *J'aime **le** cinéma.* Opinion verbs about general likes → definite article. Partitive was for unspecified quantities (food/drink). | Session 20 opinion verbs block |
| *Je préfère le ski à la mer* (wrong comparison) | *Je préfère **la montagne** à la mer.* The first element in the comparison needs to be a noun (not an activity category). Or: *Je préfère faire du ski plutôt que d'aller à la mer.* | Session 20 if it arises |
| *Je déteste les jeux vidéo mais je joue toujours* (contradiction) | Grammatically fine — encourage! These productive conflicts are good conversation material. | No correction needed |

## Suggested Instructor Language

- **For *jouer à*:** *"Jouer à — playing a sport or game against someone. The à contracts: à + le = au, à + les = aux, but à + la stays à la. So: 'Je joue au tennis' (au because tennis is masculine); 'je joue aux échecs' (aux because échecs is plural). The same contraction you used in Week 5 for directions — à + le = au."*

- **For *faire de*:** *"Faire de — doing an activity, usually physical, usually solo. The de contracts: de + le = du, de + les = des, but de + la stays de la. 'Je fais du vélo' (du because vélo is masculine); 'je fais de la natation' (de la because natation is feminine). You know these contractions from partitive articles in Week 6 — same du, same de la."*

- **For negation of *faire de*:** *"After ne...pas, de becomes just de with no article: 'Je ne fais pas de sport.' Not 'pas du sport' — the partitive collapses to de, same rule as all negation. Same as 'Je n'ai pas de café' in Week 6."*

- **For *préférer*:** *"Préférer has a spelling change. In the stressed syllables — je, tu, il, ils — the é becomes è: je préfère, tu préfères, il préfère, ils préfèrent. In the unstressed syllables — nous, vous — the é stays: nous préférons, vous préférez. Write it and say it until the pattern feels natural."*

- **For opinion verbs + definite article:** *"When you say what you like or love in general — a whole category — use le/la/les. 'J'aime le cinéma' — I like cinema (as a thing). Not 'j'aime du cinéma' — that would mean you like some cinema, like a quantity. Opinion verbs take the full definite article."*

## What to Skip If Time Is Tight

- *Autant que* comparison (the most nuanced — save if the group isn't ready)
- Extended cultural activities vocabulary beyond 5–6 items
- *Préférer + infinitif vs. préférer + nom* distinction in one session
- Detailed regional note on *pétanque* vs. *boules* — mention once and move on
- The difference between *quelquefois* and *parfois* — treat as synonyms at A1

→ Students write a paragraph comparing two leisure activities using *préférer* and *plus que/moins que*; share at start of Session 21

## Cultural Context for Instructors

- **Le sport in France:** Sport is a broad word in French covering both watching and doing. *Je fais du sport* = I play sport / exercise. *Je suis sportif/sportive* = I'm sporty. *Le foot* is the dominant sport at both national and neighbourhood level. Pétanque is strongly associated with the South and older men in village squares, but is actually played across France. *Le vélo* (cycling) has a special cultural status in France — *le Tour de France* is a major national event.
- **Faire de la musique:** Making music in France — especially *apprendre un instrument* — is associated with the conservatoire system. Saying *je fais de la guitare* is natural and common; *jouer de la guitare* is equally correct and more formal. At A1, either form is fine.
- **Cultural activities:** French students can say *je vais au cinéma / je vais au musée* — using *aller à* for places. Note this is different from *faire du cinéma* (to be in films, to act) vs. *aller au cinéma* (to watch films at the cinema).
