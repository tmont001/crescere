## Partitive Articles

Partitive articles mean "some" — they refer to an unspecified quantity of something. French requires them where English often uses no article at all.

| Before… | Partitive | Example | English |
|---|---|---|---|
| Masculine singular noun | **du** | Je prends du café. | I'm having (some) coffee. |
| Feminine singular noun | **de la** | Je voudrais de la tarte. | I'd like (some) tart. |
| Noun starting with vowel or h muet | **de l'** | Elle prend de l'eau. | She's having (some) water. |
| Plural noun | **des** | Il mange des croissants. | He eats (some) croissants. |

### Partitive in Negation → de

After negation, all partitive articles collapse to **de** (or **d'** before a vowel):

| Affirmative | Negative |
|---|---|
| Je prends du café. | Je ne prends **pas de** café. |
| Elle mange de la tarte. | Elle ne mange **pas de** tarte. |
| Il boit de l'eau. | Il ne boit **pas d'**eau. |
| Nous prenons des croissants. | Nous ne prenons **pas de** croissants. |

**Rule:** *du / de la / de l' / des* → *de* after *ne...pas.* No exceptions in standard usage.

## Vouloir — Conjugation

*Vouloir* (to want) is an irregular verb. It is introduced this week as the basis for *je voudrais* (polite conditional — introduced as a fixed formula, not yet a full conditional tense).

| Subject | Vouloir |
|---|---|
| je | veux |
| tu | veux |
| il / elle / on | veut |
| nous | voulons |
| vous | voulez |
| ils / elles | veulent |

**Key note:** *Je voudrais* (I would like) is the polite form used in cafés and restaurants. Students learn it as a formula this week. The full conditional tense comes in A2.

## Pouvoir — Conjugation (Introduction)

*Pouvoir* (to be able to / can) is introduced this week.

| Subject | Pouvoir |
|---|---|
| je | peux |
| tu | peux |
| il / elle / on | peut |
| nous | pouvons |
| vous | pouvez |
| ils / elles | peuvent |

**Useful restaurant phrases:**
- *Vous pouvez me recommander quelque chose?* — Can you recommend something?
- *Je ne peux pas manger de [ingredient].* — I can't eat [ingredient].

Full modal use (*pouvoir + infinitif*) is developed in Week 9.

## Prendre — For Ordering

*Prendre* (to take) is used naturally in food/drink contexts to mean "to have" or "to order."

| Subject | Prendre |
|---|---|
| je | prends |
| tu | prends |
| il / elle / on | prend |
| nous | prenons |
| vous | prenez |
| ils / elles | prennent |

*Je prends le menu à 20 euros.* — I'll have the set menu at €20.

Full irregular verb study of *prendre* is in Week 8, Session 16. Introduce it as a useful ordering phrase this week without drilling the full paradigm.

## Pronunciation Notes

- **du / de la / de l':** In fast speech, *du* often sounds like /dy/, *de la* like /dla/. The liaison in *des enfants* (linking z-sound) is mandatory.
- **Croissant:** /kwasɑ̃/ — the *oi* sounds like /wa/, the final *nt* is silent.
- **L'addition:** /ladisjɔ̃/ — stress the final syllable; it rhymes with "mission."
- **Je voudrais:** /ʒə vudʁɛ/ — the *r* is French (uvular); the final *s* is silent.
- **Voulons / voulez:** the *ou* sounds like /u/ (as in "you"), not /ɒ/ (as in English "would").
