# French A1 — Session 12 Slide Outline
## Restaurant Scenario: Vouloir, Pouvoir, and the Full Meal

**Session length:** 55–60 minutes
**Session-end goal:** Every student can navigate a full French restaurant interaction — arriving, ordering three courses, asking questions, and paying — using *vouloir, pouvoir, je voudrais,* and partitive articles.

> **Pacing note:** The role play (Slide 7) is the session's core deliverable. Everything else serves it. If you run short on time anywhere, compress Slide 4 (restaurant vocabulary detail) or Slide 5 (cultural notes) — never the role play. Every student plays both the customer and the waiter.

---

## Core 60-Minute Plan

### Slide 1 — Warm-Up: Partitive Recall (5 min)
**Purpose:** Confirm partitive and negation from Session 11 before extending into the full restaurant.
**Teacher notes:**
- Quick round: instructor gives a food or drink item; student says the sentence with the correct partitive.
  - *café* → *Je prends **du** café.*
  - *bière* → *Je voudrais **de la** bière.*
  - *eau* → *Elle boit **de l'**eau.*
  - *croissants* → *Ils mangent **des** croissants.*
- Then: ask 3 students for a negative version: *Je ne prends **pas de** café.*
- Quick tempo — 30 seconds per item maximum. Goal: automaticity.
**Student activity:** 4 affirmative partitive prompts → 3 negative prompts from selected students.

---

### Slide 2 — Vouloir: Full Conjugation (8 min)
**Purpose:** Students can conjugate *vouloir* fully and understand the relationship to *je voudrais.*
**Teacher notes:**
- Display the table: *veux / veux / veut / voulons / voulez / veulent.*
- Note the pattern: irregular stem (*voul- → veu-*) in all forms except *nous* and *vous.* This is the same pattern as *pouvoir* — flag it.
- Choral drill × 3: slow → normal → fast.
- Address *je voudrais* directly: "You've been using *je voudrais* since Session 11. It comes from *vouloir* — it's the conditional form ('I would like'). You'll study the conditional fully in A2. For now: *vouloir* → present tense; *je voudrais* → polite ordering formula."
- Spot check: each student gives one form at random.
**Student activity:** Choral drill × 3 → spot-check (one form per student).

---

### Slide 3 — Pouvoir: Introduction (7 min)
**Purpose:** Students learn *pouvoir* as a second high-frequency modal, with immediate restaurant applications.
**Teacher notes:**
- Display the table: *peux / peux / peut / pouvons / pouvez / peuvent.*
- Note: "Same irregular pattern as *vouloir* — *pou-* → *peu-* in the boot forms."
- Introduce 3 restaurant-relevant phrases:
  - *Vous pouvez me recommander quelque chose?* — Can you recommend something?
  - *Je ne peux pas manger de noix.* — I can't eat nuts.
  - *Vous pouvez m'apporter l'addition?* — Can you bring me the bill?
- Do not drill the full paradigm this week. Recognize + use these 3 phrases is enough. Full modal study is Week 9.
**Student activity:** Repeat the 3 restaurant phrases; say one personalised *je ne peux pas* sentence (allergy, preference, or dietary restriction).

---

### Slide 4 — Restaurant Vocabulary (8 min)
**Purpose:** Students learn the vocabulary they need for the role play: menu structure, key phrases, essential words.
**Teacher notes:**
- Review the critical false friend: *l'entrée = starter* (not main course). *Le plat principal = main course.*
- Menu structure: *le menu* (set price) vs. *la carte* (à la carte). Model: *"Je vais prendre le menu à vingt euros — l'entrée salade, le plat poulet, et le dessert tarte au chocolat."*
- Key ordering phrases:
  - *Je voudrais / Je vais prendre / Je prends*
  - *Pour l'entrée... Comme plat principal... Et comme dessert...*
  - *C'est quoi exactement?* — What exactly is it?
  - *L'addition, s'il vous plaît.*
- Key waiter phrases (for students playing the waiter role):
  - *Vous avez réservé?* — Did you reserve?
  - *Qu'est-ce que vous prenez?* — What are you having?
  - *Et comme dessert?* — And for dessert?
  - *Je vous recommande...* — I recommend...
**Student activity:** Repeat key phrases → each student says their complete theoretical order (one sentence: entrée + plat + dessert).

---

### Slide 5 — Cultural Notes: French Dining (5 min)
**Purpose:** Students understand the norms of a French restaurant so the role play feels authentic.
**Teacher notes:**
- *Le menu vs. la carte:* Set price menus often include a drink (*une carafe d'eau* is always free in France — you can ask for tap water at no charge).
- *Le service est compris:* In France, a 15% service charge is typically included. Additional tipping is optional. Students should know they are not expected to tip heavily.
- *L'entrée = starter:* Emphasise this false friend one more time. Students from the US may say "main course" when they mean *le plat principal.*
- *Coffee timing:* In France, coffee is ordered at the end of a meal, not during it. *Un café* after dessert is normal; coffee with the meal is unusual.
- **Cultural tip:** French waiters do not typically check in repeatedly during a meal. Catching the waiter's eye or saying *"Excusez-moi"* is the normal way to get attention. *Garçon!* is considered rude in modern usage.
**Student activity:** No activity — listening + notes. Keep to 3–4 minutes if pressed.

---

### Slide 6 — Role Play Preparation (5 min)
**Purpose:** Students prepare their scripts before going live.
**Teacher notes:**
- Students get 4 minutes to prepare:
  - Customer role: full order (drink + entrée + plat + dessert) + one question (*C'est quoi exactement? / Qu'est-ce que vous recommandez?*) + bill request.
  - Waiter role: greeting, taking the order, responding to one question (they can invent the special), bringing the bill.
- Notes are allowed for vocabulary. The ordering phrases (*je voudrais, pour l'entrée..., l'addition*) should be from memory.
- Encourage students to use food from the vocabulary file — the more varied, the more interesting the role play.
**Student activity:** 4 minutes quiet preparation. Instructor circulates and answers vocabulary questions.

---

### Slide 7 — Full Restaurant Role Play (12 min)
**Purpose:** Every student completes a full restaurant interaction as both customer and waiter.
**Teacher notes:**
- Pairs: one student is the customer, one is the waiter. After 3–4 minutes, they switch.
- Customer checklist:
  - [ ] Greets and asks for a table (or sits down)
  - [ ] Orders a drink
  - [ ] Orders three courses (*pour l'entrée / comme plat / comme dessert*)
  - [ ] Asks one question (*C'est quoi...? / Vous pouvez me recommander...?*)
  - [ ] Requests the bill: *L'addition, s'il vous plaît*
- Waiter checklist:
  - [ ] Greets the customer
  - [ ] Takes the order
  - [ ] Responds to the customer's question (invent an answer)
  - [ ] Brings the bill
- Instructor circulates and takes notes. Correct 1–2 errors per student after they finish their turn; do not interrupt during the role play.
- If online: breakout rooms of two. Instructor joins each room for 1 minute.
**Student activity:** Full restaurant role play × 2 (both roles).

---

### Slide 8 — Wrap-Up (5 min)
**Purpose:** Assign homework; close the food/café week.
**Teacher notes:**
- Group debrief: 2 things the class did well, 1 error pattern to continue working on.
- Most likely: partitive negation (*pas du* → should be *pas de*) and false cognate (*l'entrée* ≠ main course).
- Homework preview: written restaurant order + short paragraph about food preferences.
- Preview Week 7: "Next week: shopping. You will learn how to describe clothes and shop in a French market."
**Student activity:** Note homework. Optional closer: *"Qu'est-ce que vous avez commande comme plat principal ce soir?"*

## Cut First If Running Behind

1. **Slide 3 (pouvoir):** Cover just the first restaurant phrase (*Vous pouvez me recommander quelque chose?*). Skip the full table for now — it returns in Week 9.
2. **Slide 5 (cultural notes):** Reduce to 2 minutes — the le service point and the l'entrée false friend only.
3. **Slide 4 (restaurant vocabulary):** Cover key ordering phrases only; skip waiter phrases — the waiter can improvise in the role play.
4. Do not cut Slide 7 (role play) or Slide 6 (preparation time).

---

## Extension for Fast Cohorts

- **Vouloir + infinitif:** *Je voudrais réserver une table pour ce soir.* — I'd like to book a table for tonight. Full modal + infinitif study is in Week 9, but this one sentence is highly practical.
- **Asking about allergens:** *Je suis allergique aux noix. Est-ce qu'il y a des noix dans ce plat?* — using *il y a* from Week 8 preview.
- **Prendre as a role play verb:** *Qu'est-ce que vous prenez?* vs. *Qu'est-ce que vous voulez?* — model both so students playing the waiter have options.

## Move to Homework If Needed
