# French A1 — Week 6 Formative Check

**Administered:** Start of Session 13 (first session of Week 7)
**Time needed:** 6–8 minutes total
**Purpose:** Quick checkpoint — not a test. Confirms that partitive articles, *je voudrais*, and the café/restaurant vocabulary are solid enough to enter Week 7 (shopping and demonstratives, which use *de* in a similar way in quantity expressions).

---

## Oral Prompts (5 questions — ~1 min per student for the full set)

| # | Prompt | Target response | What to listen for |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | How do you say "I'd like some coffee" in French? | *Je voudrais du café.* | Partitive *du* — not *un café* here (though *un café* is also correct for ordering a single espresso); *je voudrais* not *je veux* |
| 2 | Give me all six forms of *vouloir* — go. | veux / veux / veut / voulons / voulez / veulent | All six; *veulent* not *voulons* for ils/elles; no hesitation |
| 3 | Tell me something you don't eat or drink. | *Je ne prends pas de... / Je ne mange pas de...* | *Pas de* — not *pas du / pas de la* |
| 4 | How would you order a three-course meal in a French restaurant — what would you say? | *Je voudrais [entrée], comme plat principal [plat], et comme dessert [dessert].* | *Je voudrais* used; correct course names; food items are real |
| 5 | How do you ask for the bill in French? | *L'addition, s'il vous plaît.* | Exact phrase; not *le bill* or *la facture* (la facture is for services, not restaurants) |

---

## Written Prompts (5 questions — students write answers, ~3 min)

**Instructions to students:** Answer in French. Short answers only.

1. Write: "I'm having some water." (l'eau — before vowel)
2. Write: "She doesn't eat any cheese." (le fromage — masculine)
3. What does *le menu* mean in a French restaurant? Answer in English.
4. What does *l'entrée* mean? Answer in English.
5. Write: "They are having some croissants and some orange juice."

**Answer key:**
1. *Je prends de l'eau.*
2. *Elle ne mange pas de fromage.*
3. A set-price meal (e.g., starter + main, or main + dessert at a fixed price) — NOT the menu list (that is *la carte*).
4. The starter / first course (NOT the main course — false cognate).
5. *Ils prennent des croissants et du jus d'orange.*

---

## Instructor Scoring Notes

Not graded. Use it to make one of three decisions:

| Result | What it means | What to do |
|---|---|---|
| Students handle all 5 oral prompts | Week 6 content is solid | Move into Week 7 (shopping, demonstratives, quantities) |
| Students stumble on 1–2 prompts | Minor gaps — expected | 5-minute partitive warm-up at the start of Sessions 13 and 14; correct negation errors on the spot |
| Students stumble on 3+ prompts | Partitives or *vouloir* not yet secure | Add 10-minute review to Session 13; carry *pas de* as a running correction habit |

**Most common Week 6 gaps:**
- *Pas du café* (partitive not collapsing after negation) → correct every instance; the rule is absolute
- *Je veux* used instead of *je voudrais* in formal contexts → both are correct; model *je voudrais* as the more polite default
- *L'entrée = main course* (false cognate) — continue to correct; this one takes time to stick
- *Des croissants* → *pas des croissants* (keeping the plural) → should be *pas de croissants*
- *Voulons* for ils/elles (confusion with nous) → review the table; *ils veulent*

---

## Ready to Move On

A student is ready for Week 7 (shopping) content when they can:

- [ ] Use *du / de la / de l' / des* correctly with 6+ food/drink items
- [ ] Apply the negation rule (*pas de*) without prompting
- [ ] Say a complete café order using *je voudrais*
- [ ] Conjugate *vouloir* from memory (all six forms)
- [ ] Navigate a simple restaurant ordering exchange in 4–5 sentences
- [ ] Know the false cognate: *l'entrée* = starter; *le menu* = set meal

**Not a Week 7 gate:** Full *pouvoir* paradigm mastery, extended restaurant vocabulary, or *vouloir + infinitif.* These continue to consolidate through Week 9.
