# French A1 — Session 11 Slide Outline
## Café Vocabulary and Partitive Articles

**Session length:** 60 minutes
**Session-end goal:** Students can order food and drink at a French café using *je voudrais* and the correct partitive article; they can form the negative (*pas de*) without hesitation.

> **Pacing note:** The speaking round (Slide 6) is non-negotiable. Every student must say a complete café order in French before the session ends. Compress Slide 4 (negation drill) before cutting Slide 6.

---

## Core 60-Minute Plan

### Slide 1 — Warm-Up: Week 5 Close (5 min)
**Purpose:** Close the Week 5 loop; activate *aller* before moving on.
**Teacher notes:**
- Ask each student to say one sentence using *aller + à/au/à la/à l'*. Notes allowed.
- If the Week 5 formative check showed widespread *à le café* (non-contracted): 30-second group reminder — "In French, *à le* never appears: it contracts to *au*. This applies everywhere."
- Do not re-teach. Activation only.
**Student activity:** Each student says one *aller* sentence. (30 seconds each max.)

---

### Slide 2 — Partitive Articles: What They Are (10 min)
**Purpose:** Students understand that French requires a "some" article where English uses none, and learn all four forms.
**Teacher notes:**
- Open with a contrast: "In English you say 'I want coffee.' In French you can't. You must say 'I want some coffee' — 'Je veux **du** café.' French makes the 'some' explicit."
- Display the four-row table:
  - *du* → masculine singular: *du café, du fromage, du pain*
  - *de la* → feminine singular: *de la bière, de la tarte, de la salade*
  - *de l'* → vowel/h muet: *de l'eau, de l'orange pressée, de l'huile*
  - *des* → plural: *des croissants, des escargots, des fruits*
- After each row: students repeat the examples. Ask one student to give a new example using the same article.
- Check: "What article do you use for *le jus d'orange*?" (de l') — "And *la bière*?" (de la)
**Student activity:** Repeat each form → supply one new example per row.

---

### Slide 3 — Café Vocabulary (10 min)
**Purpose:** Students learn 8–10 high-frequency café drinks and 4–5 food items with their partitive forms.
**Teacher notes:**
- Introduce drinks first, each with its partitive: *du café, du thé, du chocolat chaud, de la bière, de l'eau (gazeuse / plate), du jus d'orange, du vin.*
- Then introduce 4–5 food items: *un croissant, un pain au chocolat, une tartine, un sandwich au jambon, une quiche.*
- Note the key false friend: **un café in France = espresso.** *Un café filtre* or *un café américain* if students want filtered coffee.
- Do not try to introduce all items in the vocabulary list. Students need to say the words aloud, not see a full menu.
- Choral pronunciation on each item: *croissant* (/kwasɑ̃/); *baguette* (/baɡɛt/); *thé* (/te/).
**Student activity:** Repeat each item → say it with its partitive: *Je prends du café.*

---

### Slide 4 — Partitive in Negation: Pas de (8 min)
**Purpose:** Students learn the most important rule about partitives — all four forms become *de* after negation.
**Teacher notes:**
- Write the rule on screen: **ne...pas → de (or d' before a vowel)**
- Show the transformations:
  - *Je prends **du** café.* → *Je ne prends **pas de** café.*
  - *Elle mange **de la** tarte.* → *Elle ne mange **pas de** tarte.*
  - *Il boit **de l'**eau.* → *Il ne boit **pas d'**eau.*
  - *Nous prenons **des** croissants.* → *Nous ne prenons **pas de** croissants.*
- Note explicitly: "Some students want to say *pas du café* — that is wrong. After *pas*, it is always *de*. No exceptions."
- Quick drill: instructor says an affirmative sentence; students give the negative. 8 items.
**Student activity:** Oral drill — affirmative → negative (8 items, table visible).

---

### Slide 5 — Je Voudrais: Core Ordering Phrase (8 min)
**Purpose:** Students learn the key ordering formula and a handful of essential café phrases.
**Teacher notes:**
- Introduce *je voudrais* as the politest and most natural way to order: "I would like." Acknowledge it is a conditional form — "you'll learn why in A2; for now, treat it as a fixed phrase."
- Model the full order: *"Bonjour. Je voudrais un café et une tartine, s'il vous plaît."*
- Introduce companion phrases:
  - *Qu'est-ce que vous prenez?* — What are you having? (waiter)
  - *Je vais prendre...* — I'll have... (alternative)
  - *C'est quoi le plat du jour?* — What's the special?
  - *L'addition, s'il vous plaît.* — The bill, please.
- Pronunciation drill: *L'addition, s'il vous plaît.* × 3. Note: *l'addition* has stress on the last syllable.
**Student activity:** Repeat each phrase → say *je voudrais* + their own café order (one item only — full order comes in Slide 6).

---

### Slide 6 — Speaking Round: Café Order (12 min)
**Purpose:** Every student places a complete café order in French.
**Teacher notes:**
- Instructor plays the waiter. Each student plays the customer.
- Prompt sequence:
  1. *"Bonjour, vous désirez?"* — student greets and orders a drink (must include partitive or *un/une*)
  2. *"Et avec ça?"* — student orders a food item
  3. *"Vous prendrez autre chose?"* — student declines or adds: *"Non merci, c'est tout."*
  4. [pause] Instructor: *"Vous voulez l'addition?"* — student: *"L'addition, s'il vous plaît."*
- Correct one item per student. Common targets: missing *s'il vous plaît*, wrong partitive, using *je veux* when *je voudrais* was emphasized.
- After all students: note 2 patterns across the group (one strength, one correction).
**Student activity:** Each student places a complete café order (drink + food item + ending phrase).

---

### Slide 7 — Wrap-Up (7 min)
**Purpose:** Preview Session 12 and assign homework.
**Teacher notes:**
- Preview Session 12: "Next session we go from café to full restaurant. You will learn *vouloir* fully — the verb behind *je voudrais* — and *pouvoir*. Then we do a full restaurant role play: ordering a three-course meal."
- Homework preview: partitive review + writing a café order + short listening task if available.
**Student activity:** Note homework. Optional: one more café item using partitive.

## Cut First If Running Behind

1. **Slide 4 (negation drill):** Reduce to 5 items. Do not cut the rule explanation — just the drill.
2. **Slide 3 (café vocabulary):** Reduce to 6 drinks; skip food items. Students already know food from the vocabulary file.
3. **Slide 2 (partitive introduction):** Reduce examples to 2 per row — just enough to establish the pattern.
4. Do not cut Slide 6 (speaking round).

---

## Extension for Fast Cohorts

- **Partitive after expressions of quantity:** *une bouteille de vin, un verre de bière, une tasse de café* — show that quantities use *de* without an article (same as negation rule). "It's the same logic: specific amount → *de*."
- **Aimer vs. partitive:** *J'aime le café* (general preference — definite article). *Je prends du café* (taking some now — partitive). Contrast the two uses.
- **Café inversion:** Students ask each other: *"Qu'est-ce que tu prends?"* / *"Tu veux du fromage?"* — using *tu* informal form.
