## Part 2 — Restaurant Dialogue (8–10 lines)

Write a short restaurant dialogue in French. You are the customer; write both sides (customer + waiter).

**Required elements:**
- [ ] Customer greets and asks for a table
- [ ] Waiter asks what the customer will have
- [ ] Customer orders at least 2 courses using *je voudrais* or *je vais prendre*
- [ ] Customer asks one question (*C'est quoi exactement? / Vous pouvez me recommander...?*)
- [ ] Customer uses at least one partitive article (*du, de la, de l', des*)
- [ ] Customer requests the bill: *L'addition, s'il vous plaît.*

**Example opener** (continue from here, or write your own from scratch):
> *Waiter:* Bonsoir! Vous avez réservé?
> *Customer:* Non, mais est-ce que vous avez une table pour deux?
> *Waiter:* Oui, bien sûr. Par ici, s'il vous plaît. Qu'est-ce que vous prenez?
> *Customer:* *(continue here...)*

## Part 3 — Food Preferences Paragraph (4–5 sentences)

Write 4–5 sentences about what you like and don't like to eat or drink. Include:

- [ ] At least 1 affirmative partitive (*Je mange du / de la / de l' / des...*)
- [ ] At least 1 negative (*Je ne mange pas de...*)
- [ ] At least 1 opinion or preference using *j'aime, j'adore,* or *je préfère*

**Note on aimer vs. partitive:** After *aimer/adorer/détester/préférer*, use the definite article — *J'aime **le** café, j'adore **la** tarte.* This is a general preference (all coffee, all tart). The partitive (*du café, de la tarte*) means "some coffee" — a specific quantity you're having right now.
