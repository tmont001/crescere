# French A1 — Session 24 Final Performance Quick Reference
## Instructor-Only

---

## 1. Session 24 Snapshot

| | |
|---|---|
| **Session title** | Final Performance and Course Close |
| **Purpose** | Demonstration of A1 competency — not a pass/fail exam |
| **Student-facing message** | *"Ce n'est pas un examen. C'est une conversation. Le but, c'est de communiquer."* |
| **Instructor goal** | Every student completes Part A and Part B; receives forward-looking feedback; knows what comes next; course ends on a high |
| **What success looks like** | Getting meaning across. Recovering when stuck. Eight clear sentences beat twelve muddled ones. A student who tries a complex structure and makes an error is doing something more impressive than one who only uses safe patterns. |

## 2. Session Flow At-a-Glance

| Block | Time (4 students) | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Warm-up: one memory from the course | 5 min | Reflection, not grammar. No review. |
| Part A: Spoken role plays (each student in sequence) | 28–30 min | Non-negotiable. See cohort table below. |
| Part B: Written message | 15 min | In-session (cohorts 1–4) or take-home (5+) |
| Feedback: group then individual | 6–8 min | Group first; 30–45 sec per student individual |
| A2 preview | 5–8 min | 3–4 sentences only. Do not teach. |
| Course close and celebration | 5 min | End on a high. |

**If behind:** Move individual feedback to post-session email (group only verbally) → cut A2 preview to 2 sentences → cut celebration to 2 minutes. Do NOT reduce or skip role plays or written message.

## 4. Part A Spoken Role Play Checklist

**Student must cover at least 3 of these 4 topic areas:**
1. Introduction — name, origin, profession, personal details
2. Plans — propose something; suggest a time and place (*aller + infinitif*)
3. Directions — help the other person get somewhere in the city
4. Description — describe home, neighbourhood, or a place they like

**Required structures — look for these naturally in conversation:**
- [ ] *Être* or *avoir* used correctly at least twice
- [ ] *Aller + infinitif* for at least one near-future plan
- [ ] At least one modal (*devoir, pouvoir,* or *vouloir*)
- [ ] At least one partitive article (*du, de la, de l', des*) in natural context
- [ ] At least one question produced by the student (any formation is fine)

**Instructor role in the role play:**
- Play the role of a French-speaking stranger. Be helpful and curious, not combative.
- Open the scene: *"Bonjour ! Vous êtes d'ici?"*
- Ask natural follow-up questions: *"Et votre quartier — comment c'est? Vous habitez là depuis longtemps? Qu'est-ce que vous aimez faire le week-end?"*
- Note 2–3 specific items for feedback. Do not correct during the role play.
- Close: *"C'était vraiment sympa de vous parler. À bientôt!"*

---

## 5. Part B Written Message Checklist

**Required elements:**
- [ ] Introduction: who you are, where from, what you do
- [ ] Description of daily routine (at least 2 sentences)
- [ ] Something you like / prefer doing
- [ ] A suggestion to meet or do something together (*aller + infinitif* or *je voudrais*)
- [ ] At least one question for the other person

**Format:** 8–10 sentences. Clear and correct over long and muddled.

**Allowed:** Phrase bank. **Not allowed:** previously written draft.

**Do not grade for perfection.** Look for coherent communication, range of structures, clear intent.

## 6. Feedback Formula

**Step 1 — Group feedback first (whole cohort, 2 min):**
Name 2–3 things the whole group did well. Be specific: *"Vous avez tous utilisé 'aller + infinitif' très naturellement — c'est exactement ce qu'on attendait."*

**Step 2 — Individual feedback (30–45 sec per student):**
| Component | What to say |
|---|---|
| **Strength** | Name one specific structure or phrase that worked: *"Votre façon d'utiliser les partitifs était très naturelle."* |
| **Keep doing** | Name one habit they may not have noticed: *"La façon dont vous vous êtes récupéré(e) quand vous ne connaissiez pas un mot — continuez comme ça."* |
| **A2 forward pointer** | One item, phrased forward: *"Pour l'A2: l'accord des adjectifs — 'grande' devenait parfois 'grand' — ce sera l'un des points de focus."* |

**Step 3 — Written Part B feedback (post-session, within one week):**
One short paragraph. What worked (first). One grammar point. One encouragement for A2. No red marks.

## 7. Freeze / Rush Protocol

| Situation | What to do |
|---|---|
| **Student freezes at the start** | Wait 4 seconds. Then: *"Alors — vous vous appelez comment?"* If frozen again: *"Et d'où venez-vous?"* Keep asking easy questions until the student is moving. Do not abandon the role play. |
| **Student finishes in under 3 minutes** | Ask: *"Et votre quartier — comment c'est?"* / *"Et votre famille — ils habitent là aussi?"* / *"Vous avez des enfants?"* Keep asking naturally until 5 minutes. |
| **Student is reading a prepared script** | Introduce an off-script question they have not prepared for: *"Ah, et votre famille — ils habitent aussi là?"* / *"Et ce week-end, vous allez faire quoi?"* Test genuine production. |
| **Student cannot recall a word** | If they ask for help, give it. If they don't ask, wait — working around a gap is the skill being demonstrated. |
| **Student makes repeated errors on one structure** | Note it; do not correct mid-role play. Address it in the A2 forward pointer during feedback. |
| **Session is running behind on time** | Move individual verbal feedback to a post-session written email. Give only group feedback verbally. Reduce A2 preview to 2 sentences. Reduce celebration to 2 minutes. Do not cut role plays. |

---

## 8. Do Not Do

- **Do not correct during the role play.** Note items, feedback after.
- **Do not turn the A2 preview into an A2 lesson.** Three to four sentences only. Name the passé composé; do not teach it.
- **Do not cut or shorten the spoken role plays.** If time is critically short, Part B can go take-home; Part A cannot.
- **Do not surprise students with new requirements.** Everything in Session 24 should feel like what was rehearsed in Session 23.
- **Do not make the final performance feel like a test.** Call it "la performance finale," never "l'examen."
- **Do not compare students to each other** in group or individual feedback.
- **Do not overcorrect Part B written feedback.** One grammar point. No red marks.
- **Do not overcorrect pronunciation.** Communication is success. Accent errors are not A1 failures.

## 9. Language-Specific Watch Points

These are the items most likely to appear, to succeed, and to need A2 forward-pointers in French A1.

| Structure | What to watch for | Feedback framing |
|---|---|---|
| *Être* vs. *avoir* | *Je suis les yeux bleus* → should be *J'ai.* *Je suis X ans* → should be *J'ai.* | "For A2: avoir vs. être — we'll reinforce this with more verb types." |
| Adjective agreement | *Elle est grand* (missing -e) or *sérieuxe* (wrong irregular) | "For A2: adjective agreement — one of our A2 points of focus." |
| *Aller + infinitif* | Watch for incorrect construction (*je vais aller à...* vs. *je vais + infinitif*) | Strong if used naturally — name it as a success |
| *Avoir mal à + article* | May surface if health topics come up in directions or description | Acknowledge if correct; don't demand it |
| Partitive articles | *du, de la, de l', des* → *de* after negation | Note correct uses; catch *de la* → *de* omission after negation |
| *Depuis + présent* | "I have been living here for five years" — *J'habite ici depuis cinq ans* | Praise if produced; don't require it |
| Question formation | Any formation is acceptable (inversion, *est-ce que*, rising intonation) | Do not correct working question formation at A1 |
| Repair phrases | *"Comment dit-on...?"* / *"Je cherche le mot pour..."* | Explicitly praise these — they are a skill, not a weakness |
