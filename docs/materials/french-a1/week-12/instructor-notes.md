# French A1 — Week 12 Instructor Notes

**Sessions:** 23 and 24
**Theme:** Review and Final Performance

---

## Week Objective

By the end of Week 12:
- Every student has completed the final performance (Part A: spoken role play, Part B: written message)
- Every student has received written or verbal feedback on their performance
- Every student knows what A2 involves and has a concrete next step
- The course ends on a high note — students leave feeling capable, not tested

**No new major grammar this week.** The curriculum lists the following as "reinforced in Session 23": reflexive verbs, aller + infinitif, modal verbs (pouvoir/vouloir/devoir), il faut, depuis, faire de/jouer à, demonstrative adjectives. These are not re-taught — they surface naturally in practice scenarios.

## How to Keep the Final Performance Low-Pressure

- **Name it correctly:** Call it "the final performance" or "la performance finale," not "the exam" or "the test." Language matters.
- **Be clear about what success looks like:** Communicating is success. Students do not need to be perfect.
- **Model communication repair openly:** In the Session 23 model role play, deliberately get stuck once and use a repair phrase (*"Comment dit-on...?"*). Show that this is a normal part of speaking.
- **Give students a topic, not a script:** Each student should choose from their real life — their real job, their real neighbourhood, their real plans. This removes the cognitive load of invention.
- **No audience during individual role plays:** Unless the group has built strong rapport, avoid having all students watch each other's individual performances. Run them sequentially with brief pauses.
- **Praise first, always:** Every piece of feedback — written or verbal — begins with something specific that worked.

## Common Student Issues During Final Performance

| Issue | What to do |
|---|---|
| Student freezes at the start | Give an easy opener: *"Alors, comment vous appelez-vous?"* Wait. Don't rescue immediately — silence of 3–4 seconds is normal. |
| Student runs out of language after introduction | Ask a follow-up: *"Et le week-end — qu'est-ce que vous aimez faire?"* Keep the conversation alive. |
| Student switches to English | Gently stay in French: *"En français, s'il vous plaît — je sais que c'est difficile!"* Model the French if needed. |
| Student can't remember a word | If they ask: give it. If they don't ask: let them work around it. Recovery is a skill; rescue is not. |
| Student rushes and finishes in 3 minutes | Extend with: *"Et votre quartier — vous y habitez depuis combien de temps?"* Keep asking until 5 minutes. |
| Student produces a pre-memorized script | Introduce an off-script question they haven't prepared for: *"Ah, et votre famille — ils habitent aussi là?"* Test genuine production. |

## Feedback Guidance

**Structure of feedback for each student:**
1. **What worked well** (2–3 specifics — name the structure or phrase, not just "your French was good")
2. **One thing to keep doing** (a strength they may not notice)
3. **One thing to watch for in A2** (one grammar or vocabulary item, phrased as a forward pointer, not a criticism)

**Example feedback:**
> *"Vous avez utilisé 'je vais + infinitif' très naturellement — c'est exactement ce qu'on attendait. Votre façon de récupérer quand vous ne connaissiez pas un mot était parfaite — continuez comme ça. Pour l'A2: les accords des adjectifs — parfois 'grande' devenait 'grand' — ce sera l'un des points de focus en A2."*

**Written feedback on Part B** should be returned within a week. Keep it brief: one paragraph maximum. No red marks all over the page.

## A2 Readiness Conversation

The A2 preview in Session 24 should be 5–8 minutes. Structure:

1. **Acknowledge the achievement:** *"Vous avez complété le niveau A1. Voici ce que ça veut dire..."*
2. **Name what A1 makes possible:** café, pharmacy, shopping, introductions, plans, directions
3. **Name what A2 adds:** talking about the past (*passé composé*), describing completed events, futur simple, comparatives, object pronouns
4. **Make it exciting, not overwhelming:** *"En A2, vous allez pouvoir parler de votre enfance, d'un voyage passé, d'un film que vous avez vu. Ça rend les conversations beaucoup plus riches."*
5. **Give concrete next steps:** see the homework file for a structured list

**Do not teach passé composé content in Session 24.** A preview of what A2 involves is different from a first lesson in it. The goal is motivation, not information delivery.

## Suggested Instructor Language

**At the start of Session 23:**
*"La semaine dernière, vous avez vu le médecin en français. Cette semaine, vous allez montrer tout ce que vous savez faire. Ce n'est pas un examen — c'est une démonstration. Le but n'est pas d'être parfait. Le but, c'est de communiquer."*

**Before each student's role play in Session 24:**
*"On est prêts? Pas de pression. Faites comme si on se rencontrait pour la première fois."*

**After each student's role play:**
*"Très bien. [Name one specific thing.] Voilà ce que je voudrais vous dire..."*

**On the A2 preview:**
*"Vous avez mis de solides bases. En A1, vous pouvez vous présenter, faire des plans, naviguer dans une ville, commander dans un café, parler de vos loisirs. En A2, vous allez apprendre à parler du passé. Ça va tout changer."*
