## Part 4 — Staying Active Between A1 and A2

The most common reason students lose ground between courses is not forgetting — it is inactivity. Five minutes of French per day does more than five hours of cramming the week before A2 starts.

**Recommended daily habits (choose at least one):**
- **Read:** *"Journal en français facile"* (online — very simple news in French, free)
- **Listen:** *"Coffee Break French"* (podcast — excellent for A1/A2 level)
- **Watch:** French children's TV or YouTube in French with French subtitles
- **Write:** Keep a one-sentence French journal — just one sentence per day about what you did: *"Aujourd'hui j'ai mangé une pizza."* (You will be using passé composé naturally before A2 even begins.)
- **App:** Anki (spaced repetition flashcards) — create cards for the irregular verbs and contractions you missed in your self-assessment

**The one-sentence journal is the single most useful recommendation.** It keeps French present in your daily life, builds writing fluency, and accidentally prepares you for the passé composé in A2.

## Part 5 — Optional A2 Preparation

If you want to get a head start before A2 begins:

**Irregular verb review (A1 → A2 bridge):**
These verbs appear constantly in A2 content. Make sure you know all 6 forms: *aller, être, avoir, faire, venir, prendre, savoir, connaître, devoir, pouvoir, vouloir.*

**Preview the passé composé (do not worry about mastering it):**
The passé composé has two parts: an auxiliary (*avoir* or *être*) + a past participle. For regular *-er* verbs: *manger → mangé / parler → parlé.* You already know both auxiliaries. The rest is just the new ending. In your one-sentence journal: try writing *"J'ai mangé..."* or *"Je suis allé(e)..."* — you will be practicing A2 grammar without realizing it.

**Cultural immersion:** Watch one French-language film (with French subtitles, not English). *Le Dîner de Cons, Amélie, Les Intouchables* are accessible and highly rewarding even at A1/A2 level. Don't worry about understanding everything — just stay with it.
