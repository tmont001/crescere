# French A1 — Session 24 Slide Outline
## Final Performance and Course Close

**Session length:** 60 minutes (on plan — adjust by cohort size per instructor notes)
**Session-end goal:** Every student has completed Part A (spoken role play) and Part B (written message or confirmed take-home); every student has received feedback; every student knows their next step; the course ends on a high.

> **Pacing note:** The spoken role plays (Slide 2) are non-negotiable and may not be shortened. If cohort size is 5+, move Part B to take-home so the session does not overrun. The A2 preview and celebration (Slides 5–6) are brief but important — do not skip them.

---

## Core Plan

### Slide 1 — Warm-Up: Last Check-In (5 min)
**Purpose:** Low-stakes entry; settle energy before the role plays.
**Teacher notes:**
- Ask each student: *"Qu'est-ce que vous vous rappelez de votre première leçon de français?"*
- Accept anything — a word, a phrase, a feeling. This is a reflection prompt, not a grammar test.
- Briefly: *"Aujourd'hui, vous allez montrer tout ce que vous savez. Ce n'est pas un examen. C'est une conversation. Allez-y."*
- Do not do a grammar review. Do not drill vocabulary. The time for preparation is over.
**Student activity:** Each student gives one memory from Week 1 or the course.

---

### Slide 2 — Final Role Plays: Part A (20–35 min depending on cohort) ★ NON-NEGOTIABLE
**Purpose:** Every student completes the spoken final performance.
**Teacher notes:**

**Process:**
1. Students wait outside or in a separate "waiting" mode while each individual is called.
2. Instructor sets the scene briefly: *"Imaginez que nous sommes dans un café à Paris. Vous venez juste de vous asseoir à côté de moi. Je commence."*
3. Instructor opens: *"Bonjour! Vous êtes d'ici?"*
4. Role play runs for 5–7 minutes.
5. Instructor ends naturally: *"C'était vraiment sympa de vous parler. À bientôt!"*
6. Instructor notes 2–3 specific items for feedback (not more).

**Per-student timing:**
- 2–3 min transition and setup
- 5–7 min role play
- 1 min quick feedback note (written down, not delivered yet)
- Total per student: 8–11 min

**If a student freezes:**
- Wait 4 seconds. If still frozen: *"Alors — vous vous appelez comment?"*
- If frozen again: *"Et d'où venez-vous?"*
- Do not abandon the role play — keep asking easy questions until the student is moving.

**If a student rushes and finishes at 3 minutes:**
- Add: *"Et votre quartier — comment c'est?"* / *"Et votre famille — ils habitent là aussi?"* / *"Vous avez des enfants?"*
- Keep asking until 5 minutes.

**Required elements to look for:**
- [ ] At least one *être* or *avoir* use
- [ ] *Aller + infinitif* for a plan
- [ ] At least one modal
- [ ] At least one partitive in context
- [ ] At least one question produced by the student

**Student activity:** Each student completes their 5–7 minute role play in sequence.

---

### Slide 3 — Written Message: Part B (12–15 min)
**Purpose:** Students complete the written message component of the final performance.
**Teacher notes:**
- **Option 1 (preferred for cohorts of 1–4):** Students write in session — 12–15 minutes, independently and silently. Instructor reviews role play notes while students write.
- **Option 2 (cohorts of 5+):** Students submit via email or message before the next session. Instructor gives written feedback within one week.
- If in-session: students may use the phrase bank. They may not use a previously written draft — this is independent production.
- When time is up: students do not need to correct or rewrite; the draft is the submission.
**Student activity:** Write Part B independently (if in-session).

---

### Slide 4 — Feedback: What You Did Well (6–8 min)
**Purpose:** Every student receives specific, positive, forward-looking feedback.
**Teacher notes:**
- Give feedback in two parts:
  1. **Group feedback:** two or three things the whole cohort did well (name the patterns — *"Vous avez tous utilisé 'aller + infinitif' très naturellement"*). Celebrate these.
  2. **Individual feedback (brief, verbal):** one thing per student that worked, and one thing to watch in A2. Keep it to 30–45 seconds per person. Written feedback on Part B will follow separately.
- Frame everything forward: *"Pour l'A2..."* not *"Vous avez fait une erreur..."*
- Do not compare students to each other.
**Student activity:** Students receive and note their individual feedback.

---

### Slide 5 — A2 Preview (5–8 min)
**Purpose:** Students understand what comes next and feel excited, not overwhelmed.
**Teacher notes:**
- Do not teach A2 grammar. Preview it. Three to four sentences only:
  - *"En A1, vous pouvez parler du présent et du futur proche. Vous pouvez vous présenter, faire des plans, naviguer dans une ville."*
  - *"En A2, vous allez apprendre à parler du passé. Qu'est-ce que vous avez fait hier? Comment était votre enfance? Le passé va tout changer."*
  - *"Vous allez apprendre le passé composé — j'ai mangé, j'ai travaillé, je suis allé(e). Et l'imparfait — quand j'étais enfant, j'habitais à... C'était comment?"*
  - *"Avec A2, les conversations deviennent beaucoup plus riches. Vous ne parlez plus seulement de ce qui se passe maintenant — vous parlez de votre vie entière."*
- Name one concrete thing students can do before A2 starts: *"Écoutez 5 minutes de français chaque jour. Podcast français facile, journal en français facile, n'importe quoi. Gardez l'oreille active."*
**Student activity:** Students note one thing they are looking forward to learning in A2.

---

### Slide 6 — Course Close and Celebration (5 min)
**Purpose:** Mark the achievement. End on a high.
**Teacher notes:**
- Read aloud what the students can now do (from the A1 readiness checklist in the final performance guide) — not as a list to check off but as a summary of real achievement.
- *"Il y a douze semaines, vous n'aviez pas de français. Maintenant, vous pouvez vous présenter, faire des plans, naviguer dans une ville, commander au café, décrire votre vie. Ce sont de vraies compétences. Félicitations."*
- If the group is comfortable: ask each student to say one sentence about what they are proud of. In French.
- Close: *"Merci d'avoir étudié avec moi. À bientôt — en français!"*
**Student activity:** Optional: one sentence each about what they are proud of, in French.

---

## Cut First If Running Behind

1. **Slide 5 (A2 preview):** Reduce to two sentences: name the passé composé; give one self-study recommendation.
2. **Slide 4 (feedback):** Move individual written feedback entirely to post-session email. Give only group feedback verbally.
3. **Slide 6 (celebration):** Reduce to the closing summary paragraph only — 2 minutes.
4. Do NOT reduce or skip Slide 2 (role plays) or Slide 3 (written message).

---

## Extension for Fast Cohorts / Ahead of Schedule

- **Longer role plays:** If a student is clearly comfortable, extend to 8–9 minutes by asking more follow-up questions — explore their neighbourhood in detail, ask about family, ask about a recent event (*"Qu'est-ce que vous avez fait ce week-end?"*) to give a glimpse of passé composé.
- **Second part B topic:** Ask students who finish their written message early to write a second paragraph — a description of a favourite place in France or a francophone country they would like to visit.
- **A2 taster sentence:** If time and energy permit, introduce one passé composé sentence as a teaser: *"J'ai mangé une baguette ce matin."* Ask students to guess what tense it is and what it means. Do not drill — just open the door.
