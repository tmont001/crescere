# French A1 — Session 23 Slide Outline
## Final Performance Preparation

**Session length:** 60 minutes (on plan)
**Session-end goal:** Every student has practiced all four role play topic areas, rehearsed one full scenario with a partner, drafted their written message, and knows exactly what to expect in Session 24.

> **Pacing note:** Pair rehearsal (Slide 6) is non-negotiable. Every student practices one full role-play scenario before the session ends. The final performance overview (Slide 2) should be warm and demystifying — not a long list of requirements. Time spent on anxiety management here pays off in Session 24 confidence.

---

## Core Plan

### Slide 1 — Warm-Up: Course Journey (5 min)
**Purpose:** Positive re-entry; remind students of how far they have come.
**Teacher notes:**
- Ask each student: *"Quelle est votre phrase préférée en français — quelque chose que vous êtes fier/fière de pouvoir dire?"*
- Accept anything: a complex sentence, a funny phrase, something from Week 1. This is not a grammar drill — it is a confidence moment.
- Acknowledge the Week 11 achievement: *"La semaine dernière vous avez fait une consultation médicale en français. C'était la semaine la plus difficile. Cette semaine, c'est plus intégré — mais c'est votre français. Vous l'avez déjà."*
**Student activity:** Each student shares one favourite phrase or sentence.

---

### Slide 2 — Final Performance Overview (8 min)
**Purpose:** Students understand exactly what will happen in Session 24 — no surprises.
**Teacher notes:**
- Walk through the final performance document together. Cover:
  - What Part A is (spoken role play, 5–7 min, four topic areas)
  - What Part B is (written message, 8–10 sentences)
  - What the instructor is looking for (communication, range, recovery — not perfection)
  - The repair phrases they can use
- Emphasize: *"Il n'y a pas de note. Il n'y a pas d'examen. C'est une démonstration — vous montrez ce que vous savez faire."*
- Answer student questions. If someone asks *"What if I go blank?"* → *"Vous utilisez une phrase de réparation: 'Pouvez-vous répéter?' ou 'Comment dit-on...?' C'est exactement ce qu'on cherche."*
**Student activity:** Students note their two chosen repair phrases.

---

### Slide 3 — Model Role Play Walkthrough (10 min)
**Purpose:** Students see what a good A1 role play looks and sounds like — including recovery moments.
**Teacher notes:**
- Instructor narrates and performs both sides of the role play, OR reads a scripted example dialogue while walking through it slide-by-slide.
- The model must include:
  - A natural introduction
  - A plan that uses *aller + infinitif*
  - Directions to a place
  - A brief description of home or neighbourhood
  - At least one moment where the student "gets stuck" and uses a repair phrase
- After the model: *"Qu'est-ce que vous avez remarqué? Quels mots ou structures vous avez reconnus?"* — brief observation round.
- Point out explicitly: the model is not perfect French. It is fluent A1 French, which is different.

**Model scenario:**
> Student meets a French-speaking new arrival at a café. They introduce themselves, discover a shared interest in food markets, agree to visit the local market together Saturday morning, and the student gives directions to the market from the café.

**Student activity:** Students note one sentence from the model they could use or adapt.

---

### Slide 4 — Model Written Message (5 min)
**Purpose:** Students see what an A1 written message looks like; understand the standard.
**Teacher notes:**
- Read through the example in the student-facing overview together (do not read it out loud yourself — ask a student to read it).
- After reading: What structures appear? (être, avoir, reflexive verb, aller + infinitif, jouer à / faire de, a question)
- Is it perfect? No. Is it clear and complete? Yes. That is the standard.
- Note: the written message uses real life. Encourage students to do the same in their own.
**Student activity:** Students note one structure from the model they want to include in their own message.

---

### Slide 5 — Scenario Practice: Four Topic Areas (15 min)
**Purpose:** Students practice each of the four topic areas in pairs or with the instructor.
**Teacher notes:**
- Run as four short drills (3–4 min each), rotating focus:
  1. **Introductions:** Students introduce themselves to each other as if meeting for the first time — name, origin, profession, one personal detail.
  2. **Making plans:** Students propose an activity and negotiate a time — *"On va faire... ce soir? Je peux vers 19h."*
  3. **Directions:** Instructor calls out a city place; student gives directions from the café where they are "sitting."
  4. **Describing a place:** Student describes their home or neighbourhood in 3–4 sentences.
- Correct one item per student per drill. Keep energy up — this should feel like conversation practice, not testing.
- For small groups: instructor participates in each drill alongside students.
**Student activity:** Students participate in all four short drills.

---

### Slide 6 — Pair Rehearsal: Full Role Play Run-Through (10 min) ★ PROTECTED
**Purpose:** Every student practices one complete role play scenario from start to finish.
**Teacher notes:**
- Each student runs through the full scenario with the instructor (or with a partner for small groups).
- They choose which starting point (Option A, B, or C from the final performance guide) they want to practice.
- This is not the final performance — it is a rehearsal. Mistakes are expected and can be discussed immediately.
- Give one piece of specific feedback per student: something that worked + one thing to try differently in Session 24.
- Aim: every student finishes this block feeling ready, not nervous.
**Student activity:** Each student completes one full role-play rehearsal.

---

### Slide 7 — Wrap-Up: Preparation Checklist and Homework (7 min)
**Purpose:** Students leave with a clear plan for what to do before Session 24.
**Teacher notes:**
- Walk through the Session 23 homework checklist together — students should leave knowing exactly what to do tonight and tomorrow.
- Reassurance: *"Si vous pratiquez à voix haute ce soir, vous serez prêt(e). Pas besoin de mémoriser un script. Pratiquez les sujets — laissez les mots venir naturellement."*
- Remind: written message should be drafted before Session 24.
- Optional: students tell the group one thing they feel most confident about and one thing they want to work on before tomorrow.
**Student activity:** Note homework; optional confidence/challenge share.

---

## Cut First If Running Behind

1. **Slide 4 (model written message):** Cut — students can read this independently before Session 24.
2. **Slide 3 (model role play):** Reduce to the introduction and plan sections only. Skip the directions walkthrough — students have practiced directions before.
3. **Slide 5 (scenario practice):** Cut "describing a place" drill — do only introductions, plans, and directions.
4. Do not cut Slide 6 (pair rehearsal).

---

## Extension for Fast Cohorts

- **Difficult follow-up questions:** Instructor adds unexpected follow-ups during Slide 6 rehearsal — questions that require students to improvise, not recall a prepared answer: *"Et votre famille — ils parlent français aussi?"* or *"Qu'est-ce que vous avez mangé ce matin?"*
- **Written message time:** Fast cohorts can draft Part B during Session 23 with instructor feedback — arrives at Session 24 already polished.
- **Error prediction:** Students predict the grammar errors they are most likely to make in Session 24 (e.g., article contractions, adjective agreement, depuis). They write one reminder note per predicted error.

---

## Move to Homework If Needed

- Model written message analysis
- Part B first draft
- Directions practice (two routes from different starting points)
- Repair phrase memorization (2–3 phrases)
