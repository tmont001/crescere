# French A1 — Week 4 Instructor Notes

**Sessions:** 7 and 8
**Theme:** Reflexive Verbs and Daily Routine

---

## Week Objective

By the end of Week 4, students can:
- Use reflexive verbs in *je, tu, il/elle* forms for daily routine description
- Place the reflexive pronoun correctly before the conjugated verb
- Sequence a morning and evening routine in 5–6 sentences using reflexive and -er verbs
- Read and say basic clock times (*il est + heure, à + heure*)
- Describe another person's routine using *il/elle se...*

Full mastery of all six reflexive pronoun forms is not a Week 4 requirement. *Me, te, se* are the priority. Students who leave Week 4 able to say their routine using *je me lève / je me couche / je me prépare* in complete sentences are on track.

**Relationship to the curriculum:** The curriculum places telling time and family vocabulary in Week 4 (Sessions 7–8). Given that Week 3 deferred reflexive verbs, Week 4 covers reflexive verbs and completes the daily routine communication goal. Basic time-reading is included in Session 8 as a support skill for the routine narration. Full telling time (half-hours, quarter-hours, 24h format) and family vocabulary are deferred to Week 5.

**-ir and -re verbs:** These appear in the vocabulary reference as extension material. Do not teach them as a grammar block in Week 4 unless the cohort finishes early. They will be covered systematically in Week 5 or 6.

## Session 7 Pacing — Reflexive Verbs: Introduction

**Theme:** What reflexive verbs are and how the pronoun works.

| Block | Content | Time |
|---|---|---|
| Warm-up | Week 3 formative check debrief; -er verb quick recall (2 sentences each) | 7 min |

| *Tu te...* and *il/elle se...* | Extend to second and third person | 8 min |

| Wrap-up | Homework preview | 5 min |
| **Total** | | **~60 min** |

**What must be protected:** The speaking round (block 6). Every student must produce at least 2 first-person reflexive sentences (*Je me lève... Je me couche...*) before the session ends. The pronoun placement is the new skill — get students saying it before they leave.

**Theme:** Sequencing a complete routine with clock times; describing another person's routine.

| Block | Content | Time |

## Common Student Mistakes — Week 4

| Mistake | Correction | When to address |
|---|---|---|
| *Je me leve* without accent | *Je me lève* — the accent grave is part of the word. In speech, just model it; in writing, correct it. | Session 7 writing practice |
| *Je me appelle* (no elision) | *Je m'appelle* — me + vowel → m'. Elision is required. | Session 7 first occurrence |
| *Je se lève* (wrong pronoun) | The pronoun must match the subject: *je → me, tu → te, il → se.* | Session 7 speaking round |
| *Je me lève moi-même* (adding emphatic pronoun unnecessarily) | Not wrong, but unnatural. The reflexive pronoun already implies "myself." | If it comes up |
| *Je me prépare me lève...* (wrong word order) | The reflexive pronoun precedes the verb it belongs to: *je me lève, puis je me prépare.* | Session 8 sequencing block |
| Forgetting the reflexive pronoun entirely | *Je lève à sept heures* → *Je me lève à sept heures.* The pronoun is required. | Every time; say "reflexive pronoun" as a reminder cue |
| *Il est sept heures et demie* said as *il est sept trente* | Both are understood, but teach the traditional French form (*et demie*) as the target | Session 8 time-telling block |

## Suggested Instructor Language

- **For introducing reflexive verbs:** *"Reflexive verbs express actions you do to yourself or for yourself — getting up, getting dressed, washing, going to bed. In French, there's an extra little pronoun that signals this: me for I, te for you, se for he/she. That's the only new piece. The verb itself conjugates exactly like any other verb."*
- **For elision:** *"Before a vowel, me becomes m' and se becomes s'. So: je m'appelle — not je me appelle. It's like how English says 'an apple' not 'a apple.' The vowel collision disappears."*
- **For the pronoun matching the subject:** *"Point at yourself: je me lève. Point at another person: elle se lève. The pronoun has to match who is doing the action. It's not interchangeable."*
- **For the routine narration:** *"I want you to walk me through your morning — from when you wake up to when you leave the house. Use reflexive verbs for the getting-ready steps and -er verbs for the rest. Use times where you know them. You can have notes."*

---

## What to Skip If Time Is Tight

- The full six-form conjugation table for each reflexive verb — *je, tu, il/elle* is enough for Week 4 production; *nous, vous, ils* can be reference-level
- The accent in *se lève* — mention it; do not drill it; correct in writing
- -ir and -re verb introduction — these are in the vocabulary reference only; skip entirely in live class
- Half-hours and quarter-hours in clock times — just teach *à + number + heures* for now; precision timing is Week 5

---

## What to Assign as Homework If Pacing Runs Long

If Session 7 runs out before the speaking round:
→ Students write 3 reflexive sentences for homework; open Session 8 with sharing

If Session 8 routine narration doesn't happen:
→ Students record a 60-second voice routine narration and be ready to share at Session 9
