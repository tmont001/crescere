# French A1 — Session 8 Slide Outline
## Full Routine Narration with Time Expressions

**Session length:** 60 minutes
**Session-end goal:** Every student narrates their complete daily routine — morning through evening — in 5+ sentences using reflexive verbs, regular -er verbs, and basic clock times. Students can describe another person's routine using *il/elle se...*

> **Pacing note:** The routine narration round (Slide 6) is non-negotiable. Every student narrates their day out loud before the session ends. Trim Slide 4 (describing another person) to 5 minutes before cutting the narration round.

---

## Core 60-Minute Plan

### Slide 1 — Warm-Up: Reflexive Verb Recall (6 min)
**Purpose:** Verify that reflexive pronoun placement is becoming automatic before adding time and sequencing complexity.
**Teacher notes:**
- Ask each student to say 2 reflexive sentences from their homework — no notes if possible.
- Listen for: missing reflexive pronoun (*je lève* instead of *je me lève*), wrong pronoun, elision errors.
- If any student cannot produce *je me lève* without the pronoun, give one quick focused correction: "The me is always there. Say it again with me."
- Do not dwell. The narration round will give everyone more practice.
**Student activity:** Each student says 2 reflexive sentences from homework without notes.

---

### Slide 2 — Basic Clock Times (8 min)
**Purpose:** Students can say simple clock times so they can anchor their routine to specific hours.
**Teacher notes:**
- Teach only what is needed to narrate a routine: *à + number + heures, vers + number + heures.*
- Key forms:
  - *Il est sept heures.* / *Il est midi.* / *Il est minuit.*
  - *À quelle heure est-ce que tu te lèves ?* — *Je me lève à sept heures.*
  - *Je me couche vers vingt-deux heures.* (24h format for evening times — natural in French)
  - *Il est sept heures et demie.* — half past
  - *Il est huit heures et quart.* — quarter past
  - *Il est huit heures moins le quart.* — quarter to
- Do not over-drill minutes and quarter-hours. Teach *et demie* and *et quart* only, plus the basic hour form. Full time-telling is Week 5.
- Quick comprehension check: instructor says a time, students write the number. 5 times. Fast.
**Student activity:** Comprehension check — write the time you hear.

---

### Slide 3 — Routine with Times (8 min)
**Purpose:** Students add clock times to the routine sentences they already have.
**Teacher notes:**
- Ask students to look at their Session 7 homework and add a specific time (or approximate time with *vers*) to each sentence.
  - Before: *Je me lève.* → After: *Je me lève à sept heures.*
  - Before: *Je me douche.* → After: *Je me douche à sept heures et quart.*
- Pairs: compare — "When do you wake up? When do you go to bed?" Use reflexive verbs + clock time.
- 2-minute pair activity, then back to full group.
**Student activity:** Add times to Session 7 sentences → pairs compare their schedules.

---

### Slide 4 — Describing Another Person's Routine (8 min)
**Purpose:** Students use *il/elle se...* confidently with times.
**Teacher notes:**
- Ask 3–4 students to describe a person from their homework — partner, family member, colleague.
- Prompt if needed: "Tell me three things about your partner's morning. Use *il* or *elle*."
- Listen for: *il me lève* (wrong pronoun — must change) vs. *il se lève* (correct). Address once with the whole group if multiple students make this error.
- After 2–3 descriptions, do a quick round: instructor says *je me lève à sept heures* — students transform to *il se lève à sept heures.* 4–5 items. Fast.
**Student activity:** 3–4 students describe another person → rapid-fire *je → il/elle* transformation.

---

### Slide 5 — Building the Full Routine Narration (8 min)
**Purpose:** Students prepare a 5–6 sentence complete routine before speaking.
**Teacher notes:**
- Students have 4–5 minutes to plan their narration covering:
  - Morning routine (at least 2 reflexive verbs)
  - Work or study during the day (at least 1 -er verb)
  - Evening routine (at least 1 reflexive verb)
  - Times where known
  - At least one sequencing word (*d'abord, ensuite, puis, enfin*)
- Model the structure on screen — do not give them the sentences, just the template:
  1. *Le matin, je me [verb] à [heure].*
  2. *Ensuite, je me [verb]...*
  3. *Je [regular -er verb]...*
  4. *Le soir, je [verb]...*
  5. *Je me couche vers [heure].*
**Student activity:** Students prepare 5-sentence narration in silence (4–5 min).

---

### Slide 6 — Routine Narration Round (12 min)
**Purpose:** Every student narrates their complete daily routine out loud.
**Teacher notes:**
- Every student takes a turn. Notes allowed. 5 sentences minimum.
- Correct one item per student: reflexive pronoun, verb ending, time expression word order. Save group patterns for a debrief.
- After everyone shares: identify 2–3 patterns you heard. Address them briefly.
- Close with: "You have just described your entire day in French. That is a real thing you can do."
**Student activity:** Each student narrates morning-through-evening routine (5+ sentences).

---

### Slide 7 — Wrap-Up (5 min)
**Purpose:** Week 4 writing task assigned; homework preview.
**Teacher notes:**
- Assign: complete written routine paragraph (6–8 sentences), reflexive + -er verbs, times, sequencing.
- Preview Week 5: "Next week we add the irregular verb *aller* and talk about getting around — cities, directions, transportation. We'll also look at telling time in more detail."
**Student activity:** Note homework. Optional: one sentence they did not say in the narration.

---

## Cut First If Running Behind

1. **Slide 4 (describing another person):** Reduce to 4-minute rapid-fire only — skip the 3–4 student descriptions.
2. **Slide 2 (clock times):** Cut *et quart* and *moins le quart* — teach *et demie* only. Assign precision times as reference reading.
3. **Slide 3 (adding times):** Skip pair work — students add times silently and use them in the narration.
4. Do not cut Slide 6 (narration round).

---

## Extension for Fast Cohorts

- **-ir verb preview:** Introduce *finir* as an -ir verb: *Je finis à dix-sept heures.* Point out the -issons pattern for *nous.* Do not drill.
- **Negation with reflexive verbs:** *Je ne me couche pas avant minuit. Il ne se lève jamais avant neuf heures.* Quick drill — 4 affirmative → 4 negative.
- **24h format:** French uses 24h in formal and written contexts. Show: *à dix-neuf heures = à sept heures du soir.* Students convert 3–4 times between formats.

---

## Move to Homework If Needed

- Full *nous/vous/ils* reflexive forms
- -ir and -re verb introduction
- 24h time format
- Quarter-hour clock times
- Written routine paragraph (assign as homework)
