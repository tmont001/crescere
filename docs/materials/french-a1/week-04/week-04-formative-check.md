# French A1 — Week 4 Formative Check

**Administered:** Start of Session 9 (first session of Week 5)
**Time needed:** 6–8 minutes total
**Purpose:** Quick checkpoint — not a test. Tells the instructor whether reflexive verb production and basic time-reading are solid enough to introduce *aller*, prepositions of place, and city vocabulary in Week 5.

---

## Oral Prompts (5 questions — ~1 min per student for the full set)

| # | Prompt | Target response | What to listen for |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | À quelle heure est-ce que tu te réveilles ? | *Je me réveille à [heure].* | Reflexive pronoun *me* present; correct verb form |
| 2 | Describe your morning routine in 3 sentences. | 3 reflexive sentences | *Je me lève / me douche / me prépare*; pronoun before verb each time |
| 3 | What time does your partner / colleague get up? | *Il/elle se lève à [heure].* | *se* — not *me*; correct third-person reflexive |
| 4 | Give me one negative reflexive sentence about your routine. | *Je ne me [verb] pas / jamais...* | *ne* before *me*, *pas* after verb; correct pronoun |
| 5 | Il est quelle heure ? (show 7:30 on a clock or say the number) | *Il est sept heures et demie.* | Correct time expression |

---

## Written Prompts (5 questions — students write answers, ~3 min)

Paste into the session chat or distribute at the start of Session 9.

**Instructions to students:** Answer in French. Short answers only.

1. Write: "I get up at 6:45." (Use *se lever* and *et quart* or approximate)
2. Write: "She doesn't shower in the morning." (*se doucher*)
3. What is the *il/elle* reflexive pronoun? (one word)
4. Write: "They (masc.) go to bed around 11 p.m." (*se coucher*, approximate time)
5. Write: "We get ready quickly." (*se préparer, nous* form)

**Answer key:**
1. *Je me lève à six heures quarante-cinq.* / *Je me lève à sept heures moins le quart.* / *Je me lève vers sept heures.* (accept any reasonable approximation)
2. *Elle ne se douche pas le matin.*
3. *se* (or *s'* before vowel)
4. *Ils se couchent vers vingt-trois heures.* / *Ils se couchent vers onze heures du soir.*
5. *Nous nous préparons rapidement.*

---

## Instructor Scoring Notes

Not graded. Use it to make one of three decisions:

| Result | What it means | What to do |
|---|---|---|
| Students handle all 5 oral prompts | Reflexive verbs are solid | Move into Week 5 (*aller*, directions) immediately |
| Students stumble on 1–2 oral prompts | Minor gaps | 5-minute reflexive warm-up at start of Sessions 9 and 10 |
| Students stumble on 3+ oral prompts | Reflexive pattern is not yet secure | Add 10-minute reflexive review to Sessions 9–10; carry forward as a warm-up habit |

**Most common Week 4 gaps:**
- *Je lève* (missing *me*) → the most common reflexive error; say "where's the me?" as a consistent cue
- *Il me lève* (wrong pronoun for third person) → "The pronoun must match the subject: *je → me, il → se*"
- *Je ne me lève ne pas* (double negation or wrong position) → *ne* before *me*, *pas* after the verb: *je ne me lève pas*
- *Il est sept heures et demi* (masculine *demi* without -e) → *et demie* — the -e is required when *heure(s)* precedes; accept either in speech, correct in writing

---

## Ready to Move On

A student is ready for Week 5 content when they can:

- [ ] Use *je me lève / me couche / me prépare / me douche* without looking at a reference
- [ ] Apply the correct reflexive pronoun for *je, tu, il/elle* without prompting
- [ ] Build a negative reflexive sentence (*ne...pas* around the pronoun + verb)
- [ ] Read and say times at the hour level (*il est sept heures*)
- [ ] Narrate 5 sentences covering their morning and evening routine without notes

Full *nous/vous/ils* reflexive forms and -ir/-re verbs are not Week 5 gates. They will be reinforced as new verbs are added.
