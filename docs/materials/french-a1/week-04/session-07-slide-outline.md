# French A1 — Session 7 Slide Outline
## Reflexive Verbs: Introduction

**Session length:** 60 minutes
**Session-end goal:** Students understand how the reflexive pronoun works, can place it correctly before the verb in first person, and can say at least 3 daily routine sentences using reflexive verbs without looking at a reference.

> **Pacing note:** The speaking round (Slide 6) is non-negotiable. Every student must say at least 2 first-person reflexive sentences out loud. The pronoun placement is the skill — get it into students' mouths before they leave.

---

## Core 60-Minute Plan

### Slide 1 — Warm-Up: Week 3 Check and -er Verb Recall (7 min)
**Purpose:** Activate -er verbs from Week 3 before adding reflexive verbs. If Week 3 formative check showed gaps, this is the moment for brief repair.
**Teacher notes:**
- Ask each student: give me one sentence about your daily routine using an -er verb. No reflexive yet — just consolidate what they know.
- Listen for: verb endings, negation issues, word order.
- If formative check showed persistent *je travailles* errors → quick drill: "What ending does *je* take? What ending does *il* take? Now say *je travaille* and *il travaille* back to back."
- Do not over-review. 2–3 minutes maximum on repair, then move forward.
**Student activity:** Each student says one -er verb routine sentence.

---

### Slide 2 — What Is a Reflexive Verb? (10 min)
**Purpose:** Students understand the concept before learning the forms — they already know two reflexive phrases, so this is mostly recognition.
**Teacher notes:**
- Say: "You already know two reflexive verbs — *je me lève* and *je me couche.* What's that *me* doing there?"
- Explain: "Reflexive verbs express actions you do to yourself or for yourself. Getting up, getting dressed, washing, going to bed. In French, there's a small pronoun that signals this — *me* for *je*, *te* for *tu*, *se* for *il* and *elle.* That pronoun lives between the subject and the verb."
- Write the table on screen: *je me, tu te, il/elle se, nous nous, vous vous, ils/elles se.*
- Point out elision: *me + vowel → m', se + vowel → s'.* Write the two rules and give examples: *je m'appelle, elle s'habille.*
- The verb itself conjugates normally: *lever → je me lève* (like any -er verb, plus the accent). "The grammar of the verb has not changed. Only the pronoun is new."
**Student activity:** Students repeat each pronoun pair after you: *je me, tu te, il/elle se* × 2.

---

### Slide 3 — Core Reflexive Verbs (12 min)
**Purpose:** Students hear 5 core reflexive verbs in context and begin to associate them with their daily routine.
**Teacher notes:**
- Introduce each verb with a sentence and a brief anchor:
  - *s'appeler:* *Je m'appelle [name]. Comment vous appelez-vous ?* — "You've known this since Week 1. It was a reflexive verb all along."
  - *se réveiller:* *Je me réveille à six heures et demie. Je n'aime pas me réveiller tôt.*
  - *se lever:* *Je me lève vers sept heures. Mon chien se lève avant moi.*
  - *se doucher:* *Je me douche le matin — ça m'aide à me réveiller.*
  - *se préparer:* *Je me prépare en vingt minutes. Vous vous préparez en combien de temps ?*
- After each: students repeat the sentence. Then ask the group to give the *tu* form: *tu te réveilles, tu te lèves.* Just these two additional forms — don't drill all six.
- Note *se coucher* for later: "We'll use this in the evening routine."
**Student activity:** Repeat each sentence → give the *tu* form.

---

### Slide 4 — Building *Je me...* Sentences (10 min)
**Purpose:** Students write or note 3–4 first-person reflexive routine sentences that are true for them.
**Teacher notes:**
- Show the model structure on screen:
  - *Je me réveille à [heure].*
  - *Je me lève vers [heure].*
  - *Je me douche [le matin / le soir].*
  - *Je me prépare en [X] minutes.*
  - *Je me couche à [heure].*
- Give students 3 minutes to write their sentences — they choose the ones that fit their real routine. They do not need to use all five.
- Walk the virtual room and look for: missing reflexive pronoun, wrong pronoun, elision errors.
**Student activity:** Students write 3–4 first-person reflexive sentences (3 min silent work).

---

### Slide 5 — *Tu te...* and *Il/elle se...* (8 min)
**Purpose:** Students extend the pattern to second and third person.
**Teacher notes:**
- Ask pairs to exchange one sentence: student A says a *je* sentence, student B transforms it to *tu* form. "Je me lève à sept heures → Tu te lèves à sept heures ?"
- Then ask 3–4 students to tell you about another person: a partner, parent, or colleague. "Tell me when your partner gets up, using *il* or *elle.*"
- Correct: *il me lève* (wrong — the pronoun must change with the subject) → *il se lève.*
**Student activity:** Pairs transform *je* → *tu* → instructor asks 3–4 students for *il/elle* sentences.

---

### Slide 6 — Speaking Round (8 min)
**Purpose:** Every student says at least 2 reflexive sentences from memory.
**Teacher notes:**
- Each student says 2–3 reflexive sentences about their own routine. Notes allowed.
- Correct one item per student: pronoun agreement, elision, verb form. Prioritize missing pronoun above all else.
- Affirm good reflexive pronoun use explicitly: "Good — *je me lève*. The pronoun is there. That's the key."
**Student activity:** Each student says 2–3 reflexive routine sentences out loud.

---

### Slide 7 — Wrap-Up (5 min)
**Purpose:** Homework preview; close with the concept.
**Teacher notes:**
- Say: "Homework tonight is your morning routine in 4 sentences — reflexive verbs only. Then try describing someone else's routine using *il/elle se...*"
- Preview Session 8: "Next session we add times, sequence the full day, and each of you will narrate your complete daily routine from morning to evening."
**Student activity:** Note homework. Optional: one more reflexive sentence before logging off.

---

## Cut First If Running Behind

1. **Slide 5 (*tu/il/elle* forms):** Skip the pair activity. Just ask 2–3 students for *il/elle* sentences directly. 2 minutes maximum.
2. **Slide 3 (core verbs):** Cover 4 verbs instead of 5. Skip *se réveiller* — students can learn it from the vocabulary sheet.
3. **Slide 4 (building sentences):** Reduce silent work to 90 seconds. Students build one sentence only.
4. Do not cut Slide 6 (speaking round).

---

## Extension for Fast Cohorts

- **Nous/vous/ils forms:** If the cohort picks up *me/te/se* quickly, drill *nous nous levons, vous vous levez, ils se lèvent.* Note the double *nous* and double *vous* with humor.
- **Negation with reflexive verbs:** *Je ne me lève pas tôt. Il ne se douche pas le matin.* The *ne* goes before the reflexive pronoun; *pas* goes after the verb.
- **Additional reflexive verbs:** *se brosser les dents* (to brush teeth), *s'habiller* (to get dressed), *se maquiller* (to put on makeup), *se raser* (to shave). Students add one more to their morning routine.

---

## Move to Homework If Needed

- *Nous/vous/ils/elles* forms of reflexive verbs
- Negation with reflexive verbs (*ne... pas* around the pronoun + verb)
- Additional reflexive verbs (*se brosser, s'habiller*)
- Clock times (*il est... heures et demie / et quart*)
