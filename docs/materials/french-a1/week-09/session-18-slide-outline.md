# French A1 — Session 18 Slide Outline
## Obligations and Ability: Devoir, Vouloir, Pouvoir

**Session length:** 50 minutes (on plan) — can run 60 min for slower cohorts
**Session-end goal:** Every student can conduct a short planning role play — proposing, declining, and negotiating — using *aller + infinitif, devoir, vouloir,* and *pouvoir* in combination.

> **Pacing note:** The scheduling role play (Slide 7) is non-negotiable. Every student participates in at least one exchange before the session ends. Compress the *vouloir* and *pouvoir* review blocks (Slides 4–5, each to 3 min) before cutting Slide 7. Students already know both verbs from Week 6.

---

## Core Plan

### Slide 1 — Warm-Up: Aller + Infinitif Recall (5 min)
**Purpose:** Re-activate Session 17 material before adding the modal layer.
**Teacher notes:**
- Each student gives one sentence from their homework week plan. Correct *aller* form and time expression only.
- Preview: "Today we add three more tools for talking about plans: *devoir* (you have to), *vouloir* (you want to), *pouvoir* (you can). With these four — *aller, devoir, vouloir, pouvoir* — you can have a real planning conversation in French."
**Student activity:** One *je vais + inf + time expression* sentence each.

---

### Slide 2 — Devoir + Infinitif (8 min)
**Purpose:** Students learn and can use *devoir* to express obligation.
**Teacher notes:**
- Write the full conjugation:

  | je | tu | il/elle | nous | vous | ils/elles |
  |---|---|---|---|---|---|
  | dois | dois | doit | devons | devez | doivent |

- Address the forms: "Je dois / tu dois — identical. Il doit. Nous devons. Vous devez. Ils doivent — the -ent is silent."
- *Devoir* is genuinely new (first introduced formally here). It expresses obligation — something you have to or must do.
- Three examples with different contexts:
  - *Je dois travailler demain.* — I have to work tomorrow. (work obligation)
  - *Tu dois appeler le médecin.* — You need to call the doctor. (advice/necessity)
  - *Nous devons partir à 8h.* — We have to leave at 8. (time constraint)
- Key warning: *Je dois travailler*, NOT *Je dois de travailler.* No preposition between *devoir* and the infinitive.
- Quick round: each student gives one *je dois + infinitif* sentence about their week.
**Student activity:** Give one *je dois* sentence.

---

### Slide 3 — Devoir in Contrast with Aller + Infinitif (5 min)
**Purpose:** Students distinguish obligation from neutral future and use both in context.
**Teacher notes:**
- Contrast drill — students decide which is more natural:
  - *"I'm going to leave at 8"* (neutral plan) → *Je vais partir à 8h.*
  - *"I have to leave at 8"* (there's a reason/obligation) → *Je dois partir à 8h.*
  - Both grammatically correct — the difference is whether obligation is implied.
- Practical planning phrase: *Je vais devoir annuler.* — I'm going to have to cancel. (combining *aller + devoir + infinitif* — stacking modals, which is natural in French).
- Quick drill: give a scenario; students produce either *je vais* or *je dois.*
  - "You have a meeting at 9." → *J'ai une réunion à 9h — je dois partir maintenant.*
  - "You decided to cook dinner tonight." → *Je vais faire la cuisine ce soir.*
**Student activity:** Classify 4 scenarios as *je vais* or *je dois.*

---

### Slide 4 — Vouloir + Infinitif Review (5 min)
**Purpose:** Students recall *vouloir* in full modal use (not just *je voudrais*).
**Teacher notes:**
- Not a re-teach. Students have *vouloir* from Week 6. This slide moves them from *je voudrais* (polite restaurant ordering) to *je veux/veut/veulent* in planning contexts.
- Quick form recall: *je veux, tu veux, il veut, nous voulons, vous voulez, ils veulent.* Write on board; ask students to supply forms.
- Address *ils veulent* — the most irregular-feeling form: "The -eu- of the singular disappears in the plural."
- Quick drill: 3 students give one *je veux + inf* sentence about their week.
**Student activity:** Three *je veux* sentences from three students.

---

### Slide 5 — Pouvoir + Infinitif Review (5 min)
**Purpose:** Students recall *pouvoir* for planning conversations, especially *je ne peux pas.*
**Teacher notes:**
- Not a re-teach. Students have *pouvoir* from Week 6. This slide focuses on *je ne peux pas* as a planning response and the positive *je peux* for suggesting/accepting.
- Quick form recall: *je peux, tu peux, il peut, nous pouvons, vous pouvez, ils peuvent.* Write on board.
- Key planning uses:
  - *Je peux venir à 18h.* — I can come at 6pm.
  - *Je ne peux pas — j'ai une réunion.* — I can't — I have a meeting.
  - *Tu peux venir demain?* — Can you come tomorrow?
- Quick drill: 3 students give one *je peux/je ne peux pas* sentence.
**Student activity:** Three *pouvoir* sentences.

---

### Slide 6 — Combining the Three Modals (10 min)
**Purpose:** Students combine *devoir, vouloir, pouvoir* in realistic planning exchanges.
**Teacher notes:**
- Introduce the four core patterns with examples. Write each on the board:
  1. *Je veux venir, mais je dois travailler.* — I want to come, but I have to work.
  2. *Je veux venir, mais je ne peux pas.* — I want to come, but I can't.
  3. *Je peux venir si tu veux.* — I can come if you like.
  4. *Je vais devoir annuler — je suis désolé(e).* — I'm going to have to cancel — I'm sorry.
- Guided scenario drill: instructor gives a situation; students produce the full response using the patterns above.
  - Situation: "You want to see friends Saturday but you have to work."
    → *Je veux voir mes amis samedi, mais je dois travailler.*
  - Situation: "You can't come Thursday — you have a meeting."
    → *Je ne peux pas venir jeudi — j'ai une réunion.*
  - Situation: "You need to cancel your dinner plans."
    → *Je vais devoir annuler le dîner — je suis vraiment désolé(e).*
**Student activity:** Respond to 3 guided scenarios using the combining patterns.

---

### Slide 7 — Role Play: Make a Plan (12 min)
**Purpose:** Every student participates in a planning exchange — proposing, declining, and negotiating.
**Teacher notes:**
- Role play setup: Student A proposes a plan; Student B declines with a reason; Student A suggests an alternative; Student B accepts or negotiates further.
- Minimum script for each role (students do NOT use scripts — they improvise):
  - A proposes: *"Tu veux [activity] [time expression]?"*
  - B declines: *"Je veux bien, mais je [devoir/ne pas pouvoir] — j'ai [reason]. Tu peux [alternative time]?"*
  - A accepts or offers another option: *"Pas de problème! Alors [alternative plan]."*
  - B confirms: *"Parfait! On se retrouve à [time] devant [place]?"*
- Each student plays both roles across two exchanges (instructor-student first, then student-student if pairs allow).
- Correct one item per student. Common targets: missing *pas* in *je ne peux pas*; using *je dois* without infinitive.
- After all students: note 2 patterns across the group.
**Student activity:** Each student participates in at least one full planning exchange.

---

**Purpose:** Assign homework; preview Week 10.
**Teacher notes:**
- Preview Week 10: "Next week we focus on hobbies and leisure — *jouer à* for sports and games, *faire de* for activities. You'll combine this with everything you know to have extended preference conversations."

- Remind students: the Week 9 formative check will ask them to produce verb forms and conduct a planning exchange.
**Student activity:** Note homework.

## Cut First If Running Behind

1. **Slide 3 (devoir vs. aller contrast):** Reduce to 2 min; just give the *je vais devoir annuler* phrase.
2. **Slides 4–5 (vouloir/pouvoir review):** Each to 2 min; just confirm form recall, no drilling.
3. **Slide 6 (combining):** Give just 2 patterns instead of 4; students produce only 2 scenarios.
4. Do not cut Slide 7 (role play).

## Extension for Fast Cohorts

- **Je dois, il faut:** Introduce *il faut + infinitif* informally: *"Il faut partir maintenant"* — we need to leave now. Contrast: *je dois* (personal); *il faut* (impersonal, like Spanish *hay que*). Full *il faut* teaching comes in Week 11 per curriculum.
- **Vous devez (polite advice):** *"Vous devez réserver à l'avance"* — You should book ahead. Extends *devoir* from first-person obligation into second-person advice.
- **Je voudrais vs. je veux:** Contrast the conditional (*je voudrais aller*) with the indicative (*je veux aller*). *Voudrais* is more polite in social invitations.
- **Counter-offer role play:** More complex negotiation: each student must propose an alternative after declining.
