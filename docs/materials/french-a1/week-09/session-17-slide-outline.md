# French A1 — Session 17 Slide Outline
## Near Future: Aller + Infinitif

**Session length:** 55 minutes (on plan) — can run 60 min for slower cohorts
**Session-end goal:** Every student can describe 4–5 upcoming plans in connected sentences, using *aller + infinitif* with accurate future time expressions.

> **Pacing note:** The week-plan speaking round (Slide 6) is non-negotiable. Every student speaks before the session ends. Compress the vocabulary block (Slide 4) before cutting Slide 6. *Aller + infinitif* itself is low risk — students already know *aller.* The time expressions and planning vocabulary need the most drilling time.

---

## Core 60-Minute Plan

### Slide 1 — Warm-Up: Week 8 Close (5 min)
**Purpose:** Close the Week 8 loop; activate irregular verbs from Session 16.
**Teacher notes:**
- Ask each student one sentence from Week 8 neighborhood context: *"Qu'est-ce que tu fais généralement le week-end?"* (using *faire* from Session 16)
- If Week 8 formative check showed widespread *savoir/connaître* errors: one 30-second group reminder. No re-teaching.
- Brief preview: "This week we move to the future — what you're going to do, when, and with whom."
**Student activity:** One *je fais* sentence each.

---

### Slide 2 — Aller + Infinitif: Structure (8 min)
**Purpose:** Students understand the structure and can produce it immediately.
**Teacher notes:**
- Build the structure on the board step by step:
  1. *Je vais au cinéma.* — going to a place (already known from Week 5)
  2. *Je vais voir un film.* — going to do something (new use)
  3. "Same *aller*, same conjugation, different thing after it: instead of a place, an action."
- Key difference: *Je vais au cinéma* (à + place) vs. *Je vais aller au cinéma* (aller + infinitive — slightly redundant but perfectly natural in French; both are said).
- Full structure: *aller (conjugated) + infinitif du verbe principal*
- Write all 6 forms with an example infinitive:
  - *je vais travailler, tu vas appeler, il va partir, nous allons dîner, vous allez voyager, ils vont prendre*
- Negation immediately: *Je ne vais pas travailler demain.* — negation wraps around *aller.*
- Quick round: each student turns a present-tense sentence into a near-future sentence.
  - *Je travaille aujourd'hui.* → *Je vais travailler demain.*
  - *Elle appelle sa mère.* → *Elle va appeler sa mère ce soir.*
**Student activity:** Transform 4 present-tense prompts into near-future sentences.

---

### Slide 3 — Future Time Expressions (8 min)
**Purpose:** Students learn 8 future time expressions and can attach them to plans naturally.
**Teacher notes:**
- Introduce time expressions in pairs of "today/near" vs. "next/far":
  - *ce soir / cet après-midi / ce week-end* — this evening / this afternoon / this weekend
  - *demain / demain matin* — tomorrow / tomorrow morning
  - *la semaine prochaine / le mois prochain / l'année prochaine* — next week / next month / next year
  - *dans deux jours / bientôt* — in two days / soon
- After each pair: students produce one *je vais + inf + time expression* sentence.
- Flag gender agreement: *la semaine prochaine* (f.) vs. *le mois prochain* (m.) — the adjective *prochain/prochaine* must agree.
- Quick slot-filling drill: instructor gives a verb + a time expression; student produces a sentence.
  - *partir / ce week-end* → *Je vais partir ce week-end.*
  - *appeler / demain matin* → *Je vais appeler demain matin.*
  - *voyager / l'année prochaine* → *Je vais voyager l'année prochaine.*
**Student activity:** Slot-filling drill (5 items); then each student gives one true plan with a time expression.

---

### Slide 4 — Planning Vocabulary (7 min)
**Purpose:** Students acquire event and activity vocabulary needed for the speaking round.
**Teacher notes:**
- Introduce in context, not as a list:
  - Events: *un rendez-vous, une réunion, une soirée, un dîner, un voyage, des vacances, un concert*
  - Actions: *travailler, étudier, voyager, voir des amis, faire la cuisine, rentrer chez soi, réserver, annuler*
- After each group: students produce one *je vais + event/action* sentence.
- Quick question drill: *"Qu'est-ce que tu vas faire ce soir?"* — 3 students answer.
**Student activity:** Give one plan using event or activity vocabulary.

---

### Slide 5 — Questions for Planning (8 min)
**Purpose:** Students can ask and answer planning questions — the conversational frame for the speaking round and role play.
**Teacher notes:**
- Introduce key questions:
  - *Qu'est-ce que tu vas faire ce week-end?*
  - *Tu es libre demain?*
  - *On se retrouve quand? / À quelle heure?*
- Quick paired exchange: Student A asks one question; Student B answers with a plan using *aller + infinitif + time expression.*
- Then switch.
- Monitor for: correct *aller* form; time expression present; infinitive (not conjugated verb after *vais*).
**Student activity:** Two paired exchanges — A asks, B answers; then switch.

---

### Slide 6 — Speaking Round: My Week's Plans (12 min)
**Purpose:** Every student describes 4–5 upcoming plans in connected sentences.
**Teacher notes:**
- Each student speaks for 60–90 seconds. Notes allowed for vocabulary; *aller + infinitif* and time expressions from memory.
- Minimum required elements per student:
  - [ ] At least 3 *je vais + infinitif* sentences
  - [ ] At least 2 different time expressions
  - [ ] At least 1 negative plan (*je ne vais pas...*)
- Correct one item per student. Common targets: missing infinitive (*je vais travaille* → *je vais travailler*); wrong *aller* form (*tu vais* → *tu vas*); time expression gender (*la semaine prochain* → *prochaine*).
- After all students: note 2 patterns across the group.
**Student activity:** Each student delivers a 4–5 sentence week plan.

---

### Slide 7 — Wrap-Up (7 min)
**Purpose:** Assign homework; preview Session 18.
**Teacher notes:**
- Preview Session 18: "Next session we add three essential verbs for explaining constraints: *devoir* (I have to), *vouloir* (I want to), *pouvoir* (I can). Combined with what you learned today, you'll be able to have a full planning conversation — proposing, accepting, declining, negotiating."
- Homework: written week plan + 3 planning questions.
**Student activity:** Note homework.

## Extension for Fast Cohorts

- **Penser + infinitif (intention):** *"Je pense voyager en Italie l'année prochaine"* — softer than *aller + inf.* Introduce as an alternative for expressing uncertainty about plans.
- **Reported plans:** *"Mon ami va partir en vacances la semaine prochaine."* Students describe someone else's plans in 3rd person.
- **Question chains:** Students ask each other 3 planning questions in sequence; answer with *aller + inf.*
