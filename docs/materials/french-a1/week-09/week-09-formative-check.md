# French A1 — Week 9 Formative Check
## Plans and Obligations

**When to administer:** At the start of Session 19, or as a closing check at the end of Session 18.
**Format:** Oral section first (5 prompts); written section second (5 prompts); short role play (1 exchange).
**Purpose:** Diagnostic tool. Not a grade. Informs whether the cohort is ready to progress to Week 10 content (*jouer à / faire de*, hobbies and leisure) or needs reinforcement of Week 9 structures.

---

## Oral Prompts (5 prompts — instructor reads aloud)

Administer to each student individually or in round-robin. Allow 10–15 seconds per prompt.

**Prompt 1:** "Tell me two plans you have for this week."
*Target:* Two *je vais + infinitif* sentences, each with a different time expression.
*Minimum pass:* Both sentences have a correct *aller* form + infinitive. One time expression correct.

**Prompt 2:** "Tell me one thing you have to do this week that you don't particularly want to do."
*Target:* *Je dois + inf... mais je ne veux pas / ce n'est pas très agréable.* Or simply a *je dois* sentence with a clear obligation.
*Minimum pass:* *Devoir* form correct (*je dois*, not *je doit* or *je devais*).

**Prompt 3:** "Tell me something you want to do but can't do this week."
*Target:* *Je veux + inf, mais je ne peux pas / je dois + inf.*
*Minimum pass:* At least two of the three verbs used correctly in one exchange.

**Prompt 4:** "Give me one form of *devoir* that is NOT *je dois* or *tu dois.* Use it in a sentence."
*Target:* *il doit / nous devons / vous devez / ils doivent* — any, in context.
*Watch for:* *Ils devont* (→ *ils doivent*); *vous devez* vs. *vous devrait* (conditional — not yet introduced).

**Prompt 5 — Mini role play:** Instructor says: *"Je vais organiser un dîner vendredi soir — tu peux venir?"* Student must respond with a natural planning exchange: accept, decline with a reason, or propose an alternative.
*Target:* Student uses at least one of *devoir/vouloir/pouvoir* in their response. *"Vendredi je ne peux pas — je dois travailler. Samedi, je suis libre!"*
*Minimum pass:* Natural response that includes a reason or alternative, however brief.

---

## Written Prompts (5 items — students write independently)

**Item 1:** Write one sentence about what you are going to do tomorrow.
*Expected:* *Je vais + infinitif + tomorrow time expression.* E.g., *Je vais voir mes amis demain soir.*
*Watch for:* Infinitive vs. conjugated verb (*je vais travaille* → *je vais travailler*).

**Item 2:** Translate: "She has to leave at 8 — she has a meeting."
*Expected:* *Elle doit partir à 8h — elle a une réunion.*
*Watch for:* *Elle doit de partir* (→ *elle doit partir* — no preposition); *elle doit* vs. *elle dois.*

**Item 3:** Fill in the blank: *Je ___________ aller au cinéma, mais je ___________.*
One blank = want (1st person), one blank = can't (1st person).
*Expected:* *veux / ne peux pas*
*Watch for:* Omitting *ne* in *ne peux pas* (accept in informal spoken contexts but flag for written form).

**Item 4:** Write one sentence using *il/elle doit* AND one using *ils/elles doivent.*
*Expected:* *Il doit travailler le week-end. Ils doivent rentrer avant minuit.*
*Watch for:* *Ils devont* / *ils doivent*; *il doit* vs. *il dois.*

**Item 5:** Write 3–4 sentences proposing a plan to someone — include a time, a place, and a question asking if they can come.
*Expected:* *Je vais [activity] [time]. Tu veux venir? On se retrouve à [time] devant [place]. Tu peux?*
*Minimum pass:* *Aller + inf* used correctly; a question about availability present.

## Scoring Decision Table

| Student output | Decision |
|---|---|
| All 5 oral prompts pass + 4–5 written correct | Ready to move on to Week 10 |
| 4 oral pass + 3–4 written correct | Ready to move on — monitor modal use in Week 10 |
| *Aller + infinitif* correct but *devoir* forms weak | Move to Week 10 — give *devoir* conjugation as warm-up drill in Session 19 |
| Modal combinations (*vouloir + mais + pouvoir*) not yet fluid | Move to Week 10 — build more role play practice into Session 19 warm-up |
| Time expressions inconsistent | Move to Week 10 — assign time expression practice as homework |
| Fewer than 3 oral prompts pass OR fewer than 3 written correct | Flag for instructor follow-up; do not delay Week 10 for whole cohort |

---

## Ready to Move On

Before beginning Week 10 (*jouer à / faire de*, frequency adverbs), a student should be able to:
- [ ] Produce *aller + infinitif* automatically for 4+ different verbs
- [ ] Use at least 3 future time expressions accurately
- [ ] Conjugate *devoir* correctly in *je, il, ils* forms
- [ ] Say *je ne peux pas* as an automatic response when declining
- [ ] Combine at least two modal verbs in a single planning exchange
- [ ] Conduct a minimal planning role play: propose → decline → negotiate

---

## Not a Gate

The following items are NOT required for progression to Week 10:
- Full *devoir* mastery in all 6 forms (*devons, devez* in active production)
- *Il faut + infinitif* (this comes in Week 11 per curriculum)
- *Je voudrais* vs. *je veux* distinction in planning contexts
- Conditional forms (*vous devriez, vous pourriez*) — A2 territory
- *Penser + infinitif* for intention (extension material at A1)
- Written exchange beyond 4–5 sentences
