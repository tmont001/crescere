# French A1 — Week 3 Instructor Notes

**Sessions:** 5 and 6
**Theme:** Regular -er Verbs and Daily Routine

---

## Week Objective

By the end of Week 3, students can:
- Conjugate 8 regular -er verbs in all six forms without looking at a table
- Build affirmative and negative sentences using ne...pas
- Use 4–5 frequency expressions correctly in a sentence
- Narrate their daily routine in 4–6 sentences using sequencing words and time expressions

Full mastery of all six forms is the goal. If the cohort is slow, protect *je*, *tu*, and *il/elle* production and assign the *nous/vous/ils* forms for homework drilling — but do not skip the forms entirely. Students who leave Week 3 without *nous* and *vous* will have gaps that affect every verb introduced from Week 4 onward.

**Reflexive verbs:** The curriculum assigns reflexive verbs to Session 6. Given the pacing constraint (realistic 60-minute class), reflexive verbs are introduced here as a preview at the end of Session 6. Full conjugation, reflexive pronoun placement, and production are Week 4 material. Do not teach them as a new grammar block in Week 3.

## Session 5 Pacing — Regular -er Verbs

**Theme:** What is a regular verb? How does the pattern work?

| Block | Content | Time |
|---|---|---|
| Warm-up | Week 2 formative check debrief; quick description sentence from each student | 7 min |
| -er verb pattern | Ending table; pronunciation of silent endings; choral drill | 10 min |
| Core verbs × 3 forms | *je, tu, il/elle* for 6 core verbs in context sentences | 12 min |
| Sentence building | Students build sentences from a subject + verb prompt | 10 min |
| Negation preview | ne...pas introduced; 4–5 sentences transformed | 8 min |
| Speaking round | Each student: 3 things they do / don't do in French | 8 min |
| Wrap-up | Homework preview; all-six-forms table assigned | 5 min |
| **Total** | | **~60 min** |

**What must be protected:** The speaking round (block 6). Every student must say at least one affirmative and one negative sentence about themselves before the session ends.

## Session 6 Pacing — Negation, Frequency, Daily Routine

**Theme:** Completing the pattern; talking about how often and when.

| Block | Content | Time |
|---|---|---|
| Warm-up | -er verb warm-up: students conjugate *travailler* and *étudier* from memory | 6 min |
| Negation: ne...pas full practice | Transformation drill; spoken French note (*ne* often dropped) | 10 min |
| Frequency expressions | 6 expressions introduced and drilled in sentences | 10 min |
| Daily routine vocabulary | Sequencing words + time-of-day expressions | 7 min |
| Daily routine narration | Students build and say a 5-sentence routine | 13 min |
| Reflexive verbs preview | *Je me lève* and *Je me couche* — introduce as phrases only | 5 min |
| Wrap-up | Homework preview; Week 3 routine paragraph assigned | 4 min |
| **Total** | | **~55 min** |

**What must be protected:** The daily routine narration (block 5). Every student must say their routine in 4–5 sentences out loud. This is the Week 3 speaking milestone.

---

## Common Student Mistakes — Week 3

| Mistake | Correction | When to address |
|---|---|---|
| *Je parles* or *Il parlez* | Only one set of endings; *je parle, il parle* (silent -e). Correct each time. | Session 5 drilling block |
| *Je ne parle pas de français* | *Ne...pas* wraps the verb; *de* is not needed here. *Je ne parle pas français.* | Session 6 negation block |
| *Je mange jamais* without *ne* | Written French requires *ne...jamais*. Spoken French drops the *ne* but written always keeps it. | Session 6 — make the spoken/written distinction explicit |
| Forgetting *ne* entirely in writing | *Je ne travaille pas.* Both parts required in writing. | Correct consistently from Session 6 onward |
| *Nous parlons* → *Nous parleons* | No extra -e for *parler*; only *manger* (and -ger verbs) need the extra -e before -ons. | Note when *manger* is introduced |
| Mixing up *aimer* + infinitive vs. *aimer* + noun | Both are correct: *J'aime le cinéma* and *J'aime regarder des films*. | Note when aimer appears |

## Suggested Instructor Language

- **For introducing the -er pattern:** *"Good news: about 90% of French verbs are regular -er verbs. Once you know this pattern, you can conjugate hundreds of verbs. Bad news: the endings in the singular and third-person plural are all silent. So when you speak, context and the subject pronoun are what tell your listener which person you mean."*
- **For ne...pas:** *"Negation in French wraps the verb like a sandwich — ne before, pas after. Je parle becomes Je ne parle pas. Easy to remember; easy to forget the ne in speech. In class and in writing, always use both halves."*
- **For jamais:** *"Jamais replaces pas — it's still a sandwich, just with different bread. Je ne mange jamais de fast-food. You would never say 'Je ne mange pas jamais' — choose one or the other."*
- **For the routine narration:** *"I want you to tell me what you do on a typical day — not the interesting days, the normal one. Use the verbs we've practiced. You can use notes. Five sentences is the goal."*

---

## What to Skip If Time Is Tight

- *Manger* and *commencer* spelling-change nous forms — mention they exist; assign as a vocabulary note; do not drill
- The full frequency adverb set — prioritize *souvent, parfois, jamais*; assign the others as reference reading
- Reflexive verb preview — skip if Session 6 is already full; introduce at the start of Week 4 instead
- Detailed time expressions (*vers, tôt, tard*) — students can say *le matin, le soir* and a specific hour; that is enough for Week 3

---

## What to Assign as Homework If Pacing Runs Long

If Session 5 runs out of time before the speaking round:
→ Students write their 3 sentences for homework; open Session 6 with sharing

If Session 6 runs out of time before the daily routine narration:
→ Students write their 5-sentence routine as homework; assign them to record it out loud and be ready to share at Session 7

---

## Week 3 Priority Reminder

The most important outcome of Week 3 is that students can **produce a sentence about what they do using a regular -er verb without hesitation**. The verb pattern itself is the foundation for the rest of A1 — every new verb added in Weeks 4–12 reinforces the same conjugation habit. A student who leaves Week 3 uncertain about *il/elle* forms or ne...pas placement will carry that gap forward. Correct both consistently.
