# French A1 — Session 5 Homework

**Complete before Session 6.**
**Estimated time: 20–25 minutes**

---

## Part 1 — The -er Ending from Memory (5 min)

Cover the table below and say all six endings from memory. Then apply them to *parler.*

| Subject | Ending | Parler |
|---|---|---|
| je | -e | parle |
| tu | -es | parles |
| il / elle | -e | parle |
| nous | -ons | parlons |
| vous | -ez | parlez |
| ils / elles | -ent | parlent |

Say *je parle, tu parles, il parle, nous parlons, vous parlez, ils parlent* three times out loud without looking.

Then try it with *habiter* on your own.

---

## Part 2 — Written Sentences (10 min)

Write 5 sentences about yourself in French. Use 5 different verbs from the Week 3 list. At least one sentence must be negative.

**Your sentences must include:**
1. Where you live (*habiter*)
2. What you do for work or study (*travailler* or *étudier*)
3. Something you like (*aimer*)
4. Something you watch or listen to (*regarder* or *écouter*)
5. Something you do NOT do (any verb + *ne...pas*)

**Example:**
> *J'habite à Seattle. Je travaille dans une entreprise de technologie. J'aime le cinéma français. Je regarde souvent des documentaires. Je n'étudie pas l'espagnol — j'étudie le français !*

---

## Part 3 — Speaking Practice (5 min)

Say your 5 sentences out loud three times. On the third time, do it without looking at your notes.

Then try describing a family member or friend in 2–3 sentences using *il/elle* forms:
> *Ma sœur s'appelle Anne. Elle habite à Lyon. Elle travaille dans une pharmacie.*

---

## Vocabulary Quick-Check

Translate without looking at your notes:

1. She works in a hospital → _______________
2. We eat at noon → _______________
3. I don't watch TV → _______________
4. They (masc.) listen to music → _______________
5. Do you study French? → _______________

**Answers:**
1. *Elle travaille dans un hôpital.*
2. *Nous mangeons à midi.*
3. *Je ne regarde pas la télé.*
4. *Ils écoutent de la musique.*
5. *Tu étudies le français ?* (or *Vous étudiez le français ?*)

---

## Optional Extension

**Practice all six forms.** Choose one more verb from the week's list and write it in all six forms. Then write one sentence for each of the six subject pronouns — six sentences total. It does not have to make perfect sense; the goal is the pattern.
