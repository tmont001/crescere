# French A1 — Session 6 Slide Outline
## Negation, Frequency, and Daily Routine

**Session length:** 60 minutes
**Session-end goal:** Students can narrate their daily routine in 5+ sentences using affirmative and negative -er verb sentences, at least two frequency expressions, and sequencing words. Students hear reflexive verbs for the first time as phrases (*Je me lève, Je me couche*) — not as a grammar topic.

> **Pacing note:** The daily routine narration (Slide 6) is non-negotiable. Every student must narrate their routine out loud. Trim Slide 5 (sequencing words) to 3 minutes before cutting the narration round.

---

## Core 60-Minute Plan

### Slide 1 — Warm-Up: -er Verb Drill (6 min)
**Purpose:** Verify that the -er pattern is solid before adding negation and frequency.
**Teacher notes:**
- Ask students to conjugate *travailler* from memory in all six forms. Students call out the forms as a group — do not ask individually yet.
- Then call on 3 students: "Give me one sentence using *étudier*, *regarder*, and *écouter* in the *il/elle* form."
- If any student cannot produce *il travaille / elle travaille* without hesitation, make a mental note — the pattern drill in Slide 2 will help.
**Student activity:** Group conjugation of *travailler* → 3 individual sentences in *il/elle* form.

---

### Slide 2 — Negation: ne...pas — Full Practice (10 min)
**Purpose:** Students can negate any -er verb sentence and know when written *ne* differs from spoken *ne*.
**Teacher notes:**
- Review the structure: *ne* + verb + *pas.* Note elision: *ne* + vowel → *n'.*
  - *J'aime → Je n'aime pas*
  - *Elle étudie → Elle n'étudie pas*
- Spoken French note: *ne* is very commonly dropped in informal speech. Demonstrate both:
  - Formal/written: *Je ne travaille pas le samedi.*
  - Spoken: *Je travaille pas le samedi.*
  - "Both are real French. In class and in your writing, use both halves. If you're in a Paris café, you'll hear the short version."
- Introduce *jamais* as a negation pair: *Je ne travaille jamais le dimanche.* It replaces *pas* — not doubles it.
- Oral transformation drill: 8 affirmative sentences → students give the negative. Mix in 2 sentences with *jamais.*
**Student activity:** Oral transformation drill — 8 affirmative → negative. Identify which use *jamais.*

---

### Slide 3 — Frequency Expressions (10 min)
**Purpose:** Students can place a frequency adverb correctly in a sentence and know what it means.
**Teacher notes:**
- Introduce on a scale from 0% to 100%:
  - *toujours* (always) → *Je travaille toujours à neuf heures.*
  - *souvent* (often) → *Je regarde souvent des films le soir.*
  - *normalement* (normally) → *Normalement, je mange à midi.*
  - *parfois / quelquefois* (sometimes) → *Je mange parfois au restaurant.*
  - *rarement* (rarely) → *Je regarde rarement la télé.*
  - *jamais* (never) → *Je ne mange jamais de fast-food.*
- Position rule: after the conjugated verb in simple sentences.
- Quick drill: instructor names a percentage ("about 75% of the time"), students give the adverb: "souvent" or "normalement."
- Students add one frequency word to each of their Session 5 sentences (oral).
**Student activity:** Frequency adverb rapid-fire → add one adverb to a sentence from Session 5 homework.

---

### Slide 4 — Daily Routine Vocabulary (7 min)
**Purpose:** Students have the time and sequencing expressions needed to narrate a routine.
**Teacher notes:**
- Time-of-day expressions: *le matin, l'après-midi, le soir.* Use in a sentence each.
- Specific time: *à + [number] heures* — *Je commence à neuf heures. Je finis vers dix-sept heures.*
- Sequencing words: *d'abord, puis / ensuite, après, enfin.*
- Model a 4-sentence routine using sequencing: "D'abord je travaille le matin. Puis je mange à midi. Ensuite je travaille encore. Enfin je rentre chez moi."
- Students do NOT need to memorize all of these. They need to recognize them and use at least two in their narration.
**Student activity:** Repeat the model routine; students call out the sequencing words.

---

### Slide 5 — Building the Daily Routine (5 min)
**Purpose:** Students plan their routine before speaking.
**Teacher notes:**
- Give students 3–4 minutes to write or note a 5-sentence routine. They can use notes; they do not have to memorize it.
- Model structure on screen:
  1. *Le matin, je [verb]...*
  2. *D'abord / Puis / Ensuite, je [verb]...*
  3. *À [time], je [verb]...*
  4. *Le soir, je [verb] + frequency expression.*
  5. *Je ne [verb] jamais / pas...*
- Walk the virtual room — listen for issues to address in the narration block.
**Student activity:** Students write or note their 5-sentence routine in silence (3–4 min).

---

### Slide 6 — Daily Routine Narration Round (13 min)
**Purpose:** Every student narrates their routine out loud.
**Teacher notes:**
- Every student takes a turn. Notes allowed. 5 sentences minimum; more is fine.
- Correct one item per student: prioritize verb endings (-e vs. -es vs. -ons), missing *ne*, word order. Save group patterns for a debrief after.
- After everyone shares: pick 2–3 patterns and briefly address with the whole group.
- Close with: "You can now describe your day in French. That is something you could not do two weeks ago."
**Student activity:** Each student narrates their daily routine out loud (5 sentences minimum).

---

### Slide 7 — Reflexive Verbs: First Look (5 min)
**Purpose:** Students hear *se lever* and *se coucher* as phrases they can start using. Not a grammar block.
**Teacher notes:**
- Say: "I want to give you two more phrases for your routine — ones you will recognize in French and start using, even though we will teach the full grammar in Week 4."
- Write: *Je me lève à sept heures. Je me couche vers onze heures.*
- Point out the reflexive pronoun: "There's an extra word in here — *me.* That is a reflexive pronoun. For now, just treat these as two phrases to memorize. Week 4 will explain the whole pattern."
- Quick practice: students say *Je me lève à [heure].* and *Je me couche vers [heure].* — everyone once.
**Student activity:** Each student says one sentence with *se lever* and one with *se coucher.*

---

### Slide 8 — Wrap-Up (4 min)
**Purpose:** Homework preview; Week 3 writing task assigned.
**Teacher notes:**
- Assign the Week 3 routine paragraph: 6–8 sentences, affirmative and negative, two frequency expressions, sequencing words.
- Close: "You have the -er pattern. You have negation. You have frequency. You can describe what you do and don't do, how often, and in what order. That is a genuinely useful slice of French."
**Student activity:** Note homework. Optional: one "surprise" sentence about their routine — something unusual they actually do.

---

## Cut First If Running Behind

1. **Slide 7 (reflexive preview):** Cut entirely — "We'll see these in Week 4." No loss to Week 3.
2. **Slide 4 (sequencing words):** Reduce to 3 words (*d'abord, puis, enfin*) and 1 minute — assign the rest as vocabulary reading.
3. **Slide 5 (build the routine):** Skip the planning silence — students speak from improvisation in Slide 6 instead.
4. **Slide 3 (frequency):** Cover 3 adverbs only: *souvent, parfois, jamais.* Assign the others as reference reading.
5. Do not cut Slide 6 (narration round).

---

## Extension for Fast Cohorts

- **-ir and -re verb preview:** Introduce *finir* (regular -ir: *finis, finis, finit, finissons, finissez, finissent*) and *répondre* (regular -re: *réponds, réponds, répond, répondons, répondez, répondent*) as a brief look-ahead. "There are two more patterns. We'll work on them after we're solid with -er."
- **Routine with time:** Students add specific hours to every sentence in their narration: *Je commence à neuf heures. Je mange à midi et demi.*
- **Describe someone else's routine:** Students narrate what a family member or partner does on a normal day using *il/elle* forms.
