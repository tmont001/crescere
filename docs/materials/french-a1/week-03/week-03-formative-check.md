# French A1 — Week 3 Formative Check

**Administered:** Start of Session 7 (first session of Week 4)
**Time needed:** 6–8 minutes total
**Purpose:** Quick checkpoint — not a test. Tells the instructor whether -er verbs and negation are solid enough to introduce telling time and possessives, or whether a short review is needed first.

---

## Oral Prompts (5 questions — ~1 min per student for the full set)

| # | Prompt | Target response | What to listen for |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Tell me three things you do on a typical day. | 3 sentences with 3 different -er verbs | Correct endings for *je* form; no extra -s on *je*; not frozen in one verb |
| 2 | Tell me something you never do. | *Je ne [verb] jamais...* | Both *ne* and *jamais* present; correct verb ending |
| 3 | How often do you watch TV or listen to music? | Sentence with *souvent, parfois, rarement,* etc. | Frequency word positioned after the verb |
| 4 | Tell me what your [partner/colleague/parent] does. | *Il/Elle [verb]-e...* | Correct *-e* ending for *il/elle*; not *il travailles* |
| 5 | Conjugate *travailler* — give me all six forms. | je travaille, tu travailles, il travaille, nous travaillons, vous travaillez, ils travaillent | All six forms; no -s on *je*; no extra endings on *il* |

---

## Written Prompts (5 questions — students write answers, ~3 min)

Paste into the session chat or distribute at the start of Session 7.

**Instructions to students:** Answer in French. Short answers only.

1. Write: "She doesn't work on Saturdays."
2. Write: "We often eat at home."
3. Write: "They (masc.) study medicine." (*étudier*, plural)
4. What is the *il/elle* form of *regarder*?
5. Write: "I never listen to podcasts."

**Answer key:**
1. *Elle ne travaille pas le samedi.*
2. *Nous mangeons souvent à la maison.*
3. *Ils étudient la médecine.*
4. *regarde*
5. *Je n'écoute jamais de podcasts.*

---

## Instructor Scoring Notes

Not graded. Use it to make one of three decisions:

| Result | What it means | What to do |
|---|---|---|
| Students handle all 5 oral prompts | -er verbs are solid | Move into Week 4 content immediately |
| Students stumble on 1–2 oral prompts | Minor gaps; expected at this stage | 5-minute -er verb warm-up at the start of Sessions 7 and 8 |
| Students stumble on 3+ oral prompts | Verb pattern is not yet secure | Add a 10-minute -er verb review to Sessions 7 and 8 before introducing new structures |

**Most common Week 3 gaps:**
- *Je travailles* (adding -s to the *je* form) → the most common ending error; correct every time through Week 4
- Missing *ne* in writing → enforce the written rule consistently; acknowledge the spoken norm without replacing it
- Frequency adverb placed before the verb (*Je souvent mange*) → correct positioning: verb first, adverb after
- Using *jamais* without *ne* in written French → reinforce: *Je ne mange jamais,* not *Je mange jamais*

---

## Ready to Move On

A student is ready for Week 4 content when they can:

- [ ] Conjugate any regular -er verb from memory in all six forms
- [ ] Build a negative sentence with *ne...pas* without prompting
- [ ] Use at least two frequency expressions correctly in a sentence
- [ ] Narrate 4–5 things they do in a day without notes

**Note on reflexive verbs:** Do not gate Week 4 on reflexive verb production. Students may have heard *je me lève* and *je me couche* as phrases but have not been taught the reflexive grammar system yet. Week 4 covers this fully.
