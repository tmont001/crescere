# French A1 — Session 5 Slide Outline
## Regular -er Verbs

**Session length:** 60 minutes
**Session-end goal:** Students can conjugate 6 regular -er verbs in *je*, *tu*, and *il/elle* forms without looking at a table, build a complete sentence using those forms, and transform an affirmative sentence to negative using *ne...pas*.

> **Pacing note:** The speaking round (Slide 6) is non-negotiable. Every student must say at least one affirmative and one negative sentence about themselves before the session ends. Trim Slide 3 (additional verbs) before cutting it.

---

## Core 60-Minute Plan

### Slide 1 — Warm-Up: Week 2 Review (7 min)
**Purpose:** Reconnect with Week 2 before introducing new grammar. Check formative check results if not already reviewed.
**Teacher notes:**
- Ask each student to say one sentence describing themselves or someone else from Week 2. Use Week 2 vocabulary: *J'ai trente ans. Je suis patient. Elle a les cheveux bruns.*
- Listen for: avoir vs. être confusion; adjective agreement. Correct quickly, do not linger.
- If Week 2 formative check revealed persistent gaps, add 2 minutes here: one rapid-fire avoir drill.
**Student activity:** Each student says one description sentence — any target from Weeks 1 or 2.

---

### Slide 2 — The -er Verb Pattern (10 min)
**Purpose:** Students understand the regular -er ending pattern and can say all six endings.
**Teacher notes:**
- Write on screen: the six endings only — *-e, -es, -e, -ons, -ez, -ent.* Then apply them to *parler.*
- Say explicitly: "These four endings — *je, tu, il, ils* — are all silent. You cannot tell them apart by ear. The subject pronoun does all the work. This is why French is written the way it is."
- Choral drill × 3: slow → normal pace → fast, pointing to each ending.
- Quick spot check: cover the table. Ask each student to give you one form at random.
**Student activity:** Choral drill × 3 → spot-check of one form per student.

---

### Slide 3 — Core Verbs in Context (12 min)
**Purpose:** Students hear and produce 6 core verbs in real sentences — not just as isolated forms.
**Teacher notes:**
- Introduce each verb with a sentence, not just a translation:
  - *parler: Je parle français, mais mon mari parle espagnol.*
  - *habiter: Tu habites à Lyon ? — Non, j'habite à Bordeaux.*
  - *travailler: Elle travaille dans une école primaire.*
  - *étudier: Il étudie l'ingénierie à Paris.*
  - *regarder: Vous regardez des séries le soir ?*
  - *écouter: Ils écoutent de la musique ensemble.*
- After each: choral repetition of the sentence. Then swap the subject: *Tu travailles dans une école ? → Non, je travaille dans un bureau.*
- Focus on *je, tu, il/elle* only. Do not drill *nous/vous/ils* yet — note that they exist, point to the table, and move on.
**Student activity:** Choral repetition → swap the subject pronoun.

---

### Slide 4 — Sentence Building (10 min)
**Purpose:** Students construct their own sentences from a verb + context prompt.
**Teacher notes:**
- Give each student a verb from the week's list and a partial context: "Tell me something true about yourself using this verb."
  - Student A: *aimer* → *J'aime la musique classique.*
  - Student B: *habiter* → *J'habite à Chicago.*
  - Student C: *travailler* → *Je travaille dans le marketing.*
- After each sentence: ask the group to transform it to *il/elle*: "She works in marketing — how do we say that?" → *Elle travaille dans le marketing.*
- Keep moving. The goal is fluency with the pattern, not precision about the content.
**Student activity:** Each student builds one sentence → group transforms it to third person.

---

### Slide 5 — Negation: ne...pas (8 min)
**Purpose:** Students can make any -er verb sentence negative.
**Teacher notes:**
- Write on screen: *Je parle → Je **ne** parle **pas**.*
- Point to the structure: "The verb is in the middle. Ne goes before. Pas goes after. Together they mean 'not.'"
- Spoken French note: "In conversation, you will often hear people drop the *ne* — *Je parle pas*. That is acceptable in informal speech. In our class and in writing, we use both halves."
- Quick transformation drill: instructor says an affirmative sentence, students give the negative. 6–8 items. Fast.
  - *Tu travailles ici.* → *Tu ne travailles pas ici.*
  - *Elle habite à Paris.* → *Elle n'habite pas à Paris.* (Note the elision: *ne* + vowel → *n'*)
  - *Nous mangeons le soir.* → *Nous ne mangeons pas le soir.*
**Student activity:** Oral transformation drill — 6–8 affirmative → negative.

---

### Slide 6 — Speaking Round (8 min)
**Purpose:** Every student uses the new pattern to say something true about themselves.
**Teacher notes:**
- Each student says two sentences: one affirmative, one negative.
  - *Je travaille dans un café. Je n'habite pas à New York.*
  - *J'étudie le droit. Je ne regarde pas beaucoup la télé.*
- Notes allowed. Memorization not required.
- Correct: missing *ne*, wrong ending (*je parles*), subject-verb order. One correction per student — prioritize verb form errors.
- Affirm the pattern after each correct sentence.
**Student activity:** Each student says one affirmative + one negative sentence about themselves.

---

### Slide 7 — Wrap-Up (5 min)
**Purpose:** Homework preview; close with the big picture.
**Teacher notes:**
- Say: "Homework tonight has two parts: say the endings from memory, then write five sentences using five different verbs. The goal is to get these forms into your hands before our next session."
- Preview Session 6: "Next session we deepen the pattern, add frequency words, and build your first real daily routine description."
**Student activity:** Note homework. Optional: one more verb sentence before logging off.

---

## Cut First If Running Behind

1. **Slide 4 (sentence building):** Skip the group transformation step. Each student says their sentence only; move immediately to Slide 5.
2. **Slide 3 (core verbs):** Cover 4 verbs instead of 6. Add the remaining 2 at the start of Session 6.
3. **Slide 5 (negation):** Introduce ne...pas with 3 examples only; assign the transformation drill as homework.
4. Do not cut Slide 6 (speaking round).

---

## Extension for Fast Cohorts

- **All six forms:** If the cohort picks up the pattern quickly in Slide 2, drill *nous, vous, ils/elles* — including the silent -ent distinction in writing vs. spoken.
- **Spelling-change verbs:** Introduce *manger → nous mangeons* and *commencer → nous commençons* as interesting exceptions. Do not test them this session.
- **Sentence variety:** Students who finish Slide 4 early can build sentences with two verbs: *J'habite à Boston et je travaille à Cambridge.*

---

## Move to Homework If Needed

- *Nous, vous, ils/elles* forms of core verbs
- Negation transformation practice (written)
- Additional verbs beyond the 6 introduced in Slide 3 (*préparer, commencer, manger*)
- Any spelling-change verb explanation
