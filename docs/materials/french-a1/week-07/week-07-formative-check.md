# French A1 — Week 7 Formative Check

**Administered:** Start of Session 15 (first session of Week 8)
**Time needed:** 6–8 minutes total
**Purpose:** Quick checkpoint — not a test. Confirms that demonstrative adjectives, color agreement, shopping phrases, and quantity expressions are solid enough to enter Week 8 (home description, *il y a*, prepositions of location, and the high-frequency irregular verb block).

---

## Oral Prompts (5 questions — ~1 min per student for the full set)

| # | Prompt | Target response | What to listen for |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | How do you say "this raincoat" in French? | *cet imperméable* | *Cet* — not *ce* — before the vowel; correct pronunciation |
| 2 | Give me the color agreement: *une robe + bleu.* | *une robe bleue* | Feminine agreement: *bleue*, not *bleu* |
| 3 | How do you ask "How much does it cost?" | *C'est combien?* or *Ça coûte combien?* | Either form accepted; stress on final syllable |
| 4 | Say "a kilo of tomatoes" and "a dozen eggs" in French. | *un kilo de tomates; une douzaine d'œufs* | *De* (not *des*) after quantity; elision *d'œufs* |
| 5 | Act out the key moment in a shop: ask if you can try on a garment. | *Je peux l'essayer?* | *L'* (not *le/la*) before vowel; natural delivery |

---

## Written Prompts (5 questions — students write answers, ~3 min)

**Instructions to students:** Answer in French. Short answers only.

1. Write "these black shoes" (les chaussures = f. pl.)
2. Write the correct color agreement: *un manteau + gris.*
3. Write "a bottle of red wine."
4. Write "She's buying that green jacket." (la veste = f.)
5. Write "How much per kilo?"

**Answer key:**
1. *ces chaussures noires* (*ces* for plural; *noires* for feminine plural)
2. *un manteau gris* (*gris* is already the masculine form — no change; *grise* would be feminine)
3. *une bouteille de vin rouge*
4. *Elle achète cette veste verte.* (or *Elle prend cette veste verte.*) — *cette* for feminine singular
5. *C'est combien le kilo?*

---

## Instructor Scoring Notes

Not graded. Use it to make one of three decisions:

| Result | What it means | What to do |
|---|---|---|
| Students handle all 5 oral prompts | Week 7 content is solid | Move into Week 8 (home, *il y a*, prepositions, irregular verbs) |
| Students stumble on 1–2 prompts | Minor gaps — expected | 5-minute demonstrative/color warm-up at the start of Sessions 15 and 16 |
| Students stumble on 3+ prompts | Demonstratives or quantity *de* rule not yet secure | Add 10-minute review to Session 15; carry demonstratives as a warm-up habit |

**Most common Week 7 gaps:**
- *Ce imperméable* instead of *cet* → the *cet* rule is the hardest part of this week; repeat it in warm-ups
- *Des chaussures marrons* (adding -s to invariable color) → *marron* never changes; continue to correct
- *Un kilo du fromage* (keeping the article) → same rule as negation; should be *de fromage*
- *Bleu* instead of *bleue* for feminine nouns → agreement reminder needed; hardest colors: *blanc/blanche, vert/verte, violet/violette*

---

## Ready to Move On

A student is ready for Week 8 (home and neighborhood) when they can:

- [ ] Use *ce / cet / cette / ces* correctly with 6+ nouns including at least one vowel-initial masculine noun
- [ ] Apply color adjective agreement for 4+ colors with 4+ clothing items
- [ ] Ask the price and buy or decline an item in a shop (4–5 sentences)
- [ ] Use 4+ quantity expressions with *de* (not *du/de la/des*)
- [ ] Complete a simple market transaction (3 items, one price question, closing)

**Not a Week 8 gate:** Full price-giving above 200, all 12 clothing items from the vocabulary list, production of *cet* without any prompting, or extended market dialogue. These continue to consolidate.
