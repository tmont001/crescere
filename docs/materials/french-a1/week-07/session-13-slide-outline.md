# French A1 — Session 13 Slide Outline
## Clothing, Colors, and the Shop

**Session length:** 60 minutes
**Session-end goal:** Every student can navigate a clothing shop interaction in French — describing what they want, asking for a size and color, trying it on, and buying or declining — using demonstrative adjectives and color agreement correctly.

> **Pacing note:** The speaking round (Slide 6) is non-negotiable. Every student plays both the customer and the shopkeeper before the session ends. Compress Slide 4 (color agreement, to 5 min) before cutting Slide 6.

---

## Core 60-Minute Plan

### Slide 1 — Warm-Up: Week 6 Close (5 min)
**Purpose:** Close the Week 6 loop; activate *vouloir* and partitive before moving to shopping.
**Teacher notes:**
- Ask each student to say one sentence: either a *vouloir* form or a *je voudrais* ordering sentence from Week 6.
- If the Week 6 formative check showed widespread *pas du* (wrong negation): 30-second group reminder — "After *pas*, it's always just *de.* Same rule applies in quantity expressions this week."
- Do not re-teach. Activation only.
**Student activity:** One *vouloir* or café/restaurant sentence each.

---

### Slide 2 — Demonstrative Adjectives: Ce, Cet, Cette, Ces (10 min)
**Purpose:** Students learn all four forms of the demonstrative adjective and can apply them to clothing nouns.
**Teacher notes:**
- Display the four-form table: *ce / cet / cette / ces.*
- Explain *cet* before vowels/h muet: "When a masculine noun starts with a vowel sound, we say *cet* instead of *ce.* So: *ce manteau* (consonant) but *cet imperméable* (vowel). This is purely for pronunciation."
- Drill: instructor says a clothing noun; students supply the correct demonstrative.
  - *pantalon (m.)* → *ce pantalon*
  - *imperméable (m., vowel)* → *cet imperméable*
  - *robe (f.)* → *cette robe*
  - *chaussures (f. pl.)* → *ces chaussures*
  - *manteau (m.)* → *ce manteau*
  - *écharpe (f.)* → *cette écharpe*
  - *pull (m.)* → *ce pull*
  - *chaussettes (f. pl.)* → *ces chaussettes*
- Key point: "In French, *ce/cet/cette/ces* means both 'this' and 'that.' Context tells you which."
**Student activity:** 8-item demonstrative drill (nouns provided, students give the form).

---

### Slide 3 — Clothing Vocabulary (10 min)
**Purpose:** Students learn 10–12 core clothing items with demonstratives and practice saying them.
**Teacher notes:**
- Introduce clothing items in pairs (m./f.) where possible: *un pantalon / une jupe; un pull / une veste; un manteau / une robe.*
- After each pair: students repeat the item + say it with a demonstrative: *"Ce pantalon / cette jupe."*
- Include at least one *cet* example (*cet imperméable, cet anorak*) to reinforce the rule in context.
- Also introduce: *un jean, une chemise, un tee-shirt, des chaussures, des bottes, des baskets.*
- Do not try to cover all items in the vocabulary file. 10–12 items said aloud beats 20 items seen in a list.
**Student activity:** Repeat each item → say it with the correct demonstrative.

---

### Slide 4 — Colors and Adjective Agreement (8 min)
**Purpose:** Students apply color adjectives to the clothing items they just learned, with correct gender agreement.
**Teacher notes:**
- Introduce 6 core colors: *noir/noire, blanc/blanche, gris/grise, bleu/bleue, vert/verte, rouge/rouge.*
- Highlight the agreement pattern: "The color comes AFTER the noun and agrees in gender and number. So: *un pantalon noir, une jupe noire, des pantalons noirs, des jupes noires.*"
- Point out invariable colors: "Some colors never change — *marron, orange, rose, beige.* They're borrowed words or compound colors. Remember them as exceptions."
- Quick drill: instructor says a clothing item + color; students combine them with the correct agreement.
  - *chemise + blanc* → *une chemise blanche*
  - *pull + vert* → *un pull vert*
  - *chaussures + noir* → *des chaussures noires*
  - *manteau + gris* → *un manteau gris*
  - *robe + bleu* → *une robe bleue*
  - *baskets + marron* → *des baskets marron* (invariable!)
**Student activity:** 6-item color-agreement drill.

---

### Slide 5 — Shopping Phrases (7 min)
**Purpose:** Students learn the core phrases for a shop interaction.
**Teacher notes:**
- Introduce phrases in sequence of a natural shop visit:
  1. Shopkeeper: *"Bonjour! Je peux vous aider?"*
  2. Customer: *"Oui, je cherche..."* or *"Non merci, je regarde."*
  3. Customer: *"Vous avez ce pantalon en noir, taille M?"*
  4. Customer: *"Je peux l'essayer? / Je peux les essayer?"*
  5. Customer: *"C'est combien?"*
  6. Customer: *"Je vais le prendre."* or *"Non merci, je ne le prends pas."*
- Note the *l'/les* pronoun: *Je peux l'essayer* (one item); *Je peux les essayer* (a pair/multiple). Introduce as a functional formula — full object pronoun study is in A2.
- Cultural note: "In France, always say *Bonjour* when entering a shop. If you don't, the shopkeeper may not respond warmly."
- Pronunciation drill: *Je peux l'essayer?* × 3. *Je vais le prendre.* × 3.
**Student activity:** Repeat key phrases → each student says the core sequence once (1 sentence per step).

---

### Slide 6 — Speaking Round: Clothing Shop (12 min)
**Purpose:** Every student navigates a clothing shop interaction as both customer and shopkeeper.
**Teacher notes:**
- Pairs: one student is the customer, one is the shopkeeper. 3 minutes each role; switch.
- Customer checklist:
  - [ ] Greets (*Bonjour*)
  - [ ] States what they are looking for: *Je cherche... en [color], taille [M/L/etc.]*
  - [ ] Uses a demonstrative to point at something: *Vous avez cette veste en rouge?*
  - [ ] Asks to try it on: *Je peux l'essayer?*
  - [ ] Asks the price: *C'est combien?*
  - [ ] Buys or declines: *Je vais le/la prendre* or *Non merci*
- Shopkeeper checklist:
  - [ ] Greets the customer
  - [ ] Responds to the request (they can invent a price and a stock answer)
  - [ ] Responds to the try-on request: *Oui, bien sûr. Les cabines sont là-bas.*
- Instructor circulates; corrects one item per student after their turn.
- Most likely corrections: *ce imperméable* → *cet*; wrong agreement (*une chemise vert* → *verte*).
**Student activity:** Full clothing shop role play (both roles).

---

### Slide 7 — Wrap-Up (8 min)
**Purpose:** Assign homework; preview Session 14.
**Teacher notes:**
- Preview Session 14: "Next session we go to the French market — outdoor food stalls, quantities, prices. You will need the shopping phrases from today plus new quantity vocabulary."
- Homework preview: demonstrative drill + clothing description paragraph.
**Student activity:** Note homework. Optional closer: name one clothing item you're wearing in French with its color.

## Cut First If Running Behind

1. **Slide 4 (color agreement):** Reduce to 5 minutes — cover 4 colors instead of 6; skip the invariable color explanation (assign as vocabulary reference).
2. **Slide 3 (clothing vocabulary):** Cover 8 items instead of 12. Focus on the items most likely to appear in the role play.
3. **Slide 5 (shopping phrases):** Cover steps 1–4 only (find, ask for item, try on, price). Buy/decline can be improvised in the role play.
4. Do not cut Slide 6 (speaking round).

---

## Extension for Fast Cohorts

- **Adjective position:** Most adjectives come after the noun in French — *un manteau gris.* But BAGS adjectives (Beauty, Age, Goodness, Size) come before: *un beau manteau, un petit sac, un vieux pull.* Introduced briefly; students can try one BAGS adjective.
- **Plural agreement complexity:** *Des chaussures blanches → des chaussures blanc* (no, wrong); reinforce that plural adjectives must agree: *blanches.* Also: *des chaussures marron* (invariable, stays singular form).
- **Color combinations:** *Bleu marine* (navy blue), *vert foncé* (dark green), *rouge bordeaux* (burgundy) — compound colors are invariable and students can use them in the role play.
