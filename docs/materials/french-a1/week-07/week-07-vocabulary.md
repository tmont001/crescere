## Demonstrative Adjectives

Demonstratives point to specific things: "this," "that," "these," "those." In French, they agree in gender and number with the noun they modify — and there is a special masculine singular form before a vowel sound.

| | Masculine (before consonant) | Masculine (before vowel/h muet) | Feminine | Plural |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Form** | **ce** | **cet** | **cette** | **ces** |
| Example | ce pantalon | cet imperméable | cette robe | ces chaussures |
| English | this/that trouser | this/that raincoat | this/that dress | these/those shoes |

**Key note:** *Ce, cet, cette,* and *ces* all mean both "this" and "that." French does not distinguish near vs. far with separate forms (unlike Spanish *este/ese*). Context makes the meaning clear.

**Cet before vowels:** *cet imperméable, cet homme, cet anorak.* The -t is sounded when followed by a vowel (liaison-like).

### Examples in context

| French | English |
|---|---|
| Je voudrais **ce** pantalon noir. | I'd like these black trousers. |
| **Cet** imperméable coûte combien? | How much is this raincoat? |
| Je cherche **cette** robe bleue. | I'm looking for this blue dress. |
| **Ces** chaussures sont trop petites. | These shoes are too small. |

## Colors and Adjective Agreement

Color adjectives in French agree with the noun in gender (m./f.) and number (sing./pl.). They usually come **after** the noun.

| Color | Masculine sing. | Feminine sing. | Masculine pl. | Feminine pl. |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| black | noir | noire | noirs | noires |
| white | blanc | blanche | blancs | blanches |
| grey | gris | grise | gris | grises |
| red | rouge | rouge | rouges | rouges |
| blue | bleu | bleue | bleus | bleues |
| green | vert | verte | verts | vertes |
| yellow | jaune | jaune | jaunes | jaunes |
| purple | violet | violette | violets | violettes |

**Invariable colors (never change for gender or number):**
- *orange* — *un pull orange, une robe orange, des chaussures orange*
- *marron* (brown) — *un sac marron, des bottes marron*
- *rose* (pink) — *une veste rose, des baskets rose*
- *beige* — *un manteau beige*
- *bleu marine* (navy blue) — compound colors are invariable: *des pantalons bleu marine*

## Prices and Numbers

Large numbers for prices:

| Number | French |
|---|---|
| 100 | cent |
| 101 | cent un |
| 200 | deux cents |
| 201 | deux cent un |
| 250 | deux cent cinquante |
| 1 000 | mille |

**Price format in French:**
- €15 → *quinze euros*
- €15,50 → *quinze euros cinquante* (or *quinze euros et cinquante centimes* formally)
- €89 → *quatre-vingt-neuf euros*
- Prices use a comma as the decimal separator in France: €15,99

**Key price questions:**
- *C'est combien?* — How much is it? (casual)
- *Ça coûte combien?* — How much does it cost?
- *Le prix est affiché?* — Is the price displayed?
- *Vous faites les soldes?* — Are you having a sale? (*les soldes* = the sales — January and July in France)

## Pronunciation Notes

- **Ce / cet / cette / ces:** *ce* = /sə/, *cet* = /sɛt/, *cette* = /sɛt/ (cet and cette sound identical), *ces* = /se/
- **Chemise:** /ʃəmiz/ — the *ch* sounds like English "sh," not like "ch" in "church"
- **Imperméable:** /ɛ̃pɛʁmeabl/ — five syllables; the final -e is silent; stress on the second-to-last syllable
- **Douzaine:** /duzɛn/ — two syllables, rhymes with "main" in English
- **Trop grand:** the *p* at the end of *trop* is silent when followed by a consonant
- **Quatre-vingt-neuf:** /katʁəvɛ̃nœf/ — literally "four-twenty-nine" (80+9); the -f in *neuf* is sounded when neuf stands alone or before a consonant, but silent before heures in time
