# French A1 — Session 14 Slide Outline
## The Market: Prices and Quantities

**Session length:** 55–60 minutes
**Session-end goal:** Every student can complete a French market transaction — asking for items by quantity, asking the price, and finishing the exchange — using quantity expressions and prices correctly.

> **Pacing note:** The market role play (Slide 6) is the session's core deliverable. Compress Slide 5 (cultural notes) to 3 minutes before cutting the role play. Every student buys at least 3 items and asks a price.

---

## Core 60-Minute Plan

### Slide 1 — Warm-Up: Demonstrative Recall (5 min)
**Purpose:** Confirm Session 13 content before extending into the market.
**Teacher notes:**
- Quick drill: 4 clothing items → students supply the correct demonstrative form.
  - *veste (f.)* → *cette veste*
  - *anorak (m., vowel)* → *cet anorak*
  - *bottes (f. pl.)* → *ces bottes*
  - *pull (m.)* → *ce pull*
- Then: ask each student to say one clothing sentence from Session 13: *"Je cherche cette veste en noir, taille L."*
**Student activity:** 4-item demonstrative prompt → one clothing sentence each.

---

### Slide 2 — Numbers and Prices (8 min)
**Purpose:** Students can understand and say prices, including numbers above 100.
**Teacher notes:**
- Quick review: numbers 1–100 (students already know these from Week 1). Focus on the ones that cause problems: *soixante-dix* (70), *quatre-vingts* (80), *quatre-vingt-dix* (90).
- Introduce numbers 100+: *cent, deux cents, deux cent cinquante, cinq cents, mille.*
- Price format: *€15 → quinze euros; €15,50 → quinze euros cinquante; €89 → quatre-vingt-neuf euros.*
- Key price questions:
  - *C'est combien?* — casual
  - *Ça coûte combien?* — also casual
  - *Quel est le prix?* — slightly more formal
- Quick price drill: instructor shows a price (€5, €23, €89, €150, €200); students say it in French.
**Student activity:** 5-item price pronunciation drill.

---

### Slide 3 — Quantity Expressions (10 min)
**Purpose:** Students learn the quantity + *de* pattern and can use it with market products.
**Teacher notes:**
- Open with the rule: "After any quantity word, the article disappears — you just use *de* (or *d'* before a vowel). This is the same rule as after negation and in negative sentences: whenever you specify an amount, French uses bare *de.*"
- Introduce quantity expressions one by one with an example:
  - *un kilo de tomates*
  - *500 grammes de fromage*
  - *une livre de beurre* — explain that *une livre* in France = 500g, not 1 pound
  - *un litre de lait*
  - *une bouteille de vin*
  - *une douzaine d'œufs* — note the elision before vowel
  - *une tranche de jambon*
  - *un morceau de fromage*
- After each: students repeat → give their own example with a different product.
- Error to anticipate: *un kilo du fromage* — correct immediately: "No definite article after quantity words — just *de.*"
**Student activity:** Repeat each expression → give one new example with a different food item.

---

### Slide 4 — Market Vocabulary (8 min)
**Purpose:** Students learn 10–12 market food items with their genders and can combine them with quantities.
**Teacher notes:**
- Introduce fruits first: *une tomate, une pomme, une orange, une fraise, un citron, des raisins.*
- Then vegetables: *une pomme de terre, une carotte.*
- Then staples: *des œufs, du fromage, du jambon, du pain, du beurre.*
- After each item: students say a quantity expression with it: *un kilo de tomates, une douzaine d'œufs, 200 grammes de jambon.*
- Note pronunciation: *œufs* (/ø/) — unusual vowel sound; the final -fs is silent in *des œufs* but the f is sounded in *un œuf.*
**Student activity:** Repeat each food item → combine with a quantity.

---

### Slide 5 — Cultural Notes: Le Marché (5 min)
**Purpose:** Brief cultural context so the role play feels authentic.
**Teacher notes:**
- *Le marché* in France: open-air markets are held 1–3 days per week in most towns. Produce, cheese, meat, bread, clothing, and household goods are all sold at markets.
- *Et avec ça?* — the standard market phrase for "Anything else?" Students will need to recognize it.
- *C'est tout, merci* — how to signal you're done.
- *Les soldes:* French sales season (January and July). *"Vous faites les soldes?"* is a useful phrase at clothing stalls.
- Cultural tip: in France, say *bonjour* to every stall holder before you begin. Moving from stall to stall without greeting is considered rude.
**Student activity:** No activity — listening. Keep to 3 minutes if pressed.

---

### Slide 6 — Market Role Play (12 min)
**Purpose:** Every student completes a full market transaction — buying 3+ items, asking a price, and finishing the exchange.
**Teacher notes:**
- Pairs: one student is the customer, one is the stall holder. 3–4 minutes each; switch.
- Customer checklist:
  - [ ] Greets (*Bonjour!*)
  - [ ] Asks for at least 3 items with quantities: *Je voudrais un kilo de tomates...*
  - [ ] Uses a demonstrative at least once: *Donnez-moi ce fromage-là.*
  - [ ] Asks the price of one item: *C'est combien le kilo?*
  - [ ] Ends the transaction: *C'est tout, merci. Au revoir!*
- Stall holder checklist:
  - [ ] Greets the customer
  - [ ] Responds to each request (invents prices and stock)
  - [ ] Asks *Et avec ça?* at least once
  - [ ] Gives the total: *Ça fait [X] euros.*
- Instructor circulates. Most likely corrections: *un kilo du fromage* → *de fromage*; wrong demonstrative.
**Student activity:** Full market role play (both roles).

---

### Slide 7 — Wrap-Up (5 min)
**Purpose:** Assign homework; close Week 7.
**Teacher notes:**
- Group debrief: 2 things the class did well, 1 error pattern.
- Most likely: the *de* rule after quantities (students reverting to *du/de la/des*) and prices above 70 (seventy-related numbers).
- Homework preview: market dialogue writing + shopping paragraph.
- Preview Week 8: "Next week: home and neighborhood — describing where things are, *il y a*, prepositions of location, and a big block of high-frequency irregular verbs."
**Student activity:** Note homework.

2. **Slide 2 (prices above 100):** Cover only *cent* and *deux cents.* Skip the full large-number system.
3. **Slide 4 (market vocabulary):** Cover 8 items instead of 12.
4. Do not cut Slide 6 (role play) or reduce preparation time below 2 minutes.

## Extension for Fast Cohorts

- **Partitive review in market context:** *Je prends du fromage* (some — partitive) vs. *un kilo de fromage* (quantity + de). The distinction: partitive = unspecified amount; quantity expression = specific amount.
- **Comparing prices:** *Ce fromage-ci est moins cher que ce fromage-là.* — *-ci* (here) and *-là* (there) can be added to demonstratives for extra precision: *ce manteau-ci / ce manteau-là.*
- **Numbers: soixante-dix, quatre-vingts:** Work through the full Belgian/Swiss alternative forms (*septante, huitante, nonante*) as cultural enrichment.
