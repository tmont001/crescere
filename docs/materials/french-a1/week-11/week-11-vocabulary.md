## Avoir Mal À — Expressing Pain

*Avoir mal à* = to hurt / to have pain in. Use *avoir* conjugated + *mal* + *à* + body part.

**The critical rule:** *à* contracts with the article of the body part.

| Body part gender | Contraction | Example |
|---|---|---|
| feminine singular (*la*) | *à + la → à la* | *J'ai mal à la gorge.* |
| masculine singular (*le*) | *à + le → au* | *J'ai mal au dos.* |
| plural (*les*) | *à + les → aux* | *J'ai mal aux pieds.* |
| before vowel (*l'*) | *à + l' → à l'* | *J'ai mal à l'oreille.* |

**Full examples:**
- *J'ai mal à la tête.* — I have a headache.
- *Tu as mal au dos?* — Does your back hurt?
- *Il a mal aux genoux.* — His knees hurt.
- *Elle a mal à l'épaule.* — Her shoulder hurts.
- *Nous avons mal à la gorge.* — We have sore throats.

**Negation:**
- *Je n'ai pas mal.* — I don't hurt / I'm not in pain.
- *Je n'ai pas mal à la tête.* — My head doesn't hurt.

## Il Faut + Infinitif — Impersonal Obligation / Advice

*Il faut* = it is necessary / you must / one should. Invariable — it never changes form. No subject except *il.*

| Example | English |
|---|---|
| *Il faut se reposer.* | You need to rest. |
| *Il faut boire beaucoup d'eau.* | You need to drink lots of water. |
| *Il faut prendre ce médicament.* | You need to take this medicine. |
| *Il faut consulter un médecin.* | You need to see a doctor. |
| *Il ne faut pas travailler.* | You shouldn't work. |
| *Il ne faut pas faire de sport cette semaine.* | You shouldn't do sport this week. |

**Compare with *devoir*:**
- *Il faut se reposer.* — General advice (applies to anyone in this situation).
- *Vous devez vous reposer.* — Specific advice to this person (doctor to patient).

## Depuis + Présent — Duration of Symptoms

*Depuis* + present tense expresses how long something has been going on. This is different from English (English uses past tense; French stays in present).

| French | English |
|---|---|
| *J'ai de la fièvre depuis trois jours.* | I've had a fever for three days. |
| *Je tousse depuis une semaine.* | I've been coughing for a week. |
| *J'ai mal à la gorge depuis hier.* | I've had a sore throat since yesterday. |
| *Je me sens mal depuis lundi.* | I've felt unwell since Monday. |
| *Il a mal au dos depuis ce matin.* | His back has been hurting since this morning. |

**Rule:** If the situation is still ongoing now, use *depuis* + present. If it has ended, use past tense.
- *J'ai de la fièvre depuis trois jours.* (still have it now → present)
- *J'ai eu de la fièvre pendant trois jours.* (it's over → past + *pendant*)

## Pronunciation Notes

| Word / phrase | Notes |
|---|---|
| *J'ai mal* | /ʒe mal/ — *ai* = /e/ here, same as *j'ai* for possession |
| *au dos* | /o do/ — the *au* contraction sounds like "oh" |
| *aux pieds* | /o pje/ — *aux* sounds like "oh"; liaison optional at A1 |
| *à l'oreille* | /a lɔrɛj/ — smooth elision, no pause |
| *fièvre* | /fjɛvr/ — two syllables: "fyev-ruh" |
| *enrhumé(e)* | /ɑ̃ryme/ — nasal vowel at start: "on-rü-may" |
| *il faut* | /il fo/ — "eel fo"; *t* is silent unless followed by vowel |
| *depuis* | /dəpɥi/ — "duh-pwee"; the *ui* glide is like "wee" |
| *les genoux* | /le ʒənu/ — final -x silent; *ge-* is soft like "zh" |
