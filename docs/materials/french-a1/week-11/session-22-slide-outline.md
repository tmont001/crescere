# French A1 — Session 22 Slide Outline
## At the Doctor: Il Faut, Devoir, and Depuis

**Session length:** 50 minutes (on plan) — can run 60 min for slower cohorts
**Session-end goal:** Every student can describe symptoms with duration (*depuis*), understand advice using *il faut* and *devoir*, and navigate a doctor's consultation from arrival to departure.

> **Pacing note:** The doctor's role play (Slide 6) is non-negotiable. Every student participates in a full consultation before the session ends. The *il faut* vs. *devoir* distinction (Slides 2–3) can be taught in one combined block if pressed for time. *Depuis* (Slide 4) is the highest-priority new grammar this session and should not be skimped — it is a high-frequency pattern that appears throughout A2.

---

## Core Plan

### Slide 1 — Warm-Up: Pharmacy Recall + Bridge to Doctor (5 min)
**Purpose:** Re-activate Session 21 pharmacy content; bridge to doctor's appointment.
**Teacher notes:**
- Each student says one pharmacy scenario sentence from their homework.
- Correct any *avoir mal à* contraction errors quickly.
- Bridge: *"La pharmacie, c'est pour les petits problèmes. Mais si c'est plus grave, vous allez chez le médecin. Aujourd'hui: la consultation médicale."*
- Preview the session: *il faut, devoir* for advice, *depuis* for duration, then role play.
**Student activity:** One pharmacy sentence each.

---

### Slide 2 — Il Faut + Infinitif: Impersonal Advice (8 min)
**Purpose:** Students learn *il faut + infinitif* as the standard form for general health advice.
**Teacher notes:**
- *Il faut* = it is necessary to / one must / you need to. It is always impersonal — never conjugated differently, never takes a subject other than *il.*
- Write the five core examples:
  - *Il faut se reposer.* — You need to rest.
  - *Il faut boire beaucoup d'eau.* — You need to drink lots of water.
  - *Il faut prendre ce médicament.* — You need to take this medicine.
  - *Il faut consulter un médecin.* — You need to see a doctor.
  - *Il ne faut pas travailler.* — You shouldn't work.
- Pronunciation: *"eel fo" — il faut.* The -t is silent except before a vowel.
- Common mistake to address immediately: *Il faut vous reposer* (adding a person → wrong at A1). *Il faut se reposer.* (reflexive infinitive stays as-is; the *se* is not conjugated).
- Quick drill: instructor gives the situation, student produces the *il faut* advice:
  - *Vous avez de la fièvre.* → *Il faut se reposer / boire de l'eau.*
  - *Vous avez mal à la gorge.* → *Il faut prendre des pastilles.*
**Student activity:** Three-item *il faut* advice drill.

---

### Slide 3 — Devoir for Advice: Doctor's Instructions (7 min)
**Purpose:** Students understand the difference between *il faut* (general) and *devoir* (personal, directed) in advice contexts.
**Teacher notes:**
- *Devoir* is known from Week 9 (obligation). New application here: the doctor uses *vous devez* to give direct personal advice.
  - *Vous devez vous reposer.* — You must rest.
  - *Vous devez prendre ce sirop deux fois par jour.* — You must take this syrup twice a day.
  - *Vous devez boire beaucoup d'eau.* — You must drink lots of water.
  - *Vous ne devez pas faire de sport cette semaine.* — You mustn't do sport this week.
- Distinction: *"Il faut se reposer"* = advice for anyone in this situation. *"Vous devez vous reposer"* = I am telling you, specifically, to rest.
- Note: at A1, students do not need to master the distinction perfectly. They need to produce both forms and recognize them when the doctor uses them.
- Quick round: student takes the doctor role — give two pieces of advice using *vous devez* and *il faut.*
**Student activity:** Each student gives two advice phrases (one *il faut*, one *vous devez*).

---

### Slide 4 — Depuis + Présent: Duration of Symptoms (8 min)
**Purpose:** Students learn *depuis + présent* for ongoing situations — the most productive grammar point of Session 22.
**Teacher notes:**
- *Depuis* + present tense = how long something has been happening — and is still happening now.
- The key contrast with English: English uses *past* tense (*"I've had a fever for three days"*); French uses *present* tense (*"j'ai de la fièvre depuis trois jours"*) because the situation is still ongoing.
- Write on board:

  | French (still ongoing) | English translation |
  |---|---|
  | *J'ai de la fièvre depuis trois jours.* | I've had a fever for three days. |
  | *Je tousse depuis une semaine.* | I've been coughing for a week. |
  | *J'ai mal à la gorge depuis hier.* | I've had a sore throat since yesterday. |
  | *Je me sens mal depuis lundi.* | I've felt unwell since Monday. |
  | *J'apprends le français depuis trois mois.* | I've been learning French for three months. |

- Add the last example deliberately — it shows *depuis + présent* is not a health structure, it is a general structure. Students are already living the last example.
- *Depuis + time period* (trois jours, une semaine) vs. *depuis + point in time* (hier, lundi): both work, same structure.
- Quick drill: instructor gives a situation, student produces the *depuis* sentence:
  - *J'ai commencé à avoir de la fièvre il y a trois jours.* → *J'ai de la fièvre depuis trois jours.*
  - *Je tousse depuis quand?* → *Je tousse depuis lundi.*
**Student activity:** Three-item *depuis* transformation drill; one student adds an original *depuis* sentence.

---

### Slide 5 — Doctor's Vocabulary: Appointment Script (3 min)
**Purpose:** Students have the 4–5 phrases they need to navigate the role play.
**Teacher notes:**
- Keep this brief — students need the vocabulary to understand the doctor, not to produce all of it.
- Doctor's side (students understand and respond):
  - *Qu'est-ce qui ne va pas?* — What's the matter?
  - *Depuis quand?* / *Depuis combien de temps?* — Since when? How long?
  - *Où avez-vous mal exactement?* — Where exactly does it hurt?
  - *C'est grave?* → *Non, ce n'est pas grave. Ça va aller.* — Is it serious? No, nothing serious.
  - *Je vous fais une ordonnance.* — I'll write you a prescription.
- Patient's side (students produce):
  - *J'ai de la fièvre depuis trois jours.*
  - *J'ai mal au dos et à la gorge.*
  - *Je me sens mal depuis lundi.*
  - *C'est grave?*
  - *Merci, docteur.*
**Student activity:** Read through the script together once; proceed to role play.

---

### Slide 6 — Doctor's Appointment Role Play (14 min) ★ PROTECTED
**Purpose:** Every student navigates a complete medical consultation.
**Teacher notes:**
- Role play structure (instructor models first, then students do it in pairs or instructor-student):
  1. Patient arrives: *Bonjour, docteur. Je ne me sens pas bien.*
  2. Doctor asks: *Qu'est-ce qui ne va pas?*
  3. Patient describes: *J'ai de la fièvre depuis trois jours. J'ai aussi mal à la gorge et je tousse beaucoup.*
  4. Doctor asks: *Depuis quand exactement? Vous avez combien de fièvre?*
  5. Patient responds with *depuis* sentence: *Depuis lundi. Environ 38 degrés et demi.*
  6. Doctor gives advice: *Vous devez vous reposer. Il ne faut pas travailler. Je vous fais une ordonnance.*
  7. Patient: *C'est grave, docteur?*
  8. Doctor: *Non, ce n'est pas grave. Revenez dans une semaine si ça ne va pas mieux.*
  9. Patient: *Merci beaucoup, docteur.*

- Each student must produce at minimum: symptom sentence, *depuis* sentence, and response to advice.
- Correct one item per student. Priority targets:
  - *depuis* + passé composé → *depuis* + présent
  - missing *de la* in *j'ai de la fièvre*
  - *il faut que je repose* → *il faut se reposer*

**Minimum checklist for each student's output:**
- [ ] One *j'ai mal à...* sentence with correct contraction
- [ ] One *depuis* sentence (present tense)
- [ ] One piece of advice received and acknowledged

**Student activity:** Each student completes a full doctor's consultation (minimum 4 student turns).

---

### Slide 7 — Wrap-Up: Week 11 Close and Week 12 Preview (5 min)
**Purpose:** Assign homework; preview Week 12; reassure students about the final performance task.
**Teacher notes:**
- Homework: symptom description with *depuis* + advice paragraph with *il faut* and *devoir.*
- Week 12 preview: *"La semaine 12, c'est la semaine finale. Pas un examen — une performance. Vous allez avoir une vraie conversation en français. Ce sera des scénarios intégrés — comme ce qu'on fait en classe depuis douze semaines. Si vous pouvez faire la consultation médicale aujourd'hui, vous pouvez faire la performance finale."*
- Note: *depuis + présent* is not just a health structure — it will appear naturally in Week 12 self-introductions: *"J'apprends le français depuis trois mois."* Flag this explicitly.
**Student activity:** Note homework; ask one question about Week 12 if they have one.

## Cut First If Running Behind

1. **Slide 3 (devoir for advice):** Reduce to two examples (*Vous devez vous reposer, Il ne faut pas travailler*). Skip the *il faut* vs. *devoir* distinction analysis — students can produce both forms without the theory.
2. **Slide 4 (*depuis*):** Reduce to three examples, skip the point-in-time vs. period distinction (*depuis hier* vs. *depuis trois jours*) — both work the same way.
3. **Slides 2 and 3 combined:** Teach as one 10-minute block if pressed: *"Il faut + infinitif for general advice; vous devez + infinitif for specific advice."* Move directly to role play.
4. Do not cut Slide 6 (doctor's role play).

---

## Extension for Fast Cohorts

- **Depuis in other contexts:** *J'habite ici depuis cinq ans. Je travaille depuis huit heures ce matin.* Students produce two non-health *depuis* sentences — this previews Week 12 self-description.
- **Pharmacist role:** Students take turns as the doctor and must give at least three pieces of advice using *il faut* and *vous devez.*
- **Complex symptoms:** Student describes multiple symptoms with duration: *J'ai mal à la tête depuis deux jours, je tousse depuis une semaine, et j'ai de la fièvre depuis hier soir.* Produces a more realistic medical monologue.
- **Appointment by phone:** *"Bonjour, je voudrais prendre rendez-vous avec le docteur..."* Brief introduction to appointment vocabulary if time permits.
