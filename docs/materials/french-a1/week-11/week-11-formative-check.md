# French A1 — Week 11 Formative Check
## Health and the Body

**When to administer:** At the start of Session 23 (Week 12), or as a closing check at the end of Session 22.
**Format:** Oral section first (5 prompts); written section second (5 prompts).
**Purpose:** Diagnostic tool. Not a grade. Informs whether students are ready for Week 12 final performance tasks or need targeted reinforcement before the integrated sessions.

---

## Oral Prompts (5 prompts — instructor reads aloud)

Administer to each student individually or in round-robin. Allow 10–15 seconds per prompt.

**Prompt 1:** "Tell me where you have pain — give me two body parts, each with the correct contraction."
*Target:* Two *avoir mal à* sentences using different contractions — e.g., *J'ai mal au dos* (au) and *J'ai mal à la gorge* (à la). Or *aux pieds* / *à l'oreille.*
*Minimum pass:* Both contractions correct. *J'ai mal à le dos* → fail. *J'ai mal les pieds* (missing *à*) → fail.

**Prompt 2:** "Tell me three symptoms — something you feel, something you have, and something you're doing."
*Target:* e.g., *Je me sens mal* (feel) + *j'ai de la fièvre* (have) + *je tousse* (doing).
*Watch for:* *J'ai fièvre* (missing *de la*); *je suis rhume* (wrong structure — should be *je suis enrhumé(e)*).

**Prompt 3:** "Tell me something you've had for three days — use *depuis* correctly."
*Target:* *J'ai de la fièvre depuis trois jours.* Or *J'ai mal à la tête depuis trois jours.*
*Watch for:* *J'ai eu de la fièvre depuis trois jours* (passé composé → wrong; still ongoing = present). *J'ai de la fièvre pour trois jours* (wrong preposition — *pour* ≠ *depuis*).

**Prompt 4:** "Give me two pieces of advice for someone who is sick — one with *il faut* and one with *vous devez*."
*Target:* *Il faut se reposer.* AND *Vous devez boire beaucoup d'eau* (or any correct combination).
*Watch for:* *Il faut vous reposer* (adding subject pronoun — wrong; use *se reposer* with impersonal *il faut*). *Il faut que vous reposez* (subjunctive construction — A2 territory; bring back to infinitive form).

**Prompt 5:** "Walk me through the first three turns of a pharmacy or doctor's encounter."
*Target:* Student enters, greets, describes at least one symptom, and either asks for a product (pharmacy) or responds to the doctor's first question with a *depuis* sentence.
*Minimum pass:* Three coherent student turns. Pronunciation can be imperfect; grammar must include at least one *avoir mal à* or *depuis* sentence.

---

## Written Prompts (5 items — students write independently)

**Item 1:** Write four body parts with their articles. Include at least one masculine, one feminine, one plural, and one starting with a vowel.
*Expected:* e.g., *le dos (m.), la gorge (f.), les pieds (pl.), l'oreille (vowel)*
*Watch for:* Missing articles; wrong gender on *le / la.*

**Item 2:** Complete the sentence: *J'ai mal _____ tête, _____ dos et _____ oreilles.*
*Expected:* *à la tête, au dos, aux oreilles.*
*Watch for:* *à le dos* (contraction failure); *à les oreilles* (must be *aux*).

**Item 3:** Rewrite in French: "I've had a sore throat since Monday."
*Expected:* *J'ai mal à la gorge depuis lundi.*
*Watch for:* Passé composé (*j'ai eu*) instead of present (*j'ai*); *pour* instead of *depuis.*

**Item 4:** Write two pieces of advice for a sick friend — one with *il faut* and one with *vous devez.*
*Expected:* *Il faut se reposer* + *Vous devez prendre ce médicament* (or any correct pair).
*Watch for:* *Il faut vous reposer* (add reflexive *se*, not *vous*, after *il faut*); any *il faut que* construction.

**Item 5:** Write 4–5 sentences describing an illness — include one *avoir mal à* sentence, one symptom phrase, one *depuis* sentence, and one piece of advice.
*Expected:* Connected sentences that demonstrate integration of Week 11 grammar. Contractions correct; *depuis* in present tense; advice using *il faut* or *devoir.*
*Minimum pass:* All four elements present; *depuis* in present tense; at least one contraction correct.

## Scoring Decision Table

| Student output | Decision |
|---|---|
| All 5 oral prompts pass + 4–5 written correct | Ready for Week 12 final performance |
| 4 oral pass + 3–4 written correct | Ready for Week 12 — monitor *depuis* construction in Session 23 warm-up |
| *Avoir mal à* contractions correct but *depuis* still in passé composé | Ready for Week 12 — give one *depuis* drill at Session 23 open |
| *Il faut* construction still wrong (*il faut vous reposer*) | Ready for Week 12 — note correction in Session 23 warm-up; one practice item |
| *De la* missing from *j'ai de la fièvre* consistently | Flag for Session 23 warm-up; one partitive review item (connect to Week 6) |
| Fewer than 3 oral prompts pass OR fewer than 3 written correct | Flag for instructor follow-up; do not delay Week 12 for whole cohort |

---

## Ready for Week 12

Before beginning Week 12 (final performance), a student should be able to:
- [ ] Name 8+ body parts with correct article and gender
- [ ] Produce *avoir mal à* with correct contraction (*au/aux/à la/à l'*) for 4+ body parts
- [ ] Describe 3 symptoms accurately (*fièvre, rhume, toux* minimum)
- [ ] Navigate a pharmacy encounter (entry → symptom → request) without preparation
- [ ] Use *depuis + présent* correctly for at least one ongoing situation
- [ ] Give advice using *il faut + infinitif* and *vous devez + infinitif*
- [ ] Handle the opening and description phases of a doctor's consultation

---

## Not a Gate

The following items are NOT required for progression to Week 12:
- Full range of body part vocabulary beyond the core 12
- *Pendant* vs. *depuis* distinction (only *depuis* for ongoing is required)
- *Il faut que + subjonctif* (A2 grammar — not taught in A1)
- Extended healthcare vocabulary (*radiographie, analyses de sang, piqûre*)
- Phone appointment booking vocabulary
- *Avoir du mal à + infinitif* (to have difficulty doing something) — extension only
- Perfect pronunciation of *fièvre, enrhumé, éternuer*
