# French A1 — Session 21 Slide Outline
## Body Parts and Pain Expressions

**Session length:** 50 minutes (on plan) — can run 60 min for slower cohorts
**Session-end goal:** Every student can describe at least two symptoms and navigate a pharmacy encounter from entry to exit without preparation notes.

> **Pacing note:** The pharmacy role play (Slide 6) is non-negotiable. Every student participates in a real pharmacy exchange before the session ends. The body parts vocabulary (Slide 2) can move quickly — students retain more from the *avoir mal à* drill than from exhaustive vocabulary lists. Twelve body parts is enough for the role play; do not expand to 20+.

---

## Core Plan

### Slide 1 — Warm-Up: Week 10 Debrief + Bridge to Health (5 min)
**Purpose:** Re-activate Week 10 closing content; create a natural transition to health vocabulary.
**Teacher notes:**
- Each student gives one sentence from their Session 20 homework preference paragraph.
- Correct any lingering *préférer* accent or *jouer à / faire de* errors quickly — do not extend this block.
- Bridge: *"Vous aimez faire du jogging. Mais qu'est-ce qui se passe si vous avez mal au genou et vous ne pouvez pas courir? Aujourd'hui on parle de la santé — la santé, c'est le sujet de la semaine 11."*
- Preview the week: pharmacy scenario today, doctor's appointment in Session 22.
**Student activity:** One preference sentence each.

---

### Slide 2 — Body Parts: Les Parties du Corps (8 min)
**Purpose:** Students learn 12 core body parts with correct gender and article.
**Teacher notes:**
- Teach head to foot. Say each word, students repeat. Do NOT attempt to teach all 20 body parts — 12 is the minimum for the pharmacy and doctor scenarios.
- Core 12 (must cover): *la tête, les yeux, le nez, la bouche, la gorge, l'oreille, le dos, le ventre/l'estomac, la poitrine, le bras, la jambe, le pied.*
- Gender matters — students must learn *la gorge* (f.) and *le dos* (m.) to form the correct *avoir mal à* contractions.
- Quick round: point to a body part on yourself or a diagram, students name it.
- Note: *les yeux* is the irregular plural of *l'œil* — flag this, do not drill extensively.
**Student activity:** Point-and-name round for 12 body parts.

---

### Slide 3 — Avoir Mal À: The Contraction Rule (10 min)
**Purpose:** Students learn and immediately apply the four contraction forms of *avoir mal à.*
**Teacher notes:**
- Write the four forms visually:

  | Article | Contraction | Body part | Result |
  |---|---|---|---|
  | *le* (m.) | *à + le = **au*** | dos | *J'ai mal **au** dos.* |
  | *la* (f.) | *à + la = **à la*** | gorge | *J'ai mal **à la** gorge.* |
  | *les* (pl.) | *à + les = **aux*** | pieds | *J'ai mal **aux** pieds.* |
  | *l'* (vowel) | *à + l' = **à l'*** | oreille | *J'ai mal **à l'** oreille.* |

- Connect to known contraction: *"Vous connaissez déjà 'au' — je vais au cinéma, je joue au tennis. C'est la même chose. 'À + le' devient toujours 'au.'"*
- Say each form aloud; students produce the contraction for the next body part on the list.
- Quick drill: say a body part, students produce the full *j'ai mal à...* sentence with correct contraction.
  - *le dos* → *J'ai mal au dos.*
  - *la tête* → *J'ai mal à la tête.*
  - *les genoux* → *J'ai mal aux genoux.*
  - *l'oreille* → *J'ai mal à l'oreille.*
  - *le bras* → *J'ai mal au bras.*
  - *la cheville* → *J'ai mal à la cheville.*
- Most common error: *j'ai mal à le dos.* Address immediately — *à le* does not exist in French.
**Student activity:** Six-item contraction drill.

---

### Slide 4 — Common Symptoms (8 min)
**Purpose:** Students acquire the 6–8 most useful symptom phrases for the pharmacy scenario.
**Teacher notes:**
- Teach as ready-made phrases, not separate vocabulary items. Students need to produce these in the role play:
  - *Je ne me sens pas bien.* (I don't feel well) — most important opener
  - *J'ai de la fièvre.* (I have a fever) — note partitive *de la* — *j'ai fièvre* is wrong
  - *Je suis enrhumé(e).* (I have a cold) — adjective agrees with gender
  - *Je tousse beaucoup.* (I'm coughing a lot) — *tousser* = regular -er verb
  - *J'ai mal au cœur.* (I feel nauseous) — idiom; cannot be guessed from individual words
  - *Je suis très fatigué(e).* (I'm very tired / run down)
- Quick round: one student says *"Je ne me sens pas bien"* and gives a reason (*J'ai de la fièvre et j'ai mal à la gorge*). Continue around the group.
**Student activity:** Each student produces one symptom sentence.

---

### Slide 5 — Pharmacy Vocabulary and Phrases (4 min)
**Purpose:** Students have the 4–5 essential pharmacy phrases they need for the role play.
**Teacher notes:**
- Do not over-teach this block — students need enough to do the role play, not a full pharmacist vocabulary.
- Four essential phrases:
  - *Vous avez quelque chose pour...?* (Do you have something for...?)
  - *Je voudrais quelque chose pour le rhume / la toux.* (I'd like something for a cold / cough) — *je voudrais* is from Week 6
  - *Est-ce qu'il faut une ordonnance?* (Do I need a prescription?)
  - *Combien de fois par jour?* (How many times a day?)
- Note product vocabulary: *un sirop, des comprimés, de l'aspirine, des pastilles pour la gorge.* Enough to understand the pharmacist's response.
**Student activity:** Class reads the pharmacy phrases aloud once; proceed to role play.

---

### Slide 6 — Pharmacy Role Play (12 min) ★ PROTECTED
**Purpose:** Every student navigates a full pharmacy encounter from entry to exit.
**Teacher notes:**
- Role play structure (instructor models first, then students do it in pairs or instructor-student):
  1. Enter: *Bonjour. Je ne me sens pas bien.*
  2. Describe: *J'ai de la fièvre depuis deux jours. J'ai aussi mal à la gorge.*
  3. Ask: *Vous avez quelque chose pour ça?*
  4. Pharmacist responds (instructor or partner): recommends a product, gives dosage instructions
  5. Student responds: *Est-ce qu'il faut une ordonnance? Merci beaucoup.*

- Each student plays the patient role at minimum. The instructor or a confident student plays the pharmacist.
- Correct one item per student (do not correct everything). Priority targets:
  - *j'ai mal à le dos* → *au dos*
  - *j'ai fièvre* → *j'ai de la fièvre*
  - *je suis malade depuis trois jours passés* → *je suis malade depuis trois jours*

- For small groups: students can also play the pharmacist role — this adds productive vocabulary output.

**Student activity:** Each student completes a full pharmacy scenario (minimum 3 student turns).

---

### Slide 7 — Wrap-Up: Week 11 Open (5 min)
**Purpose:** Preview homework and Session 22.
**Teacher notes:**
- Homework: body parts learning + written symptom description.
- Preview Session 22: *"Aujourd'hui, pharmacie. La prochaine fois — vous allez chez le médecin. Vous allez devoir décrire vos symptômes et expliquer depuis combien de temps vous êtes malade. Et le médecin va vous donner des conseils avec 'il faut' et 'vous devez.' On va aussi apprendre 'depuis' — pour dire depuis combien de temps vous souffrez."*
**Student activity:** Note homework.

## Cut First If Running Behind

1. **Slide 4 (symptoms):** Reduce to 3 phrases: *Je ne me sens pas bien, j'ai de la fièvre, j'ai mal à la gorge.* The role play only requires these three plus one *avoir mal à* sentence.
2. **Slide 2 (body parts):** Reduce to 8 core items: *tête, gorge, dos, ventre, bras, jambe, pied, oreille.* Eight is sufficient for the pharmacy scenario.
3. Do not cut Slide 6 (pharmacy role play).

---

## Extension for Fast Cohorts

- **Full symptom inventory:** Add *avoir des vertiges, avoir du mal à dormir, éternuer, vomir* — useful for a richer pharmacy exchange.
- **Pharmacist role:** Students take turns as pharmacist and must recommend a product with dosage instructions.
- **Spontaneous switch:** Instructor calls out a body part at random; student produces the *avoir mal à* sentence and a symptom phrase without preparation.
- **Bridge to *depuis*:** Fast cohorts can preview *"j'ai de la fièvre depuis trois jours"* at the end of the role play — they will work with it in depth in Session 22.
