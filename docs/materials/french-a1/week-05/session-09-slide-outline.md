# French A1 — Session 9 Slide Outline
## Aller and Places in Town

**Session length:** 60 minutes
**Session-end goal:** Students can conjugate *aller* in all six forms, say where they are going using the correct *à/au/à la/à l'* form for at least 6 different places, and use *aller* in a sentence to describe their real week.

> **Pacing note:** The speaking round (Slide 6) is non-negotiable. Every student must produce *Je vais au/à la/à l'...* at least twice using different places. Trim Slide 4 (the pattern drill) before cutting it.

---

## Core 60-Minute Plan

### Slide 1 — Warm-Up: Week 4 Debrief and Recall (7 min)
**Purpose:** Close the Week 4 loop and activate production before introducing a new verb.
**Teacher notes:**
- If Week 4 formative check showed reflexive verb gaps, give 2 minutes here: "Give me one reflexive sentence about your morning — no notes." Correct *me* placement errors quickly and move on.
- Ask 3–4 students to say one -er/-ir verb sentence from Week 3/4 about their routine. This keeps prior verbs active while aller is new.
- Do not linger. The formative check data tells you what to repair; do not re-teach.
**Student activity:** Each student says one routine sentence from Weeks 3–4.

---

### Slide 2 — Aller: Conjugation (8 min)
**Purpose:** Students learn and can recall all six forms of *aller* without looking.
**Teacher notes:**
- Write the table. Say: "*Aller* is completely irregular — the forms come from three different Latin verbs. There is no pattern to derive from the infinitive. You simply memorize these six forms. The good news: it is the most common verb for talking about going somewhere, so you will use it every day."
- Pronunciation:
  - *je vais* — "veh" (rhymes with "may" in French)
  - *il va* — "va"
  - *ils vont* — "vôn" (nasal); liaison in *ils vont au café* → "vôn-toh"
- Choral drill × 3: slow → normal → fast.
- Quick spot-check: cover the table. Call on each student for one form at random.
- Note: *allons* and *allez* will be familiar — they look like their infinitive.
**Student activity:** Choral drill × 3 → spot-check (one form per student, table covered).

---

### Slide 3 — Places in Town (10 min)
**Purpose:** Students recognize and can say 10 core places, with gender visible from the outset.
**Teacher notes:**
- Introduce places in two groups — masculine and feminine — so gender is never separated from the noun:
  - **Masculine (→ au):** le café, le restaurant, le cinéma, le supermarché, le parc, le bureau
  - **Feminine (→ à la):** la banque, la gare, la pharmacie, la boulangerie
- After each group, students repeat: *le café → au café; la banque → à la banque.*
- Then introduce vowel-start places: *l'hôpital → à l'hôpital; l'école → à l'école.*
- Say: "When you learn a new place in French, learn its *le/la* at the same time. That's the only way to know whether to say *au* or *à la*."
**Student activity:** Repeat each place with its gender article → give the *je vais + à/au/à la/à l'* form.

---

### Slide 4 — à/au/à la/à l' as a Functional Pattern (8 min)
**Purpose:** Students can use the correct contracted form without needing to consciously apply a grammar rule.
**Teacher notes:**
- Put the four forms on screen in a simple table:
  - *à + le → au* (masc. singular)
  - *à + la → à la* (fem. singular)
  - *à + l' → à l'* (vowel/silent h)
  - *à + les → aux* (plural — note only; won't drill this session)
- Say: "French never says *à le.* It always contracts to *au.* This is not optional — it is just how the language works."
- Quick drill: instructor says a place with its article (*le restaurant*), students give *au restaurant.* Then try: *la pharmacie → à la pharmacie; l'école → à l'école.*
- Do 8 items. Vary masc./fem./vowel so students have to think each time.
- Common error to prevent: *à la restaurant* (treating masculine noun as feminine). When students make this error, give the correct form and note the gender: "*Restaurant* est masculin — *au restaurant.*"
**Student activity:** Rapid drill — instructor says place + article, students give *à/au/à la/à l'* form.

---

### Slide 5 — Building *Je vais...* Sentences (10 min)
**Purpose:** Students produce sentences about their real week using *aller* + place + time or transport.
**Teacher notes:**
- Show the target structure: *Je vais [à/au/à la/à l'] [place] [time expression or day].*
- Give students 3 minutes to prepare 3 sentences about their real week. They may use notes.
- Model first: "Je vais au bureau lundi et mardi. Je vais à la bibliothèque mercredi. Je vais au cinéma samedi soir."
- After preparation: pairs share their sentences; partner listens and asks one follow-up: "Tu vas comment ?"
- If transport vocabulary hasn't been formally introduced yet, accept whatever students say naturally (*en voiture, par le bus*) — it is introduced in Session 10.
**Student activity:** Write 3 sentences → pairs share → one follow-up question each.

---

### Slide 6 — Speaking Round: Where Are You Going? (12 min)
**Purpose:** Every student uses *aller* in first and third person at least once.
**Teacher notes:**
- Each student answers: "Tell me three places you are going this week — real or invented. Use *je vais à/au/à la/à l'.*"
- Then ask one student about another: "Where is [name] going? Use *il/elle va.*" → The group transforms a sentence to third person.
- Correct one item per student: *à* contraction error (priority), then verb form, then word order.
- Close with: "You now have the verb for going anywhere. Everything else in Week 5 — transport, directions — builds on this."
**Student activity:** Each student says 3 *je vais...* sentences → one transformation to *il/elle va.*

---

### Slide 7 — Wrap-Up (5 min)
**Purpose:** Homework preview; preview of Session 10.
**Teacher notes:**
- Assign homework: the aller conjugation from memory + 5 *je vais...* sentences + describe how you go somewhere.
- Preview Session 10: "Next session we add transportation and how to give simple directions — the basics of getting around a French-speaking city."
**Student activity:** Note homework. Optional: say one more *je vais...* sentence before logging off.

---

## Cut First If Running Behind

1. **Slide 4 (pattern drill):** Reduce to 5 items. Say: "Masculine → au, feminine → à la, vowel → à l'. Use the vocabulary list if you're unsure of a gender."
2. **Slide 3 (places):** Cover 7 places instead of 10 — 4 masculine, 3 feminine. Add the rest as vocabulary reference.
3. **Slide 5 (building sentences):** Skip pairs; students say their 3 sentences directly in the speaking round.
4. Do not cut Slide 6 (speaking round).

---

## Extension for Fast Cohorts

- ***Aux* (plural):** *Je vais aux restaurants du quartier.* Give 2 examples; note it completes the contraction set.
- **Aller + infinitif (near future preview):** *Je vais travailler demain.* "Notice: *je vais* + infinitive = 'I am going to...' We'll cover this fully in Week 9."
- **Full conversation:** Students ask each other where they are going and why using *pourquoi? — Parce que...*
