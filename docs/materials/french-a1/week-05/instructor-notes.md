# French A1 — Week 5 Instructor Notes

**Sessions:** 9 and 10
**Theme:** Aller, Places in Town, Transportation, and Simple Directions

---

## Week Objective

By the end of Week 5, students can:
- Conjugate *aller* in all six forms and use it in context sentences
- Say where they are going using *je vais au / à la / à l' + place*
- Say how they are getting somewhere using *en + transport* or *à pied / à vélo*
- Use 3–4 prepositions of place to describe where things are
- Ask and give simple directions using *tout droit, à droite, à gauche*

**The à/au/à la/à l' rule** is the main grammar challenge of this week. Teach it as a functional pattern with examples, not as an abstract rule about article contraction. Students who can say *je vais au café / à la banque / à l'école* correctly are meeting the Week 5 target — they do not need to be able to articulate the rule under pressure.

**Relationship to the curriculum:** The curriculum places *aller*, prepositions, and city places in Week 5 (Sessions 9–10). This aligns directly with the task. Note that -ir and -re verbs (deferred from Week 4) are not covered this week — they will be picked up systematically in Week 6 when the verb inventory expands.

## Common Student Mistakes — Week 5

| Mistake | Correction | When to address |
|---|---|---|
| *Je vais à le café* (not contracting) | *à + le = au* — always. The contraction is mandatory. *Je vais au café.* | Session 9 pattern block |
| *Je vais à la restaurant* (wrong gender) | *Restaurant* is masculine: *au restaurant.* Gentle correction; gender takes time. | Session 9; note the gender when introducing each place |
| *Je vais à la hôpital* (using à la before vowel) | *à + l' (vowel/h muet)* — *à l'hôpital.* Note: h aspiré (like *le hibou*) does not elide, but these are rare at A1 | Session 9 pattern block |
| *Je vais en pied* (*en* instead of *à* for foot) | *à pied* — foot is not a vehicle. The pattern: *en* = inside a vehicle; *à* = on or using your own body/a bike | Session 10 transport block |
| *C'est à côté la banque* (missing *de*) | *à côté **de** la banque* — all these prepositions need *de*: *près de, loin de, à côté de, en face de* | Session 10 preposition block |
| *Tournez le droit* | *Tournez à droite / à gauche / tout droit* — the direction phrases use *à*, not *le* | Session 10 directions block |

## Suggested Instructor Language

- **For aller:** *"Aller is the most irregular verb in French — it doesn't look anything like its infinitive. Vais, vas, va — these come from an entirely different Latin root. The good news: it's so high-frequency that you'll have it memorized after this week."*
- **For au/à la/à l':** *"Think of it as a shortcut: we never say 'à le' in French — it always becomes 'au'. And before a vowel, we always say 'à l'.' The gender of the place tells you which form to use. When I introduce a new place, I'll always show you its gender so you know which form to pick."*
- **For en vs. à (transport):** *"In French, if you travel inside something — a car, a bus, a train — use 'en.' If you're on top of something or using your own body — a bike, your feet — use 'à.' En voiture. En bus. À vélo. À pied."*
- **For directions:** *"This is survival French. Even if your accent is imperfect, people will understand you if you point and say 'tout droit, puis à droite.' Let's make sure everyone can say a direction before we leave today."*

---

## What to Skip If Time Is Tight

- *Aux* (plural form of à + les) — mention it exists; the core use cases this week are all singular
- The full *de + article* contraction table (*du, de la, de l', des*) — just tie it to *près de / à côté de* examples; do not lecture on it separately
- More than 3 direction phrases — *tout droit, à droite, à gauche* is enough for the role play
- All the direction phrases with *prenez* — just *prenez la rue...* as a bonus phrase for fast cohorts
- Detailed city map work — the role play can be entirely verbal; no visual props needed

## What to Assign as Homework If Pacing Runs Long

If Session 9 runs out before the speaking round:
→ Students write 3 *je vais au/à la/à l'...* sentences for homework; open Session 10 with sharing

If Session 10 role play doesn't happen:
→ Students write out a 4-line dialogue (asking and giving one direction) and share at Session 11
