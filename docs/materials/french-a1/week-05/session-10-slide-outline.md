# French A1 — Session 10 Slide Outline
## Transportation, Prepositions, and Simple Directions

**Session length:** 60 minutes
**Session-end goal:** Students can say how they travel using *en/à + transport*, describe where places are using 3–4 prepositions of place, and give and receive simple directions using *tout droit, à droite, à gauche*.

> **Pacing note:** The role play (Slide 6) is non-negotiable. Every student must ask for and give at least one direction before the session ends. Trim the directions block (Slide 5) to 5 minutes before cutting the role play.

---

## Core 60-Minute Plan

### Slide 1 — Warm-Up: Homework Sharing and Aller Recall (6 min)
**Purpose:** Verify that *aller* and *à/au/à la/à l'* are becoming automatic.
**Teacher notes:**
- Ask each student: say 2 *je vais...* sentences from your homework — no notes if possible.
- Listen for: *à le* (non-contracted form, most common error) → correct immediately: *au.*
- Do not re-explain the rule. Just give the correct form and move on: "*Restaurant* est masculin — *au restaurant.*"
- If homework revealed a widespread *à/au* error, spend 60 extra seconds on a quick 5-item drill before Slide 2.
**Student activity:** Each student says 2 *je vais...* sentences from homework.

---

### Slide 2 — Transportation Vocabulary (8 min)
**Purpose:** Students can say how they travel to a place.
**Teacher notes:**
- Present transportation in two groups:
  - **En (inside/enclosed):** *en voiture, en bus, en train, en avion, en métro*
  - **À (on/using):** *à pied, à vélo*
- Rule: "If you travel inside something — a car, a bus, a train — use *en.* If you use your own body or something you sit on top of — your feet, a bike — use *à.*"
- Give sentences for each: *Je vais au bureau en métro. Je vais à la boulangerie à pied.*
- Quick drill: instructor says a transport mode, students say the sentence *Je vais au travail + [mode].*
**Student activity:** Repeat each transport phrase → quick sentence drill.

---

### Slide 3 — Prepositions of Place (8 min)
**Purpose:** Students can describe where 4 places are relative to each other.
**Teacher notes:**
- Introduce: *près de, loin de, à côté de, en face de.* These are the four most functional at A1.
- Use example sentences tied to real places students have seen:
  - *La pharmacie est à côté du supermarché.*
  - *La gare est loin du centre.*
  - *La boulangerie est en face de l'école.*
  - *Le parc est près d'ici.*
- Note *de + article* briefly but do not over-explain: "*de + le = du, de + la = de la, de + l' = de l'* — the same pattern you saw with *à.* You already know it."
- Practice: students describe where 2–3 places are in their own neighborhood or city.
**Student activity:** Listen and repeat each preposition sentence → describe 2 places in your neighborhood.

---

### Slide 4 — Brief: *de + article* Contractions (5 min)
**Purpose:** Students can use *du, de la, de l'* in preposition phrases without stumbling.
**Teacher notes:**
- This is not a full grammar lesson — tie it directly to the prepositions above.
- Say: "You have seen this pattern with *à.* The same thing happens with *de*: *de + le = du, de + la = de la, de + l' = de l'.*"
- Put 4 examples on screen:
  - *à côté du café* (de + le)
  - *près de la gare* (de + la, no change)
  - *loin de l'hôpital* (de + l')
  - *au bout du boulevard* (de + le)
- Quick 4-item drill: instructor says a preposition + place, students give the contracted form.
**Student activity:** 4-item drill: give the correct *du/de la/de l'* form with each preposition.

---

### Slide 5 — Simple Directions (8 min)
**Purpose:** Students can give and understand three core direction phrases.
**Teacher notes:**
- Introduce: *tout droit* (straight ahead), *tournez à droite* (turn right), *tournez à gauche* (turn left).
- Also: *prenez la première/deuxième rue à droite/gauche.* (optional — for fast cohorts)
- Use a simple verbal map — no visual needed. "You are outside the supermarket. The pharmacy is: go straight, then turn right." Instructor narrates; students point with their hand.
- Useful phrases: *C'est à [X] minutes à pied. Vous ne pouvez pas le rater. C'est tout droit.*
- Quick drill: instructor describes a route using directions; students write down the turns. 3 items.
**Student activity:** Hand gestures for each direction → 3-item route comprehension (write the turns).

---

### Slide 6 — Role Play: Getting Around Town (12 min)
**Purpose:** Every student asks for and gives at least one direction in French.
**Teacher notes:**
- Set the scenario: student A is a tourist; student B is a local. Student A asks how to get from Point X to Point Y. Student B gives directions.
- Useful phrases to have on screen:
  - A: *Excusez-moi, je cherche [place]. Pour aller à [place], s'il vous plaît ?*
  - B: *C'est [près d'ici / loin d'ici]. Vous allez tout droit, puis vous tournez [à droite / à gauche].*
  - A: *C'est à combien de temps d'ici ? / C'est loin ?*
  - B: *C'est à [X] minutes à pied / en bus.*
- Pairs practice for 4 minutes (swap roles once). Then 2–3 pairs share their exchange with the group.
- After each pair shares: quick group feedback on one item.
**Student activity:** Pairs ask and give directions → 2–3 pairs share with group.

---

### Slide 7 — Wrap-Up (5 min)
**Purpose:** Homework preview; Week 5 paragraph assigned.
**Teacher notes:**
- Assign: Week 5 writing task — a short paragraph about getting around your neighborhood or city.
- Preview Week 6: "Next week we go into cafés and restaurants — ordering food, partitive articles, *je voudrais.* Very practical."
**Student activity:** Note homework. Optional: one direction sentence before logging off.

---

## Cut First If Running Behind

1. **Slide 4 (*de + article*):** Skip as a block — just note *du* when it appears in examples and correct it in context.
2. **Slide 5 (directions):** Cover only 2 minutes: *tout droit, à droite, à gauche.* Skip route comprehension drill.
3. **Slide 3 (prepositions):** Cover 3 prepositions instead of 4. Skip *au bout de / au coin de.*
4. Do not cut Slide 6 (role play).

---

## Extension for Fast Cohorts

- **More direction phrases:** *Prenez la deuxième rue à gauche. C'est juste après le feu (traffic light). C'est au coin de la rue.*
- **Aller + infinitif in the directions context:** *Je vais prendre le métro. Tu vas tourner à droite.* — A preview of using *aller* for near-future intentions.
- **Neighborhood description:** Students describe their own neighborhood in 4–5 sentences using *aller + place + transport + preposition.*
