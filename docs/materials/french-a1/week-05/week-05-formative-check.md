# French A1 — Week 5 Formative Check

**Administered:** Start of Session 11 (first session of Week 6)
**Time needed:** 6–8 minutes total
**Purpose:** Quick checkpoint — not a test. Confirms that *aller*, the *à/au/à la/à l'* pattern, and basic navigation vocabulary are solid enough to enter the food and café content in Week 6 (which introduces partitive articles and a new layer of contraction with *de*).

---

## Oral Prompts (5 questions — ~1 min per student for the full set)

| # | Prompt | Target response | What to listen for |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Tu vas où ce week-end ? | *Je vais au/à la/à l' [place].* | Correct à-form for the gender of the place they choose; aller form correct |
| 2 | Give me all six forms of *aller* — go. | vais, vas, va, allons, allez, vont | All six; *vont* not *vaient*; no hesitation on *vont* |
| 3 | How do you get to work or school? | *Je vais [au/à la...] en/à [transport].* | Correct transport preposition (*en* vs. *à*) |
| 4 | Tell me where a place near you is — use a preposition of place. | *[Place] est près de / à côté de / en face de...* | *de + article* contraction (du/de la/de l') if applicable |
| 5 | Give me a simple direction: how do I get from here to the nearest café? | *Vous allez tout droit / vous tournez à droite/gauche...* | Three direction phrases used; comprehensible delivery |

---

## Written Prompts (5 questions — students write answers, ~3 min)

**Instructions to students:** Answer in French. Short answers only.

1. Write: "I am going to the pharmacy." (la pharmacie)
2. Write: "She goes to work by bus."
3. Write: "The café is next to the bank."
4. What is the contracted form of *à + le* ?
5. Write: "They (masc.) are going to the hospital."

**Answer key:**
1. *Je vais à la pharmacie.*
2. *Elle va au travail en bus.*
3. *Le café est à côté de la banque.*
4. *au*
5. *Ils vont à l'hôpital.*

---

## Instructor Scoring Notes

Not graded. Use it to make one of three decisions:

| Result | What it means | What to do |
|---|---|---|
| Students handle all 5 oral prompts | Week 5 content is solid | Move into Week 6 (food, café, partitives) immediately |
| Students stumble on 1–2 prompts | Minor gaps — expected | 5-minute *aller* + *au/à la* warm-up at start of Sessions 11 and 12 |
| Students stumble on 3+ prompts | Aller or à-contraction is not yet secure | Add 10-minute review to Sessions 11–12; carry *aller* as a warm-up habit |

**Most common Week 5 gaps:**
- *À le café* (non-contracted) → the most common error this week; the rule is mandatory in French; never accept *à le*
- *Je vais en pied* (*en* instead of *à*) → *à pied* is fixed expression; does not follow the en/vehicle rule
- *C'est à côté la banque* (missing *de*) → "All of these prepositions need *de*: *près **de**, à côté **de**, en face **de***"
- *Ils vont au hôpital* (failing to use à l' before vowel) → "Before a vowel: *à l'hôpital*. The elision is required."

---

## Ready to Move On

A student is ready for Week 6 (food and café) content when they can:

- [ ] Give all six forms of *aller* from memory without hesitation
- [ ] Use *au, à la,* and *à l'* correctly for 6+ places
- [ ] Say how they travel somewhere using *en* or *à*
- [ ] Use at least 2 prepositions of place correctly
- [ ] Give a simple 3-step direction using *tout droit, à droite, à gauche*

**Not a Week 6 gate:** Full *de + article* mastery, *aux* (plural), or *aller + infinitif.* These continue to be reinforced as the course progresses.
