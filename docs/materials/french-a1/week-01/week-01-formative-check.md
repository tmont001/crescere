# French A1 — Week 1 Formative Check

**Administered:** Start of Session 3 (first session of Week 2)
**Time needed:** 6–8 minutes total
**Purpose:** Quick checkpoint — not a test. Tells the instructor which items need a brief review before moving into Week 2 content.

---

## Oral Prompts (5 questions — allow ~1 min per student for the set)

Ask these to the group or call on individual students. Listen for accuracy of the target item, not overall fluency.

| # | Prompt | Target response | What to listen for |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | *Comment tu t'appelles ?* | *Je m'appelle [name].* | Correct phrase — not *Je suis appelle* |
| 2 | *Tu es d'où ?* | *Je suis de [city/country].* | Correct use of *suis de*; clean vowels |
| 3 | Give me three forms of être — je, tu, il. | *je suis / tu es / il est* | All three; pronunciation of *suis* (no final -s audible) |
| 4 | Count from 1 to 10. | un, deux, trois, quatre, cinq, six, sept, huit, neuf, dix | Pronunciation: final -x in *deux* silent; the *r* in *trois, quatre* |
| 5 | How do you say "Nice to meet you" in French? | *Enchanté/e* | Correct word; bonus if student notes the -e ending for women |

---

## Written Prompts (5 questions — students write answers, ~3 min)

Paste into the session chat or distribute at the start of Session 3.

**Instructions to students:** Answer in French. Short answers only.

1. Write the French equivalent of: "I live in Chicago."
2. Write the French phrase for "Hello" (formal, daytime).
3. What is the French word for 28?
4. Write a sentence using *tu es* to say "You are American" (informal).
5. Write a question in French asking someone where they are from. Use the spoken word order we practiced in class.

**Answer key:**
1. *J'habite à Chicago.*
2. *Bonjour.*
3. *vingt-huit*
4. *Tu es américain/américaine.*
5. *Tu es d'où ?* (accept *D'où tu es ?* or any natural spoken formulation; do not require *est-ce que* — that was not taught in Week 1)

---

## Instructor Scoring Notes

This is not graded. Use it to make one of three decisions:

| Result | What it means | What to do |
|---|---|---|
| Students handle all 5 oral prompts confidently | Week 1 content is secure | Move directly into Week 2 content |
| Students stumble on 1–2 prompts | Minor gaps; expected at this stage | Quick review at start of Session 3 (5 min max), then move on |
| Students stumble on 3+ prompts | Week 1 needs reinforcement | Build a 10-minute review into Sessions 3 and 4 before adding new content |

**Most common Week 1 gaps:**
- *Je suis appelle* instead of *Je m'appelle* — correct once, clearly; repetition will fix it
- Uncertainty with être forms *tu es* and *il/elle est* (students often only remember *je suis*) — quick two-minute drill resolves it
- Numbers in the teens: *seize* (16) and *dix-sept* through *dix-neuf* trip students up — 1–2 min of spot-drilling usually closes the gap

**Note:** Do not assess numbers in the 70s/80s/90s at this checkpoint. Those patterns are in the Session 2 homework, not the live class content. Students may or may not have worked through them yet. If they have not, that is expected — address the irregular tens in Week 2, not as a Week 1 failure.

---

## Ready to Move On

A student is ready for Week 2 content when they can:

- [ ] Give a 4–5 sentence self-introduction without the script
- [ ] Produce *je suis / tu es / il/elle est* without hesitation
- [ ] Count to 20 reliably; some hesitation in the 20s is acceptable
- [ ] Use *Bonjour* vs. *Salut* and *tu* vs. *vous* correctly in context

No student should be held back from Week 2 for pronunciation errors alone. Pronunciation is a process — content readiness is the gate.

**Looking ahead:** Week 2 introduces the full six-form être conjugation. Students who are uncertain about *je suis / tu es / il est* will struggle with the transition. If those three forms are shaky, spend the first 5 minutes of Session 3 on them before moving forward.
