# French A1 — Session 2 Slide Outline
## Complete Introduction and First Questions

**Session length:** 60 minutes
**Session-end goal:** Students can give a complete self-introduction (name, origin, residence, nationality, profession), ask 2–3 questions about another person, and hold a short back-and-forth exchange.

> **Pacing note:** Numbers 30–100 have been moved out of Session 2 core. The session ends with a full group introduction round — that is the non-negotiable block. Trim earlier slides before cutting it.

---

## Core 60-Minute Plan

### Slide 1 — Warm-Up: Back in French (8 min)
**Purpose:** Re-activate Session 1 language; identify and correct the most persistent errors before adding new content.
**Teacher notes:**
- Start in French: *Bonjour tout le monde !* Wait for *Bonjour !* back.
- Ask each student: *Comment tu t'appelles ?* → *Je m'appelle...*
- Then: *Tu es d'où ?* → *Je suis de...*
- Listen for: *Je suis appelle* (most common S1 error — correct it once, clearly); vowel quality; the r.
- Do not dwell. 1–2 corrections max. The purpose is activation, not review drilling.
**Student activity:** Each student answers both questions when called on.

---

### Slide 2 — Être: Expanding from Je Suis (10 min)
**Purpose:** Add *tu es* and *il/elle est* so students can use être in dialogue — without requiring the full 6-form conjugation.
**Teacher notes:**
- Do not start by drilling all six forms from homework. Start by using the forms students actually need today.
- Introduce in context:
  - *Je suis de New York.* → *Tu es d'où ?* — *Tu es de...* (adds *tu es*)
  - *Elle est médecin. Il est professeur.* (adds *il/elle est*)
- After modeling in context: show a small table with just these three forms. Ask students to produce them.

| | |
|---|---|
| je suis | I am |
| tu es | you are |
| il / elle est | he / she is |

- Say: "We'll add the other forms in Week 2 when we start describing other people. Today we need these three."
- **Gender alert:** nationality and profession adjectives change. Model *il est canadien / elle est canadienne*. Do not teach the rule — just demonstrate. Full adjective agreement is Week 2.
**Student activity:** Instructor gives 5 sentence starters, students complete them using je suis / tu es / il/elle est.

---

### Slide 3 — Nationality and Profession: Just What You Need (8 min)
**Purpose:** Students can say their own nationality and profession in French.
**Teacher notes:**
- Start with the students in the room, not a vocabulary list. Go around: "What's your nationality?" → teach that word immediately. "What's your profession?" → teach that word.
- Do not pre-teach 15 nationalities and 15 professions. Give each student the 1–2 words they personally need. They can explore the full list in the vocabulary reference.
- Two rules to flag briefly:
  1. No article after être + profession: *Je suis médecin.* (Not *un médecin.*)
  2. Nationalities have gender forms: *français / française.* Just point to it — do not teach adjective agreement yet.
**Student activity:** Each student says their nationality and profession in French. Instructor provides any words students do not know.

---

### Slide 4 — Three Questions That Open a Conversation (8 min)
**Purpose:** Students can ask someone 2–3 questions in French.
**Teacher notes:**
- Teach three question frames only. Keep them functional and memorable.
  1. *Tu t'appelles comment ?* — What's your name? (spoken French word order)
  2. *Tu es d'où ?* — Where are you from?
  3. *Tu fais quoi dans la vie ?* — What do you do? (very natural spoken French)
- Optionally add: *Tu habites où ?* — Where do you live?
- Do NOT teach inversion (*Êtes-vous...?*) or *est-ce que* as a system. These are solid spoken questions — students can use them immediately.
- Model: ask these questions to a student, get their answers, model the exchange.
**Student activity:** Students practice asking all three questions to a partner, getting real answers.

---

### Slide 5 — The Complete Introduction Dialogue (18 min)
**Purpose:** Students hold a real back-and-forth exchange in French.
**Teacher notes:**
- Display the model dialogue. Give students 2 minutes to read silently.
- Instructor models with one student (instructor is the new person, student is themselves).
- Pairs practice for 8–10 minutes. Walk the virtual room — listen in, take notes.
- After pairs: debrief the 2–3 most common errors with the whole group. Do not list every error — pick what matters most.

**Model dialogue:**
> A: *Bonjour ! Je m'appelle Sophie. Et toi ?*
> B: *Bonjour ! Je m'appelle [name]. Enchanté/e !*
> A: *Enchanté/e ! Tu es d'où ?*
> B: *Je suis de [city/country]. Et toi ?*
> A: *Je suis de Lyon. Tu habites où maintenant ?*
> B: *J'habite à [city]. Tu fais quoi dans la vie ?*
> A: *Je suis professeure. Et toi ?*
> B: *Je suis [profession]. Je suis [nationality].*
> A: *Super ! Très content/e de te connaître.*

**Student activity:** Read silently (2 min) → instructor models with one student (3 min) → pair practice (8–10 min) → brief group debrief.

---

### Slide 6 — Full Introduction Round (8 min)
**Purpose:** Every student gives a complete introduction — the week's main speaking milestone.
**Teacher notes:**
- Every student takes a turn. Allow notes — do not require memorization.
- Format: each student gives 4–5 sentences. *Je m'appelle... Je suis de... J'habite à... Je suis [profession]. Je suis [nationality].*
- Correct one thing per student, maximum two. Pick the most important error and address it. Save the rest.
- End by asking one student to introduce a classmate based on what they heard. This rewards listening.
- Close with: "You just introduced yourself in French. That's a real thing."
**Student activity:** Each student gives their introduction. One student introduces a classmate.

---

### Slide 7 — Homework Preview and Wrap-Up (3 min)
**Purpose:** Set clear, manageable homework expectations; close positively.
**Teacher notes:** Keep this brief. Say: "The homework has three parts — all short. Say your introduction out loud again, write it down, and look at the numbers reference."
**Student activity:** Students note homework. Optional: one French word each before logging off.

---

## Cut First If Running Behind

Cut in this order — most dispensable first:

1. **Slide 3 (nationalities/professions):** If students already have their words from Slide 2 context, skip the dedicated slide and move on.
2. **Slide 4 (questions):** Reduce to just the first two questions (*Tu t'appelles comment? / Tu es d'où?*) — the third question is optional.
3. **Slide 5 (dialogue):** Shorten pair practice to 5 minutes instead of 8–10. Skip the full group debrief.
4. Do not cut Slide 6 (introduction round). That is the session's reason for existing.

---

## Extension for Fast Cohorts

Only if you have time remaining after the introduction round:

- **Numbers 30–69:** Teach the regular pattern (*trente, quarante, cinquante, soixante* + same units) and count together. (~5 min)
- **Common expressions with être:** *C'est sympa / C'est difficile / C'est parfait / Ce n'est pas vrai.* Quick recognition drill. (~3 min)
- **Additional questions:** *Quel âge as-tu ? / Tu as des frères et sœurs ?* — preview of Week 2 vocabulary. (~4 min)
