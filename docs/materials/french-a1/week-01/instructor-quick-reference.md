# French A1 — Week 1 Instructor Quick Reference

---

## 1. Week 1 Snapshot

| | |
|---|---|
| **Theme** | Sounds, Greetings, and First Introductions |
| **Sessions** | 1 (Sounds, Greetings, First Words) and 2 (Complete Introduction and First Questions) |
| **Communicative goal** | Students greet, introduce themselves in 4–5 sentences, and hold a short two-way exchange |
| **Grammar focus** | *Être* — 3 forms in Session 2 (*je suis / tu es / il-elle est*); *tu* vs. *vous* |
| **Vocabulary focus** | Greetings, *je m'appelle / je suis de / j'habite à*, nationalities, professions, numbers 1–30 |
| **End-of-week outcome** | Every student gives a full spoken introduction; counts to 20; uses *tu* vs. *vous* correctly |

**Session 1 scope note:** Do not present the full *être* conjugation in Session 1. One form (*je suis*) is enough to speak. The three-form table (*je / tu / il-elle*) is Session 2. The full six-form conjugation is Session 2 homework and Week 2.

---

## 2. Session 1 At-a-Glance

**Title:** Sounds, Greetings, and First Words
**Goal:** Students greet in French, give name and origin, use *je suis*, know *tu* vs. *vous*, count to 20.

| Block | Time | Must protect? |
|---|---|---|
| Welcome and setup | 5 min | No |
| Pronunciation awareness (r, silent endings, nasals — hear and try once) | 7 min | Yes — exposure before speaking |
| Greetings and leave-takings | 10 min | Yes |
| Tu vs. vous | 5 min | Yes — state the rule even if you skip the scenarios |
| Je m'appelle / je suis de / j'habite à | 12 min | Yes — students must have these phrases before the speaking activity |
| First speaking activity (pairs exchange) | 12 min | Yes — every student speaks before leaving |
| Numbers 1–20 | 7 min | Reduce first if behind |
| Wrap-up and homework preview | 2 min | No |

**Cut first:** Shorten numbers to 1–10 live (assign 11–20 with homework). Cut *tu/vous* scenarios (just state the rule). Shorten pair time to 3 min.

**Move to homework:** Alphabet, numbers 21–30, nasal vowel drilling, French r drilling, liaison rules.

**Homework assigned after Session 1:** Say intro out loud × 3; numbers 1–30; write 3 sentences; alphabet reference (read once, no memorization needed).

## 3. Session 2 At-a-Glance

**Title:** Complete Introduction and First Questions
**Goal:** Full introduction (name, origin, residence, nationality, profession); 2–3 questions; back-and-forth exchange.

| Block | Time | Must protect? |
|---|---|---|
| Warm-up (reactivate S1 phrases; correct *je suis appelle*) | 8 min | Yes |
| Être — 3 forms in context (*je / tu / il-elle*) — NOT the full table yet | 10 min | Yes |
| Nationality and profession — students' own words only | 8 min | No — skip if handled in être block |
| Three question frames (*Tu t'appelles comment? / Tu es d'où? / Tu fais quoi?*) | 8 min | Reduce to 2 frames if behind |
| Dialogue practice (model → pairs → debrief) | 18 min | Shorten pairs to 5 min; skip full debrief if needed |
| Full introduction round — every student speaks | 8 min | **Non-negotiable** |
| Homework preview and wrap-up | 3 min | No |

**Cut first:** Slide 3 (nationalities) if already handled. Slide 4 reduce to first 2 questions. Slide 5 shorten pairs. Do not cut the introduction round.

**Move to homework:** Numbers 21–100, full 6-form *être*, extended nationality/profession vocab, inversion question formation.

**Homework assigned after Session 2:** Numbers 31–100; write 5-sentence introduction; say it from memory; answer 4 questions out loud.

---

## 4. Do Not Cut

| Block | Why it is protected |
|---|---|
| Full introduction round (S2 Slide 6) | Week's main milestone — every student must speak French to the group |
| First speaking activity (S1 Slide 6) | Students must produce French out loud in Session 1, not just listen |
| *Je m'appelle / je suis de / j'habite à* (S1 Slide 5) | Core phrases — nothing else in Week 1 works without them |
| *Tu* vs. *vous* rule (even if cut to one sentence) | Students will use *vous* with everyone by default and get stuck |
| Pronunciation awareness — hear and try once (S1 Slide 2) | Exposure before speaking prevents the worst early habits |

## 5. Top Student Mistakes to Watch For

| Mistake | Quick correction cue | When to correct |
|---|---|---|
| *Je suis appelle X* | "Two different phrases: *je m'appelle* for your name, *je suis de* for where you're from. *Je m'appelle* is a fixed phrase — we'll explain why in Week 3." | Immediately — very common |
| Pronouncing the final -s of *vous* before a consonant | Demonstrate: *vous* alone → s is silent; *vous êtes* → the s is voiced (liaison). Model both. | On first occurrence |
| Using *vous* with classmates out of politeness | "In this class, *tu* is correct with each other. *Vous* with your classmates sounds odd — like calling a friend 'sir.'" | As it comes up |
| French *e* sounding like English "ay" | Model the open è vs. closed é. Students conflate them early. A brief demo is enough for Week 1. | Gently, in context |
| *Elle est français* (no feminine agreement) | "*Elle est française* — the -e on the end. Adjective agreement is all of Week 2, so don't worry about the rule yet — just flag this one." | Note it; don't over-explain |

## 6. Instructor Language to Use

| Moment | What to say |
|---|---|
| Pronunciation awareness (S1) | *"I'm going to say a few things that sound strange. You're just listening and trying once. Don't worry about getting it right — that's what the next twelve weeks are for."* |
| Introducing *je m'appelle* | *"Don't ask me why it works this way yet. Just know the phrase — je m'appelle [name]. We'll explain the grammar in Week 3."* |
| Presenting *être* (S2) | *"We're starting with three forms today. That's all you need this week. The other three are in your homework — they're not hard, just more."* |
| *Tu* vs. *vous* | *"Think of tu as the word you use with people you'd call by their first name. In this class, use tu with each other."* |
| Before the introduction round (S2) | *"You now know enough to introduce yourself in French. I want each of you to do it. I'll go first."* |
| Correcting gently | Correct one item per student per round, maximum two. Say: "Almost — try *je suis de* instead." Then move on. |
| Student who freezes | Wait 3–4 seconds. Then: "Start with *Bonjour*. Just that word." |

## 7. Homework and Between-Session Reminder

| | What students do |
|---|---|
| **After Session 1** | Say intro out loud × 3 (last time from memory); numbers 1–30 out loud and written; write 3 French sentences; read alphabet reference once |
| **After Session 2** | Numbers 31–100 (irregular patterns in homework); write 5-sentence introduction; say it from memory; answer 4 spoken questions out loud |
| **Before Week 2** | Remind students at end of Session 2: "Next session starts with a quick check — nothing to memorize, just the phrases from this week." |

**Formative check:** Filed at `week-01/week-01-formative-check.md`. Administer at the **start of Session 3** (first session of Week 2). Takes 6–8 minutes. Not graded — tells you whether to review before moving into Week 2 content.

**If you have to move the introduction round to homework** (Session 2 ran out of time): ask each student to record a 30-second voice introduction and send it before Session 3. Not ideal — the group moment matters — but better than skipping.

---

## 8. Ready for Week 2

A student is ready to move into Week 2 when they can:

- [ ] Give a 4–5 sentence self-introduction without the script
- [ ] Produce *je suis / tu es / il/elle est* without hesitation (3 forms — not all 6)
- [ ] Count to 20 reliably (some hesitation in 20s is acceptable)
- [ ] Use *Bonjour* vs. *Salut* and *tu* vs. *vous* correctly in context

**Pronunciation errors alone do not gate Week 2.** A student who says the sounds imperfectly but produces the right phrases and grammar is ready to move forward.

**Watch going into Week 2:** Students uncertain about *je suis / tu es / il est* will struggle with the full 6-form expansion in Sessions 3–4. If those three forms are shaky after the formative check, spend the first 5 minutes of Session 3 on them before introducing new content.
