# French A1 — Week 8 Formative Check
## Home, Neighborhood, and High-Frequency Irregular Verbs

**When to administer:** At the start of Session 17, or as a closing check at the end of Session 16.
**Format:** Oral section first (5 prompts); written section second (5 prompts).
**Purpose:** Diagnostic tool. Not a grade. Informs whether the cohort is ready to progress to Week 9 content (near future and modal verbs) or needs reinforcement of Week 8 structures.

---

## Oral Prompts (5 prompts — instructor reads aloud)

Administer to each student individually or in round-robin. Allow 10–15 seconds per prompt.

**Prompt 1:** "Tell me one thing that is in your home and where it is."
*Target:* *Il y a un [noun] dans [room]. Le/La [noun] est [preposition] [noun].*
*Minimum pass:* Correct use of *il y a* AND one accurate preposition. *Du* contraction not required but welcomed.

**Prompt 2:** "Tell me one thing that is NOT in your home."
*Target:* *Il n'y a pas de [noun].*
*Minimum pass:* *Pas de* (not *pas du / pas d'un / pas des*). Article disappears after negation.

**Prompt 3:** "Place the sofa in relation to the television."
*Target:* *Le canapé est devant la télévision.* (Or another accurate preposition.)
*Watch for:* *En face de le salon* → should be *du salon.* Accept answers without *du* only if they choose a preposition that doesn't require it (*sur, sous, entre, devant*).

**Prompt 4:** "Give me one form of *faire* and use it in a sentence."
*Target:* Any correctly conjugated form used in context. *Je fais la cuisine. Vous faites du sport. Ils font du vélo.*
*Watch for:* *Je fait* (→ *je fais*); *ils faisent* (→ *ils font*).

**Prompt 5:** "Tell me something you know how to do and something or someone you are familiar with."
*Target:* One sentence with *savoir* (skill or fact) + one sentence with *connaître* (person or place). *Je sais nager. Je connais bien Paris.*
*Watch for:* *Je sais Paris* (→ *je connais Paris*); *je connais que* (→ *je sais que*).

---

## Written Prompts (5 items — students write independently)

**Item 1:** Translate: "There is a bathroom next to the kitchen."
*Expected:* *Il y a une salle de bains à côté de la cuisine.*
*Note: accept à côté de la cuisine — no contraction needed here (de + la, not de + le).*

**Item 2:** Translate: "There isn't a garage but there is a balcony."
*Expected:* *Il n'y a pas de garage mais il y a un balcon.*
*Watch for:* *Il n'y a pas du garage / pas d'un garage / pas des garages* — all wrong.

**Item 3:** Fill in the blank: *Le canapé est en face ___ salon.*
*Expected:* *du* (*de + le = du*)
*Watch for:* *de le* — impossible in French; must contract.

**Item 4:** Write one sentence using *venir* or *prendre* in context.
*Expected:* *Je viens de Paris. Je prends le métro pour aller au travail.*
*Minimum pass:* Correct yo/je form used in a meaningful sentence.

**Item 5:** Write one sentence with *savoir* and one with *connaître.* Both must be different from your oral answers.
*Expected:* Two sentences, each using the correct verb. *Je sais où est la boulangerie. Je connais ce quartier depuis longtemps.*
*Watch for:* Using the same verb both times; mixing up the contexts.

---

## Scoring Decision Table

| Student output | Decision |
|---|---|
| All 5 oral prompts pass + 4–5 written correct | Ready to move on to Week 9 |
| 4 oral pass + 3–4 written correct | Ready to move on — monitor *savoir/connaître* in Week 9 |
| *Il y a* and *il n'y a pas de* both correct, but prepositions weak | Move to Week 9 — assign preposition consolidation as homework |
| *Savoir/connaître* distinction not yet stable | Move to Week 9 — give one focused drill in Session 17 warm-up |
| *Il y a* not yet automatic OR *pas de* rule wrong on multiple items | Consider spending 5–8 minutes at Session 17 start on targeted drilling before introducing new content |
| Fewer than 3 oral prompts pass OR fewer than 3 written correct | Flag for instructor follow-up; do not delay Week 9 for whole cohort |

---

## Ready to Move On

Before beginning Week 9 (*aller + infinitif*, *devoir, pouvoir, vouloir*), a student should be able to:
- [ ] Say *il y a / il n'y a pas de* automatically, without hesitation
- [ ] Use at least 3 prepositions of place accurately in speech
- [ ] Apply *de + le = du* in at least 1 preposition context
- [ ] Conjugate *faire* correctly in *je, vous, ils* forms
- [ ] Conjugate *venir* correctly in *je, ils* forms
- [ ] Choose correctly between *savoir* and *connaître* in most cases
- [ ] Describe their home in 4+ spoken sentences

---

## Not a Gate

The following items are NOT required for progression to Week 9:
- Full mastery of all 5 verb conjugations (gaps in *prendre* or *connaître nous/vous* forms are acceptable)
- Perfect *du/de la/de l'/des* contraction selection in all preposition contexts
- Extended neighborhood vocabulary beyond 3–4 core words
- *Venir de + infinitif* (recent past) — this is extension material
- Distinction between *il y a* and *être* in every context (instinctive use develops over A1–A2 transition)
