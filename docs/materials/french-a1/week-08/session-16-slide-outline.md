# French A1 — Session 16 Slide Outline
## High-Frequency Irregular Verbs and the Neighborhood

**Session length:** 53 minutes (on plan) — can run 60 min for slower cohorts
**Session-end goal:** Every student delivers a 5–6 sentence description of their home AND neighborhood, using at least one of the five irregular verbs (*faire, venir, prendre, savoir, connaître*) and demonstrating the *savoir/connaître* distinction.

> **Pacing note:** The combined home+neighborhood speaking round (Slide 7) is non-negotiable. Compress individual verb blocks (each by 1–2 min) before cutting the speaking round. If 5 verbs is too dense in class, assign *savoir/connaître* as a vocabulary reference and drill only *faire* and *venir* live — but the speaking round stays.

---

## Core Plan

### Slide 1 — Warm-Up: Il y a Recall (5 min)
**Purpose:** Re-activate Session 15 material; bridge to neighborhood.
**Teacher notes:**
- Ask each student one sentence: *"Dis-moi une chose qui est dans ta maison."* (One *il y a* sentence with a preposition.)
- Quickly correct any *de le* errors (should be *du*). Do not re-teach.
- Brief preview: "Today we go outside — into the neighborhood — and add five verbs that appear in almost every French conversation."
**Student activity:** One *il y a + preposition* sentence each.

---

### Slide 2 — Faire (7 min)
**Purpose:** Students learn and can use *faire* in context.
**Teacher notes:**
- Write the full conjugation:

  | je | tu | il/elle | nous | vous | ils/elles |
  |---|---|---|---|---|---|
  | fais | fais | fait | faisons | **faites** | **font** |

- Address the irregular forms explicitly: "*Vous faites* — not 'faisez.' *Ils font* — completely irregular. These two are the ones to memorize; the rest follow a pattern."
- Three key expressions to drill immediately:
  - *faire la cuisine / faire à manger* — to cook
  - *faire du sport / faire de la gym* — to exercise
  - *il fait beau / il fait chaud / il fait froid / il fait mauvais* — weather (note: *il* here is impersonal, not a person)
- Quick round: "What do you do at the weekend?" → *Je fais...* — each student gives one answer.
- Flag: *faire* + du/de la/de l' for leisure activities (*faire du vélo, faire de la natation, faire du yoga*) — same partitive *de* rule they know.
**Student activity:** Give one *je fais* sentence about a weekend activity.

---

### Slide 3 — Venir (6 min)
**Purpose:** Students learn and can use *venir* for origin and arrival.
**Teacher notes:**
- Write the full conjugation:

  | je | tu | il/elle | nous | vous | ils/elles |
  |---|---|---|---|---|---|
  | viens | viens | vient | venons | venez | **viennent** |

- Address *ils viennent*: "The stem changes: *vien-* in the singular, *vienn-* in the 3rd person plural. 'Ils vienvent' is a very common error."
- Two key uses:
  - Origin: *Je viens de France / du Canada / d'Espagne.* (Note: *de + country* — same *du* contraction for masculine countries: *du Maroc, du Japon.*)
  - Coming: *À quelle heure est-ce que tu viens?* — What time are you coming?
- Quick round: "Where are you from?" → *Je viens de...* — each student.
**Student activity:** One *je viens de* sentence (origin).

---

### Slide 4 — Prendre (5 min)
**Purpose:** Students learn *prendre* primarily for transportation and ordering.
**Teacher notes:**
- Write the full conjugation:

  | je | tu | il/elle | nous | vous | ils/elles |
  |---|---|---|---|---|---|
  | prends | prends | prend | prenons | prenez | **prennent** |

- Address *ils prennent*: same pattern as *venir* — the double-n appears in the 3rd person plural.
- Key contexts:
  - Transport: *Je prends le métro / le bus / le train / l'avion.* (Students have seen this in Week 5 directions.)
  - Ordering: *Je prends une baguette.* (Students have seen this in Week 6 restaurant role play.)
- Quick round: "How do you get to work / class?" → *Je prends...* — each student.
**Student activity:** One *je prends* sentence (transport or food).

---

### Slide 5 — Savoir and Connaître (8 min)
**Purpose:** Students learn both verbs, distinguish between them, and can use each correctly.
**Teacher notes:**
- Write BOTH conjugation tables side by side:

  **Savoir:**
  | je | tu | il/elle | nous | vous | ils/elles |
  |---|---|---|---|---|---|
  | sais | sais | sait | savons | savez | savent |

  **Connaître:**
  | je | tu | il/elle | nous | vous | ils/elles |
  |---|---|---|---|---|---|
  | connais | connais | connaît | connaissons | connaissez | connaissent |

- Key: *savoir* = facts, skills, information. *Connaître* = familiarity with people or places.
- Four contrast examples — say both versions; students identify which is correct:
  1. "I know Paris." → *Je connais Paris* ✓ / *Je sais Paris* ✗
  2. "I know what time it is." → *Je sais quelle heure il est* ✓ / *Je connais quelle heure il est* ✗
  3. "Do you know Marie?" → *Tu connais Marie?* ✓ / *Tu sais Marie?* ✗
  4. "I don't know." → *Je ne sais pas.* (universal — when you don't know a fact)
- Quick test: say a sentence stem; students finish with *sais/connais.*
  - *Je ___ nager.* → *sais*
  - *Tu ___ ce restaurant?* → *connais*
  - *Vous ___ où est la station?* → *savez*
  - *Elle ___ bien mon quartier.* → *connaît*
- Note: *je ne sais pas* is one of the most common French phrases — it should be automatic.
**Student activity:** 4-item *sais/connais* identification → rapid-fire round.

---

### Slide 6 — Neighborhood Vocabulary (5 min)
**Purpose:** Students acquire the 5–6 key neighborhood words and adjectives needed for the speaking round.
**Teacher notes:**
- Core places: *une boulangerie, une pharmacie, une station de métro / un arrêt de bus, un parc, une école, un supermarché, une mairie.*
- Location phrases: *au coin de la rue, en face de chez moi, à cinq minutes à pied, au bout de la rue, près de.*
- Neighborhood adjectives: *calme, animé(e), bruyant(e), pratique, agréable, résidentiel(le).*
- Quick round: "Describe your neighborhood in one adjective." → *Mon quartier est...*
**Student activity:** One adjective + location phrase about their neighborhood.

---

### Slide 7 — Speaking Round: My Home and My Neighborhood (12 min)
**Purpose:** Every student integrates Session 15 and Session 16 content in a connected description.
**Teacher notes:**
- Each student speaks for 60–90 seconds. Notes allowed for vocabulary; *il y a, être + preposition,* and at least one of the five verbs from memory.
- Minimum required elements per student:
  - [ ] *Il y a + [at least 2 home features]* with at least one preposition
  - [ ] At least one of: *faire, venir, prendre*
  - [ ] *Je connais* OR *Je sais* correctly used
  - [ ] At least 2 neighborhood sentences (adjective + what's nearby)
- Correct one item per student. Common targets: *je fait* (→ *je fais*); *ils vienvent* (→ *ils viennent*); *je sais mon quartier* (→ *je connais*).
- After all students: note 2 patterns across the group.
**Student activity:** Each student delivers a 5–6 sentence home + neighborhood description.

---

### Slide 8 — Wrap-Up: Week 8 Close (5 min)
**Purpose:** Assign homework; preview Week 9; close Week 8.
**Teacher notes:**
- Preview Week 9: "Next week we look forward — how to talk about plans and intentions. *Je vais aller au cinéma ce soir. Je dois travailler demain.*"
- Homework: written home+neighborhood paragraph + all five verb conjugations from memory.
- Remind students: the Week 8 formative check will ask them to produce all five verb forms and demonstrate the *savoir/connaître* distinction.
**Student activity:** Note homework.

## Cut First If Running Behind

1. **Slide 4 (prendre):** Reduce to 2 minutes — just show the conjugation and one sentence. Students have seen *prendre* before.
2. **Slide 6 (neighborhood vocabulary):** Reduce to 3 items; move vocabulary to homework reference.
3. **Slides 2–4 combined:** If very behind, do *faire* only in full; give *venir* and *prendre* conjugations on a reference slide; drill orally without explanation.
4. Do not cut Slide 7 (speaking round).

---

## Extension for Fast Cohorts

- **Venir de + infinitif (recent past):** *Je viens de rentrer chez moi.* — I've just got home. *Il vient de manger.* — He's just eaten. This structure is common in spoken French; introduce it if there is time.
- **Faire du + de la + de l':** Extended sports vocabulary with partitive rule (*faire de la randonnée, faire de l'escalade*).
- **Connaître + indirect question vs. savoir + indirect question:** *Je connais ce café mais je ne sais pas si c'est ouvert le dimanche.* — advanced use combining both.
- **Compare two neighborhoods:** *Mon quartier est plus calme que le quartier de X mais moins animé.*
