# French A1 — Session 15 Slide Outline
## Describing a Home: Il y a and Prepositions of Place

**Session length:** 60 minutes
**Session-end goal:** Every student can describe their home in 5–6 sentences — what rooms there are, what furniture is in those rooms, and where things are — using *il y a, être + preposition,* and correct *de + article* contractions.

> **Pacing note:** The home description speaking round (Slide 6) is non-negotiable. Every student speaks before the session ends. Compress Slide 4 (furniture, to 6 min) before cutting Slide 6.

---

## Core 60-Minute Plan

### Slide 1 — Warm-Up: Week 7 Close (5 min)
**Purpose:** Close the Week 7 loop; activate demonstratives and shopping vocabulary before switching topics.
**Teacher notes:**
- Ask each student one sentence: describe one item of clothing using a demonstrative and a color. (*Cette veste verte coûte combien?*)
- If Week 7 formative check showed widespread *ce imperméable* errors: 30-second group reminder — "*Cet* before a vowel or silent h: *cet imperméable.*"
- Do not re-teach. Activation only.
**Student activity:** One demonstrative + color sentence each.

---

### Slide 2 — Il y a / Il n'y a pas de (8 min)
**Purpose:** Students learn the existence phrase and its negation.
**Teacher notes:**
- Write *il y a* and say: "These three little words mean 'there is' or 'there are.' They never change: one room, twenty rooms — *il y a.* Same phrase."
- Examples: *Il y a une cuisine. Il y a trois chambres. Il n'y a pas de garage.*
- Negation: "After *pas*, the article disappears — same rule you already know from Week 6. 'Il n'y a **pas de** garage' — never *pas d'un, pas du, pas des.*"
- Quick drill: instructor describes a home feature; students give the affirmative and then the negative.
  - *un jardin* → *Il y a un jardin. / Il n'y a pas de jardin.*
  - *une cave* → *Il y a une cave. / Il n'y a pas de cave.*
  - *un balcon* → *Il y a un balcon. / Il n'y a pas de balcon.*
- Ask 3 students: *"Il y a combien de pièces chez toi?"*
**Student activity:** Affirmative/negative drill (3 items) → each student gives one real fact about their home.

---

### Slide 3 — Rooms Vocabulary (10 min)
**Purpose:** Students learn 8–10 room names with gender and can use them with *il y a.*
**Teacher notes:**
- Introduce rooms in pairs where possible: *le salon / la salle à manger; la chambre / la salle de bains; la cuisine / le bureau.*
- Also: *les toilettes, le couloir, l'entrée, le balcon, le jardin, le garage.*
- After each room: students repeat + say *il y a [room] chez moi* or *il n'y a pas de [room] chez moi* — real fact.
- Flag the double meaning of *l'entrée*: "Remember — *l'entrée* means entrance hall here, not the starter from a restaurant menu."
- Floors: *au rez-de-chaussée, au premier étage, en haut, en bas.* One quick example: *"La chambre est au premier étage."*
**Student activity:** Repeat each room → give one true sentence about their home.

---

### Slide 4 — Furniture and Household Items (8 min)
**Purpose:** Students learn 8–10 core furniture items and can combine them with rooms and *il y a.*
**Teacher notes:**
- Introduce items grouped by room:
  - *Chambre:* un lit, une armoire
  - *Salon:* un canapé, une étagère, une table basse
  - *Cuisine:* un réfrigérateur (frigo), un four, une machine à laver
  - *Salle de bains:* une baignoire / une douche, un lavabo
- After each group: students repeat + say one item that IS or ISN'T in their home.
- Do not try to cover all items in the vocabulary file. 8–10 items used actively beats 20 on a list.
**Student activity:** Repeat each item → give one *il y a* sentence per group.

---

### Slide 5 — Prepositions of Place (10 min)
**Purpose:** Students learn 7 prepositions for interior space and apply *de + le = du.*
**Teacher notes:**
- Introduce each preposition with a clear physical demonstration (point at / mime the position if possible):
  - *sur* (on): *Le chat est sur le canapé.*
  - *sous* (under): *Le chat est sous le lit.*
  - *devant* (in front of): *Le canapé est devant la télévision.*
  - *derrière* (behind): *Le jardin est derrière la maison.*
  - *entre* (between): *Le bureau est entre la fenêtre et l'armoire.*
  - *à côté de* (next to): *L'armoire est à côté du lit.*
  - *en face de* (facing): *La cuisine est en face du salon.*
- Address *du* immediately: "When these prepositions are followed by *de + le*, the two words contract to *du.* You already know *du* from partitive articles (du café). Same word, same rule: *de + le = du.* You can never say 'de le' in French."
- Quick drill: instructor says two items; student places one in relation to the other using a preposition.
  - *le lit / la fenêtre* → *Le lit est devant la fenêtre.*
  - *le canapé / la télévision* → *Le canapé est devant la télévision.*
  - *la cuisine / le salon* → *La cuisine est à côté du salon.*
  - *l'armoire / le lit* → *L'armoire est à côté du lit.*
**Student activity:** 4-item placement drill using prepositions + *du* contraction.

---

### Slide 6 — Speaking Round: Describe Your Home (12 min)
**Purpose:** Every student describes their home in 5–6 sentences.
**Teacher notes:**
- Each student speaks for 60–90 seconds. Notes allowed for vocabulary; *il y a* and prepositions from memory.
- Minimum required elements per student:
  - [ ] *Il y a + [at least 3 rooms/features]*
  - [ ] *Il n'y a pas de + [something missing]*
  - [ ] At least 2 items placed with a preposition
  - [ ] At least one *du* contraction (*à côté du lit, en face du salon*)
- Correct one item per student. Common targets: *de le* (should be *du*); *il y a* used for location (*il y a le canapé devant* → should be *le canapé est devant*).
- After all students: note 2 patterns across the group.
**Student activity:** Each student delivers a 5–6 sentence home description.

---

### Slide 7 — Wrap-Up (7 min)
**Purpose:** Assign homework; preview Session 16.
**Teacher notes:**
- Preview Session 16: "Next session we stay in the neighborhood — but we add five high-frequency irregular verbs: *faire, venir, prendre, savoir, connaître.* These appear in almost every conversation in French."
- Homework: written home description + speaking practice.
**Student activity:** Note homework.

## Extension for Fast Cohorts

- **Adjectives for home:** *grand/grande, petit/petite, moderne, ancien/ancienne, lumineux/lumineuse, sombre, confortable.* Students add an adjective to each room: *Il y a un petit salon mais une grande cuisine.*
- **Venir de + infinitif preview:** *Je viens de rentrer chez moi.* — I've just got home. (introduces the *venir de + inf* structure informally; full study in Session 16.)
- **De + les = des (prepositions):** *À côté des chambres, il y a...*— Using *des* (de + les) in a preposition context.
