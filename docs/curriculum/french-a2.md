# French A2 — Elementary

**Language:** French
**Level:** A2 (CEFR Elementary)
**Course ID:** fr-a2
**Status:** Approved draft — not yet taught

---

## Course Purpose

Unlocks the ability to talk about the past — the single most transformative grammar step for early French learners. Built around the passé composé and imparfait distinction, which governs how all storytelling works in French. Students exit with the confidence to discuss their life story, travel experiences, opinions, and future plans in connected, multi-sentence French.

---

## Ideal Student Profile

Completed French A1 or placed into A2 by placement test. Handles present tense and basic transactional exchanges. Cannot talk about the past reliably. Wants to tell stories, discuss life experiences, and move beyond simple present-tense conversations.

---

## Prerequisites

French A1 or demonstrated A1-level competency.

---

## End-of-Course Outcomes

By the end of French A2, students can:

- [ ] Use passé composé (être + avoir auxiliaries; past participle agreement with être verbs)
- [ ] Use imparfait for habitual past, descriptions, and ongoing states
- [ ] Distinguish passé composé from imparfait in narration
- [ ] Use futur simple for predictions and future plans
- [ ] Express opinions, preferences, and comparisons
- [ ] Handle workplace, travel, and accommodation scenarios
- [ ] Use direct and indirect object pronouns (me/te/le/la/les; me/te/lui/leur) in position
- [ ] Use y and en pronouns
- [ ] Use relative clauses qui and que
- [ ] Understand authentic French at moderate speed on familiar topics
- [ ] Write a connected multi-paragraph text on a familiar topic (12–15 sentences)

## 24-Session Breakdown

### Week 1 — A1 Consolidation and A2 Roadmap

**Session 1 — Fluency Check and Opinion Structures**
- Focus: Targeted gap identification through conversation
- Grammar: Opinion vocabulary: je pense que, je crois que, à mon avis, selon moi; agreeing/disagreeing
- Speaking: Open A1 conversation; opinion exchanges on familiar topics
- Session-end goal: Instructor has identified each student's specific A1 gaps; students can express a basic opinion

**Session 2 — Comparatives, Superlatives, and A2 Preview**
- Grammar: Comparatives: plus...que, moins...que, aussi...que; superlatives: le plus, le moins; irregular: meilleur/pire, mieux/moins bien; bon vs. bien distinction reinforced
- Speaking: Comparing cities, foods, activities; preview of what A2 unlocks — "the ability to tell your story"
- Session-end goal: Student can make accurate comparisons and understands the course arc

---

### Week 2 — Passé Composé (Part 1: Avoir)

**Session 3 — PC with Avoir: Regular Verbs**
- Grammar: Passé composé = avoir/être (present) + past participle; regular past participles: -er → -é, -ir → -i, -re → -u; time markers: hier, la semaine dernière, l'année dernière, il y a deux jours, ce matin (in past context)
- Speaking: Narrating recent events; sequencing with d'abord, ensuite, puis, finalement
- Session-end goal: Student can narrate a sequence of past events using regular verbs

**Session 4 — PC with Avoir: Irregular Past Participles**
- Grammar: High-frequency irregular past participles (must be memorized): avoir → eu, être → été, faire → fait, prendre → pris, mettre → mis, voir → vu, lire → lu, écrire → écrit, dire → dit, pouvoir → pu, vouloir → voulu, devoir → dû, savoir → su, venir → venu, tenir → tenu, ouvrir → ouvert, offrir → offert, recevoir → reçu
- Speaking: Storytelling using irregular verbs; past weekend / past trip narration
- Session-end goal: Student knows and can produce the 15 most common irregular past participles

---

### Week 3 — Passé Composé (Part 2: Être)

**Session 5 — Être Verbs: DR MRS VANDERTRAMP**
- Grammar: The 17 DR MRS VANDERTRAMP verbs take être as auxiliary; past participle agreement with subject: il est allé / elle est allée / ils sont allés / elles sont allées
- Speaking: Motion and state-change narratives; Je suis allé(e) à Paris, Elle est née en 1985, Ils sont partis à midi
- Session-end goal: Student can identify être verbs and apply agreement correctly

**Session 6 — Reflexive Verbs in PC; Full PC Review**
- Grammar: All reflexive verbs take être in PC; se lever → je me suis levé(e); agreement with subject; reflexive PC in negation and questions
- Speaking: Describing a past day using reflexive + être + avoir verbs in combination; cultural note: French weekend routines
- Session-end goal: Student can narrate a past day using all three PC patterns (avoir, être, reflexive) correctly

---

### Week 4 — Imparfait

**Session 7 — Imparfait Formation**
- Grammar: Imparfait = nous-stem + -ais/-ais/-ait/-ions/-iez/-aient; only one irregular: être → ét-; uses: habitual past (used to), ongoing state/description in past, background, age, weather, il y avait, c'était
- Speaking: Childhood memories and habitual past: Quand j'étais enfant..., Je jouais souvent..., Il y avait...
- Session-end goal: Student can form imparfait correctly for all verbs and use it in habitual/descriptive contexts

**Session 8 — Imparfait Deepened: Descriptions and States**
- Grammar: Imparfait for: ongoing state (j'avais faim, elle était fatiguée), description of a scene (Il faisait beau, La ville était calme), interrupted action (background for PC event)
- Speaking: Describing a photo or a scene from the past; cultural note: the French école primaire experience
- Session-end goal: Student can use imparfait to paint a detailed picture of the past

---

### Week 5 — PC vs. Imparfait: The Core Distinction

**Session 9 — Foreground vs. Background**
- Grammar: The key rule: PC = completed event (what happened, the foreground); imparfait = background, state, habit (what was happening or how things were). Classic frame: *scene description (imparfait) + event (PC)*. Il faisait nuit quand il est arrivé.
- Speaking: Storytelling using both tenses; building a narrative in pairs
- Session-end goal: Student can apply the PC/imparfait contrast in a 10-sentence story

**Session 10 — Narration Practice and Error Analysis**
- Focus: Targeted practice on the most common errors; common pitfalls: using PC for all past tenses; using imparfait for a one-time completed event
- Speaking: Retelling a film plot or a personal anecdote; peer correction
- Session-end goal: Student can self-correct the most common PC/imparfait errors in their own production

---

### Week 6 — Future Tense

**Session 11 — Futur Simple: Formation**
- Grammar: Futur simple = infinitif + -ai/-as/-a/-ons/-ez/-ont (for -re verbs, drop the -e); irregular stems: être → ser-, avoir → aur-, aller → ir-, faire → fer-, pouvoir → pourr-, vouloir → voudr-, venir → viendr-, tenir → tiendr-, voir → verr-, devoir → devr-, savoir → saur-, envoyer → enverr-, recevoir → recevr-
- Speaking: Plans and predictions; Demain, je serai...; Dans cinq ans, nous aurons...
- Session-end goal: Student can conjugate futur simple for all verbs, including irregulars

**Session 12 — Futur Simple in Use: Real Conditions**
- Grammar: Si + présent → futur (real condition): Si j'ai le temps, je viendrai; comparing near future (aller + inf) vs. futur simple — immediacy vs. projection, certainty vs. prediction
- Speaking: Life plans conversation; discussing future goals; conditional real scenarios
- Session-end goal: Student can use futur simple in predictions and real si-clauses

---

### Week 7 — Work and Professional Life

**Session 13 — Workplace Vocabulary and Professional French**
- Grammar: Formal register markers; review of vous for formal contexts; vocabulary in context
- Speaking: Professional scenario: workplace interactions — une réunion, un projet, un délai, un collègue, une entreprise; describing professional history using PC and imparfait
- Vocabulary: Workplace vocabulary (~40 words); professional role titles; formal phrases
- Session-end goal: Student can discuss professional life and career history in past tenses

**Session 14 — Job Interview Role Play**
- Grammar: PC and imparfait for professional narrative; expression of ability and experience
- Speaking: Full job interview in French: Parlez-moi de votre parcours, Quelles sont vos forces?, Pourquoi voulez-vous travailler ici?; formal register throughout
- Session-end goal: Student can conduct a basic French-language job interview

---

### Week 8 — Travel and Accommodation

**Session 15 — Booking and Travel Vocabulary**
- Grammar: Review of être verbs in context (partir, arriver, retourner, rester, descendre, monter)
- Speaking: Booking a hotel by phone: Je voudrais réserver une chambre double pour deux nuits, le petit-déjeuner est-il inclus?; travel planning vocabulary
- Vocabulary: Travel vocabulary (~40 words); hotel vocabulary; flight/train vocabulary
- Session-end goal: Student can book accommodation and navigate travel scenarios in French

**Session 16 — Travel Story in Past Tenses**
- Grammar: PC + imparfait in travel narrative; travel expressions and discourse connectors
- Speaking: Describing a past trip combining both tenses; problems: J'ai perdu mes bagages, Le vol était en retard
- Vocabulary: Travel problems; complaint vocabulary
- Session-end goal: Student can narrate a complete travel experience using both past tenses

---

### Week 9 — Object Pronouns

**Session 17 — Direct Object Pronouns**
- Grammar: Direct object pronouns: me/te/le/la/nous/vous/les; position before the verb (or before the infinitif in a modal construction); in PC with avoir, past participle agrees with preceding direct object: Je les ai vus
- Speaking: Avoiding repetition in conversation; J'ai mangé la tarte → Je l'ai mangée; paired pronoun drills in context
- Session-end goal: Student can replace direct objects with pronouns and apply past participle agreement

**Session 18 — Indirect Object Pronouns; y and en**
- Grammar: Indirect object pronouns: me/te/lui/nous/vous/leur; y (replaces à + place or thing); en (replaces de + noun or quantity); order when two pronouns appear: [me/te/nous/vous] before [le/la/les] before [lui/leur] before [y] before [en]
- Speaking: Natural conversation using all pronoun types; J'y vais demain, Il m'en a donné
- Session-end goal: Student can use direct, indirect, y, and en pronouns correctly in present and past

---

### Week 10 — Media and Culture

**Session 19 — Relative Clauses qui and que**
- Grammar: Relative clauses: qui (subject relative) = who, which, that; que (object relative) = whom, which, that; agreement of past participle with que in PC: Le film que j'ai regardé était bien
- Speaking: Discussing films, books, music using relative clauses; recommendations
- Vocabulary: Media and entertainment vocabulary
- Session-end goal: Student can use qui and que correctly, including PC agreement with que

**Session 20 — Cultural Discussion and Authentic Text**
- Grammar: Relative clauses reinforced; opinion structures reviewed
- Speaking: Discussion of an authentic French text (adapted news item or cultural article); structured opinions and reactions
- Session-end goal: Student can read a simple authentic French text and discuss its content

---

### Week 11 — Storytelling and Anecdotes

**Session 21 — Anecdote Structure and Discourse Connectors**
- Grammar: All past tenses in combination; connector vocabulary: au moment où, soudain, c'est alors que, du coup, finalement, au bout du compte
- Speaking: Structuring an anecdote: scene-setting (imparfait) → events (PC) → resolution/reflection; student stories
- Session-end goal: Student can tell a complete personal anecdote using both past tenses with appropriate connectors

**Session 22 — Reported Speech and Indirect Quotation**
- Grammar: Reported speech: il a dit que + imparfait (tense shift from present to imparfait when main verb is past); il a demandé si; il m'a demandé de + infinitif
- Speaking: Retelling conversations; news-style narrative; group storytelling
- Session-end goal: Student can report what someone said using basic tense-shift patterns

---

### Week 12 — Review and Final Performance

**Session 23 — Integration and Error Repair**
- Focus: Extended role play combining all A2 structures; targeted error analysis; pronunciation and fluency coaching
- Speaking: Multi-skill scenarios: job interview + travel story + opinion discussion

**Session 24 — Final Assessment and B1 Preview**
- Final performance tasks (see below)
- Preview of B1: the subjunctive, the conditional, and complex sentences
- Resources for independent practice

## Final Performance Task

**Part A — Spoken: past narration and discussion (5–7 minutes):**
Student tells a real personal story (travel, work, or life event) using both past tenses and discourse connectors. Must include imparfait scene-setting and PC for events. Then defends an opinion on a related topic.

**Part B — Written: mixed-tense text (12–15 sentences):**
Narrative using PC and imparfait, followed by a short paragraph on future plans using futur simple.

---

## Readiness Criteria for B1

A student is ready for B1 when they can:

- [ ] Narrate a past sequence of events using PC correctly (auxiliary + participle agreement)
- [ ] Describe habitual past and background using imparfait
- [ ] Distinguish PC from imparfait appropriately in narration
- [ ] Discuss future plans using futur simple (not only near future)
- [ ] Express and defend an opinion with 2–3 sentences of reasoning
- [ ] Handle workplace, travel, and health scenarios without preparation
- [ ] Write a connected 12–15 sentence text with appropriate tense use

## Instructor Notes

- **PC vs. imparfait (Weeks 5 and beyond):** This is the core difficulty of A2. Students will understand the rule intellectually before they apply it spontaneously. Expect errors for several weeks after Week 5. The goal is correct instinct, not perfect accuracy, by the end of A2.
- **Irregular past participles (Week 4):** These must be memorized. A list-and-drill approach in Session 4 should be followed by integration in every subsequent session that uses PC. Avoid allowing students to only practice regular verbs in storytelling tasks.
- **Past participle agreement with être verbs (Week 5):** Students who learned PC with avoir first consistently forget the agreement rule with être verbs. Return to it in Sessions 6 and 9–10.
- **Futur simple irregular stems (Week 11):** The irregulars that students most commonly miss: être → ser-, avoir → aur-, aller → ir-. These should be tested explicitly before moving on.
- **Object pronouns (Week 9):** Position in the sentence (before the verb) is the most common error. Position in a modal construction (before the infinitif) is the second most common. Drill both.
- **Session 22 (reported speech) is lower priority.** If the cohort is struggling with PC/imparfait or pronouns, reduce time on reported speech and increase consolidation.
