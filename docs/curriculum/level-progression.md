# Level Progression: A1 → A2 → B1

## Overview

All Crescere courses align to the Common European Framework of Reference for Languages (CEFR). The practical meaning of each level — and the specific criteria students should meet before advancing — is documented here.

---

## A1 — Beginner

### What A1 means in practical terms
A student at A1 can introduce themselves, answer simple questions about their life, make a basic request, and use a handful of high-frequency phrases. Communication is slow, heavily dependent on repetition and context, and limited to familiar topics. Grammar is instinctive only for the most common patterns.

### Vocabulary target
~650 active words (words the student can produce, not just recognize)

### Grammar coverage
| Language | Core structures |
|---|---|
| French | Être/avoir; regular -er/-ir/-re verbs; négation ne/pas; article genders; adjective agreement; basic question formation; aller + infinitif; devoir/pouvoir/vouloir; partitif |
| Spanish | Ser vs. estar (foundational distinction); regular -ar/-er/-ir verbs; reflexive verbs; tener; ir a + lugar; ir a + infinitivo; gustar/doler structure; tener que / hay que / poder; demonstratives |

### Speaking expectations at end of A1
- Handles introductions and basic personal information without preparation
- Navigates transactional scenarios (café, directions, shopping) with some hesitation
- Can produce 4–6 sentence descriptions of familiar topics
- Accuracy: correct on high-frequency patterns; errors expected on irregular verbs and agreement

### Writing expectations at end of A1
- Writes 8–10 grammatically sound simple sentences on familiar topics
- Can compose a short personal message or note
- Errors expected on agreement, accent marks, and low-frequency vocabulary

### Readiness criteria for A2
- [ ] Uses high-frequency irregular verbs in present tense without visible hesitation
- [ ] Handles 3+ transactional scenarios without preparation
- [ ] Describes daily routine in 6+ sentences
- [ ] Uses near-future construction correctly (French: aller + inf; Spanish: ir a + inf)
- [ ] Writes a coherent short text of 8–10 sentences

## A2 — Elementary

### What A2 means in practical terms
A student at A2 can talk about the past and discuss future plans. They can give opinions, make comparisons, and navigate workplace, travel, and accommodation contexts. Grammar errors are frequent but rarely prevent communication. This is the level at which most students begin feeling genuinely communicative.

### Vocabulary target
~1,400–1,500 active words (cumulative)

### Grammar coverage
| Language | Core structures |
|---|---|
| French | Passé composé (avoir + être auxiliaries; past participle agreement); imparfait; PC vs. imparfait distinction; futur simple; conditional for politeness; comparative/superlative; direct and indirect object pronouns; y and en pronouns; relative clauses que/qui |
| Spanish | Pretérito indefinido (regular + all major irregulars); imperfecto; pretérito vs. imperfecto distinction; futuro simple; real si-clause (si + present → future); comparatives/superlatives; gustar-type verbs; direct and indirect object pronouns; regional vocabulary awareness (Spain vs. Latin America) |

### Speaking expectations at end of A2
- Narrates a past story with sequenced events and background description
- Handles workplace, travel, and service scenarios with confidence
- Expresses and supports a personal opinion with 2–3 sentences of reasoning
- Accuracy: correct on regular past tenses; errors on irregular verbs and object pronoun placement

### Writing expectations at end of A2
- Writes a connected multi-paragraph text on a familiar topic (12–15 sentences)
- Can compose a professional email or travel narrative
- Tenses are mostly correct; errors on participle agreement and complex sentences

### Readiness criteria for B1
- [ ] Narrates a past sequence using correct tense contrast (PC/imparfait or pretérito/imperfecto)
- [ ] Discusses future plans using simple future (not only near-future)
- [ ] Handles 4+ scenarios (workplace, travel, health, opinion) without preparation
- [ ] Expresses and defends an opinion for 2+ minutes
- [ ] Writes a connected text of 12–15 sentences with appropriate tense use

## B1 — Intermediate

### What B1 means in practical terms
A student at B1 can hold a real conversation on abstract, professional, and cultural topics. They use complex sentence structures, express nuance, and adjust their register. Grammar is sophisticated enough to participate in discussions about politics, social issues, career, and culture. They can read authentic texts with solid comprehension and write a structured argument.

### Vocabulary target
~2,500 active words (cumulative)

### Grammar coverage
| Language | Core structures |
|---|---|
| French | Plus-que-parfait; present subjunctive (main triggers, purpose, concession, temporal); conditional present; si + imparfait → conditional (present hypothesis); conditional past + past regret si clauses; reported speech with tense shift; relative clauses dont/où/lequel series; argumentation connectors; formal register |
| Spanish | Pluscuamperfecto; present subjunctive (formation, all types — main triggers, temporal, purpose, concession, emotional); conditional (formation, hypotheticals); imperfecto de subjuntivo; si-clauses (all three types); conditional perfect + past regret; reported speech; complex relatives (lo que, quien, donde); passive (ser + participle); se pasivo/impersonal; argumentation connectors; formal register |

### Speaking expectations at end of B1
- Sustains a structured argument for 5+ minutes without preparation
- Uses subjunctive and conditional in natural conversation
- Switches between formal and informal registers appropriately
- Accuracy: subjunctive and conditional largely correct; errors on compound tenses and edge cases

### Writing expectations at end of B1
- Writes a cohesive 200–250 word structured essay
- Uses paragraphing, discourse connectors, and a conclusion
- Register is consistent; vocabulary is varied and appropriate

### Readiness criteria for B2
- [ ] Uses present subjunctive accurately with 8+ common triggers
- [ ] Constructs si-clause hypotheticals in both present and past forms
- [ ] Sustains a structured 5-minute argument without preparation
- [ ] Reads an authentic article with ~80% comprehension
- [ ] Writes a 200+ word structured essay with clear thesis and conclusion
- [ ] Understands near-native-speed audio on familiar topics with minimal repetition

## Cross-Level Principles

### Speaking first
Every level prioritizes speaking from Session 1. Grammar is introduced in service of talking, not as a prerequisite to it.

### Homework load
Homework is capped at 30 minutes per session. If any assignment runs consistently over this limit in practice, it should be shortened.

### Progression rate
- A1 → A2 requires completion of A1 or demonstrated placement-test readiness
- A2 → B1 requires completion of A2 or demonstrated placement-test readiness
- Students who complete B1 are directed to the placement test to confirm B2 readiness or to contact the instructor for private B2 options

### What students typically underestimate
- **A1:** The number of irregular verbs that must be memorized in present tense
- **A2:** The complexity of the past-tense distinction (PC/imparfait; pretérito/imperfecto)
- **B1:** The subjunctive — formation and trigger recognition both take time; this is the grammar point students most commonly avoid or confuse
