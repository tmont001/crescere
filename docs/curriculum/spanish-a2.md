# Spanish A2 — Elementary

**Language:** Spanish
**Level:** A2 (CEFR Elementary)
**Course ID:** es-a2
**Status:** Approved draft — not yet taught

---

## Course Purpose

Unlocks the ability to talk about the past — the single most transformative grammar step for early Spanish learners. Built around the **pretérito indefinido vs. imperfecto** distinction, which governs all storytelling in Spanish. This distinction is harder than its French equivalent (PC vs. imparfait) because Spanish has more irregular preterites and a greater number of mandatory memorization items. Students exit able to narrate life experiences, discuss professional history, handle service scenarios, and hold conversations on familiar topics in connected, multi-sentence Spanish.

---

## Ideal Student Profile

Completed Spanish A1 or placed into A2 by placement test. Handles present tense and basic transactional exchanges. Cannot talk about the past reliably. Wants to tell stories, discuss experiences, and communicate beyond the present moment.

---

## Prerequisites

Spanish A1 or demonstrated A1-level competency.

---

## End-of-Course Outcomes

By the end of Spanish A2, students can:

- [ ] Use pretérito indefinido for completed past actions (regular and all major irregular forms)
- [ ] Use imperfecto for habitual past, ongoing background states, and description
- [ ] Distinguish pretérito from imperfecto in narration
- [ ] Use futur simple (futuro simple) and ir a + infinitivo for future reference
- [ ] Express opinions, preferences, and comparisons
- [ ] Handle workplace, travel, and accommodation scenarios
- [ ] Use direct and indirect object pronouns (lo/la/los/las; le/les; and le/les → se before lo/la/los/las)
- [ ] Recognize key regional vocabulary differences (Spain vs. Latin America)
- [ ] Understand authentic Spanish at moderate speed on familiar topics
- [ ] Write a connected multi-paragraph text on a familiar topic (12–15 sentences)

## 24-Session Breakdown

### Week 1 — A1 Consolidation and A2 Roadmap

**Session 1 — Fluency Check and Opinion Structures**
- Focus: Targeted gap identification through conversation
- Grammar: Opinion vocabulary: creo que, en mi opinión, me parece que, a mi juicio; agreeing: estoy de acuerdo, tienes razón; disagreeing: no estoy de acuerdo, no creo que eso sea así (que sea → subjunctive formula at this stage)
- Speaking: Open A1 conversation; opinion exchanges on familiar topics
- Session-end goal: Instructor identifies student gaps; students can express a basic opinion

**Session 2 — Comparatives, Superlatives, and A2 Preview**
- Grammar: Comparatives: más...que, menos...que, tan...como; superlatives: el/la más, el/la menos; irregular forms: mejor/peor, mayor/menor; bueno vs. bien distinction reviewed
- Speaking: Comparing cities, foods, activities; preview — "this course teaches you to tell your story in Spanish"
- Session-end goal: Student makes accurate comparisons and understands the A2 course arc

---

### Week 2 — Pretérito Indefinido (Part 1)

**Session 3 — Regular Preterite**
- Grammar: Preterite formation — regular -ar: hablar → hablé/hablaste/habló/hablamos/hablasteis/hablaron; -er/-ir: comer → comí/comiste/comió/comimos/comisteis/comieron; key time markers: ayer, anteayer, la semana pasada, el mes pasado, el año pasado, hace dos días, esta mañana (in past context)
- Speaking: Narrating recent events; sequencing: primero, después, luego, por último, finalmente
- Session-end goal: Student can narrate a past-day sequence using regular preterite verbs

**Session 4 — High-Frequency Irregular Preterites**
- Grammar: The irregular group that does NOT follow a pattern and must be memorized: ser/ir → fui/fuiste/fue/fuimos/fuisteis/fueron (identical forms!); tener → tuve/tuviste/tuvo/tuvimos/tuvisteis/tuvieron; hacer → hice/hiciste/hizo/hicimos/hicisteis/hicieron (note: hizo not *hice for 3rd sing.); estar → estuve; poder → pude; querer → quise; saber → supe; venir → vine; decir → dije/dijiste/dijo/dijimos/dijisteis/dijeron; traer → traje/trajiste/trajo/trajimos/trajisteis/trajeron
- Speaking: Storytelling using irregular verbs; past weekend / past trip narration
- **Note:** This session has high memory load. Prioritize ser/ir, hacer, tener, estar, and decir as the top 5. The others can be reinforced in subsequent sessions.
- Session-end goal: Student knows and can produce the 10 most common irregular preterites

---

### Week 3 — Pretérito (Part 2)

**Session 5 — Stem-Changing and Spelling-Change Preterites**
- Grammar: Stem-changing -ir verbs in preterite (only 3rd person singular and plural): dormir → durmió/durmieron; pedir → pidió/pidieron; sentir → sintió/sintieron; preferir → prefirió/prefirieron; servir → sirvió/sirvieron; spelling-change verbs in 1st person singular: leer → leí but leyó/leyeron; creer → creí but creyó/creyeron; caer → caí but cayó/cayeron; oír → oí but oyó/oyeron
- Speaking: Narrating stories; sequences with connectors; combining regular + irregular verbs
- Session-end goal: Student can use stem-changing preterites correctly in 3rd person

**Session 6 — Spain vs. Latin America: Present Perfect**
- Grammar: Regional note: Spain uses present perfect (he comido esta mañana, ¿has visto la película?) where most of Latin America uses preterite (comí esta mañana, ¿viste la película?). Present perfect formation: haber (he/has/ha/hemos/habéis/han) + past participle; irregular past participles: ver → visto, hacer → hecho, escribir → escrito, abrir → abierto, volver → vuelto, romper → roto, decir → dicho, poner → puesto
- Speaking: Practice both registers; recognizing which one to use based on your interlocutor; cultural awareness
- Session-end goal: Student can recognize and produce present perfect forms and understands regional distribution

---

### Week 4 — Imperfecto

**Session 7 — Imperfecto Formation**
- Grammar: Imperfecto formation: -ar verbs → -aba/-abas/-aba/-ábamos/-abais/-aban; -er/-ir verbs → -ía/-ías/-ía/-íamos/-íais/-ían; only three irregular imperfects (they must be memorized): ser → era/eras/era/éramos/erais/eran; ir → iba/ibas/iba/íbamos/ibais/iban; ver → veía/veías/veía/veíamos/veíais/veían; uses: habitual/repeated past action (used to), ongoing state/background, age, weather, time in past
- Speaking: Childhood memories and habitual past: Cuando era niño/a..., Siempre íbamos a..., Había un parque cerca de mi casa...
- Session-end goal: Student can form imperfecto correctly and use it for habitual and descriptive contexts

**Session 8 — Imperfecto for Description and Ongoing States**
- Grammar: Imperfecto for: ongoing state or emotion in past (tenía hambre, estaba cansado/a), description of a scene (Hacía sol, La ciudad era tranquila), background for an event (ongoing action interrupted by preterite event — will be formalized in Week 5)
- Speaking: Describing a scene from the past; photo description; cultural note: childhood and family experiences across Spanish-speaking cultures
- Session-end goal: Student can use imperfecto to paint a detailed picture of the past

---

### Week 5 — Pretérito vs. Imperfecto: The Core Distinction

**Session 9 — Foreground vs. Background**
- Grammar: The essential rule: **pretérito = completed event (foreground); imperfecto = background, habit, state, or description**. Classic construction: imperfecto scene + preterite event: Cuando llegué, todos dormían. Hacía frío cuando salimos. Comía cuando sonó el teléfono.
- Speaking: Paired narrative building; building a story in stages: scene (imperfecto) → events (pretérito)
- Session-end goal: Student can narrate a 10-sentence story applying the pretérito/imperfecto contrast correctly

**Session 10 — Narration Practice and Error Correction**
- Focus: Targeted practice on the most common errors; preterite for all past → imperfect for completed single events; cuándo + preterite vs. cuando + imperfecto/preterite patterns
- Speaking: Retelling a film plot or personal anecdote; peer correction; comparing correct and incorrect versions
- Session-end goal: Student can self-correct the most common pretérito/imperfecto errors in their own production

---

### Week 6 — Future Tense

**Session 11 — Futuro Simple: Formation**
- Grammar: Futuro simple = infinitivo + -é/-ás/-á/-emos/-éis/-án; irregular stems (same as condicionaL): tener → tendr-, poder → podr-, hacer → har-, salir → saldr-, venir → vendr-, querer → querr-, decir → dir-, saber → sabr-, haber → habr-, poner → pondr-, valer → valdr-; predictions and distant plans; time expressions: mañana, la próxima semana, en el futuro, algún día, dentro de cinco años
- Speaking: Plans and predictions; Mañana estaré...; Cuando sea mayor, tendré...
- Session-end goal: Student can conjugate futuro simple for all verbs including irregulars

**Session 12 — Futuro Simple in Use; Si + Present → Future**
- Grammar: Real conditional: Si + present indicative → futuro simple: Si tengo tiempo, estudiaré; Si llueve, no saldremos; comparing ir a + infinitivo (near, certain) vs. futuro simple (projection, prediction, distant future); futuro of probability: ¿Qué hora será? — used for present guessing
- Speaking: Life goals discussion; si-clause real conditions; planning conversation
- Session-end goal: Student uses futuro simple in predictions, real si-clauses, and future probability

---

### Week 7 — Work and Professional Life

**Session 13 — Workplace Vocabulary and Professional Register**
- Grammar: Usted forms in professional register; professional formal phrases; past tense use in professional narrative
- Speaking: Professional history using preterite and imperfect: Trabajé en..., La empresa era...; workplace interaction scenarios: una reunión, un proyecto, un plazo, el sueldo, las vacaciones
- Vocabulary: Workplace vocabulary (~40 words); professional role titles; formal phrases
- Session-end goal: Student can discuss professional history and career in past tenses

**Session 14 — Job Interview Role Play**
- Grammar: Preterite and imperfect for professional narrative; ability and experience expressions
- Speaking: Full job interview in Spanish: ¿Qué experiencia tiene? ¿Cuáles son sus fortalezas? ¿Por qué quiere trabajar aquí?; usted forms throughout; professional register
- Session-end goal: Student can conduct a basic Spanish-language job interview

---

### Week 8 — Travel and Accommodation

**Session 15 — Booking and Travel Vocabulary**
- Grammar: Preterite and imperfect in travel contexts; estar verbs (ir, llegar, salir, volver) reviewed
- Speaking: Booking a hotel by phone: Quisiera reservar una habitación doble para dos noches, ¿está incluido el desayuno?; travel problems: Perdí la maleta, El vuelo se retrasó, No había habitaciones disponibles
- Vocabulary: Travel vocabulary (~40 words); hotel, airport, train station vocabulary; regional: billete/boleto, vuelo, equipaje
- Session-end goal: Student can book accommodation and navigate travel scenarios in Spanish

**Session 16 — Travel Story in Mixed Tenses**
- Grammar: Preterite + imperfect in combined travel narrative; discourse connectors reinforced
- Speaking: Describing a past trip: what happened (preterite) + what it was like (imperfect); authentic adapted travel blog reading
- Vocabulary: Travel problems; complaint vocabulary
- Session-end goal: Student can narrate a complete travel experience using both past tenses

---

### Week 9 — Object Pronouns

**Session 17 — Direct Object Pronouns**
- Grammar: Direct object pronouns: me/te/lo/la/nos/os/los/las; position: before conjugated verb, or attached to infinitive/gerund; lo/la agreement with noun gender; common error: *lo vi a ella → correct is: la vi; in past narration with preterite: agreement only applies for a prior direct object in another clause (not triggered the same way as French PC agreement — clarify this explicitly for students who did French)
- Speaking: Avoiding repetition: ¿Viste la película? → Sí, la vi; paired drills in context
- Session-end goal: Student can replace direct object nouns with pronouns and place them correctly

**Session 18 — Indirect Object Pronouns; Double Pronouns**
- Grammar: Indirect object pronouns: me/te/le/nos/os/les; le/les → se when preceding lo/la/los/las (the pronoun combination rule): *Le lo di → Se lo di; position same as direct pronouns; high-frequency patterns: se lo dije, se la doy, me lo explicó
- Speaking: Retelling transactions and gifts; J'y vais → Voy allí (note: Spanish does not have a direct equivalent to French y; address this if students did French); natural pronoun use in conversation
- Session-end goal: Student can use indirect object pronouns and the le → se combination rule correctly

---

### Week 10 — Media, Culture, and Regional Vocabulary

**Session 19 — Discussing Film, Music, and Books**
- Grammar: Relative clauses que introduced (subject and object): La película que vi era muy buena; preterite for plot summary; recommendation expressions: te recomiendo, vale la pena ver/leer, merece la pena
- Speaking: Describing a film, book, or album; giving and receiving recommendations; Spanish and Latin American cultural touchpoints
- Vocabulary: Media and entertainment vocabulary
- Session-end goal: Student can discuss and recommend cultural content in Spanish

**Session 20 — Regional Vocabulary in Everyday Life**
- Grammar: No new grammar — consolidation session
- Focus: Key regional vocabulary differences (everyday items — not an exhaustive list): el coche/el carro/el auto; el piso/el departamento/el apartamento; el móvil/el celular; coger/tomar; vosotros/ustedes; el ordenador/la computadora; el zumo/el jugo; el autobús/el camión/la guagua
- Speaking: Short authentic Spanish-language media clip (one from Spain, one from Latin America); discussion comparing registers and vocabulary
- Session-end goal: Student recognizes the most common regional vocabulary pairs and is not confused when encountering an unfamiliar regional form

---

### Week 11 — Storytelling and Indirect Speech

**Session 21 — Anecdote Structure and Discourse Connectors**
- Grammar: All past tenses in combination; anecdote connectors: de repente, en ese momento, resulta que, al final, lo que pasa es que, para colmo, total que, a estas alturas
- Speaking: Anecdote structure: scene-setting (imperfecto) → events (pretérito) → outcome/reflection; student stories and cultural anecdotes
- Session-end goal: Student can tell a personal anecdote using both past tenses with natural connectors

**Session 22 — Indirect Speech**
- Grammar: Indirect speech: dijo que + imperfecto (tense shift from present); preguntó si + imperfecto; me pidió que... (introduced as a formula at A2 — subjunctive will be formalized in B1); pronoun combination review: se lo di a María
- Speaking: Retelling conversations; group storytelling; reporting news
- Session-end goal: Student can report what someone said using basic tense-shift patterns

---

### Week 12 — Review and Final Performance

**Session 23 — Integration and Error Repair**
- Focus: Extended role plays combining all A2 structures; targeted error analysis; pronunciation and fluency coaching
- Speaking: Multi-skill scenarios: job interview + travel story + opinion discussion

**Session 24 — Final Assessment and B1 Preview**
- Final performance tasks (see below)
- Preview of B1: subjunctive, conditional, and complex sentences
- Independent study resources

## Readiness Criteria for B1

A student is ready for B1 when they can:

- [ ] Narrate a past sequence of events using preterite correctly
- [ ] Describe habitual past and background using imperfecto
- [ ] Distinguish pretérito from imperfecto appropriately in narration
- [ ] Discuss future plans using futuro simple (not only ir a)
- [ ] Express and defend an opinion with 2–3 sentences of reasoning
- [ ] Handle workplace, travel, and health scenarios without preparation
- [ ] Write a connected 12–15 sentence text with appropriate tense use

## Instructor Notes

- **Irregular preterites (Session 4) have higher memory load in Spanish than in French.** The French PC has ~15 common irregular past participles; Spanish has more. Prioritize ser/ir (same forms), hacer, tener, estar, poder, querer, venir, decir, traer — these 10 cover the vast majority of real usage. Distribute practice across Sessions 4–10 rather than front-loading.
- **Ser and ir share identical preterite forms:** fui/fuiste/fue... Context disambiguates. This is surprising to students — address it proactively.
- **Hacer 3rd person preterite is hizo, not hice.** This is a consistent error that slips through if not explicitly flagged.
- **Pretérito vs. imperfecto (Weeks 5+):** Students will understand the rule before they apply it spontaneously — same as French A2. Expect production errors to persist for several weeks. Prioritize correct instinct over perfect accuracy by Week 12.
- **Present perfect and regional variation (Session 6):** Do not treat this as a minor note. A student who says comí esta mañana in Spain will be understood but will immediately be identified as a Latin American speaker (or a non-native). Neither is wrong; both should be in the student's recognition range.
- **Regional vocabulary (Session 20):** This session should feel light and interesting, not overwhelming. The goal is awareness, not memorization. A short Spain-vs-Latin America content comparison (two clips, same topic) makes this concrete.
