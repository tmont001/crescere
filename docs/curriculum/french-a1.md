# French A1 — Beginner

**Language:** French
**Level:** A1 (CEFR Beginner)
**Course ID:** fr-a1
**Status:** Approved draft — not yet taught

---

## Course Purpose

Builds a complete foundation for students who have never studied French or who are returning after years away. The course is built around speaking from Session 1. Grammar is introduced in the order it unlocks conversation — greetings, then identity, then daily life, then navigating the world — rather than by textbook chapter logic. Students leave with enough French to handle real situations and enough confidence to keep going.

---

## Ideal Student Profile

Absolute beginner or lapsed learner. Motivated by travel, cultural connection, heritage, career, or personal interest. May have studied French briefly in school but retains little. Wants to speak, not just recognize words.

---

## Prerequisites

None.

---

## End-of-Course Outcomes

By the end of French A1, students can:

- [ ] Use être and avoir correctly in present tense
- [ ] Conjugate regular -er, -ir, -re verbs and the core irregulars: aller, venir, faire, prendre, pouvoir, vouloir, devoir, savoir, connaître
- [ ] Handle transactional exchanges: introductions, café orders, directions, shopping
- [ ] Describe themselves, their family, daily routine, home, and city
- [ ] Use numbers, dates, time expressions, and the days/months accurately
- [ ] Form basic questions and negatives
- [ ] Use aller + infinitif for near future; devoir/pouvoir/vouloir + infinitif for obligation and ability
- [ ] Use partitive articles in food and drink contexts
- [ ] Understand slow, clear spoken French on familiar topics
- [ ] Write correct simple sentences on familiar topics (8–10 sentences)

## 24-Session Breakdown

### Week 1 — Sounds and Greetings

**Session 1 — Sounds, Alphabet, First Words**
- Grammar: Être (suis/es/est/sommes/êtes/sont) — full conjugation introduced
- Speaking: Pronunciation: nasal vowels, liaison, the silent -e, h aspiré vs. h muet; formal (vous) vs. informal (tu) distinction; alphabet with French letter names; numbers 1–30
- Vocabulary: Greetings and leave-takings (formal and informal)
- Session-end goal: Student can greet someone, give their name, count to 30, and say the alphabet

**Session 2 — Introductions and First Conversations**
- Grammar: Être for identity and origin; question formation with est-ce que and inversion (light introduction); tu vs. vous reviewed
- Speaking: Meeting-someone dialogue: Je m'appelle..., J'habite à..., Je suis de..., Je suis [profession/nationality]...; numbers 30–100
- Vocabulary: Nationalities; common professions; question words (comment, où, d'où)
- Session-end goal: Student can introduce themselves completely and ask basic questions

---

### Week 2 — Describing Yourself and Others

**Session 3 — Avoir and Physical Description**
- Grammar: Avoir (ai/as/a/avons/avez/ont) — full conjugation; avoir for age (j'ai vingt ans)
- Speaking: Describing appearance: J'ai les yeux bleus, Il est grand, Elle a les cheveux noirs; comparing descriptions
- Vocabulary: Physical description adjectives; basic color vocabulary
- Session-end goal: Student can describe a person's appearance and give ages

**Session 4 — Adjective Agreement and Personality**
- Grammar: Adjective agreement: gender (grand/grande) and number (grand/grands/grande/grandes); position (most adjectives follow noun; BAGS adjectives precede)
- Speaking: Personality descriptions: Il est sympa, Elle est sérieuse, Ils sont drôles; describing classmates or family
- Vocabulary: Personality adjectives (~20); profession vocabulary expanded
- Session-end goal: Student can describe people's character with correct adjective agreement

---

### Week 3 — Regular Verbs and Daily Routine

**Session 5 — Regular -er Verbs**
- Grammar: Regular -er conjugation pattern; subject pronouns reviewed; negation: ne...pas
- Speaking: Common -er verbs in context: parler, habiter, travailler, étudier, aimer, écouter, regarder, voyager; sentence building from prompt
- Vocabulary: Daily activities vocabulary
- Session-end goal: Student can produce 8+ -er verb sentences with negation

**Session 6 — Negation, Frequency, and Daily Routine**
- Grammar: ne...pas in full practice (spoken French note: ne often dropped); jamais as negation; frequency adverbs (toujours, souvent, quelquefois, jamais, rarement); reflexive verbs introduced as phrases only — *je me lève, je me couche* — full conjugation and reflexive pronoun placement deferred to Week 4
- Speaking: Daily routine narration in 4–6 sentences using sequencing words and time expressions; ne...pas and jamais in first-person sentences
- Vocabulary: Sequencing words (d'abord, puis, ensuite, enfin); time-of-day expressions (le matin, l'après-midi, le soir); daily activity vocabulary
- Session-end goal: Student can narrate their daily routine in 4–6 sentences and use 3–4 frequency expressions

---

### Week 4 — Reflexive Verbs and Daily Routine

**Session 7 — Reflexive Verbs: Introduction**
- Grammar: Reflexive verbs — concept and reflexive pronoun table (me/te/se/nous/vous/se); priority forms: je me..., tu te..., il/elle se...; elision before vowels: me + vowel/h muet → m' (je m'appelle, je m'habille — not je me appelle); core verbs: se lever, se coucher, se préparer, s'appeler, se doucher
- Speaking: First-person routine sentences using reflexive verbs; extend to tu and il/elle forms; speaking round — each student produces at least 3 first-person reflexive routine sentences
- Vocabulary: Daily routine reflexive verbs (se lever, se coucher, se préparer, se doucher, s'habiller, se laver)
- Session-end goal: Student can produce first-person reflexive sentences for daily routine with correct pronoun placement — je me, je m' (before vowel)

**Session 8 — Full Routine Narration**
- Grammar: Reflexive verbs extended to sequenced narration; basic clock times in context (à + heure, il est... heures, vers + heure — 5–6 examples); describing another person's routine with il/elle se...; full time-telling system (half-hours, quarter-hours, 24h format), possessive adjectives, and family vocabulary are deferred
- Speaking: Complete morning-to-evening routine narration in 5–6 sentences mixing reflexive and -er verbs; at least one clock time included; third-person routine (il/elle se lève à...); speaking round — every student narrates their full day
- Vocabulary: Sequencing words (d'abord, puis, ensuite, enfin) used with reflexive verbs; basic clock-time expressions (à sept heures, vers midi, le soir)
- Session-end goal: Student can narrate their complete daily routine in 5+ sentences using reflexive and -er verbs with correct pronoun placement throughout

---

### Week 5 — Getting Around

**Session 9 — City Places and Prepositions**
- Grammar: Prepositions of place: à droite, à gauche, tout droit, en face de, à côté de, près de, loin de, au bout de; article contraction à + le = au, à + les = aux
- Speaking: City vocabulary in context; giving and following directions; Où est...? / Pour aller à...?
- Vocabulary: City places (la boulangerie, la pharmacie, la mairie, la gare, l'hôpital, le supermarché, la poste)
- Session-end goal: Student can ask for and give directions using prepositions

**Session 10 — Transportation and Aller**
- Grammar: Aller — irregular conjugation (vais/vas/va/allons/allez/vont); aller + au/à la/à l'/aux + place; transportation prepositions: en (voiture, avion, train, bus), à (pied, vélo)
- Speaking: Travel scenarios: Je vais à Paris en train; buying a ticket: Un aller simple / un aller-retour pour... S'il vous plaît; navigation role play in a French city
- Vocabulary: Transportation vocabulary; ticket-buying phrases
- Session-end goal: Student can discuss getting around a city using aller and transportation vocabulary

---

### Week 6 — Food and Cafés

**Session 11 — French Food Culture and Partitives**
- Grammar: Partitive articles: du (m. sing.), de la (f. sing.), de l' (before vowel), des (plural); partitive in negation → de: Je n'ai pas de café
- Speaking: French food vocabulary in context; café scenario: Qu'est-ce que vous prenez? / Je voudrais...; food preferences
- Vocabulary: French cuisine (le croissant, la baguette, le fromage, le jambon, les escargots); café drinks; menu vocabulary
- Session-end goal: Student can order confidently in a French café using partitives and je voudrais

**Session 12 — Restaurant Scenario and Vouloir/Pouvoir**
- Grammar: Vouloir (veux/veux/veut/voulons/voulez/veulent); pouvoir (peux/peux/peut/pouvons/pouvez/peuvent) — both introduced; je voudrais (polite conditional — as a formula)
- Speaking: Full restaurant role play: ordering, asking about the menu, paying; l'addition, s'il vous plaît; cultural norms around dining
- Vocabulary: Restaurant phrases; cooking methods; menu items (plat principal, entrée, dessert)
- Session-end goal: Student can navigate a full French restaurant experience

---

### Week 7 — Shopping

**Session 13 — Clothing and Colors**
- Grammar: Demonstrative adjectives: ce/cet/cette/ces; color adjectives and agreement; large numbers for prices
- Speaking: Clothing shopping: Je cherche..., Quelle taille faites-vous?, Je peux l'essayer?; price negotiation; colors with agreement (une robe rouge, un pull bleu)
- Vocabulary: Clothing vocabulary; colors; sizes; price expressions
- Session-end goal: Student can conduct a full shopping dialogue for clothing

**Session 14 — Market and Quantities**
- Grammar: Quantity expressions: un kilo de, une bouteille de, un litre de, une douzaine de, 100 grammes de; combien de...?
- Speaking: Market scenario: buying produce, cheese, bread; comparing le supermarché vs. le marché culturally; regional French market vocabulary
- Vocabulary: Food at the market; weights and measures; market expressions
- Session-end goal: Student can shop at a French market using quantity expressions

---

### Week 8 — Home and Neighborhood

**Session 15 — Describing a Home**
- Grammar: Il y a / il n'y a pas de; prepositions of location (sur, sous, devant, derrière, entre, à côté de, en face de)
- Speaking: Home vocabulary; describing a floor plan; Il y a trois chambres, La cuisine est en face du salon
- Vocabulary: Rooms (la chambre, le salon, la cuisine, la salle de bains, le bureau); furniture; appliances
- Session-end goal: Student can describe the layout of their home

**Session 16 — Neighborhood and High-Frequency Irregular Verbs**
- Grammar: Core irregulars reviewed in depth — faire (fais/fais/fait/faisons/faites/font), venir (viens/viens/vient/venons/venez/viennent), prendre (prends/prends/prend/prenons/prenez/prennent), savoir, connaître
- Speaking: Describing your neighborhood; what you like and dislike about where you live; irregular verbs used in context
- Vocabulary: Neighborhood vocabulary; opinion phrases
- Session-end goal: Student can use all core irregular verbs in context without looking them up

---

### Week 9 — Plans and Obligations

**Session 17 — Near Future: Aller + Infinitif**
- Grammar: Aller + infinitif for near future; time expressions: demain, ce soir, la semaine prochaine, le mois prochain, l'année prochaine
- Speaking: Planning a week; making appointments; Je vais travailler demain, Nous allons dîner ensemble
- Vocabulary: Future time expressions; event vocabulary
- Session-end goal: Student can discuss upcoming plans using aller + infinitif

**Session 18 — Obligations and Ability**
- Grammar: Devoir + infinitif (obligation: je dois travailler); vouloir + infinitif (desire); pouvoir + infinitif (ability/permission); all three modal patterns reviewed and compared
- Speaking: Planning scenarios combining modals: Je dois..., Je veux..., Je ne peux pas... — describing constraints and desires
- Vocabulary: Work and commitment vocabulary; ability/inability expressions
- Session-end goal: Student can express obligation, desire, and ability using all three modal verbs

---

### Week 10 — Hobbies and Leisure

**Session 19 — Sports and Activities**
- Grammar: Jouer à + sport/game (je joue au tennis); faire de + activity (je fais du yoga, je fais de la natation, je fais du vélo); article contraction review
- Speaking: Describing hobbies; asking about leisure; frequency adverbs: toujours, souvent, quelquefois, rarement, jamais
- Vocabulary: Sports; cultural activities (le cinéma, le théâtre, le musée); outdoor activities
- Session-end goal: Student can discuss hobbies using jouer à and faire de correctly

**Session 20 — Expressing Opinions and Preferences**
- Grammar: Aimer / adorer / détester / préférer + noun or infinitif; comparison: plus que, moins que, autant que
- Speaking: Discussing preferences: J'adore le cinéma, Je préfère la montagne à la mer; opinion exchanges in pairs
- Vocabulary: Leisure and cultural vocabulary deepened
- Session-end goal: Student can express preferences and make comparisons

---

### Week 11 — Health and the Body

**Session 21 — Body Parts and Pain Expressions**
- Grammar: Avoir mal à + body part (J'ai mal à la tête, J'ai mal aux pieds); à + le = au, à + les = aux reviewed
- Speaking: Pharmacy scenario: Je ne me sens pas bien, J'ai de la fièvre, Je suis enrhumé(e), Vous avez quelque chose pour...?; describing symptoms
- Vocabulary: Body parts; common symptoms; pharmacy vocabulary
- Session-end goal: Student can describe symptoms and navigate a French pharmacy

**Session 22 — At the Doctor**
- Grammar: Devoir reinforced for advice (vous devez vous reposer); il faut + infinitif (impersonal obligation); depuis + present tense for ongoing duration
- Speaking: Doctor's appointment role play; describing symptom duration: J'ai de la fièvre depuis trois jours; medical vocabulary in context
- Vocabulary: Healthcare vocabulary; advice-giving phrases; appointment scheduling
- Session-end goal: Student can handle a basic medical consultation in French

---

### Week 12 — Review and Final Performance

**Session 23 — Integrated Role Plays and Final Performance Rehearsal**
- Focus: All A1 skills in extended scenarios; pronunciation refinement; performance preparation
- Speaking: Combined scenarios — meeting someone, making a plan, ordering food, navigating the city, shopping, describing home
- Session-end goal: Student is ready to perform the Part A spoken role play in Session 24; knows the phrase bank is available for reference during Part B

**Session 24 — Final Assessment and A2 Preview**
- Final performance tasks (see below)
- Brief introduction to what A2 will cover: talking about the past
- Resources for independent practice between A1 and A2

## Final Performance Task

**Part A — Spoken role play (5–7 minutes):**
Integrated scenario covering: meeting someone, making a plan, giving directions, describing a place. Student must deploy être/avoir, aller + infinitif, at least one modal, and partitives in natural conversation.

**Part B — Written message (8–10 sentences):**
Note to a French contact: introduce yourself, describe your daily routine, and suggest a plan for getting together.

## Readiness Criteria for A2

A student is ready for A2 when they can:

- [ ] Use être and avoir in all forms without hesitation
- [ ] Conjugate regular -er/-ir/-re verbs and the core irregulars (aller, faire, venir, prendre, pouvoir, vouloir, devoir) accurately
- [ ] Navigate 3+ transactional scenarios (café, directions, shopping) without preparation
- [ ] Describe their daily routine in 6+ sentences using reflexive and regular verbs
- [ ] Use aller + infinitif for near-future plans
- [ ] Write 8–10 grammatically sound simple sentences on a familiar topic
- [ ] Understand slow, clear spoken French on familiar topics with minimal repetition

## Instructor Notes

- **Pronunciation in Week 1 is foundational.** Nasal vowels, liaison, and the silent -e cause persistent errors if not addressed early. Spend real time here — it saves correction work later.
- **Adjective agreement (Week 2, Session 4) is often underestimated.** Students who pass over it quickly produce agreement errors for the rest of the course. The BAGS rule (Beauty, Age, Goodness, Size before noun) is a useful but imperfect heuristic — flag exceptions.
- **Partitives (Week 6) confuse students coming from English.** The food context (du café, de la bière, des croissants) is the easiest anchor. Reinforce the negation rule (pas de) immediately — students will use pas du or pas des if not corrected early.
- **Avoir mal à (Week 11):** The contraction à + le = au / à + les = aux trips many students. This is a good place to review the au/aux contraction that first appeared in Weeks 5–6.
- **High-frequency irregular verbs (Week 8, Session 16):** These must be memorized. Distributed practice over Weeks 5–16 is more effective than a single cramming session.
- **Session 24 timing:** The final performance takes the full session. Do not introduce new content in this session.
